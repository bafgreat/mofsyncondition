Abstract

Hydrothermal reactions of a divalent copper salt, adipic acid (H2adp), and a dipyridylamide coligand have afforded four new crystalline coordination polymer solids, whose topologies depend critically on the specific dipyridylamide geometric isomer. [Cu(adp)(3-pina)]n (1, 3-pina = 3-pyridylisonicotinamide) has a standard (4,4) grid layer topology, while {[Cu(adp)(3-pna)]·2.5H2O}n (2, 3-pna = 3-pyridylnicotinamide) possesses a dimer-based 3-D network with 658 cds topology. Use of 4-pyridylnicotinamide (4-pna) as coligand afforded {[Cu3(adp)3(4-pna)2]·2H2O}n (3), which manifests a unique binodal 4,6-connected self-penetrated (658)(44611) 3-D topology. {[Cu(adp)(4-pna)2]n (4) is a simple 1-D chain polymer, obtained as a minor product during the synthesis of 3. Thermal properties of these materials are also discussed, along with the variable temperature magnetic properties of 2 and 3.

1. Introduction

The preparation, structural characterization, and physical property measurements of divalent metal coordination polymer solids have been intensely studied over the past two decades. This research focus is predicated on the materials’ potential utility in gas storage [1], selective molecular separations [2], ion exchange [3], catalysis [4], laser frequency doubling [5], and for explosives residue detection [6]. Among the plethora of possible choices for anionic organic components, aromatic dicarboxylate ligands have received the most significant attention, as they can instill thermally robust networks [7] or provide chromophores for emissive properties or sensing applications [8]. Somewhat less studied have been divalent metal coordination polymers containing aliphatic dicarboxylate ligands [9], [10], [11], [12], [13], [14]. The conformational flexibility of their σ-bonded polymethylene tethers causes greater degrees of freedom during self-assembly. This in turn permits conformational adjustment in response to carboxylate binding mode and metal coordination geometry preference, synergistically affecting the dimensionality and topology of the resulting coordination polymer.

Bearing three distinct four-C atom torsion angles, adipate (adp, Scheme 1) ligands can possess varied conformations in coordination polymer crystals, from splayed-open anti-anti-anti, to “S-shaped” gauche-anti-gauche, to “staple-like” anti-gauche-anti, to twisted gauche-gauche-gauche. Some previously reported divalent metal adipate/dipyridyl coordination polymers adopt 2-fold interpenetrated pcu 41263 networks based on {M2(OCO)2} dinuclear units, such as [Co(adp)(dpe)]n [9a] (dpe = 1,2-bis(4-pyridyl)ethane) and [Co(adp)(dpa)]n [9b] (dpa = 4,4′-dipyridylamine). [Ni(adp)(dpa)(H2O)]n, on the other hand, showed an adjustment in topology because of the presence of aqua ligands and cis disposition of pyridyl nitrogen donor atoms; this material exhibited the first example of a 3-fold interpenetrated 4284 pts net [9b].

The donor disposition and length of a dipyridyl coligand imposes significant constraints on the topologies of cadmium adipate coordination polymers [10]. Incorporating the chelating and capping ligand 1,10-phenanthroline (phen), [Cd(adp)(phen)]n [10a] possesses a simple 1-D chain structure. The dipodal tethering ligands 4,4′-bipyridine (bpy) [10b] and dpa [10c] both generated 2-fold interpenetrated pcu nets with pillared [Cd(adp)]n layers, based on {Cd2(OCO)2} dimeric units. The longer dipyridyl ligand bis(4-pyridylmethyl)piperazine (bpmp) enforced large incipient voids within 4-connected 3-D diamondoid [Cd(adp)(bpmp)]n networks, allowing 5-fold interpenetration [10d]. Employment of an analogous ligand to bpmp, bis(4-pyridylformyl)piperazine (bpfp), afforded the 3-D phase {[Cd2(adp)(bpfp)2(H2O)2](ClO4)2·2H2O}n [10e]. This material exhibits {Cd2O2} dimeric units connected through bridging adp and exotridentate bpfp ligands into an uncommon (3,8)-connected tfz-d (43)2(4661884) binodal topology. The varied topologies within these previously reported adp phases indicates that the conformational fluxionality of the adp central polymethylene backbone allows response to the presence of neutral nitrogen-base coligands in divalent coordination polymer systems and to specific metal coordination environment.

The dipyridylamides 3-pyridylnicotinamide (3-pna), 3-pyridylisonicotinamide (3-pina), 4-pyridylnicotinamide (4-pna), and 4-pyridylisonicotinamide (4-pina) are easily prepared isomeric ligands (Scheme 1) whose divalent metal dicarboxylate coordination polymer chemistry has to this point remained relatively under-explored [15]. These dipyridylamides all possess internal hydrogen bonding donor and acceptor groups at their central amide linkage, which would likely exert similar supporting structure directing effects. Although synthetic predictability remains an elusive overarching goal in coordination polymer chemistry, a systematic study of nitrogen donor disposition could possibly reveal structure-directing trends in a series of complexes containing the same divalent metal ion and dicarboxylate ligand. Varying nitrogen donor disposition across the dipyridylamide series is expected to instill significant structural diversity in a particular dual-ligand coordination polymer system. Our recent work with these dipyridyalmides in a cadmium adipate system illustrates this effect [15a]. {[Cd2(adp)(nic)2(3-pna)2]·6H2O}n, in which some of the 3-pna precursor underwent in situ hydrolysis, displays a 5-connected 4664 bnn 3-D network. Use of other isomeric dipyridylamides resulted in a layered phase in {[Cd(adp)(3-pina)]·H2O}n or a simple 1-D chain compound {[Cd(adp)(4-pna)2(H2O)2]·2H2O}n.

We thus sought to extend this work into the preparation a series of copper adipate coordination polymers containing the isomeric dipyridylamides. Although 4-pina failed to yield any isolable coordination polymers, use of 3-pna, 3-pina, or 4-pna resulted in the successful preparation and structural characterization of four new crystalline solids: [Cu(adp)(3-pina)]n (1), {[Cu(adp)(3-pna)]·2.5H2O}n (2), and {[Cu3(adp)3(4-pna)2]·2H2O}n (3), along with the minor by-product {[Cu(adp)(4-pna)2]n (4). Thermal properties of 1–3 were also investigated, along with variable temperature magnetic susceptibility properties of copper dimer-based phases 2 and 3.

2. Experimental

2.1. General considerations

Copper salts and adipic acid were purchased commercially. The dipyridylamides 3-pna, 3-pina, 4-pna, and 4-pina were prepared by a published procedure [16]. Water was deionized above 3 MΩ-cm in-house. IR spectra were recorded on polycrystalline samples using a Perkin Elmer Spectrum One DRIFT instrument. Variable temperature magnetic susceptibility data (2–300 K) was collected on a Quantum Design MPMS SQUID magnetometer at an applied field of 0.1 T. After each temperature change the sample was kept at the new temperature for five minutes before magnetization measurement to ensure thermal equilibrium. The susceptibility data was corrected for diamagnetism using Pascal’s constants [17], and for the diamagnetism of the sample holder. Thermogravimetric analysis was performed on a TA Instruments high-resolution Q500 thermal analyzer under flowing N2. Powder X-ray diffraction on ground samples of 1–3 were obtained viaΘ–2Θ scans performed on a Rigaku Rotaflex instrument.

3. X-ray crystallography

Single crystal X-ray diffraction was performed using a Bruker-AXS ApexII CCD instrument at 173 K. Diffraction data for 1–4 was acquired using graphite–monochromated Mo Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å). The data was integrated via saint [18]. Lorentz and polarization effect and empirical absorption corrections were applied with sadabs [19] (1, 3, 4) or twinabs [20] (2). The structures were solved using direct methods and refined on F2 using shelxtl [21]. The crystal of 2 was non-merohedrally twinned; its twin laws were found using CELL NOW [22]. For 2, the structure was refined using only the reflections from the major twin component, and the adipate ligand disorder was modeled successfully using partial occupancies. For 3, although some very high angle data was incomplete, the data out to Θ = 25° was 100% complete. All non-hydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically. Hydrogen atoms bound to carbon atoms were placed in calculated positions and refined isotropically with a riding model. The hydrogen atoms bound to the amide nitrogen atoms and water molecule oxygen atoms were found by Fourier difference map where possible, and refined with restrained distances and isotropic thermal parameters. 4. Results and discussion

4.1. Synthesis and spectral characterization

Compounds 1–3 were prepared as crystalline products by hydrothermal reaction of a divalent copper salt, adipic acid, and the requisite dipyridylamide, under basic conditions. Compound 4 was obtained as a minor product during the synthesis of 3; the few crystals of 4 were easily manually separated because of the difference in color. Further synthetic efforts to produce a monophasic sample of 4 proved unsuccessful. Powder XRD spectra of as-synthesized samples of 1–3 are shown in Figs. S1–S3. Compound 2 was formed along with a small amount of a flocculent minor product, as evidenced by some extra impurity peaks in the PXRD spectrum. The impurity phase could be removed by flotation in water and decantation. Multiple attempts at hydrothermal synthesis of a related coordination polymer containing 4-pina resulted only in amide hydrolysis and crystallization of the known coordination complex [Cu(isonicotinate)2(H2O)4] [23]; no crystalline products could be obtained in room temperature solvent layering attempts.

The infrared spectra of 1–3 were consistent with their structural characteristics as determined by single-crystal X-ray diffraction. Medium intensity bands in the range of ∼1600 to ∼1200 cm−1 can be ascribed to stretching modes of the pyridyl rings within the ligands. Puckering modes of the pyridyl rings are evident in the region between 820 and 600 cm−1. Asymmetric and symmetric C–O stretching modes of the fully deprotonated adipate ligands were visible at 1552 and 1383 cm−1 (for 1), 1552 and 1388 cm−1 (for 2), and 1600 and 1415 cm−1 (for 3). The carbonyl stretching bands for dipyridylamide ligands were evident as sharp features at 1681 cm–1 (1), at 1685 cm−1 (2), and at 1689 cm–1 (for 3). Broad features in the vicinity of 3200 cm−1 represent any O–H stretching bands for the aqua ligands and water molecules of crystallization and N–H groups within the dipyridylamide ligands.

4.2. Structural description of [Cu(adp)(3-pina)]n (1)

The asymmetric unit of compound 1 contains two divalent copper atoms on crystallographic inversion centers (Cu1, Cu2), an adp ligand, and a 3-pina ligand. Both crystallographically distinct copper atoms show {CuN2O2} square planar coordination environments with trans 3-pina pyridyl nitrogen donor atoms and trans oxygen atom donors from monodentate adp carboxylate groups (Fig. 1a). Bond lengths and angles within the coordination environments are standard for square planar coordinated divalent copper (Table 2).

The adp ligands in 1 adopt an anti–anti–gauche conformation (torsion angles = 173.2°, 174.7°, 62.1°). They connect Cu1 and Cu2 atoms with a μ2-κ2O:O″ bridging mode, and form [Cu(adp)]n chains down [11¯1] with a Cu⋯Cu distance of 8.217 Å (Fig. 1b). Long-range “4 + 2” interactions between unligated adp oxygen atoms and the copper atoms (2.594, 2.600 Å) stabilize these chain motifs. In turn these [Cu(adp)]n chains are linked into standard [Cu(adp)(3-pina)]n (4,4) grid coordination polymer layers (Fig. 1c) by dipodal 3-pina ligands, which span a Cu⋯Cu distance of 11.664 Å. The apertures within the grid are extremely pinched, with through-space Cu⋯Cu distances measuring 5.974 Å × 19.272 Å and Cu⋯Cu⋯Cu angles of 28.9° and 151.1°. Hydrogen bonding donation from 3-pina N–H groups to unligated adp oxygen atoms (Table S1, ESI) provides an ancillary stabilization for the layer motif. Adjacent layers stack along the b crystal direction by means of C–H⋯O non-classical supramolecular interactions between pyridyl rings and formyl groups of the 3-pina ligands (Fig. S4).

These dimers show Cu⋯Cu and O⋯O distances of 3.431 and 2.773 Å, and a Cu–O–Cu angle of 102.39°. They are linked by the full span of pairs of anti–gauche–anti conformation adp ligands to construct [Cu2(adp)2]n chains that are aligned along the [1 0 1] crystal direction (Fig. 2b). Each [Cu2(adp)2]n chain connects to two others via pairs of trans conformation 3-pna ligands, that span a Cu⋯Cu distance of 11.850 Å. As an alternate perspective, individual {Cu2O2} dimeric units can be seen to form [Cu2(O)2(3-pna)2]n chain motifs that are oriented parallel to the [1 1 0] and [11¯0] crystal directions (Fig. 2c). Through adp ligand pairs and 3-pna ligand pairs, the {Cu2O2} dimers are connected into a 3-D [Cu(adp)(3-pna)]n coordination polymer net (Fig. 3), which can be simplified as a non-interpenetrated 4-connected 658 cds topology (Fig. 4) when individual {Cu2O2} dimers are treated as connecting nodes. While there are several known coordination polymers that adopt the cds topology [25], it is a much rarer 4-connected net than the more common 66 dia diamondoid net [26].

Incipient 1-D channels aligned along [1 0 1] and filled with co-crystallized water molecules are evident within the 3-D coordination polymer lattice of 2. Oriented towards these channels are 3-pna N–H groups and adp carboxylate oxygen atoms. These moieties engage in hydrogen bonding with disordered water molecules chains, which occupy 10.8% of the unit cell volume according to PLATON [27]. 4.4. Structural description of {[Cu3(adp)3(4-pna)2]·2H2O}n (3)

The asymmetric unit of compound 3 contains two divalent copper atoms (Cu1, Cu2), one of which (Cu2) is sited on a crystallographic inversion center, halves of three adp ligands (adp-A, adp-B, adp-C) whose centroids are situated on crystallographic inversion centers, a 4-pna ligand, and a water molecule of crystallization. A Jahn–Teller elongated, nearly ideal {CuNO4} square pyramidal coordination environment (τ = 0.007) is observed at Cu1, with trans oxygen atom donors from two adp-A ligands and trans oxygen atom donors from two adp-B ligands in the basal plane; a nicotinamide nitrogen donor atom from a 4-pna ligand occupies the apical position. In contrast, a {CuN2O2} square planar coordination environment is seen at the special position Cu2 atom, with trans 4-pyridyl nitrogen atom donors from two 4-pna ligands and trans oxygen atom donors from two adp-C ligands. The adp-A and adp-B ligands act as exotetradentate ligands with a μ4-κ4O:O′:O″:O″′ bridging mode and thereby connect the {Cu2(OCO)4} dimers into [Cu2(adp)2]n (4,4) grid coordination polymer layers (Fig. 6a), that are arranged parallel to the ac crystal planes. Different conformations are observed for adp-A and adp-B, with an anti–anti–anti conformation for adp-A (torsion angles 177.7°, 180°, 177.7°) and a gauche-anti-gauche conformation for adp-B (torsion angles 68.1°, 180°, 68.1°). Because of the more twisted conformation of the latter, the centroid-to-centroid distance between {Cu2(OCO)4} dimers along the adp-A ligands (9.55 Å) is significantly shorter than the comparable distance along the adp-B ligands (11.18 Å). These values denote the a and c lattice parameters, respectively.

Coursing parallel to the b crystal axis and through all of the apertures within the Cu1-based [Cu2(adp)2]n (4,4) layers are [Cu(adp)]n chains (Fig. 6b), formed by Cu2 atoms and bis(chelating) μ2-κ4O,O′:O″,O″′ anti-anti-anti conformation adp-C ligands (torsion angles = 179.5°, 180°, 179.5°). The Cu2-based [Cu(adp)]n chains and Cu-1 based [Cu2(adp)2]n (4,4) layers are connected by dipodal 4-pna ligands that span a Cu⋯Cu distance of 12.556 Å. Through the connectivity provided by the 4-pna ligands, a 3-D [Cu3(adp)3(4-pna)2]n coordination polymer network is formed (Fig. 7).

Treating the Cu1-based {Cu2(OCO)4} dimers as 6-connected nodes and individual Cu2 atoms as 4-connected nodes reveals that the underlying network of 3 is an unprecedented yet simple 4,6-connected binodal self-penetrated (658)(44611) topology (Fig. 8). The 4-pna ligands conjoin the 1D + 2D → 3D interpenetrated system of [Cu(adp)]n chains and [Cu2(adp)2]n (4,4) layers, thereby forcing self-penetration. The full topological analysis output generated with TOPOS software [28] is given in the Supplementary information. The water molecules of crystallization are anchored to the self-penetrated network by accepting hydrogen bonds from 4-pna N–H moieties and donating hydrogen bonds to unligated adp-C carboxylate oxygen atoms within the Cu2 based [Cu(adp)]n chains.

4.5. Structural description of {[Cu(adp)(4-pna)2]n (4)

The asymmetric unit of compound 4 contains a divalent copper atom on a crystallographic inversion center, half of an adp ligand whose centroid rests on another crystallographic inversion center, and a 4-pna ligand. Operation of the symmetry reveals a {CuN2O2} square planar coordination environment, with trans 4-pyridyl donors from the N-side of two 4-pna ligands, and trans oxygen donors from two adp ligands (Fig. 9a). Long range “4 + 2” interactions between the copper atoms and unligated adp ligands are present (Cu⋯O distance = 2.567 Å) above and below the square planar coordination sphere. 4.6. Magnetic properties of 2 and 3

The variable temperature data for 2 fit the Curie–Weiss law over the entire temperature range (Fig. S6), with C = 0.376 cm3 K mol−1 and Θ = 1.07 K, portending weak ferromagnetic coupling within the {Cu2O2} dimeric units. The χmT value at 300 K was 0.373 cm3 K mol−1, consistent with the value expected for a S = 1/2 d9 copper atom with g = 2 (0.375 cm3 K mol−1). The χmT product increased slightly upon cooling, reaching 0.379 cm3 K mol−1 at 155 K, 0.388 cm3 K mol−1 at 70 K, and 0.396 cm3 K mol−1 at 17 K. Below this temperature, the χmT product increased more rapidly, finally reaching 0.440 cm3 K mol−1 at 2 K. The data were fit to the Bleaney–Bowers expression [29] for a Heisenberg pair of S = 1/2 spins (Eq. (1)). The best fit to the data, plotted in Fig. 10, gave g = 2.021(2) and J = 1.06(4) cm−1 with R = 2.4 × 10−5 = {∑[(χmT)obs – (χmT)calc]2/∑[(χmT)obs]2}. Deviations from idealized square pyramidal geometry provide enough overlap of the magnetic dx2–y2 orbitals via the apical-basal bridging mode to cause the weak ferromagnetic superexchange.

The variable temperature data for 3 fit the Curie–Weiss law, but rather poorly, from 300 to 90 K range (Fig. S7), with C = 1.96 cm3 K mol−1 and Θ = −244 K, indicating strong antiferromagnetic coupling within the {Cu2(OCO)4} paddlewheel dimers. The χmT value at 300 K was 1.117 cm3 K mol−1, consistent with the value expected for three isolated S = 1/2 spins with g = 2 (1.125 cm3 K mol−1). The χmT product decreased rapidly upon cooling, reaching 0.783 cm3 K mol−1 at 175 K, 0.584 cm3 K mol−1 at 100 K, 0.499 cm3 K mol−1 at 60 K. Decreasing more slowly below this temperature, the value reached 0.455 cm3 K mol−1 at 20 K, and 0.428 cm3 K mol−1 at 2 K. The residual χmT value at the minimum temperature of the experiment is ascribed to the presence of the isolated copper ions within the structure of 3. To account for these isolated S = 1/2 paramagnetic ions, a modified Bleaney–Bowers equation (Eq. (2)) was utilized to fit the variable temperature magnetic susceptibility data for 3. The best fit values were g = 2.21(3), g′ = 2.203(7) and J = −233(6) cm−1 with R = 5.4 × 10−4 (Fig. 11). 4.7. Thermogravimetric analysis

Thermogravimetric analyses were carried out on compounds 1–3 to investigate their degradation behavior. The mass of compound 1 remained largely constant until 225 °C, whereupon ligand combustion occurred. At the experiment conclusion temperature of 600 °C, the mass remnant of 20.9% corresponds roughly to a deposition of CuO (19.6%). Dehydration of compound 2 occurred in three stages between 25 °C and 205 °C. The mass loss of 8.2% was slightly less than predicted (10.0% for 2.5 molar equivalents of water), likely indicating some dehydration on long term storage (∼60 d). Ligand combustion took place above 205 °C, with a final mass remnant of 18.8% at 600 °C corresponding to a deposition of CuO (17.6% calc’d). Compound 3 underwent dehydration between 25 and 235 °C, with a mass of loss of 3.9% roughly consistent with that expected for ejection of two molar equivalents of water (3.4%). Ligand ejection occurred above 235 °C, with a final mass remnant of 23.1% corresponding to a deposition of CuO (22.6% calc’d). Thermograms for 1–3 are depicted in Figs. S8–S10, ESI.

5. Conclusions

The significant conformational flexibility and divergent possible carboxylate binding modes of adipate ligands has allowed the self-assembly and crystallization of a series of copper coordination polymers containing three isomeric dipyridylamide ligands. Seemingly subtle shifts in nitrogen donor disposition within the dipyridylamide coligands resulted in wholesale changes in coordination polymer topology. Although 3-pina and 4-pna have similar kinked nitrogen donor orientations, they resulted in a standard (4,4) grid topology in 1 and a simple binodal self-penetrated topology in 3, respectively. The longer span of the 4-pna linkers in 3 permits self-penetration via connection of the 1D chain and 2D paddlewheel dimer-containing layer motifs. The trans conformation 3-pna ligands in 2 promoted formation of a 4-connected cds net based on dimeric clusters.

The adipate tether conformation and carboxylate binding mode plays a predominant role in dictating the magnetic properties of this series of coordination polymers. The anti–anti–gauche adipate conformation in 1 connects isolated copper atoms. While this was not directly tested, it is fully expected that the long distance between neighboring unpaired spins would result in simple paramagnetic behavior. Within 2, pairs of anti–gauche–anti conformation adipate ligands promote formation of apical-basal bridged {Cu2O2} dimeric units that exhibit weak ferromagnetism. The situation in 3 is more complicated, with anti–anti–anti and gauche–anti–gauche bis(chelating) adipate ligands forming strongly antiferromagnetically coupled paddlewheel dimer-based layers, and other anti–anti–anti conformation adipate ligands constructing chain motifs with isolated copper atoms.

Within this system of four copper adipate coordination polymers, two have common grid or chain topologies, one has a less common non-diamondoid 4-connected topology, and one has an unprecedented self-penetrated topology. Taken together with other reported divalent metal aliphatic dicarboxylate coordination polymers, this work reveals that significant structural diversity can be instilled by utilizing these isomeric dipyridylamide ligands, although specific structural prediction remains a remote goal.

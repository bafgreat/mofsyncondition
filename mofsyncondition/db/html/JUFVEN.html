A helical salicyladoxime-based manganese triangle chain with single-molecule magnet behavior

Abstract

The reaction of 2-hydroxyphenylpropanone oxime (Et-H2salox) with Mn(NO3)2·4H2O, NEt3 and 1,3-bis(4-pyridyl)propane-N,N′-dioxide (bppo) in EtOH/H2O mixture afforded a one-dimensional coordination polymer with the formula [Mn3O(Et-salox)3(bppo)(MeOH)(H2O)3](NO3)0.5(Et-Hsalox)0.5·MeOH·H2O (1·MeOH·H2O). 1·MeOH·H2O had a manganese triangle structure, [MnIII3O]7 +, which was used as a building unit for a subsequent assembly of an oximate and central oxide. The flexible bppo ligand linked the units, resulting in the formation of a 1D helical structure. Variable temperature direct current magnetic susceptibility measurements of complex 1·MeOH·H2O were carried out. Exchange interactions of the metal centers of complex 1·MeOH·H2O were examined, and the results indicate that both ferro- and antiferromagnetic interactions simultaneously coexist in 1·MeOH·H2O, resulting in an S = 2 ground state. Moreover, complex 1·MeOH·H2O shows the frequency dependence of the out-of-phase component in alternating current magnetic susceptibility measurements, indicating single-molecule magnet behavior with Ueff = 28 K and τ0 = 1.8 × 10− 9 s.

The development of new polynuclear manganese clusters has attached considerable interest in recent years, largely because of their ability to functionalize as so-called single-molecule magnets (SMMs) and single-chain magnets (SCMs). SMMs show a significant barrier to magnetization relaxation arising from the combination of a large ground state spin, S, with easy-axis anisotropy (negative zero-field splitting parameter, D) [1] and SCMs possess a large uniaxial anisotropy, participate in strong intrachain exchange interactions without spin compensation between the high-spin magnetic units, and the chains show good isolation, thus avoiding two- (2D) and three-dimensional (3D) ordering [2]. Recently, the use of manganese clusters as building-blocks in the rational design of multi-dimensional coordination polymers is not only attended by their interesting magnetic properties but also their great diversity of structural motifs [3], [4], [5]. The salicylaldoxime-based manganese triangle, [MnIII3O]7 +, is considered to be a good candidate for use in the construction of these types of materials owing its nearly parallel Jahn–Teller (JT) distortion axes of high-spin Mn(III) ions, which are magnetically interesting due to their pronounced magnetic anisotropy [6], [7], [8].

In the present study, a 1D helical coordination polymer [Mn3O(Et-salox)3(bppo)(MeOH)(H2O)3](NO3)0.5(Et-Hsalox)0.5·MeOH·H2O (1·MeOH·H2O), assembled from salicylaldoxime-based [Mn3O]7 + by bppo linker was investigated. Complex 1·MeOH·H2O has the infinite helical structure of [MnIII3O]7 + and displays an obvious frequency-dependent out-of-phase component in alternating current (ac) magnetic susceptibility measurements, which are indicative of SMM behavior.

Mn(ClO4)2·6H2O, NEt3, bppo, and Et-H2salox in a 1:1:1:1 ratio form a dark green methanol solution. When this solution stood undisturbed for two weeks, [Mn3O(Et-salox)3(bppo)(MeOH)(H2O)3](NO3)0.5(Et-Hsalox)0.5·MeOH·H2O (1·MeOH·H2O) was formed as dark green-brown crystals in a 23% yield [9]. 1·MeOH·H2O crystallizes in the monoclinic space group P21/c (Table S1), and the asymmetric unit contains a Mn3 unit, half an NO3− anion, half an Et-Hsalox− anion, one MeOH molecule, and one H2O molecule (Fig. 1) [10]. 1·MeOH·H2O forms a covalently bonded 1D structure, which is built from a Mn3 cluster connected by one bppo ligand. Bond valence sum calculations (BVS) [11] (Table S2) are in agreement with the Mn ions being Mn(III). The structure of the Mn3 unit adopts a near-equilateral MnIII3 triangle-shaped core connected by the center μ3-oxide (O1) which is located about 0.014 Å above the Mn3 plane. Each edge of the Mn3 triangle is bridged by a dianionic oximate group of an Et-salox2 − ligand in the η1:η1:η1:μ2 mode, in which the deprotonated hydroxyl group is terminally bound to a Mn(III) ion, to form a Mn–O–N–Mn chelating ring. The chelating rings were found to be slightly distorted from planarity with torsion angles of 18.89(39)°, 33.87(33)°, and 45.24(31)° for Mn1–N1–O3–Mn2, Mn2–N2–O5–Mn3, and Mn3–N3–O7–Mn1, respectively. The oxide/oximate coordination saturates the equatorial plane of the distorted octahedron around each Mn ion, leaving the axial positions to be occupied by flexible monodentate ligands; these define the JT elongation axes for the three Mn sites. The flexible bppo ligand adapts a trans-gauche conformation and connects Mn3 to Mn2′ in the neighbor Mn3 unit forming a one-dimensional helical structure (Fig. 2) along the b axis. In addition, the adjacent left- and right-hand helical chains are cross-linked into a 2D supramolecular layer along with the bc plan by interchain hydrogen bonding interactions as shown in Fig. S1. XPS spectrum for 1·MeOH·H2O was recorded in the Mn 2p1/2 and 2p3/2 energy regions, where the peaks at 653.3 and 641.8 eV are assigned to the Mn(III) ion (Fig. S2). The peak distance between 2p3/2 and 2p1/2 of 11.5 eV is also in good agreement with previously reported results [12].

The direct current (dc) magnetic susceptibility data were collected and the χMT value of 1·MeOH·H2O decreases steadily from 8.2 emu mol− 1 K at 300 K, reaching 7.0 emu mol− 1 K at 50 K, and then decreases sharply to 1.4 emu mol− 1 K at 2.0 K (Fig. 3). The observed lower value at 300 K than 9.0 emu mol− 1 K, the value expected for a MnIII3 complex with noninteracting metal centers with g = 2.0, and the decrease in the χMT value with decreasing temperature may both suggest the antiferromagnetic interactions between the Mn ions. The rapid decrease below 50 K is most likely due to zero-field splitting effects and perhaps weak intermolecular interactions mediated by hydrogen bonding. The data were fitted using a three-J model with the addition of an interunit interaction by a mean-field approximation (zJ′), where z = 2 [13]. The best fit parameters of g = 1.92, J1 = − 2.7 cm− 1, J2 = 1.3 cm− 1, J3 = 0.9 cm− 1 and J′ = − 0.2 cm− 1 result in an S = 2 ground state. The first excited state is S = 3, which lies 1.7 cm− 1 above the ground state. The fitting parameters can be interpreted in terms of Mn–N–O–Mn torsion angle dependence [14], [15]. The antiferromagnetic interaction of J1 arises from the oximate pathway, with a small torsion angle of 18.89° and the ferromagnetic exchange pathways, J2 and J3, are assigned to the edges of the triangle that are bridged through the oximate ligands with large torsion angles (33.87 and 45.24°), which are due to the small overlapping of the dz2 magnetic orbital of Mn(III) ions [16]. The magnitude of J′ provides information of the weak antiferromagnetic interunit interactions in 1·MeOH·H2O.

From fitting of field dependent magnetization (M) measurements in the 2.0–4.0 K and 1–5 T ranges (Fig. S3) by using the ANISOFIT program [17], assuming that only the ground state is populated, were poor and suggest complications from low-lying excited states even at these relatively low temperatures.

Measurements of the ac magnetic susceptibility for 1·MeOH·H2O were performed in a zero dc field and a 3.5 G ac field oscillating in the 10–10000 Hz range (Fig. 4). The frequency dependent out-of-phase (χM″) signals were observed below 5.0 K, indicative of the onset of slow magnetic relaxation, which are a characteristic signature of superparamagnetic-like properties. The extrapolation of the curve to 0 K gives a χM′T value of ca. 1.9 emu K mol− 1, in excellent agreement with the S = 2 ground state. To rule out the presence of glassiness, a quantitative measure of the frequency dependence was estimated with the frequency shift parameter first introduced by Mydosh [18]. The peak temperatures of χM″ can be measured by the parameter ϕ = (ΔTp/Tp)/Δ(logf) = 0.16, which is in good agreement with that expected for superparamagnetic behavior (0.1 ≤ ϕ ≤ 0.3) [19], [20]. The Arrhenius equation was applied to fit the data and gave parameters of τ0 = 1.8 × 10− 9 s and ΔE = 28 K (Fig. S4). Furthermore, the Cole–Cole diagrams (χM″ vs χM′) were fitted using a generalized Debye model [21] with α parameters in the range of 0.18–0.45 at 2.2–3.3 K (Table S3 and Fig. S5), indicating a moderate distribution in relaxation times.

The slow relaxation observed in 1·MeOH·H2O is largely contributed by individual [Mn3O]7 + units, since the nearest distance between two Mn ions from different [Mn3O]7 + units in a helix is determined to be ~ 10.8 Å, a distance too long to mediate a strong coupling interaction, while the fitting results of the dc data with a small magnitude of zJ′ also confirms this assumption, i.e. SMM behavior rather than SCM behavior. Moreover, the non-zero zJ′ value can be attributed to interchain interactions, since the nearest neighboring [Mn3O]7 + subunits are not found in the helix but instead in the Mn3–Μn3 (5.112(1) Å) connections through interchain hydrogen bonds. A number of 1D coordination polymers comprised of [Mn3O]7 + subunits have been reported [6], [7], [8], [22], [23], [24]. The magnetic properties of these complexes are distinct from one another by virtue of the inconsistency of the adopted bridging ligand which links the [Mn3O]7 + units to form a chain structure. Among them, Gao et al. reported on the preparation of a chain compound based on [Mn3O]7 + units which are linked by azide bridges, affording a positive value of zJ′ and SCM behavior with a large energy barrier [6]. Although the formate-bridged chain compound reported in the same work by Gao et al. shows the SMM behavior without any interunit interactions between adjacent Mn3 units, Bai et al. reported two acetate-bridged chain compounds that also exhibit SCM behavior [8]. The zJ′ values of the remaining [Mn3O]7 +-based chain complexes are all small, since the bridging ligands used such as 4,4′-bipyridine [7], [22], trans-1,2-di(4-pyridyl)ethylene [7], [23], or even [Mn(H2O)4]2 + moiety [24] are larger than the azido or carboxylate groups, efficiently separating the [Mn3O]7 + units in the molecular chain and thus result in the magnetic behavior represented by individual [Mn3O]7 + units. Consequently, the bppo ligand used in preparation of complex 1·MeOH·H2O belongs to the latter category which separates the adjacent [Mn3O]7 + subunits thus preventing any interunit interactions, that is, SMM behavior within complex 1·MeOH · H2O. It is noteworthy that complex 1·MeOH·H2O is slightly different from those chain compounds with small zJ′ values by virtue of the probable interchain exchange coupling mediated by several hydrogen bonds in the complex.

In summary, the combination of 2-hydroxyphenylpropanone oximate (Et-salox−) and the bridging ligand bppo permitted a 1D helical coordination polymer with a [MnIII3O]7 + subunit to be prepared. Magnetic analysis indicates that the magnetic couplings in complex 1·MeOH·H2O are strongly dependent on the Mn–O–N–Mn torsion angle resulting from an S = 2 ground state. The ac magnetic susceptibility data reveals that 1·MeOH·H2O shows superparamagnetic behavior with a significant magnetic anisotropy.

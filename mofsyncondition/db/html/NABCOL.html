Syntheses and structures of three entangled coordination polymers based on the bifunctional ligand 4-((3-(pyridin-4-yl)-1H-pyrazol-1-yl)methyl)benzoic acid

Abstract

Self-assembly of transition metal salts with long bifunctional ligand affords three entangled coordination frameworks, namely, [Co(ppmba)2] (1), [Zn(ppmba)2]·0.5H2O (2) and [Cd(ppmba)2]·0.25H2O (3) (Hppmba = 4-((3-(pyridin-4-yl)-1H-pyrazol-1-yl)methyl)benzoic acid). Their structures were determined by single-crystal X-ray diffraction analyses and further characterized by element analyses, IR spectra, X-ray powder diffraction, and TG analyses. Compound 1 is a peculiar (2D → 3D) interdigitated architecture that is assembled from bilayer motifs. Compounds 2 and 3 exhibit fascinating (2D → 3D) entangled frameworks constructed from undulating 44-sql layers, which have both polycatenane and interdigitation characters. In addition, the magnetic properties of compound 1 and the photoluminescence properties of compounds 2 and 3 were studied.

1. Introduction

In the past decade, the design and construction of entangled coordination networks have developed into one of the most attractive subclass of metal–organic frameworks (MOFs), not only due to their enormous variety of interesting molecular architectures and topologies [1], but also because of their potential applications stemming directly from the intertwining nature of the lattices [2]. Since the rapid development of network-based crystal engineering, it has led to new and more complex types of entanglement being discovered, such as interdigitated [3], polycatenated [4] and polyrotaxane structures. It is well-known that interdigitation, an important subclass of entangled systems, is distinguished from ordinary interpenetration by the feature that the interdigitated structures could be disentangled without the demands for breaking links [4], [5]. Moreover, in contrast to the large majority of interdigitated frameworks assembled from undualting single layer motifs [3a,b], the interdigitated architectures assembled by bilayer motifs are particularly rare [4](a), [6], [7], [8]. Of concurrent interest has been the construction of polycatenated networks. According to the reviews by Ciani and co-workers, the main feature of polycatenation is that the dimensionality of the total catenated array increases, and each individual motif is not catenated with all the others in the array and only with the surrounding ones [1], [9]. To date, several synthetic strategies for these two types of entangled networks have been developed, due to an important reason, that is, these overall entangled structures are more flexible than the usual MOFs based entirely on coordination bonds [10], thus having potential applications ranging from sensor devices to drug delivery vehicles [4](a), [11]. Hence, the studies of these new types of entangled species would not only increase the structural diversity for coordination networks, but also provide new insight to elucidate the relations between structure and function in these materials.

During the course of our attempts to synthesize novel entangled networks [12], we choose the long flexible bifunctional ligand 4-((3-(pyridin-4-yl)-1H-pyrazol-1-yl)methyl)benzoic acid (Hppmba) (Scheme S1a), based on the following considerations: (i) because long flexible ligands have various conformations and geometries [13], they have exhibited the ability to produce unique entangled structures [3], [14]. In a very loose and common sense, it could be said that the entanglement may occur more easily if the ligands are longer in a network [15]. (ii) So far, MOFs based on bifunctional ligands are still rare [16]. Being a bifunctional ligand, the Hppmba ligands can simultaneously provide N- and O-donors, which may adopt a variety of connection modes with metal ions and thus favor the formation of multidimensional coordination polymers [17]. (iii) Coordination polymers constructed from the Hppmba ligand remain unexplored to date. Therefore, much work is still necessary to understand the coordination chemistry of the Hppmba ligand. Fortunately, by trial and error we have isolated three entangled coordination networks, namely, [Co(ppmba)2] (1), [Zn(ppmba)2]·0.5H2O (2) and [Cd(ppmba)2]·0.25H2O (3) (Hppmba = 4-((3-(pyridin-4-yl)-1H-pyrazol-1-yl)methyl)benzoic acid). Structural research revealed that compounds 1–3 exhibit novel (2D → 3D) entangled structures. Compound 1 shows the antiferromagnetic property. The photoluminescent properties of compounds 2 and 3 are discussed in detail.

2. Experimental

2.1. Materials and general methods

All chemicals were commercially purchased and used without further purification. Elemental analyses (C, H and N) were performed on a Perkin-Elmer 2400 CHN Elemental Analyzer. Co, Zn and Cd were determined by a tps-7000 Plasma-Spec(I) inductively coupled plasma-atomic emission spectrometer (ICP-AES). IR spectra were recorded in the range 400–4000 cm−1 on a Bio-Rad FTS-185 FT/IR Spectrophotometer using KBr pellets. TG analyses were performed on a NETZSCH STA 449C instrument in flowing N2 with a heating rate of 10 °C·min−1. Excitation and emission spectra were performed on an F-7000 fluorescence spectrophotometer equipped with a 150 W xenon lamp as the excitation source. XRPD data were recorded on a XD-3 diffractometer using Cu Kα radiation. Variable-temperature magnetic susceptibility data were obtained on a SQUID magnetometer (Quantum Design, MPMS-7) in the temperature range of 2–300 K with an applied field of 1 kOe.

2.2. Synthesis of [Co(ppmba)2] (1)

A mixture of Co(Ac)2·4H2O (0.050 g, 0.2 mmol), Hppmba (0.056 g, 0.2 mmol), Et3N (0.25 mL) and distilled water (9 mL) was stirred about 15 min in air, then transferred and sealed in a 17 mL Teflon-lined autoclave, which was heated at 433 K for 72 h. After slow cooling to room temperature, purple block crystals of 1 were filtered off, washed with distilled water, and dried at ambient temperature (yield: 43% based on Co). Elemental Anal. 2.5. X-ray crystallography

Suitable single crystal of 1–3 were selected for single-crystal X-ray diffraction analysis (diffractometer device type: SuperNova, Dual, Cu at zero, EosS2 for compound 1 and Bruker Smart Apex CCD for compounds 2 and 3). Compound 1 was collected with Cu Kα radiation (λ = 1.54184 Å) at 298.48 K. Compounds 2 and 3 were collected with Mo Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å) at 293 K and 296.15 K, respectively. Using olex 2 [18], the structure of 1 was solved with the shelxs [19] structure solution program using Direct Methods, while the structure of 2 and 3 were solved with the SIR2004 [20] structure solution program using Direct Methods. Moreover, the structures of 1–3 were refined with shelxl [19] refinement package using Least Squares minimization. All non-hydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically. The organic hydrogen atoms were generated geometrically. The aqua hydrogen atoms of compound 3 were located from difference Fourier maps, and those in compound 2 were not located. The disordered O atoms (O2 and O2A) of ppmba ligand in compound 3 were refine using O atoms split over two sites, with a total occupancy of 1. The crystal data and structure refinement of compounds 1–3 are summarized in Table 1. Selected bond lengths for 1–3 are listed in Table S1. The topological analyses were done with the topos program [21].

3. Results and discussion

3.1. Description of crystal structures

3.1.1. Crystal structures of 1

Single-crystal X-ray diffraction analysis reveals that compound 1 crystallizes in the triclinic system with a space group P1¯ and exhibits (2D → 3D) interdigitated arrays assembled from 2D bilayer motifs (Fig. 3a). The asymmetric unit of 1 contains one crystallographically unique Co atom and two ppmba ligands. As shown in Fig. 1, the Co atom is coordinated by four carboxylate oxygen atoms from three different ppmba ligands, and two nitrogen atoms of two ppmba ligands, to yield a distorted octahedral geometry. The ppmba ligands in 1 adopt two different coordination modes: the first type of ppmba acts as a tridentate ligand (appmba, Scheme S1b); the carboxylate group adopts bidentate chelating mode, while the pyridine group acts as a monodentate coordination mode. However, the carboxylate group of the second type of ppmba ligand (bppmba, Scheme S1c) adopts a bidentate bridging mode, the other pyridine group acts as a monodentate coordination mode.

Based on the above connection modes, two Co atoms are connected by two carboxylate groups of bppmba ligands to form a dinuclear cobalt core, which is further joined by two carboxylate groups, and four nitrogen atoms to generate a bimetallic cluster [Co2(CO2)4N4] as a structurally directing secondary building unit (SBU) (Fig. S1). Each bimetallic SBU is further connected by eight ppmba ligands (Fig. S2), which orient in only four different directions in two approximately vertical planes and each pair of them chelates to another SBU, to generate an unusual 2D framework of large parallelogrammic cavities (13.519 × 11.774 Å, Figs. S3). The distances between two SBUs connected by four pairs of ppmba ligands in different planes are 13.519 and 13.627 Å (based on the distances between the baricentres of the bimetallic clusters), respectively (Fig. S2a). Interestingly, a careful examination of the structure of 1 reveals that the 2D network can be considered as a 2D bilayer, which is constructed from two single layer linked by the Co–O bond (Fig. 2 and Fig. 3b). As described above, the cobalt atom is ligated to six donor atoms, five of which have been used in construction of one single layer, thereby the remaining one provides additional binding site to connect the ppmba ligand in the other single layer. Another noteworthy aspect of 1 is that the 2D structure can be simplified to a binodal (3,5)-connected network with a (42.6)(42.67.8) topology by considering Co atoms and bppmba ligands as 5-connected and 3-connected nodes, respectively (Fig. S4).

The most striking feature of 1 is that the 2D bilayer motifs are superimposed with interdigitation involving the nearest neighbours, probably due to the spacious nature of the single bilayer, thus finally giving an unusual (2D → 3D) interdigitated array (Fig. 3). As far as we know, such (2D → 3D) interdigitated architecture self-assembled from 2D bilayer motifs is still rare in entangled systems [6], [7], [8].

3.1.2. Crystal structures of 2 and 3

When Zn and Cd were used instead of Co, we got compounds 2 and 3 under similar reaction conditions. X-ray crystallography shows that compound 2 and 3 are isostructural, since they crystallize in the same space group with almost identical cell parameters. Therefore, only the structure of 2 is described in detail. The figures for the structure of 3 are shown in Figs. S10–S11. Compound 2 crystallizes in the monoclinic space group P21/c and the asymmetric unit contains one Zn atom and two ppmba ligands. As illustrated in Fig. 4, the Zn center adopts a distorted tetrahedral geometry, being coordinated with two carboxylate oxygen atoms from two different ppmba ligands and two nitrogen atoms of pyridine group from two ppmba ligands. The two crystallographically independent ppmba ligands in 2 act as bidentate bridging ligands connecting two Zn atoms (cppmba, Scheme S1d).

On the basis of these connection modes, the Zn(II) cations are bridged by ppmba ligands to form the left- and right-handed helical chains running along the crystallographic b axis with a pitch of 20.076 Å (Fig. 5a–d and Fig. S5a). The adjacent helical chains are further interconnected by ppmba ligands to generate a 2D layer containing the helical channels along the b axis with dimension of 14.255 × 13.078 Å (Fig. 5e and Fig. S5b). Moreover, the helices in the 2D layer are arranged in a left- and right-handed sequence, so that the whole structure does not show chirality. From the topological point of view, each Zn center, connected with four ppmba ligands, can be simplified as a four-connected node (Fig. S6a). And finally, this 2D layer framework can be rationalized as a 2D 44-sql net as shown in Fig. S6b.

The most outstanding feature for 2 is that each undulating 44-sql layer is catenated with two nearest neighbour layers (one above and one below) to give rise to a fascinating (2D → 3D) parallel polycatenated network (Fig. 6). According to the notation suggested by Ciani and co-workers [1a], the “degree of catenation” (Doc) here is equal to 2. The other attractive feature of 2 is that the 2D layer frameworks are superimposed with interdigitation involving the secondary nearest neighbouring layers (Figs. S7–S8). In other words, compound 2 not only has polycatenane character, but also has interdigitation character. In addition, there exist the significant π–π stacking interactions between the pyridine groups of ppmba ligands from the adjacent 2D layers (Fig. S9). As shown in Fig. S9b, the dihedral angle, slippage, centroid-to-centroid and plane-to-plane distances between two near-parallel pyridyl rings are 9.100°, 0.931 Å, 3.672 Å and 3.521 Å, respectively. These acting forces presumably make a great contribution to the structural stabilization [22].

In a comparison of the structures of 1–3, it is evident that the coordination modes of the organic ligands may have a very important influence on the formation of the resulting structures. Although compounds 1 and 3 are both six-coordinated, as demonstrated by the comparison of compounds 1 with 3, the coordination modes of ppmba ligands may play a key role in determining the formation of the resulting structures. For the ppmba ligand, it adopts two different coordination modes as mono-bidentate coordination mode (Scheme S1b) and tri-monodentate coordination mode (Scheme S1c) in 1, but it only adopts mono-bidentate coordination mode (Scheme S1b) in 3, which results in the final structural variation of the two compounds.

On the other hand, many other factors, such as the pH value, temperature and solvent, play unpredicted roles in the formation of the resulting products. Because these factors get together to influence the overall structure obtained, it is difficult to separate and rationalize what are the reasons for determining a structural motif of the compound. However, by the examination of the structures of 1–3, it is believed that the selection of suitable metal ions and long flexible bifunctional ligands is a feasible route for the construction of MOFs with various types of entanglements.

3.2. X-ray powder diffractions and thermal properties for compounds 1–3

In order to check the phase purity of compounds 1–3, the X-ray powder diffraction (XRPD) patterns were recorded at room temperature. As shown in Figs. S15–S17, the peak positions of simulated and experimental patterns are in good agreement with each other, demonstrating the phase purity of the products. The differences in intensity may be due to the preferred orientation of the crystalline powder samples.

To further investigate their thermal stabilities, thermal gravimetric analyses (TG) were carried out for compounds 1–3. For 1, the TG curve of 1 exhibits two steps of weight losses (Fig. S12). It keeps relatively stable up to 355 °C, then the weight of 1 begins to decrease and drops quickly until 460 °C giving the first weight loss of 57.33%. The decomposition continues with the second weight loss of 31.43% in the temperature range of 460–656 °C. The two weight loss stages assigned to the release of ppmba ligands. The residue is CoO. The whole weight loss (88.76%) is in good agreement with the calculated value of 87.90%. In the DSC curve of 1, the endothermic peak at 405 °C, the exothermic peaks at 528 and 676 °C are attributed to the decomposition of the ppmba ligands.

The curve of 2 displays a relatively high stability up to 350 °C, after this temperature the sample begins to decompose and shows three steps of weight loss (Fig. S13). The first weight loss is 1.85% from 40 °C to 250 °C, corresponding to the loss of guest water molecules (calcd 1.43%). Then the second step weight loss is 57.89% from 250 °C to 450 °C, and the third step weight loss of 15.85% is on-going and continues up to 773 °C, both attributed to the decomposition of ppmba ligands. The total weight loss of 75.59% is less than the calculated value of 86.96% if the final product is assumed to be ZnO, which indicated that the decomposition process is not complete due to the use of nitrogen protection. In the corresponding DSC curve of 2, the endothermic peak at 382 °C and the exothermic peak at 593 °C are related to the release of the coordinated water molecules and the ppmba ligands. The TG curve of 3 (Fig. S14) exhibits weight loss stages similar to those of compound 2. The first weight loss is 0.96% from 40 °C to 150 °C, corresponding to the loss of guest water molecules (calcd 0.67%). Then the second weight loss is 56.11% from 150 °C to 420 °C. Then the decomposition continues with the second weight loss of 21.68% in the temperature range of 420–775 °C. The last two weight loss stages assigned to the decomposition of ppmba ligands. The total weight loss of 78.75% is less than the calculated value of 80.84% if the final product is assumed to be CdO, which indicated that the decomposition process is not complete due to the use of nitrogen protection. In the DSC curve of 3, the endothermic peak at 393 °C and the exothermic peak at 554 °C is attributed to the decomposition of the coordinated water molecules and the ppmba ligands.

3.3. Photoluminescent properties of compounds 2 and 3

Taking into account the excellent luminescent properties of d10 metal complexes, the photoluminescence properties of 2 and 3 were investigated in the solid state at room temperature. As shown in Fig. 7a, compound 2 exhibits an intense fluorescent emission band with two emission maxima at 385 nm and 465 nm when excited at 283 nm. However, compound 3 shown an intense emission with a peak maximum at 441 nm using the excitation wavelength of 304 nm (Fig. 7b). There is a remarkable blue-shift with respect to the free Hppmba ligand whose photoluminescence property was also analyzed by us (λem = 532 nm, λex = 468 nm, Fig. S21). Therefore, the emissions of 2 and 3 may be assigned to ligand-to-metal charge transfer.

3.4. Magnetic properties of compound 1

The temperature dependence of magnetic susceptibility for 1 is measured in the temperature range of 2–300 K at a direct current field of 1.0 kOe (Fig. 8). The χMT value of 2.737 cm3 K mol−1 at 300 K (4.679 μB) is much higher than the expected value of 1.875 cm3 K mol−1 (3.873 μB) for one isolated spin-only CoII ions (S = 3/2, g = 2.0), which can be ascribed to the strong orbital contribution to the magnetic moment of CoII centers. As T is lowered, χMT decreases continuously to a value of 0.387 cm3 K mol−1 at 2 K, while the χM value increases to a maximum of 0.262 cm3 mol−1 at ca. 4 K and then drops rapidly. These behaviors indicate a dominant antiferromagnetic interaction between the CoII ions in the structures. Because the dinuclear cobalt clusters are interconnected by large ppmba ligands, the overall antiferromagnetic interaction should be attributed to the magnetic exchange coupling within the dinuclear cobalt cluster. Similar magnetic behaviors have also been observed in other coordination polymers containing polynuclear cobalt clusters [23]. The fit of the curve for 1/χM versus T plot to the Curie–Weiss law gives a good result in the temperature range of 2–300 K with C = 2.823 cm3 K mol−1 and θ = −6.320 K. The effective magnetic moment per Co(II) ion, defined as μeff = 2.828C1/2, is 4.752 μB for 1, which is commonly observed for octahedrally coordinated Co2+ ions with 4T1g electronic state [24].

4. Conclusions

In summary, we successfully synthesized and characterized three related coordination polymers with different types of entanglements through hydrothermal reactions of transition mental salts with long flexible bifunctional ligands. Compound 1 is a novel (2D → 3D) interdigitated architecture assembled by 2D bilayer frameworks. Compounds 2 and 3 exhibit fascinating (2D → 3D) entangled frameworks with coexistence of polycatenation and interdigitation. The successful isolation of these three complexes not only provides new examples in the realm of entanglement, but also indicates that the use of long flexible bifunctional ligands with N- and O-donors may be a promising strategy for the construction of novel entangled structures. Further work is progressing.

  • Three unique MOFs constructed by 1,4-bis(4-pyridyl)-2,3-diaza-1,3-butadiene and dicarboxylates: From rhombic tetrameric zinc(II) 2D layer to 2D + 2D → 3D polycatenated frameworks

    2016, Inorganic Chemistry Communications

    Three metal–organic networks, namely, [Zn2(Hnip)(4-bpdb)(nip)2(μ3-OH)] (1), {[Zn(tbip)(4-bpdb)1.5]·CH3OH}n (2), and {[Ni(1,3-bdc)(4-bpdb)]·2H2O}n (3) (4-bpdb = 1.4-bis(4-pyridyl)-2,3-diaza-1,3-butadiene, H2nip = 5-nitroisophthalic acid, H2tbip = 5-tert-butylisophthalic acid, and 1,3-H2bdc = 1,3-benzenedicarboxylic acid) are synthesized under hydrothermal conditions. The structure of 1 is built around uncommon rhombic {Zn4} clusters with double Td and double Oh Zn(II) geometries, which extend into a 2D network by the rigid deprotonated H2nip and 4-bpdb bridges. Meanwhile, 2 presents a novel 2D → 3D parallel polycatenated framework assembled from 2D bilayers with cuboids as the fundamental building units. Compound 3 features an interesting 2D → 3D inclined polycatenated network based on 44-sql subunits. Moreover, the thermal stabilities, photoluminescence, and magnetic properties of the compounds are also discussed.

  • A series of coordination polymers with 2D → 3D interdigitated structures self-assembled from 1,4-bis(4-(imidazole-1-yl)benzyl)piperazine

    2016, Inorganica Chimica Acta
    Citation Excerpt :

    For example, Stuart R. Batten and co-workers have synthesized interdigitating chains which stack together parallelly to generate a 1D + 1D → 2D interdigitated structure [9]. Our group has reported one compound showing a novel (1D + 1D → 3D) interdigitated structure [10]. This compound is obtained from the self-assembly of 1D tubelike structures, in which each 1D tube is interdigitated by dangling arms from four adjacent tubes.

    Six new coordination polymers, namely [Co2(1,2-bdc)2(bibp)2]·H2O (1), [Zn2(1,2-bdc)2(bibp)2]·H2O (2), [Ni2(1,2-bdc)2(bibp)3]·7H2O (3), [Cd(1,2-bdc)(bibp)0.5] (4), [Co(Hbtc)(bibp)] (5), [Cd(Hbtc)(bibp)] (6) (bibp = 1,4-bis(4-(imidazole-1-yl)benzyl)piperazine, 1,2-bdc = 1,2-benzenedicarboxylate, btc = 1,3,5-benzenetricarboxylate), have been synthesized under hydrothermal conditions. Their structures were determined by single-crystal X-ray diffraction analyses and further characterized by elemental analyses, IR spectra and TG analyses. Compounds 1–6 display different 2D → 3D interdigitated structures due to their different 2D layers. In 1 and 2, the 2D layers contain single-stranded meso-helical chains. In 3, there are large rhombic cavities in the 2D sheet. In 4, the 2D layer is built from rod-shaped SBUs. In 5 and 6, the 2D layers exhibit highly undulating 44-sql networks. In addition, the magnetic properties for compounds 1, 3 and 5, and the photoluminescence properties for compounds 2, 4 and 6 have also been investigated.

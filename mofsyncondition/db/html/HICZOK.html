Introduction

The design and synthesis of functional metal–organic frameworks (MOFs) by the self-assembly of organic ligands with appropriate functional groups and metal ions acting as connecting nodes has attracted considerable interest, because of their intriguing variety of topologies and potential applications.1,2 Generally, the organic tectons with suitable functional groups and molecular skeletons have been illustrated to be most critical to fine-tune the coordination motifs.3,4 Besides the metal–ligand coordination, the typical non-covalent interactions (such as H-bonding and π–π stacking)5 and reaction conditions such as temperature, pH, solvent also play a key role in metallosupramolecular assemblies.6,7 Accordingly, N-heterocyclic bridging ligands such as imidazolate, tetrazolate, triazolate, and pyrazolate moieties have emerged as a unique class of building subunits.8,9 Typically, most of those can serve not only as neutral ligands but also as anionic ones by deprotonation of acidic N–H groups in the free ligand, and as a result, will exhibit more versatile coordination modes in the self-assembly of coordination frameworks.10 As for the imidazole-containing ligands, we have made systematic studies on the their application in the construction of coordination polymers as effective building blocks.8a–f Among these, the 1-imidazole-containing ligands, for example, the 1,4-di(1-imidazolyl)benzene or its analogue can only act as neutral ligands to utilize terminal nitrogen atoms to ligate metal ions,8g–i therefore, showing conventionally invariable coordination modes. Following the study, we have designed another series of 4-imidazole-containing ligands such as 1,4-di(1H-imidazol-4-yl)benzene and 1,3,5-tri(1H-imidazol-4-yl)benzene which apparently possess the typically deprotonated characteristic and show versatile coordination modes.11 Significantly, porous metal–imidazolate complexes have been successfully constructed based on the metal–imidazolate moieties and show exceptional gas adsorption properties.11 Apart from N-heterocyclic bridging ligands, multicarboxylic acids have also proven to be good candidates in the preparation of MOFs because of their abundant coordination modes to metal ions,1a,12 and especially the mixed polycarboxylate and N-containing ligands with more tunable factors are good candidates for the construction of novel MOFs.13,14 Considering the super compatibility in the construction of MOFs with mixed ligands, we have also focused our attention on reactions of 1,4-di(1H-imidazol-4-yl)benzene and 1,3,5-tri(1H-imidazol-4-yl)benzene, varied metal salts together with different benzenecarboxylic acids as the auxiliary ligands, and a series of novel complexes have been obtained based on the mixed system.15 As a continuation and extension of our study, most recently we have designed a novel 4-imidazole- and 5-tetrazole-containing multi-nitrogen rigid ligand, namely 1-(1H-imidazol-4-yl)-3-(4H-tetrazol-5-yl)benzene (H2L), which may apparently exhibit more flexible coordination modes. Following such a mixed ligand strategy, we have carried out the study on reactions of ligand H2L together with different carboxylate ligands and metal salts. Herein we report the synthesis, crystal structure and photoluminescent property of six new cadmium(ii)/zinc(ii) complexes [Cd2(HL)2(pbda)(H2O)2]·4H2O (1), [Cd2(HL)2(mbda)(H2O)2]·6H2O (2), [Cd(H2L)(mmbda)(H2O)] (3), [Cd2(H2L)2(btca) (H2O)2]·4H2O (4), [Zn2(H2L)2(btca)] (5) and [Zn6(H2L)6(btca)3] (6) obtained by reactions of H2L and different carboxylic acids, respectively.

Experimental

Materials and methods

All commercially available chemicals are of reagent grade and were used as received without further purification. The ligand H2L was prepared according to the literature.16 Elemental analyses of C, H, and N were taken on a Perkin-Elmer 240C elemental analyzer at the analysis center of Nanjing University. Infrared spectra (IR) were recorded on a Bruker Vector22 FT-IRspectrophotometer by using KBr pellets. Thermogravimetric analyses (TGA) were performed on a simultaneous SDT 2960 thermal analyzer under nitrogen with a heating rate of 10 °C min−1. The luminescence spectra for the powdered solid samples were measured on an Aminco Bowman Series 2 spectrofluorometer with a xenon arc lamp as the light source. In the measurements of emission and excitation spectra the pass width is 5 nm, and all the measurements were carried out under the same experimental conditions. Powder X-ray diffraction (PXRD) patterns were measured on a Shimadzu XRD-6000 X-ray diffractometer with Cu Kα (λ = 1.5418 Å) radiation at room temperature.

The mixture was then sealed into a 16 mL Teflon-lined stainless steel container and heated at 160 °C for 3 days. After cooling to the room temperature, colorless block crystals of 1 were collected by filtration and washed by water and ethanol for several times with a yield of 72%. Results and discussion

Synthesis and thermal stabilities of the complexes

Hydro/solvothermal reaction has been demonstrated to be an effective tool for the assembly of MOFs, especially for systems with combined N-donor ligand and carboxylic acid because such systems usually form precipitates, making it difficult to grow suitable single crystals for X-ray diffraction studies.20 Complexes 1–6 were readily prepared with reasonable yields by hydrothermal reactions. The IR spectra exhibit strong absorption centered at 3400–3100 cm−1 for 1–6, corresponding to the N–H/O–H stretching vibration of ligand or water molecule (see Experimental section). Strong characteristic bands of the carboxylic group are observed in the range of 1630–1570 cm−1 for asymmetric vibrations and 1480–1400 cm−1 for symmetric vibrations, respectively. Therefore, the complete deprotonation of the carboxylic acid to give the corresponding carboxylate ligand in 1–6 was confirmed by crystal structural analysis (see later) as well as the IR spectral data since no vibrational bands in the range of 1760–1680 cm−1 were observed in the IR spectra of 1–6, and the strong absorption bands between 1630–1350 cm−1 in the IR spectra of six complexes can be assigned to the coordinated carboxylate groups.21 The pure phase of the synthesized 1–6 was confirmed by PXRD measurements, as shown in Fig. S6, ESI,† each PXRD pattern of the as-synthesized sample is consistent with the simulated one.

Thermogravimetric analyses (TGA) were performed to verify the thermal stability of the complexes and the results are shown in Fig. S7, ESI.† For 1, the first weight loss of 7.72% in 90–155 °C indicates the exclusion of two lattice water molecules (calc. 7.83%). Then, the weight loss of waterligands (obsd. 3.84%; calc. 3.91%) occurs at 155–190 °C. Pyrolysis of the residue occurs at 460 °C with consecutive weight losses, which did not stop until heated to 700 °C. Complex 2 shows a weight loss of 14.98% in the temperature range of 100–210 °C corresponding to the release of water molecules (calc. 15.07%) and the decomposition of the residue occurred at 450 °C. Complex 3 exhibits weight loss in the temperature range of 80–170 °C, and the total weight loss is about 3.30%, which is in agreement with the departure of coordinated water molecules (calc. 3.46%) and the residue is stable up to 460 °C. A total weight loss of 10.58% was observed for 4 in the temperature range of 130–220 °C, which is attributed to the loss of water molecules (calc. 10.72%), and the residue is stable up to about 390 °C. No obvious weight loss was found for 5 and 6 before the decomposition of the framework occurred at about 380 and 355 °C, respectively, which are in good agreement with the results of the crystal structure.

Crystal structure of [Cd2(HL)2(pbda)(H2O)2]·4H2O (1)

The results of X-ray diffraction analysis revealed that complex 1 crystallizes in monoclinic form with space group of P21/c (Table 1) and the asymmetric unit consists of one Cd(ii) atom, one partially deprotonated (HL)−ligand, a half of pbda2−, one free and two coordinated water molecules. As shown in Fig. 1a, the central Cd(ii) atom lies in distorted octahedral coordination environment with N3O3 donor set, in which the equatorial plane contains O1, O2 atoms of chelating carboxylate groups from pbda2−ligand and N1A, N6B atoms from two distinct (HL)−ligand respectively, and O3 from a coordinated water molecule together with N4 from (HL)−ligand occupies the axial positions with O3–Cd1–N4 angle of 164.26(10)° (Table S1, ESI†). In 1, the deprotonated tetrazole fragment of (HL)− coordinating with Cd(ii) atoms result in the formation of a one-dimensional (1D) chain structure (Type I, Scheme 1), which is connected by the 4-imidazolyl groups of (HL)− to give a two-dimensional (2D) double-layer fes network with 4·82 topology in the bc plane (Fig. 1b and 1c).22 And the Cd(ii)–(HL)− 2D sheets are further pillared by rod-type pbda2− (Type I, Scheme 2) to form a three-dimensional (3D) framework with Cd⋯Cd separation of 11.27 Å between two adjacent 2D layers (Fig. 1d). To gain a better insight into the 3D framework structure, topological analysis by reducing the multidimensional structure to simple node-and-linker net was performed on 1. As discussed above, each (HL)− links three Cd(ii) atoms, which can be regarded as a 3-connected node. As for each Cd(ii) atom, it in turn links two (HL)− and two pbda2−, hence, it can be treated as a 4-connector. According to the simplification principle, topology analysis calculated by the TOPOS program23 suggests that the resulting structure of the complex 1 is a binodal (3,4)-connected dmc net with Point (Schläfli) symbol of (4·82)(4·85) (Fig. 1e), defined by Proserpio and O'Keeffe.24 Apart from the dmc net mentioned in this paper, the 3,4-connected fry and moc nets also contain the basal fes 4·82 net. It is noticed that only a few examples of (3,4)-connected (4·82)(4·85) dmc net have been reported in the literature to date: the inorganic compound Ag2HgS2, two coordination compounds [H2N(CH2)2NH2]0.5·ZnHPO3, [Lu(3,5-pdc)(C2O4)0.5(H2O)2]·H2O (3,5-H2pdc = 3,5-pyridinedicarboxylic acid, H2C2O4 = oxalic acid) and [Cd2(L)2(PBDA)] (H2L = 3,5-di(1H-imidazol-1-yl)benzoic acid, H2PBDA = 1,4-benzenedicarboxylic acid).25

Crystal structure of [Cd2(HL)2(mbda)(H2O)2]·6H2O (2)

When H2mbda with an angle of 120° between the two carboxylic groups, instead of H2pbda, was used in the reaction, complex 2 was isolated. Complex 2 crystallizes in monoclinic space group C2/c, rather than space group P21/c as in 1 (Table 1). As depicted in Fig. 2a, the central Cd(ii) atom is six-coordinated with distorted octahedral coordination geometry surrounded by two carboxylate oxygen (O1 and O2) atoms from chelating carboxylate groups of mbda2− and two imidazolenitrogen (N4A and N6B) atoms from two different (HL)−ligands in the equatorial plane, and one water molecule (O3) as well as N1 from (HL)− in axial positions. The Cd–O bond lengths are 2.284(2) to 2.527(2) Å, while Cd–N ones are in the range of 2.216(2) and 2.467(3) Å (Table S1, ESI†), which are consistent with the reported six-coordinated Cd(ii) complexes with O and N donors.26 Similar to the complex 1, the partially deprotonated (HL)−ligand in 2 also employs a μ3-bridge (Type I, Scheme 1) to connect three Cd(ii) atoms by means of the heterocyclic nitrogen atoms to form a 2D double-layer fes network in the bc plane (Fig. 2b). On the other hand, mbda2−ligands employ as two-connector pillars to link Cd(ii) atoms of adjacent 2D nets to form 3D frameworks using its two terminal carboxylate groups with μ1-η1:η1-chelating coordination mode (Type II, Scheme 2). From a topological point of view, this 2D Cd(ii)–(HL)− net is also described by the fes 4·82 net, where both of the Cd(ii) atoms and (HL)− linkers act as 3-connected nodes. The 3D framework can also be viewed to be a layer-pillared net, where the layer is a Cd(ii)–(HL)−fes 4·82 sheet and the pillar is a mbda2−ligand. As discussed above, the mbda2−ligands ligate to Cd(ii)–(HL)−fes 4·82 sheets via both Cd–O contacts, therefore the 3-connected Cd node in the fes 4·82 net is extended to be a 4-connected node in the layer-pillared framework (Fig. 2c). Thereby, this layer-pillared framework is ascribed to be a binodal (3,4)-connected net with a Point (Schläfli) symbol (4·6·8)(4·62·83),27 which has been referred to as the fsc-3,4-C2/c topology other than the dmc net in 1 (Fig. 2d).

Crystal structure of [Cd(H2L)(mmbda)(H2O)] (3)

When H2mmbda with steric hindrance of 5-methyl substituted group replace H2mbda, a 2D undulating sql net 3 different from 2, is obtained. The crystallographically independent Cd(ii) atom adopts a distorted octahedral geometry with N2O4 coordination sphere, provided by four oxygen atoms in the basal plane from one monodentate, one chelating carboxylates of mmbda2− and one waterligand, and two nitrogen donors at the apical position from the tetrazolyl groups of a pair of H2L (Fig. 3a). The Cd1–O bond distances are in the range of 2.234(16)–2.411(19) Å, and the Cd1–N ones are 2.357(2) and 2.390(2) Å. The bond angles around the Cd(ii) atom are in the range of 54.84(6)–169.49(7)° (Table S1, ESI†). In contrast to the 2D double-layer network of complexes 1 and 2 built from Cd(ii)–(HL)−, the tetrazole fragments of the neutral H2L ligands in 3 exhibit a bridging μ2-N1,N4 coordination mode to extend the metal Cd(ii) centers into a 1D chain, leaving the 4-imidazole group uncoordinated (Type II, Scheme 1). The 1D chains are further connected to generate a 2D undulating network with (4,4) sheets (Fig. 3b and S1, ESI†) by the mmbda2− with its two carboxylate groups in μ1-η1:η0-monodentate and μ1-η1:η1-chelating coordination mode (Type III, Scheme 2). In addition, hydrogen bonding interactions play an important role in forming this structure. The adjacent 2D layers are assembled by two kinds of intermolecular hydrogen bonding interactions [N3–H3A⋯O5: 2.919(3); N5–H5⋯O2: 2.673(3) Å] forming a 3D supramolecular framework along the b direction (Fig. S2 and Table S2, ESI†).

Crystal structure of [Cd2(H2L)2(btca)(H2O)2]·4H2O (4)

To further investigate the effect of multi-carboxylate ligands, the former dicarboxylic acid was changed to 1,2,4,5-benzenetetracarboxylic acid, and a new complex 4 with a different structure was obtained. The result of X-ray crystallographic analysis revealed that there is one Cd(ii) atom, one H2L, half of the complete deprotonated btca4−ligand, one coordinated water molecule and two free water molecules in the asymmetric unit. The Cd1 lies in a distorted octahedral environment, in which the equatorial plane contains N1A, N2 from two distinct H2L ligands, and a pair of O3B, O4B atoms from one chelating carboxylate groups of btca2−ligand. The atoms O1 from btca4− and O5 from the free water molecule occupy the axial positions with an O1–Cd1–O5 angle of 178.6(10)° (Table S1, ESI†). Different from the complexes 1–3, as a multi-dentate group, the tetrazole fragment utilizes two adjacent N atoms in μ2-N1,N2 coordination mode to ligate two Cd(ii) atoms (Type III, Scheme 1), and two tetrazole fragments from different H2L ligands together with two Cd(ii) atoms forming a binuclear [Cd2N4] subunit with a Cd⋯Cd distance of 3.74(3) Å. On the other hand, each btca4−ligand acts as a μ4-bridge to coordinate with four Cd(ii) atoms by its four carboxylate groups. Two carboxylate groups adopt μ1-η1:η0-monodentate coordination mode to connect two Cd(ii) atoms while each of the other two carboxylate groups adopts a μ1-η1:η1 bridging coordination mode to link one Cd(ii) atom respectively (Type IV, Scheme 2). Each such binuclear [Cd2N4] subunit links adjacent identical ones through four btca4−ligandsvia Cd–O coordination leading to formation of a 2D network with (4,4) topology by taking the subunit as 4-connecting nodes and the btca4−ligands as 4-connecting linkers (Fig. 4b and S3, ESI†). The 2D layers stack in sequence along the c axis, and N–H⋯O, C–H⋯O, C–H⋯N intramolecular hydrogen bonds of the same layers further consolidate the 2D framework (Fig. S4, ESI†). Particularly, the benzene ring and imidazole rings between the adjacent 2D layers are nearly parallel with a dihedral angle of 4.26° and are separated by a centroid–centroid distance of 3.56 Å, indicating the presence of strong π–π stacking interactions.28 Such π–π interactions link the 2D layers to a 3D framework (Fig. S4, ESI†) with 1D channels occupied by water molecules along the b axis. PLATON29 analysis shows that the structure of 4 consists of voids of 135.3 Å3 that represent 15.4% per unit cell volume of 877.7 Å3 upon removal of the guest water molecules.

Crystal structure of [Zn2(H2L)2(btca)] (5)

When Zn(SO4)2·7H2O, instead of Cd(NO3)2·6H2O, was used in the reaction for 4, 5 with a different structure was isolated. Compound 5 crystallizes in the orthorhombic space group Pbca (Table 1). The asymmetric unit of 5 contains one Zn(ii) atom, one H2L ligand and half of btca4−ligand. Zn1 is 4-coordinated with distorted tetrahedral coordination geometry (Fig. 5a) defined by two nitrogen atoms (N1 and N5A) from two H2L ligands and two oxygen atoms (O1 and O2B) from two different btca4−ligands. The average Zn1–O and Zn1–N distances are 2.001(2) and 1.990(2) Å, respectively, and the coordination angles around Zn1 vary from 95.67(8) to 123.59(9)° (Table S1, ESI†). On the other hand, each btca4−ligand connects four Zn(ii) atoms with two opposite carboxylate groups both adopting μ2-η1:η1-bridging coordination mode (Type V, Scheme 2). As shown in Fig. 5b, the opposite carboxylate groups of the btca4−ligand connect the Zn(ii) atoms to form a 2D network. There exists a 26-membered macrocycle [Zn4(btca4−)4] formed by four btca4−ligands together with four Zn(ii) atoms in the 2D sheet of 5. Topologically, btca4− anions serve as the square-planar 4-connected nodes to link the adjacent Zn(ii) atoms into (4,4) sql net (Fig. 5c). Furthermore, such 2D layers are pillared by H2L ligand to complete the 3D framework structure of 5 (Fig. 5d). From the viewpoint of topology, the extended Zn(ii) atoms of 2D sheet by H2L ligands and btca4− anion can be referred to as the most common 4-connected nodes. In general, there are two types of nodes to be involved in this 4-connected category, and the different target nets produced rely on which type is present. For example, the square-planar node favors the generation of NbO (64·82-a), CdSO4 (65·8), Ivt (42·84) and quartz dual net (75·9), whereas the tetrahedral node would typically lead to quartz (64·82-b), diamond (66) and SrAl2 (42·63·8) nets.30 An exception is PtS (42·84) net in which equal numbers of planar and tetrahedral nodes (1 : 1) are included. In our case, btca4− anion as the square-planar node has a long topological vertex symbol of 62·62·82·810·87·87. The Zn(ii) atom functions as a tetrahedral node with the vertex symbol 6·6·6·6·86·86. The ratio of the two nodes is 1 : 2, obviously being different from the PtS net. Based on the definition of these nodes, the resulting structure of 5 is an extended uninodal (4,4)-connected 3D net with Point (Schläfli) symbol of (62·84)(64·82)2 (Fig. 5e). The current framework represents, to the best of our knowledge, only a limited number of examples having previously been found to have the same Schläfli symbol.31

Crystal structure of [Zn6(H2L)6(btca)3] (6)

Reaction temperature is one of the key factors in controlling the topology and dimensionality of the frameworks.32 When the reaction temperature was changed to 220 °C in the preparation reaction for complex 5, a new supramolecular isomer 6 possessing the same molecular building blocks but a different structure from 5 was isolated. Complex 6 crystallizes in monoclinic space group P21/c, rather than the orthorhombic space group Pbca that appeared in 5 (Table 1). There are three Zn(ii) centers, one and half of completely deprotonated btca4− and three H2L ligands in the asymmetric unit of 6 as shown in Fig. 6a. The Zn1 atom is four-coordinated by two N atoms (N4, N12) from two different H2L and two O ones (O2, O6A) from two different btca4−ligands. The average Zn1–O and Zn1–N distances are 1.982(2) and 1.991(3) Å, respectively, and the coordination angles around Zn1 varies from 98.09(10) to 121.66(11)° (Table S1, ESI†). Zn2 and Zn3 are both coordinated by two O atoms from two different btca4−ligands, and two N ones from two H2L to complete the distorted tetrahedral coordination environment with normal bond lengths and angles as listed in Table S1, ESI.† On the other hand, both H4btca ligands in the asymmetric unit are completely deprotonated to give the btca4− form similar to the coordination mode in 5 (Type V, Scheme 2). Each btca4− only utilizes the opposite couple of carboxylate groups to coordinate with four Zn(ii) atoms in μ2-η1:η1-bridging coordination mode, and other two ones just deprotonate to act as an anionic component to balance the positive charge of the metal ions. Different from that in 5, the carboxylate groups of btca4− bridge the Zn(ii) atoms to form a 2D layer with (4·62)2(42·62·82) topology where two different btca4− linkers act as 4- and 3-connected nodes respectively (Fig. 6b and 6c). There are three different kinds of macrocycles in the 2D sheet of 6, one is a 34-membered macrocycle formed by six btca4−ligands together with six Zn(ii) atoms [Zn6(btca4−)6] and the other ones are 26-membered one of [Zn4(btca4−)4] formed from four btca4−ligands and four Zn(ii) atoms and 18-membered one of [Zn2(btca4−)2] unit, respectively. The neutral H2L ligands act as two-connector pillars to link Zn(ii) atoms of adjacent 2D nets constructed by Zn2(btca)4− to form 3D pillar-layered frameworks (Fig. 6d). From a topological view, each 2-connected bridging H2L molecule can be regarded as a linear linker while each btca4−ligand connects four Zn(ii) atoms and can be treated as a 4-connector. Each Zn(ii) atom can be regarded as 4-connected node because it links two btca4−ligands and two Zn(ii) atoms through the H2L bridges. Therefore, the overall structure of 6 is a 4-connected uninodal 3D net with stoichiometry (4-c), as shown in Fig. 6e. The Point (Schläfli) symbol for the unprecedented penta-nodal 4-connected net is (4·62·83)2(4·64·8)2(62·84)(64·82)4 calculated by TOPOS program.23

Comparison of the structures

Complexes 1–6 were synthesized under hydrothermal conditions and characterized by single crystal X-ray diffraction, IR, and thermogravimetric analyses. Due to the aromaticity and multiple N-donor atoms, both of the tetrazolate and 4-imidazolate are excellent donor groups with potential applications in the construction of coordination frameworks.8,9 Therefore, the mixed tetrazolyl and 4-imidazole-containing H2L ligands are of polydentate aromatic nitrogen-donor ligand compounds, which can act as multidentate bridging ligands to exhibit different coordination modes in 1–6 as shown in Scheme 1. In addition to act as a three-connector in the case of the deprotonation of the tetrazolyl group to give the tetrazolate anion participating in the construction of 1 and 2 (Type I, Scheme 1), compound H2L can mainly serve as a neutral ligand and utilize differently positioned nitrogen atoms of heterocyclic rings to ligate metal atoms in 3–6 (Type II–IV, Scheme 1). The results further confirm that the versatile coordination modes of H2L can tune the fine structure to meet with coordination geometries of metal ions. On the other hand, the carboxylate groups in varied carboxylate ligands adopt μ1-η1:η1-chelating, μ1-η1:η0-monodentate, μ2-η1:η1-bismonodentate coordination modes to meet the different coordination requirements of metal centers (Scheme 2). From the crystal structures described above, it is interesting to note that the partially deprotonated (HL)−ligand possessing the same conformations and the dihedral angles among the aromatic rings connect metal Cd(ii) centers to form the same 2D double-layer fes network for 1 and 2, but the carboxylate ligands of pbda2− and mbda2−ligands with a different orientational angle of 180° or 120° between the two carboxylic groups which pillar the 2D network into a 3D framework with dmc and fsc-3,4-C2/c topology respectively. Although Cd(ii) complexes 2 and 3 were synthesized under the same conditions by using H2L with different substituted groups of isophthalates, the different coordination modes of anion (HL)− and neutral H2L ligands together with the carboxylates around the central Cd(ii) atom resulted in completely different structures of fsc-3,4-C2/c network of 2 and 2D (4,4) sql net of 3, which can be properly ascribed to the substituent effect of isophthalates. In 4, the Cd(ii) atoms are extended by virtue of the versatile H2L and btca4− to form the 2D net with (4,4) topology based on the binuclear [Cd2N4] subunit. In addition, a pair of Zn(ii) supramolecular isomers of 5 and 6 were constructed from the same H2L and btca4−ligands as 4, however, the distinct 3D structure of 5 and 6 is totally different from those of 4 due to the different metal centers. The results imply that the different coordination geometry around the central metal atom has a great impact on the structure of the complexes. The remarkable difference between the supramolecular isomers of 5 and 6 implies the great influence of the hydrothermal reaction temperature. 5 is an uninodal (4,4)-connected 3D net with Point (Schläfli) symbol of (62·84)(64·82)2 while 6 is an unprecedented penta-nodal 4-connected net with (4·62·83)2(4·64·8)2(62·84)(64·82)4 topology. A structural comparison of these polymers suggests that different dispositions of the carboxyl sites, reaction conditions, diversiform configurations and geometry around the central metal atom play important roles in the construction of resulting architectures for 1–6, which can also be well regulated by the polydentate aromatic nitrogen-donor H2L ligand.

Photoluminescent properties

Recently, inorganic–organic hybrid complexes, especially comprising the d10 closed-shell metal center and aromatic-containing system, have been intensively investigated for attractive fluorescence properties and potential applications, such as chemical sensors, white light-emitting diodes, and electroluminescent materials for displays.33 The photoluminescence properties of the stable materials 1–6, together with the H2L ligand and all of the carboxylic acids have been investigated in the solid state at room temperature, as depicted in Fig. S5, ESI.† The free H2L ligand shows an intense emission band at 440 nm upon excitation at 372 nm, which may be attributed to the π* → π transition of the intraligands.34 It can be seen that all of the complexes 1–6 exhibit emissions under the experimental conditions. Emission bands were observed at 455 nm (λex = 410 nm) for 1, 414 nm (λex = 296 nm) for 2, 448 nm (λex = 375 nm) for 3, 459 nm (λex = 386 nm) for 4, 449 nm (λex = 398 nm) for 5, 452 nm (λex = 405 nm) for 6, respectively. Emissions were also observed at 390, 370, 350 and 340 nm for H2pbda, H2mbda, H2mmbda and H4btca respectively, as reported previously.35 However, the emission bands of the carboxylate ligands originated from the π* → n transition are weak compared with that of the π* → π transition of the H2L ligand, and it was considered that the carboxylate ligands have no significant contribution to the fluorescent emission of as-synthesized coordination polymers1–6 with the presence of the N donor ligand.36 In contrast to the case for the free H2L ligand, the emission bands of complexes 1 and 3–6 may be tentatively assigned to intraligand fluorescence since the free ligand exhibited a similar emission under the same conditions.37 Compared with the free H2L ligand, however, the emission bands of complex 2 is 26 nm blue-shifted. Similar emissions have been observed in the previously reported Cd(ii) complexes, e.g. [Cd(m-BDC)(BIE)] [BIE = 2,2-bis(1H-imidazolyl)ether] and [Cd3(p-BDC)3(L1)2(H2O)2] [L1 = 1,4-bis(1,2,4-triazol-1-yl)butane] also with N-donor ligand together with the carboxylate one.38 The observed emissions are considered to be originated from the energy transfer from the ligands to the Cd(ii) centers.38 Thus the photoluminescence of 2 is tentatively assigned to the ligand-to-metal charge transfer (LMCT). In addition, it is noteworthy that the enhancement of luminescence for the complexes 1–6 compared with the free ligand under the same conditions may mainly originate from the coordination interactions between the metal Cd(ii) atom and the ligand, which enhanced its conformational rigidity and then decreased the nonradiative energy loss.39

Conclusions

Six new coordination polymers with rigid 1-(1H-imidazol-4-yl)-3-(4H-tetrazol-5-yl)benzene and varied multi-carboxylates have been obtained and found to show different structures and topologies. The results not only imply that 4-imidazolyl and 5-tetrazole-containing ligands possesses variable coordination modes in construction of MOFs, but also show that the distinct organic acidligands, the nature of metal ions and reaction conditions play crucial roles in modulating the formation of the resulting coordination complexes. And the combination of rigid N-donor ligand with multi-carboxylate is a good choice for construction of MOFs with specific structures and properties.

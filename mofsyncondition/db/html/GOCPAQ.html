Syntheses, structures and luminescent properties of four d10-metal coordination polymers based on the 2,4,5-tri(4-pyridyl)-imidazole ligand

Author links open overlay panel
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.poly.2008.04.051Get rights and content

Abstract

Four d10-metal coordination polymers based on the 2,4,5-tri(4-pyridyl)-imidazole ligand (Htpim), {[Zn2(Htpim)4Cl4] · 8H2O}n (1), {[Cd(tpim)2(H2O)2] · 4CH3OH}n (2), {[Cu2(Htpim)(PPh3)2I2] · CH3CN}n (3) and {[Ag(Htpim)](NO3) · CH2Cl2}n (4), have been synthesized and characterized by elemental analyses, IR, thermogravimetric and X-ray structural analyses. Both complexes 1 and 2 show one dimensional ribbon-like structures. Via intermolecular hydrogen bonds, a 2D supramolecular network and 3D framework are formed for 1 and 2, respectively. Complex 3 shows a 1D zigzag chain with a CuI2Cu rhomboid dimer. Complex 4 shows a 1D ladder-like polymer with two different metallacycles. The luminescent properties of all the complexes have been studied in the solid state.

Graphical abstract

Four new d10-metal coordination polymers based on 2,4,5-tri(4-pyridyl)-imidazole have been successfully synthesized and structurally characterized. All the complexes, together with the free ligand, display photoluminescence at room temperature.

1. Introduction

In the past few decades, considerable attention has been devoted to the design and syntheses of coordination polymers not only due to their aesthetic architectures and new topologies [1], [2], [3], but also because of their potential applications [4], [5], [6], such as gas storage [7], [8], [9], catalysis [10], [11], ion exchange [12], [13], photoluminescence [14], [15], [16], [17], [18], [19], non-linear optics [20], [21] and magnetism [22], [23], [24], [25], [26]. Recently, increasing investigations carried out on coordination polymers were focused on the use of the rigid multi-pyridyl ligands, such as 4,4′-bipyridine [27], 2,4,6-tri(4-pyridyl)-1,3,5-triazine [28], [29], 5,10,15,20-tetra-4-pyridy-21H,23H-porphine [30] and 2,5-bis(4-pyridyl)-1,3,4-oxadiazole [31], [32] as building blocks, because they can afford structural diversities, robustness and stability of metal–organic coordination polymers.

Among the rigid multi-pyridyl ligands, the 2,4,5-tri(4-pyridyl)-imidazole (Htpim) ligand has three pyridine N atoms and two imidazole N atoms (Scheme 1), and is suitable to be a good spacer in the construction of new metal–organic coordination polymers. To our best knowledge, coordination polymers of the Htpim ligand have not been documented so far. Herein, we report the syntheses, crystal structures and luminescent properties of four 1D coordination polymers of d10-metal ions: {[Zn2(Htpim)4Cl4] · 8H2O}n (1), {[Cd(tpim)2(H2O)2] · 4CH3OH}n (2), {[Cu2(Htpim) (PPh3)2I2] · CH3CN}n (3) and {[Ag (Htpim) ](NO3) · CH2Cl2}n (4).

2. Experimental

2.1. General procedures

The rigid ligand 2,4,5-tri(4-pyridyl)-imidazole (Htpim) was synthesized according to the literature method [33]. All other reagents and solvents were commercially available and were used without further purification. Elemental analyses for C, H and N were performed on a CHN-O-Rapid analyzer and an Elementar Vario MICRO analyzer. The IR spectra were recorded on a Bruker Vector 22 FT-IR spectrometer using KBr discs in the 4000–400 cm−1 range. Thermogravimetric analyses were carried out on a Perkin–Elmer Pyris 1 TGA analyzer from room temperature to 750 °C with a heating rate of 20 °C/min under nitrogen. Photoluminescence analyses for the solid samples were taken on an AMINCO Bowman Series2 luminescence spectrometer.

2.2. Preparation of the complexes

2.2.1. Preparation of {[Zn2(Htpim)4Cl4] · 8H2O}n (1)

A methanolic solution (4 ml) of Htpim (15 mg, 0.05 mmol) was added to an aqueous solution (6 ml) of ZnCl2 (7 mg, 0.05 mmol) with stirring. Orange diamond-like single crystals of 1 were obtained after the mixture was allowed to stand at room temperature for a week. Yield: 44%. Anal. Calc. for C72H68Cl4N20O8Zn2: C, 53.58; H, 4.25; N, 17.36. Found: C, 53.70; H, 4.65; N, 16.90%. IR (KBr, cm−1): 3445(br), 1614(vs), 1430(s), 1261(m), 1128(s), 1062(m), 1285(m), 1015(w), 839(s), 747(m), 690(s), 614(m), 511(m).

2.2.2. Preparation of {[Cd(tpim)2(H2O)2] · 4CH3OH}n (2)

Cd(ClO4)2 · 6H2O (21 mg, 0.05 mmol) in 10 ml of CH3OH was layered onto a solution of Htpim (15 mg, 0.05 mmol) in 10 ml of DMF. The resultant solution was allowed to slowly diffuse for several weeks to give yellow plate-like crystals. The crystals were collected, washed with methanol and dried in vacuum. Yield: 32%. Anal. Calc. for C40H44N10O6Cd: C, 55.01; H, 5.08; N, 16.04. Found: C, 54.71; H, 5.35; N, 16.74%. IR (KBr, cm−1): 3448(br), 3205(s), 1600(vs), 1504(m), 1429(m), 1341(w), 1245(w), 1213(m), 1149(w), 1061(w), 983(m), 872(w), 839(m), 751(m), 719(m), 689(m), 522(m).

2.2.3. Preparation of {[Cu2(Htpim)(PPh3)2 I2] · CH3CN}n (3)

A mixture of CuI (19 mg, 0.10 mmol), Htpim (15 mg, 0.05 mmol), PPh3 (22.6 mg, 0.10 mmol) in CH3CN (8 ml) and MeOH (0.2 ml) was sealed in a Teflon-lined stainless steel vessel, heated at 180 °C for 3 days and cooled to room temperature. The reaction mixture was filtered and left to stand at room temperature. Red-dark rod-like crystals suitable for X-ray analysis were obtained by slow evaporation of the solvent over two days. Yield: 20%. Anal. Calc. for C56H46Cu2I2N6P2: C, 54.00; H, 3.72; N, 6.74. Found: C, 54.47; H, 3.70; N, 7.10%. IR (KBr, cm−1): 3447(br), 3261(s), 3048(m), 2253(w), 1952(w), 1637(w), 1602(vs), 1571(m), 1503(s), 1479(m), 1433(s), 1309(w), 1212(m), 1182(m), 1091(m), 1066(w), 831(m), 739(m), 694(s), 519(s), 509(s).

2.2.4. Preparation of {[Ag(Htpim)](NO3) · CH2Cl2}n (4)

A buffer layer prepared by mixing MeCN and CH2Cl2 (1:1, v/v) was carefully covered on solution of Htpim (15 mg, 0.05 mmol) in CH2Cl2/DMF (5 ml, 4:1). Then, a solution containing AgNO3 (8.5 mg, 0.05 mmol) in MeCN (5 ml) was slowly added above the buffer layer. After four weeks, light yellow rod-like crystals formed at the buffer layer. Yield: 35%. Anal. Calc. for C19H15AgCl2N6O3: C, 41.25; H, 2.55; N, 15.20. Found: C, 41.12; H, 2.83; N, 15.28%. IR (KBr, cm−1): 3446(br), 1606(vs), 1582(s), 1559(w), 1508(w), 1490(w), 1384(s), 1281(m), 1131(w), 1066(w), 1004(w), 833(s), 745(m), 703(w), 688(m), 617(m), 516(w).

2.3. X-ray crystallography

Suitable single crystals of 1–4 were selected and mounted on glass fibers in air. X-ray intensity data were collected on a Bruker SMART APEX CCD diffractometer [34] using graphite monochromatized Mo Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å) at 291(2) K. The raw data were reduced and corrected for Lorentz and polarization effects using the saint program and for absorption using the sadabs program. The structures were solved by direct methods and refined with the full-matrix least-squares technique using shelxtl version5.1 [35]. Anisotropic thermal parameters were assigned to all non-hydrogen atoms. The hydrogen atoms were found in a difference Fourier map and the N–H hydrogen atoms were refined with an N–H distance restraint [0.86(7) Å] and Uiso(H) = 1.2Ueq(N). The remaining hydrogens were added at calculated positions and refined riding on their parent atoms. The crystallographic data are given in Table 1. Selected bond lengths and bond angles are listed in Table 2. The corresponding H-bond data are listed in Table 3.

3. Results and discussion

3.1. Syntheses and IR

The reaction of Htpim with ZnCl2 in MeOH/H2O afforded compound 1. Compound 2 was prepared by layering a solution of Cd(ClO4)2 · 6H2O in CH3OH with a Htpim solution in DMF. The hydrothermal reaction of CuI, Htpim and PPh3 in CH3CN gave compound 3. Complex 4 was obtained by the slow diffusion of Htpim in CH2Cl2/DMF into AgNO3 in MeCN.

In the IR studies, there is a broad band centered at 3445 cm−1 for 1 and it can be assigned to the O–H stretching of the crystalline water molecules. For 2, the broad band around 3448 cm−1 results from the O–H stretching of the crystalline methanol molecules and the coordinated water molecules. For 3 and 4, the very strong absorptions at 3446 and 3447 cm−1 are attributed to the N–H stretching of the ligand. The characteristic absorption band of the crystalline CH3CN molecules (νCN) for 3 appears at 2253 cm−1. For 4, the characteristic absorption of the NO3- anion is at 1384 cm−1. In addition, the absorption bands resulting from the skeletal vibrations of the aromatic rings of the ligand for all the four complexes appear in the 1400–1600 cm−1 region.

3.2. Description of the crystal structures

The X-ray crystallographic analysis reveals that the asymmetric unit of complex 1 contains one ligand molecule, half of one Zn2+ ion, one Cl− ion and two solvated water molecules. As shown in Fig. 1, the Zn(II) center has a slightly distorted octahedral geometry and is six-coordinated with four N atoms of equivalent pyridines from four different ligands in the equatorial plane and two Cl− ions in the axial sites. The Zn–N distances are 2.162(3) and 2.145(3) Å. The Cl–Zn–Cl bond angle is 177.91(6)°, which is close to a linear geometry. As depicted in Fig. 2, two Zn(II) atoms and two ligands form a 22-membered macrocylic ring and the rings are further connected by the Zn–N coordination bonds to generate a 1D ribbon-like polymer. In the macrometallacycle, the neighboring Zn⋯Zn diagonal distance is 10.032 Å. Moreover, the hydrogen bond occurring at O3–H3Y⋯N4(#5) (2.861(4) Å, symmetry code: x + 1, y, z) from the uncoordinated N4 atom of the pyridine of the ligand and one solvated water molecule gives rise to a two dimensional network (Fig. 3). In addition, the other solvated water molecule forms an intermolecular hydrogen bond with the N3 atom of imidazole and the N3–H3A⋯O2 distance is 2.936(5) Å. All the above hydrogen-bonding interactions may stabilize the solid-state structure of 1.

For 2, the asymmetric unit consists of one deprotonated ligand, half of one Cd2+ ion, one-coordinated aqua molecule and two solvated methanol molecules. As shown in Fig. 4, each Cd(II) center is in an octahedral coordination geometry with four N atoms of equivalent pyridines from four different ligands and two O atoms of two H2O molecules. It has the same 1D motif as that of complex 1, as described above. It is noteworthy that the supramolecular architecture of 2 is different from 1. In complex 2, the O–H⋯O and O–H⋯N hydrogen bonds from coordinated aqua molecules, solvated MeOH molecules and the uncoordinated N atom of the pyridine connect the 1D chain to give a 3D network (Fig. 5). The O1–H1Y⋯O2 and O2–H2A⋯O1 distances are 2.630(4) and 2.630(4) Å, and angles are found to be 164.7° and 150.3°, respectively. The O3–H3A⋯N5(#4) distance is 2.722(5) Å with an angle of 173.0° (symmetry code: x − 1/2, y + 1/2, z − 1).

There is one molecule of the Htpim ligand, two molecules of PPh3, two Cu+ ions, two I− ions and one solvated MeCN molecule in the asymmetric unit in the structure of complex 3. As illustrated in Fig. 6, two Cu(I) centers have the same coordination mode. Each Cu(I) center lies in a distorted tetrahedral coordination sphere which contains of one N atom from pyridine, one P atom from triphenylphosphine and two μ2-I− ions. The bond lengths and angles relating to Cu(I) are listed in Table 2. Two μ2-I− ions bind to two adjacent Cu(I) centers to give a CuI2Cu rhomboid dimer, which is further connected to N atoms of the ligands, leading to the formation of a zig-zag chain (Fig. 7). The triphenylphosphine and Htpim ligands linking the CuI2Cu rhomboid dimer are located in trans positions.

The asymmetric unit of complex 4 consists of one ligand molecule, one Ag+ ion, one NO3- ion and one solvated CH2Cl2 molecule. The Ag(I) center is located in a distorted trigonal arrangement consisting of three N atoms from two equivalent pyridines and one imidazole, as shown in Fig. 8. The Ag–N bond lengths are somewhat different, with relatively longer bond lengths for Ag–Npy. As illustrated in Fig. 9, along the symmetry center, the repeating unit is further extended into a 1D ladder-like polymer with two different metallacycles. In the larger one, two Ag (I) atoms and N atoms of equivalent pyridines from two ligands form a 22-membered macrocylic ring with an Ag⋯Ag diagonal distance of 9.825 Å. In the smaller one, two Ag(I) atoms and N atoms from the pyridine and imidazole of two ligands form a 14-membered macrocylic ring with an Ag⋯Ag diagonal distance of 6.948 Å. Additionally, an intermolecular hydrogen bond formed by the N3 atom of imidazole and O2 atom of the NO3- ion (N3–H3⋯O2(#4), symmetry code: −x + 2, −y + 1, −z + 2) makes for the stabilization of the architecture.

3.3. Thermogravimetric analyses

Thermogravimetric analyses (TGA) have been taken on complexes 1–4 by heating to 750 °C under flowing N2. As shown in Fig. 10, the TGA curve for 1 showed a weight loss between 36 and 157 °C, corresponding to the loss of eight water molecules (8.43% weight loss observed; 8.92% calculated), and the decomposition of the product is observed at ca. 340 °C. Complex 2 displayed two main steps of weight losses: the first step completed the loss of the crystalline methanol molecules at 70 °C (7.43% weight loss observed; 7.34% calculated). The second step occurred between 130 and 230 °C with the loss of two-coordinated water molecules (8.50% weight loss observed; 8.79% calculated). The TGA curve of 3 indicated a weight loss of 3.50% corresponding to the loss of MeCN (calcd. 3.29%) occurring between 120 and 220 °C. Complex 4 lost a guest CH2Cl2 molecule in the temperature range of 34–83 °C with a weight loss of 14.76% (calcd. 15.32%).

3.4. Photoluminescent properties

The photoluminescent properties of the free Htpim ligand and compounds 1–4 have been investigated in the solid state at room temperature. As shown in Fig. 11, the free ligand exhibits two emission maxima at 411 and 496 nm upon excitation at 376 nm. The maximum emission peaks of complexes 1 and 4 are located at 540 and 539 nm upon excitation at 362 and 348 nm, respectively. Complex 2 has a very strong emission band centered at 495 nm upon excitation at 375 nm. It is noteworthy that complex 3 displays an emission band in the red region at 623 nm when excited at 346 nm. The emissions could be assigned to a ligand-to-metal charge-transfer (LMCT) transition. Furthermore, the fluorescent intensity of 2 is stronger than compounds 1, 3 and 4, which may be attributed to deprotonation of the Htpim ligand in compound 2 [36].

4. Conclusions

In this paper, four new coordination polymers based on a novel rigid multi-pyridyl ligand have been successfully synthesized and characterized. Complexes 1 and 2 have the same one-dimensional motif. Complexes 3 and 4 consist of 1D zigzag and 1D ladder-like chains, respectively. All the complexes reported here, together with the free ligand, display photoluminescence at room temperature.

Two new interpenetrating networks assembled from 5-methylisophthalic acid and bipyridyl ligand with copper(II) salts

Author links open overlay panelBo Xu a b, , , ,
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.inoche.2011.01.009Get rights and content

Abstract

Hydrothermal reaction of copper(II) salt with mixed ligands afford two new coordination polymers formulated as [Cu(mip)(bpmp)0.5]·H2O (1) and [Cu(mip)(bpe)0.5] (2) (H2mip = 5-methylisophthalic acid, bpmp = N,N′-bis-(4-pyridyl-methyl) piperazine, bpe = 1,2-bis-(4-pyridyl) ethane). X-ray diffraction analyses show that both 1 and 2 are 3D doubly interpenetrating networks based on α-Po primitive cubic units.

Graphical Abstract

Two 3D doubly interpenetrating networks based on α-Po primitive cubic units assembled from mixed flexible neutral ligands and rigid charge compensated ligand systems are reported.

The fine-tuning design and synthesis of metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) has been attracting an extensive interest from the worldwide researchers, not only because of their intriguing varieties of molecular architectures and topologies [1], but also attributing to their potential applications in gas sorption, magnetism, as well as molecular catalysis and so on [2]. In recent years, our group has been involved in the construction of MOFs based on flexible organic ligands [3]. In comparison with rigid ligands, the versatile coordination modes of the functional groups of flexible ligands provide more opportunity to form some particular structures with intriguing properties but also a much more difficulty in predicting and designing the assembly process. Our current research interest focus on the mixed N-coordinated ligand and polycarboxylate ligand, as a mixed ligand system is undoubtedly providing much more good chance for fascinating architectures, and therefore much more opportunity for understanding the assembly process and mechanism of MOFs [4]. Inspired by our previous experience [5], in this contribution, we focus our research on N,N′-bis-(4-pyridyl-methyl) piperazine (bpmp), an organic linker we have long been using [5], and its analog 1,2-bis-(4-pyridyl) ethane (bpe). The 5-methylisophthalic acid (H2mip) was chosen as an auxiliary ligand for its rich coordination modes to increase the diversity of the final products. One feature of the H2mip ligand is that it is anionic when coordinating to metal cation and thus fulfills the charge balance of the coordination complex. Usually, when neutral organic moieties are engaged in the construction of coordination polymers, anions must be involved in the complexes to achieve charge compensation. Thus anions must coordinate to central metal cations and reduce the coordination variety between metal cations and the ligands. By involving the anionic H2mip ligand in our work may somewhat avoid the above problem and therefore gain some interesting frameworks.

Herein, we report the syntheses and crystal structures of two new Cu(II) polymers named [Cu(mip)(bpmp)0.5]∙H2O (1) and [Cu(mip)(bpe)0.5] (2) [6]. Single crystal X-ray analyses show that both of the two complexes show doubly interpenetrated α-Po structure [7]. The topological analysis and some diagrams were produced using TOPOS program [8]. The as-synthesized samples of the two complexes have also been measured by PXRD, elemental analysis, IR and TG analysis (see the supporting information).

Determination of the structure of compound 1 by a single crystal X-ray diffraction analysis indicates that it crystallizes in the monoclinic system, C2/c space group and features a 3D doubly interpenetrated α-Po structure. The asymmetric unit of 1 consists of one copper cation, one mip2−, a half bpmp ligand and an uncoordinated water molecule. The central copper ion is four-coordinated by three carboxyl oxygen atoms from three different mip2− ligands and a pyridyl nitrogen atom from a bpmp ligand in a distorted tetrahedral geometry (Fig. 1). The average Cu–O distance is 1.970 Å, while the Cu–N band length is 1.980 Å. The O–Cu–O bond angles range from 90.40(8) to 159.05(8)°, and O–Cu–N bond angles range from 93.66(8) to 154.98(9)° (Table S1). The basic building unit of 1 is a dinuclear copper SBU formulated as [Cu2(COO)2], which is generated by two carboxylate groups bridging two copper ions. The mip2− ligand holds a η2μ2–η1 coordination mode in 1, that is, one of the two carboxylate groups displays a bidentate bridging coordination mode while the other one holds a monodentate bridging mode. The dinuclear copper SBU are then linked by the mip2− ligands to form a series of 2D [Cu(mip)]n (4,4) rhomboid grid coordination polymer layers packing paralleling to the bc crystal plane as shown in Fig. 2. Neighboring [Cu(mip)]n layers are further connected by bpmp ligands coordinated to Cu ions on the perpendicular direction to form a 3D framework. Thus the dinuclear [Cu2(COO)2] unit can be considered as a 6-connected node, and the 3D framework comes out to be a α-Po primitive cubic lattice with a Schläfli symbol of (41263) (Fig. 3). Further investigation into the topology of complex 1 reveals that there are two identical independent 3D networks that interpenetrate with each other (Fig. 4). The two 3D subunits are related to each other by one translation, placing the interpenetration into Class Ia (only one translation) [9]. In a word, the structure of 1 exhibits a 3D coordination network with 2-fold interpenetration of α-Po primitive cubic units.

Single-crystal X-ray diffraction analysis reveals that 2 crystallizes in the monoclinic system, P21/c space group. As shown in Fig. 5, the asymmetric unit of 1 consists of one copper cation, one mip2−, and a half bpe ligand. The coordination environment of the central Cu(II) ion in 2 is similar as that in 1. The average Cu–O distance is 1.961 Å, while the Cu–N band length is 1.992 Å, which are very similar to that of 1. The O–Cu–O bond angles range from 87.92(9) to 161.10(8)°, and O–Cu–N bond angles range from 93.30(9) to 160.96(10)°, which are also very similar to that of 1. Further insight into the topological structure of 2 reveals that complex 2 is also a 3D 2-fold interpenetration coordination network based on α-Po primitive cubic units (Fig. 6).

As known, design and synthesis of coordination compounds based on mixed N-coordinated ligand and polycarboxylate ligand system is now a research focus of chemists [10]. Some other interpenetrated networks based on α-Po primitive cubic units have been previously reported [11]. The structures of 1 and 2 reported in this contribution is somewhat similar with some of the reported complexes such as [Cd(glutarate)(bpe)] [12] or [Co(adipate)(bpe)] [13].

In summary, two new copper coordination polymers have been constructed from 5-methylisophthalic acid (H2mip) and N,N′-bis-(4-pyridyl-methyl) piperazine (bpmp) for 1 or 1,2-bis-(4-pyridyl) ethylene (bpe) for 2 mixed ligand systems. X-ray diffraction analyses show that both 1 and 2 are 3D doubly interpenetrating networks based on α-Po primitive cubic units. The search for other coordination networks with unusual topology along this line is underway in our laboratory.

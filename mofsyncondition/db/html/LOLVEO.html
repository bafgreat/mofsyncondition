Spectroscopic and structural studies of the 2D Mn(II) coordination polymer from a bridging Noxa, Npy bidentate ligand; tetra-n-butylammonium[5-(3-pyridyl)-1,3,4-oxadiazole-2-thiolate]

Author links open overlay panel
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.poly.2008.10.008Get rights and content

Abstract

A coordination polymer {[Mn(3-pyt)2(H2O)2][N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−}n (3) has been synthesized from the reaction of tetra-n-butylammonium[5-(3-pyridyl)-1,3,4-oxadiazole-2-thiolate] {[N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−} and Mn(OAc)2 · 4H2O. The ligand and the Mn(II) complex 3 have been characterized by elemental analyses, IR, 1H and 13C NMR spectroscopy, in addition 3 has also been studied crystallographically. The single crystal X-ray structure of the 3 shows that the ligand is bonded to Mn(II) via its pyridyl and oxadiazole nitrogen atoms. This bonding pattern connects the Mn(II) centres to afford an uniform two dimensional coordination frame work where the [Mn(3-pyt)2(H2O)2]n molecules are stacked in a parallel layers. The overall arrangement of molecules in crystal lattice results in open channels in which the enclosed tetra-n-butylammonium cations are stabilized by anionic oxadiazole molecules through N+···S− ionic interactions.

Graphical abstract

The newly synthesized ligand tetra-n-butylammonium{5-(3-pyridyl)-1,3,4-oxadiazole-2-thiolate {[N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−} bonds to Mn(II) via Npy, Noxa. in a bidentate bridging mode and generates a coordination polymer {[Mn(3-pyt)2(H2O)2][N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−}n. The specific arrangement of molecules along b axis, afforded two dimensional metal–organic framework which stacked the molecules in parallel layered manner resulting in a supramolecular architecture.

1. Introduction

There has been a considerable interest in the coordination polymers of 1,3,4-oxadiazole-2-thione because of their interesting role in supramolecular chemistry and crystal engineering [1], [2], [3]. This type of metal–organic framework with intriguing network structures [4], [5], [6] find potential applications in catalysis [7], gas storage [8] and photoluminescence [9], [10]. Recently, pyridyl oxadiazoles have been systematically explored for their potential biological activities [11] and also as promising bridging ligands in coordination chemistry [12], [13], [14]. The design and assembly of supramolecular architectures with variable cavities or channels are currently of great interest owing to their novel topologies [15], [16], [17]. There are a few reports on the metal complexes of 5-phenyl-1,3,4-oxadiazole-2-thione [18], [19], [20], [21], 5-(4-pyridyl)-1,3,4-oxadiazole-2-thione [12], [13], [14], [22], 5-(2-pyridyl)-1,3,4-oxadiazole-2-thione [10] and 5-(3-pyridyl)-1,3,4-oxadiazole-2-thione [23]. The reported works mostly describe the coordination chemistry of symmetric bridging ligands used in the construction of network structures leading to porous coordination polymers [24]. Contrary to symmetrical bridging ligands, there is scanty of information on the coordination polymers of unsymmetrical organic ligands [12], [13], [14]. These unsymmetrical bridging ligands may show four types of binding modes involving monodentate (Noxo) (I), bidentate (Npy, Noxo or Npy, S) (II), tridentate (Npy, Noxo, S) (III) and tetradentate (Npy, Noxo,S, S) (IV) as shown in Fig. 1. Such a type of multidentate module has been envisaged as useful building block for constructing coordination polymers [12]. As a part of our ongoing studies on metal complexes of unsymmetrical 1,3,4-oxadiazoles [18], [23], we report herein the preparation, spectroscopic characterization of a new unsymmetrical heterocyclic oxadiazole bridging ligand {[N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−} (2) and its Mn(II) complex 3. Molecular structure of 3 has also been elucidated by X-ray crystallography.

2. Experimental

2.1. Materials and methods

All commercially available starting materials (analytical grade) were used as received and all experiments were carried out in open atmosphere. Nicotinamide was purchased from Sigma Aldrich and used as such. Carbon disulfide and hydrazine hydrate (SD fine Chemicals, India) and the solvents (Merck Chemicals, India) were used in the syntheses. Nicotinic acid hydrazide was synthesized according to the reported method [25].

2.2. Synthesis

2.2.1. Synthesis of potassium[N-(pyridine-3-carbonyl)-hydrazinecarbodithioate] K+(H2L−) (1)

K+(H2L−) was synthesized by the dropwise addition of CS2 (4 ml, 53 mmol) to a solution of nicotinic acid hydrazide (7 g, 51 mmol) and KOH (2.86 g, 51 mmol) in 35 ml of methanol (MeOH). The mixture was stirred continuously for 2 h at room temperature, thereafter the precipitated product was filtered off, washed twice with diethyl ether, m.p. 525 K. The analytical and spectroscopic data of 1 were identical to that reported previously [23].

2.2.2. Synthesis of {[N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−} (2)

Potassium[N-(pyridine-3-carbonyl)-hydrazinecarbodithioate] (5.6 g, 22.5 mmol) was dissolved in a EtOH–H2O mixture (75:25 v/v) (30 ml) and to it was added an EtOH solution (30 ml) of tetra-n-butylammonium bromide (7.2 g, 22.5 mmol). The resulting dirty yellow solution was concentrated at room temperature to afford the solid product. The separated solid was dissolved in a minimum amount of EtOH (25 ml) and water was added drop wise until the impurities settled down. The solution was decanted off and after evaporation of the solvent, a white solid product was obtained, m.p. 343 K (yield 3.60 g, 64%). Anal. 2.2.3. Synthesis of the complex {[Mn(3-pyt)2(H2O)2][N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−}n (3)

A methanolic solution (10 ml) of [Mn(OAc)2 · 4H2O] (.249 g, 1 mmol) was mixed with the ethanolic solution (10 ml) of {[N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−} (1.26 g, 3 mmol). The resulting yellow solution was filtered off and kept for crystallization. After 10 days, yellow crystals of 3 suitable for an X-ray crystallographic study were obtained.

3. Analyses and physical measurements

Carbon, hydrogen, nitrogen and sulfur contents were estimated on a CHN Model CE-440 Analyzer and on an Elementar Vario EL III. Magnetic susceptibility measurement was performed at room temperature on a Cahn Faraday balance using Hg[Co(NCS)4] as the calibrant and the electronic spectra were recorded on a UV-1700 Pharmaspec spectrophotometer. By following standard procedures, the complex was analyzed for its manganese content, after decomposition with a mixture of conc. HNO3 and HCl, followed by conc. H2SO4 [26]. IR spectra were recorded in the 4000–400 cm−1 region as KBr pellets on a Varian 3100 FT-IR Excalibur series. 1H and 13C NMR spectra were recorded in CDCl3 on a JEOL AL300 FT NMR spectrometer using TMS as an internal reference.

4. Crystal structure determination

X-ray single crystal refinement, diffraction data collection and data reduction for complex 3 were performed at 295(2) K with a graphite monochromated Mo Kα (λ = 0.71073 Å) radiation source using standard methods [27].

The structure was solved by direct methods (shelx-97) and refined against all data by full-matrix least-squares on F2 using anisotropic displacement parameters for all non-hydrogen atoms. All H atoms attached to C atoms were fixed geometrically and treated as riding with C–H = 0.98 Å (methyl), 0.97 Å (methylene) and 0.93 Å (aromatic) with U∼iso∼(H) = 1.2U∼eq∼(C) or 1.2U∼eq∼(methyl). H atoms of water molecules were initially located in difference Fourier maps and included in the subsequent refinement using restraints (O–H = 0.82(1) Å and H···H = 1.38(2) Å with U∼iso∼(H) = 1.5U∼eq∼(O). In the last stages of refinement they were treated as riding on their parent O atoms. One of the propyl groups is disordered over two positions with occupancy factors in the ratio 0.57/0.43. The occupancy factor has been determined by refining, in the first stage of refinement, a free variable fixing the two occupancies factor to sum to 1.0 and attributing all the disordered atoms a dummy isotropic temperature factor (2nd free variable). The atomic positions were refined using distances and angles restraints with the tools (PART and SAME) available in shelxl-97. Once the occupancy factors were determined, they were fixed and the isotropic temperature factors were freely refined. In the last stages of refinement, the atomic positions were refined with anisotropic temperature factors. The refinement of the Flack’s parameter gives a value of 0.55(2). Inverting the absolute structure gave a value of 0.45(2). In such a case, one may consider that the crystal is twinned by inversion and then the two domains were refined using the twin instruction in shelxl-97.

mercury package was used for molecular graphics [28]. Crystal structure diagrams were generated by use of the ortep-3 for Windows program [29].

5. Results and discussion

The reaction of tetra-n-butylammonium[5-(3-pyridyl)-1,3,4-oxadiazole-2-thiolate] {[N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−} (2) with Mn(OAc)2 · 4H2O in a 3:1 molar ratio generates a polymeric complex with the general formula {[Mn(3-pyt)2(H2O)2][N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−}n (Scheme 1) which has been confirmed by X-ray crystallography. The X-ray data show that the ligand is bridged between two Mn(II) centres via one pyridyl nitrogen and one oxadiazole nitrogen forming a novel coordination polymer. The ligand {[N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−} (2) and the Mn(II) complex 3 are soluble in common organic solvents and are air stable.

5.1. IR spectra

The IR spectrum of compound 3 reveals five characteristics bands due to ν(CN), ν(N–N), νas(C–O–C), νs(C–O–C) and ν(C–S) at 1612, 1084, 1196, 1156 and 942 cm−1, respectively. The IR spectra of 2 and 3 are devoid of any peak in the region of 3300–3200 cm−1, indicating the absence of –NH group.

The IR spectrum of compound 3 shows a positive shift of 49 cm−1 in ν(N–N) region due to the bonding of oxadiazole nitrogen atom to the metal ion. A further positive shift of 37 cm−1 in thioamide IV indicates a change of the C–S bond to CS during complex formation. Thus ligand 2 adopts thiolato form in the free state but is present in the thione form in complex 3. A band occurring at 3442 cm−1 shows the presence of water molecule(s) in the complex. A positive shift of 16 cm−1 in the pyridine ring vibration of the complex in comparison to the free ligand 2 suggests involvement of pyridine nitrogen in bonding with Mn(II).

The above IR data clearly indicates the participation of oxadiazole and pyridine nitrogens in bonding, conversion of the thiolato form of the ligand 2 to the thione form and the presence of water molecule(s), all of which are supported by X-ray analysis. The IR spectral bands of the co-crystallized part of the complex 3 are observed at 1584 ν(CN), 1196 and 1134 ν(C–O–C), 1034 ν(N–N) and 901 cm−1 ν(C–S) which are very close to the corresponding bands of [3-pyt]− except that of the pyridine ring vibration. The pyridine ring vibration of co-crystallized {[N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−} observed at 642 cm−1 shows a positive shift of 16 cm−1 in comparison to 2 due to the presence of hydrogen bonding between the pyridine nitrogen and hydrogen of water molecule.

5.2. Electronic spectra and magnetic moment

The UV–Vis spectrum of {[N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−} (2) exhibits bands at 322 and 224 nm attributed to intraligand transitions. The magnetic moment and electronic spectrum of complex 3 suggests its distorted octahedral geometry. The complex {[Mn(3-pyt)2(H2O)2][N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−}n exhibits a μeff of 5.93 BM and shows two bands at 834 nm (11 990 cm−1) and 434 nm (23 042 cm−1) assigned to the 5B1g → 5A1g and 5T2g transitions, respectively, for high spin distorted octahedral Mn(II). The additional high intensity bands at 342 nm (29 240 cm−1) and 298 nm (33 558 cm−1) are tentatively assigned to charge transfer/intraligand transitions [30].

5.3. 1H and 13C NMR spectra

The 1H NMR spectrum of {[N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−} exhibits no signals between 10.5 and 13.5 ppm, indicating the absence of NH protons. The protons due to pyridine ring appear as multiplets between 9.19 and 7.35 ppm. The aliphatic protons for –CH2 of tetra-n-butylammonium ion are observed at 2.14, 1.74 and 1.68 ppm while those of –CH3 are located at 0.96 ppm. The 13C NMR spectrum of {[N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−} shows 11 peaks for 23 carbon atoms, out of which the signal due to C–S appears at 180.38 ppm. The signals between 160.21 and 122.16 ppm are assigned the pyridine ring and oxadiazole carbons. The peaks appearing at δ 58.97, 24.06, 19.72 and 13.61 ppm are due to aliphatic carbons of tetra-n-butylammonium cation [31].

The X-ray study of complex 3 indicates the following characteristics of the ligand: (i) it exists in thione and thiolato forms (ii) it shows two preferential binding sites i.e. Npy (pyridyl nitrogen) and Nox (oxadiazole nitrogen) and (iii) it has strong capability of forming hydrogen bonding i.e. O–H···N(3), O–H···S(1B), O–H···S(1A) and O–H···S#5 (Fig. 3). Single crystal X-ray analyses reveal that the complex 3 possesses a two dimensional (2-D) framework constructed of Mn(II) centres and two [3-pyt]− ligands. It is very interesting that the [3-pyt]− part of the ligand adopts a bidentate bridging mode (nitrogen atoms from pyridyl and oxadiazole groups, Fig. 4a). As evident from the bond angles [trans angles ranging from 174.74(8)° to 178.75(9)° and cis angles ranging from 84.57(7)° to 96.32(8)°], the Mn(II) is in a slightly distorted six coordinated octahedral environment formed by two identical oxadiazole N donors [2.265(2) and 2.2703(19) Å for Mn–N(3B) and Mn–N(3A)], and two pyridine N-donors [2.2671(18) and 2.2680(19) Å for Mn–N(1A) and Mn–N(1B)] in equatorial positions, two water molecules [2.1854(19) Å for Mn–O(2W) and 2.2055(18) Å for Mn–O(1W)] in the axial positions (Fig. 4a, Fig. 4b). The two [3-pyt]− ligands in complex 3 act as bridging ligands, alternately employing oxadiazole or pyridine nitrogen donors to bond to a Mn(II) centre. The [3-pyt]− ligand is thus bonded in a Npy, Noxa bidentate bridged coordination mode. The dihedral angle between the pyridyl plane and the oxadiazole ring is 13.65(2)°. Since all the bonding sites of 3-pyt occupy equatorial positions in the coordination sphere of each Mn, the result is a 2-D sheet in which the molecules are stacked in parallel layers, forming a supramolecular metal–organic framework (Fig. 4b). The coordinated axial water molecules, above and below this sheet, from hydrogen bonds [O–H···S#5 and O–H···N(3) with the occluded [N(n-Bu)4]+ and [3-pyt]− moieties (Fig. 4b). The tetra-n-butylammonium cation and oxadiazole anion are enclosed and stabilized in this rectangular cavity through ionic interactions (N+···S+) (Fig. 4b). The rectangular cavity formed by one dimensional chain of the complex molecules have a dimension of 9.823 × 13.650 Å2 [adjacent Mn···Mn separation between inter (13.650 Å) and intra (9.823 Å) chain] (Fig. 4a). The enclosed {[N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−} is stabilized by two types of C–H···π interactions occurring between C–H of tetra-n-butylammonium cation [N(n-Bu)4]+] and centroid (Cg) of pyridine ring and oxadiazole ring of [3-pyt]− at the distance of 3.430 Å and 3.334 Å, respectively. There are two types of intermolecular hydrogen bonding, C21-H21B···S#5 = 2.973 Å and C41-H41B···N1 = 2.326 Å which provide stability to the occluded [N(n-Bu)4]+ and [3-pyt]− (Fig. 4b).

The pyridine nitrogen and thiolato sulfur of occluded {[N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−} are also involved in strong intermolecular hydrogen bonding [O–H···Npy and O(2W)–H(2W1)···S#5, 1.98 and 2.43 Å, respectively]. Further, the intramolecular hydrogen bonding O(1W)–H(1W1)···S(1B and O(2W)–H(2W2)···S(1A) having the distances of 2.38 and 2.33 Å, respectively, are also observed. These hydrogen bonding interactions add further stability to the overall structure.

A comparison of Mn–Npy, Mn–Owater, CS and CN bond lengths with similar systems show that the values are in good agreement with those reported in literature [32]. The bond lengths of 1,3,4-oxadiazole-2-thione/thiolate are intermediate between single and double bonds and provide evidence for a high degree of delocalization in the ring. The π-electron delocalization in the oxadiazole ring and inter/intra molecular hydrogen bonding interactions contribute to the stabilization of the molecular structure of 3. The specific arrangement of molecules along the b axis, gives rise to the 2-D sheet linked by strong hydrogen bonds formed between the axial water molecules and the occluded N(n-Bu)4]+ and [3-pyt]− moieties, resulting in a supramolecular architecture.

7. Conclusion

A new polymeric distorted octahedral Mn(II) complex {[Mn(3-pyt)2(H2O)2][N(n-Bu)4]+[3-pyt]−}n is synthesized from an unique ambidentate tetra-n-butylammonium [5-(3-pyridyl)-1,3,4-oxadiazole-2-thiolate] in which [3-pyt]− adopts the Npy, Noxa coordination mode. The hydrogen bonding and arrangement of [Mn(3-pyt)2(H2O)2]n, along the b axis generates a rectangular cavity where tetra-n-butylammonium ion is enclosed and stabilized by anionic oxadiazole-2-thiolate through N+···S− ionic interaction.

  • Syntheses, spectral and structural characterization of trinuclear and mononuclear Zn(II) complexes of N′-benzoyl hydrazine carboperthioate and 5-phenyl-1,3,4-oxadiazole-2-thione: An approach to DFT calculation, antibacterial and thermal studies

    2014, Polyhedron
    Citation Excerpt :

    Potassium salts of N-(aroyl)-hydrazine carbodithioate are important precursors to synthesize heterocyclic ligands [6,7] such as 1,3,4-oxadiazole/thiadiazole-2-thione and 1,3,4-triazole-2-thione derivatives. Their complexes have been the subject of extensive studies in recent years because of diverse biological activities [8,9] and potential applications in industry [10–12]. Metal complexes of sulfur-rich dithiocarbamates (an oxidized ligand having a S–S linkage) or perthiocarboxylates [13] are generally formed by the oxidative addition of elemental sulfur or anionic polysulfide to transition-metal dithio acid complexes [14] and have important contributions in the development of catalysis, magnetism, molecular architectures and materials chemistry.

    Potassium N′-(benzoyl)-hydrazine carbodithioate forms novel complexes [Zn3(μ-bhcp)2(py)4] (1) and [Zn(phot)2(en)2] (2) containing newly generated ligands N′-benzoyl hydrazinecarboperthioate (bhcp)3− and 5-phenyl-1,3,4-oxadiazole-2-thione (phot)− which have been characterized by elemental analyses, m.p, IR, NMR and single crystal X-ray data. During complexation, potassium N′-(benzoyl)-hydrazine carbodithioate is converted to N′-benzoyl hydrazinecarboperthioate (oxidized ligand having a S-S linkage) and 5-phenyl-1,3,4-oxadiazole-2-thione in the presence of pyridine and ethylenediamine (en), respectively which act as secondary ligands. The crystal structure of complex 1 reveals that the ligand is acting as trinegative pentadentate ON2S2, bonded to terminal zinc as ONS dinegative tridentate and to the other as NS uninegative bidentate. In the trinuclear complex 1, both terminal zinc atoms have distorted square pyramidal geometry and the middle zinc has tetrahedral geometry. Complex 2 has distorted octahedral geometry where central atom is bounded to two en and two oxadiazoles anions. The geometry of the complexes has also been optimized by the DFT method and the results of which are presented and compared with the X-ray diffraction data. The ligand has been also optimized and analyzed for its reactive site. In addition to this, frontier molecular orbitals (FMO) were also calculated. Raman analyses give the evidence of the binding of zinc with the newly generated oxadiazole and acyclic perthio ligands and the results corroborate with the IR and XRD data. Solid state electrical conductivity, bactericidal and thermal properties have been analyzed. The bioefficacy of the ligand and complexes has been examined against the growth of bacteria in vitro to evaluate their anti-microbial potential.

A 3D Cu(II) coordination polymer constructed from BIBP (BIBP = 5,5′-bis(1H-imidazol-1-yl)-2,2′-bithiophene) ligand with semiconductive property

Abstract

Hydrothermal reaction of Cu(NO3)2·3H2O with BIBP and H2hfipbb at 105 °C yielded a novel 3D Cu(II) coordination polymer, {[Cu2(BIBP)0.5(hfipbb)2(CH3CN)]·5H2O}n (1) [BIBP = 5,5′-bis(1H-imidazol-1-yl)-2,2′-bithiophene, H2hfipbb = 4,4′-(hexafluoroisopropylidene)bis(benzoic acid)]. Compound 1 displays a 3D 2-fold interpenetrating framework with {44.66} sqp topology, containing interesting ⋯LRLR⋯ double helical chains. The compound 1 was characterized by IR spectra, elemental analysis, thermal analysis and single crystal X-ray diffraction. In addition, solid-state UV–vis spectra experiment shows that compound 1 exhibits semiconductive property with a band gap of 2.46 eV.

Graphical abstract

A new 2-fold interpenetrated 3D Cu(II) coordination polymer {[Cu2(BIBP)0.5(hfipbb)2(CH3CN)]·5H2O}n with thiophene-containing ligand has been synthesized. The compound exhibits {44.66} sqp topology containing interesting ⋯LRLR⋯ double helical chains and shows semiconductive property.

Metal–organic frameworks (MOFs), also called porous coordination polymers (PCPs) are extended structures comprised of metal atoms or clusters and organic linkers [1]. As a new well-known class of crystalline porous materials, MOFs have received widespread interest in the past two decades due to their esthetic structures a0nd potential applications in diverse areas, such as gas storage/separation [2], luminescence [3], catalysis [4], sensors [5], magnetism [6], conductivity [7] drug delivery [8]. However, in contrast with the aforementioned traditional properties, relative attention has been paid on the photoelectric properties of MOFs. Thiophene, especially conjugated thiophene compounds, are kind of important and useful organic moieties in the synthesis of optical and electronic materials because of their good electron-transferring ability, high conductivity, and structural rigidity [9]. So, it is wise to construct MOFs with photoelectric properties using thiophene-containing ligand. On the basis of the above consideration, we use 5,5′-bis(1H-imidazol-1-yl)-2,2′-bithiophene (BIBP) as the main ligand to construct coordination polymer. Herein, a new 3D Cu(II) coordination polymer, {[Cu2(BIBP)0.5(hfipbb)2(CH3CN)]·5H2O}n (1) was synthesized and structurally by single crystal X-ray diffraction d (see ESI).

X-ray crystallography reveals that compound 1 crystallizes in the monoclinic space group P21/c. As shown in Fig. 1a, the asymmetric unit in compound 1 contains two independent Cu(II) cations, half a BIBP ligand, two deprotonated 4,4′-(hexafluoroisopropylidene) bis(benzoic acid), one CH3CN and five free water molecules removed by the SQUEEZE routine in PLATON [10]. Both Cu(II) cations are five-coordinated and coordinated by four oxygen atoms from three hfipbb ligands and one nitrogen atom from BIBP ligand for Cu(1) and from CH3CN for Cu(2), giving a distorted square pyramidal coordination geometry, where nitrogen atom locates at the top of the square pyramid whose bottom is occupied by four oxygen atoms. CH3CN plays the role as a terminal ligand in the fabrication of the structure. The Cu–O bond distances vary in the range of 1.948(2)–2.028(2) Å, and the Cu–N bond distances vary in the range of 2.132(2)–2.176(4) Å. All these distances are normal. The four carboxylate groups adopting bis-bidentate coordination mode bridge the two crystallographically equivalent Cu(II) cations to form a dinuclear Cu(II) “paddle-wheel” secondary building unit (SBU) with a Cu⋯Cu distance of 2.6996(7) Å. As depicted in Fig. S4, the hfipbb ligands link the paddle-wheel SBUs to form a two dimensional undulating (4,4) network. An interesting feature of the 2D network is that the left- and right-handed 1D helical chains coexist and array alternately by sharing the paddlewheel SBUs (Fig. 1b). Such 2D layers are further connected via the BPBP ligands and CH3CN to form a 3D coordination polymer framework (Fig. S5). From a topological view [11], the [Cu2(CO2)4] SBUs can serve as 5-connected nodes; thus, the whole framework of 1 can be topologically defined as a sqp network with the Schläfli symbol of {44.66} (Fig. S6). Compound 1 exhibits a two-fold parallel interpenetrating array to minimize the large void space and stabilize the framework (Fig. 1c). PLATON analysis gives a free void volume ratio of 19.5% in 1.

The UV–vis absorption spectra of 1 was carried out in the crystalline state at room temperature (Fig. 2a). The lower energy band at 687 nm can be assigned as d–d transitions of Cu(II) cation and the higher energy band 290 nm are ascribed to intraligand transitions. To explore the semiconductivity, the diffuse reflectance data is transformed into a Kubelka–Munk function to obtain the band gap (Eg). As shown in Fig. 2b, the Eg of 1 is 2.46 eV, which indicates that 1 exhibits semiconductive property.

In conclusion, a new 3D Cu(II) coordination polymer has been synthesized under hydrothermal condition based on a thiophene-containing ligand. The title compound is a 2-fold interpenetrated 3D structure with sqp {44.66} topology. The optical absorption spectra show that the title compound is a potential wide gap semiconductor material.

  • Three Co(II) metal-organic frameworks based on a substituted thiophene carboxylic acid ligand with semiconductive properties

    2018, Journal of Solid State Chemistry

    Three Co(II) metal-organic frameworks, namely, {[Co(L)(dpyb)0.5(H2O)2]·3H2O}n (1), {[Co(L)(dpyb)0.5]·CH3CN}n (2), and {[Co2(L)2(dpyp)1.5(H2O)]}n (3) have been hydrothermally synthesized based on a substituted thiophene carboxylic acid 3,3′-(thiophene-2,5-diyl)dibenzoic acid (H2L) and auxiliary N-donor co-ligands 1,4-di(pyridin-4-yl)benzene (dpyb) and 1,3-di(4-pyridyl)propane (dpyp). 1 possesses a 1D double chains structure, which is further extended into a 3D supramolecular framework via O–H···O hydrogen bonds and π···π interactions. 2 shows a novel 2D + 2D→ 2D network with unusual polyrotaxane entanglements of (4, 4)-sql subnets. The two sets of adjacent such networks are further extended via C–H···π interactions into a 3D supramolecular framework. 3 displays a non-interpenetrated 2D sheet with the point symbol {44.62} sql net topology and further extends via π···π interactions into a 3D supramolecular framework. The three MOFs were characterized by IR spectra, elemental analysis, thermal analysis and single crystal X-ray diffraction. In addition, solid-state UV–vis spectra experiments show that 1, 2, and 3 exhibit semiconductive properties with band gap of 2.23, 2.95, and 3.12 eV.

  • Double azido/cyanato bridged copper(II) dimers incorporating tridentate nitrogen donors Schiff base: Structure, EPR and magnetic studies

    2015, Polyhedron

    A neutral tridentate nitrogen donor Schiff base ligand L (L = (E)-N-(phenyl(pyridin-2-yl)methylene)-2-(pyridin-2-yl)ethanamine) has been synthesized and characterized by spectroscopic techniques, and employed to synthesize two new μ1,1-azido/cyanato bridged dinuclear copper(II) complexes, [Cu(L)(μ1,1-N3)(ClO4)]2 (1) and [Cu(L)(μ1,1-NCO)(ClO4)]2 (2). Both compounds have been spectroscopically and structurally characterized. Structural investigation reveals centro-symmetric nature of the complexes in which the center of inversion lies at the midpoint of the two copper(II) ions. The metal ions display distorted octahedral geometry. The tridentate neutral ligand L coordinates the metal ion in mer-orientation. Crystal packing reveals that hydrogen bonding and π⋯π stacking interactions play a dominant role on the solid state structure of the complexes. Low temperature susceptibility measurements support EPR studies and reveal antiferromagnetic (J = −7.2 cm–1) and ferromagnetic (J = +0.41 cm–1) behavior for 1 and 2, respectively.

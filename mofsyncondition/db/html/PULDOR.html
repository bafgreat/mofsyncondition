Crystal structures and fluorescence properties of two 2D MnII/CdII trimellitic complexes containing terpyridine

Abstract

Hydrothermal reactions of manganese (II)/cadmium(II) salts with 1,2,4-trimellitic acid (H3tma) and 2,2′:6′,2-terpyridine (tpy) result in two novel complexes formulated with [M(Htma)(tpy)]·H2O (M = Mn(1) and Cd(2)). X-ray diffraction structural analyses of two complexes reveal they are isomorphic except for the different center metal ions and crystallize in the monoclinic crystal system of P(2)/n space group. The metal ion lies in a six-coordinated distorted octahedral environment coordinated with three Htma2− anions and one tpy ligand. There is an infinite two-dimensional rhombic network based on the metallic dimmers and Htma2− anions with the tpy ligands in void. Furthermore, the tpy ligands from the adjacent network weakly interact each other by π⋯π packing interactions into 3D supramolecular structure. The fluorescence properties could be assigned to the π – π* transition of organic ligands.

Graphical abstract

Two novel isostructual MnII/CdII trimellitic complexes with 2,2′:6′,2″-terpyridine ligand are described, which exhibit two-dimensional rhombic network based on the metallic dimers and Htma2− anions with the tpy ligands in void. Fluorescence spectra exhibit the π – π* transition of two organic ligands.

1. Introduction

Design and preparation of metal-organic coordination polymers have been reported increasingly in the past decades due to their structural diversity [1] and extensive potential applications, for instance, gas storage, catalysis, magnetic, luminescent [2]. Selection of metal ions, organic ligands, reaction solvents and conditions is thought over in the formation of the material [3]. Multi-carboxylate ligands have been utilized widely for the flexible coordination modes and strong coordination ability of the carboxyl group in coordination polymers [4]. 1,2,4-trimellitic acid possesses three carboxyl group at 1-, 2-, 4-site of phenyl ring, which can serve as bridging groups to form multi-dimensional coordination polymers, or as chelating groups to increase the stability of complex [5]. Lot of complexes with 1,2,4-trimellitic acid have been investigated and exhibit abundant structural features and topologies [6]. The use of auxillary containing nitrogen ligands is also one strategy to construct the unique coordination polymers [7]. Terpyridine ligand is severally used in the construction of porous metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) for its large steric hindrance and chelating coordination mode, however, it could change the structural features or properties and decrease the dimension and the probability of interpenetration for metal-organic complexes [8]. In the paper, we have hydrothermally prepared two 2D Mn(II)/Cd(II) trimellitic complexes containing terpyridine, and reported the crystal structures, thermal stabilities and fluorescence properties.

2. Experimental

2.1. Materials and methods

All chemicals were commercially available and used as received without further purification. Elemental analyses (CHN) were performed using an Vario EL elemental analyzer. FT-IR spectra were recorded from KBr pellets in the range of 4000–400 cm −1 on a Nicolet Avatar 360 FT-IR spectrometer. Fluorescence measurements were carried out with a SHIMADZU RT5301PC spectrofluorophotometer. Thermogravimetric experiments were measured on a Netzsch STA449 F3 thermogravimetric analyzer.

2.2. Synthesis

[Mn(Htma)(tpy)]·H2O (1). A mixture of Mn(OAc)2 · 4H2O (0.013 g, 0.05 mmol), H3tma (0.016 g, 0.05 mmol) and tpy (0.012 g, 0.05 mmol) in 3 mL H2O was stirred for 15 min, then the mixture was placed in a 23-mL Teflon-lined autoclave and heated at 140 °C for 96 h. The autoclave was cooled over a period of 10 h by natural cooling. The yellow block-like crystals of 1 were collected by filtration, washed with ethanol, and dried in air (yield: 17 mg, ca. 68% based on Mn). Anal. calcd for C24H17MnN3O7:C, 56.04; H, 3.33; N, 8.17%; found C, 55.87; H, 3.09; N, 8.36%. IR data for 1 (KBr, cm−1): 3443(m), 2376(w), 1712(m), 1599(s), 1548(m), 1482(w), 1456(w), 1368(s), 1239(w), 1064(w), 1016(w), 834(w),779(m).

[Cd(Htma)(tpy)]·H2O (2). A mixture of Cd(NO3)2 · 4H2O (0.013 g, 0.05 mmol), H3tma (0.016 g, 0.05 mmol) and tpy (0.012 g, 0.05 mmol) in 3 mL H2O was stirred for 30 min, then the mixture was placed in a 23-mL Teflon-lined autoclave and heated at 160 °C for 96 h. The autoclave was cooled over a period of 10 h by natural cooling. The colorless block-like crystals of 2 were collected by filtration, washed with ethanol, and dried in air (yield: 17 mg, ca. 68% based on Mn). Anal. calcd for C24H17MnN3O7:C,56.04; H, 3.33; N, 8.17%; found C, 55.87; H, 3.09; N, 8.36%. IR data for 1 (KBr, cm−1): 3466 (m), 2365(w), 1688 (w), 1591(s), 1550(s), 1482(s), 1449(s), 1366(s), 1249(w), 1064(w), 1010(w), 852(w), 763(m).

2.3. Crystal structure determination

Single crystal X-ray diffraction analyses of the two complexes were carried out on a Bruker SMART APEX CCD diffractometer equipped with a graphite monochromated Mo-Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å). Raw data were integrated with the SAINT program [9]. The structures were solved by direct methods with SHELXS-97 and refined by full-matrix least-squares on F2 using SHELXS-97 [10]. An empirical absorption correction was applied with the program SADABS [11]. All non-hydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically. The hydrogen atoms were set in calculated positions and refined by a riding mode. The crystallographic details of complexes 1 and 2 are provided in Table 1, while the selected bond distances and angles are listed in Table 2.

3. Results and discussion

3.1. Crystal structures

X-ray single-crystal diffraction analysis of 1 show there are one Mn2+ ion, one Htma2− anion, one tpy molecule and one lattice water molecule in the asymmetric unit. The center Mn2+ ion lies in a six-coordinated distorted octahedral environment as shown in Fig. 1a. Each Mn2+ ion is surrounded with by three Htma2− anions and one tpy ligand, of which three oxygen atoms (O1,O2A and O3B) from 1- and 4-site carboxyl groups of the Htma2− anions, as well as three nitrogen atoms (N1, N2 and N3) from one tridentate tpy ligand, are coordinated to the center manganese ion (Fig. 1b). The bond lengths of Mn–O range from 2.1195(16) to 2.2554(18) Å, and the Mn–N bond lengths are 2.2697(19) and 2.296(2) Å.

Two adjacent Mn ions are bridged by two 4-site μ2-carboxyl groups forming a metallic dimer with the Mn⋯Mn distance of 4.3575 (4) Å and the Mn–O–Mn bond angles of 90.539(2)° (Fig. 2a). Two adjacent dimers are further extended via two carboxyl groups of four Htma2− anions in a η1:η1:η1:μ3 mode (Scheme 1) to form an infinite two-dimensional rhombic network with the size of 11.5327(7) × 11.5327(7) Å and the angle of 110.349(1)° (Fig. 2b and c). The tpy ligands exist in the vacant of the two-dimensional network and their parts of phenyl rings weakly interacts each other from the adjacent networks by the face-to-face π⋯π packing interactions (the face-to-face distances range from 3.2 to 3.4 Å) (Fig. 3). Similar to the others reported complexes containing tpy ligand, π⋯π packing interactions usually are observed between the outer pyridine rings of the tpy ligands [8a]. However, the π packing interactions between tpy ligands in complex 1 play an important role to link the 2D networks into 3D supramolecular architecture.

The structure of complex 2 is isostructural with that of 1 except for the center metal ion, so we no longer describe it in detail and only some differences are pointed out for 2. The bond lengths of Cd–O range from 2.207(2) to 2.399(2) Å, and the Cd–N bond lengths are 2.332(2) and 2.387(2) Å. The distance of the dimer Cd⋯Cd ions is 4.3794(4) Å, which is a little longer than that in 1 due to bigger ionic radii of Cd2+ ion than that of Mn2+ ion. The infinite two-dimensional rhombic network based on the metallic dimmers and Htma2− anions with the size of 11.6778(4) × 11.6778(4) Å and the angle of 110.556(0) ° in complex 2.

3.2. IR spectroscopy

The solid of complex 1 and 2 are stable at room temperature. IR data show that the absorption bonds resulting from the γ(C–H) of 1,2,4-substituted phenyl rings at 1064, 834 (1) and 852 (2) cm−1. The peaks at 1599 and 1456 cm−1 for 1, 1591 and 1449 cm−1 for 2, could be assigned to the asymmetric and symmetric stretching vibrations of carboxyl groups, respectively, and their differences are 143 and 142 nm, which indicates the bridging coordination mode of carboxyl groups [12]. The absorption peaks at 1712 (1) and 1688 (2) cm−1 could be due to the asymmetric stretching vibrations of 2-site protonated carboxyl group of Htma2− ligand. The middle peaks at 3443 (1) and 3466 (2) cm−1 for them can be attributed to ν(–O–H) stretching vibrations of the lattice water molecule in the two complexes. The analysis of IR spectrum of them are in agreement with the crystal structure and charge balance consideration.

3.3. Thermal stabilities

The thermal stability experiments of complexes 1 and 2 have been carried out from 30 to 900 °C in nitrogen atmosphere (Fig. 4). The thermal decomposition of 1 can be divided into two stages: firstly, the dehydration process occurs in the beginning of TG experiment until 83 °C with the residue weight of 97.0%, corresponding to the loss of one lattice water molecule (the calculated value is 96.5%). secondly, the complex is broken down with the increasing temperature from 265 °C to 439 °C and the final residue weight is 11.7%, which seems that the residue might be MnO (the calculated value is 13.8% based on MnO). For complex 2, there are also two decomposed steps: the first step is the loss of one lattice water molecule with the residue weight of 96.4% (the calculated value is 96.9%) from 178 to 267 °C, the second step is the decomposition of the framework in the temperature range of 325–696 °C and the final residue weight is 22.9% (the calculated value is 22.5% based on CdO).

3.4. Fluorescence properties

The fluorescence properties of complexes 1 and 2 are investigated in solid state at room temperature as shown in Fig. 5. Its emission spectrum of complex 1 with excitation at 328 nm exhibit one stronger bond at 392 nm, belonging to the π – π* transition of Htma2− ligand because a similar single peak has been observed at 361 nm for H3tma ligand in solid state (λex = 320 nm). And the other weaker and broader emission peak at 577 nm can be ascribed to the π – π* emission of tpy ligand. Differed from complex 1, the strongest emission band centered at 542 nm in the spectrum of 2 excited at 466 nm, can also be assigned to the π – π* transition of tpy, just for the emission band of tpy ligand in solid state is observed at 548 nm (λex = 465 nm). The difference of emission peaks for complexes 1 and 2 can be attributed to the effect of the coordination field from different center metal ions (MnII and CdII) [13].

4. Conclusions

We have successfully prepared two new transitional metal trimellitic complexes (M = MnII and CdII) with terpyridine ligand. Structural analyses show that they are isostructural with the similar formula. The complexes exhibit two-dimensional rhombic network, and the adjacent tpy ligands from next network act on each other by π⋯π packing interactions. The fluorescence properties of the two complexes show their different emission bands due to the coordination field effect of metal ions.

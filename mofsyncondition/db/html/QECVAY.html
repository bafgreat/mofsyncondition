A series of Cadmium(II) complexes with 2-substituted terephthalate building block and N-Donor co-ligands: Structural diversity and fluorescence properties

Abstract

Four new Cd(II) complexes have been prepared based on 1,2,4-trimellitic acid (H3tma) and monosodium 2-sulfoterephthalate (2-NaH2stp), formulated as [Cd2(Htma)2 (dpp)2(H2O)] (1), [Cd3 (tma)2 (2,4-bipy)4(H2O)2] (2), [Cd (2-Hstp) (2,2'-bipy)2]·2H2O (3) and [Cd (2-Hstp) (2,4-bipy) (H2O)2] (4) (dpp = dipyrido [3,2-a:2′,3'-c] phenazine, 2,4-bipy = 2,4-bipyridine, 2,2'-bipy = 2,2'- bipyridine) by hydrothermal method. X-ray diffraction structural analyses show all these complexes crystallized in triclinic crystal system of Pī space group, but their structures are diverse. Complex 1 exhibits an infinite one-dimensional chain featuring the left- and right-handed stranded chains interweaved each other. For 2, the two-dimensional network is constructed by one-dimensional ladder-like chain linked by Cd2 ions. In complex 3, the cadmium ion is surrounded with one 2-Hstp2- anion and two 2,2'-bipy molecules. Complex 4 is also a discrete structure based on a metallic dimer unit. In all these complexes, the N-donor co-ligands take the important roles in the assembly of three-dimensional supramolecular structures. The fluorescence properties of complexes 1–4 could be assigned to the π - π* transition of organic ligands.

Graphical abstract

Four Cd(II) complexes 1–4 based on 1,2,4-trimellitic acid or 2-sulfoterephthalate are described, which exhibit structural diversity due to the difference of organic ligands. Fluorescence spectra exhibit mainly the π - π* transition of these organic ligands.

1. Introduction

A growing number of transition metal-organic coordination polymers have been reported in the recent years for their structural diversity [1] and potential applications, such as adsorption, catalysis, magnetic and luminescent properties and so on [2]. Design of transition metal-organic complexes mainly is centered on selection of metal ions, organic ligands, reaction solvents and conditions [3]. The cadmium (II) ion with spherical d10 configuration is suitable for the construction of the potential functional coordination polymers, such as luminescence or photocatalytic materials [4]. Multi -carboxylate ligands have been used widely for the flexible coordination modes of the carboxyl group in the formation of transition metal coordination polymers [5]. Terephthalate (TP) is a “star” organic ligand for its reported thousands of metal-organic frameworks (MOFs), for instance, MOF-5 [6]. Many derivatives of terephthalate also have been considered as the organic building spacers when constructing the MOFs, especially 2-substituted terephthalate ligands (NH2, NO2, CO2H, SO3H, OH et al.) [7]. Both 1,2,4-trimellitic acid and 2-sulfoterephthalate are one of derivatives of terephthalate that possess three functional groups apt to constructing the high-dimensional frameworks [8]. Lot of complexes with 1,2,4-trimellitic acid and 2-sulfoterephthalate have been prepared and exhibit abundant structural features and topologies [9]. But a little of cadmium complexes with 1,2,4-trimellitic acid and 2-sulfoterephthalate have been reported [10], so we are interested in the cadmium complexes for their potential luminescence properties dependenting on their structural changes. The use of auxillary containing N-donor ligands is also one strategy to construct the unique coordination polymers [11]. For example, 2,2'- bipyridine and its derivatives could coordinate to metal ions, alter the coordination environment of central ions, form supramolecular interactions, and affect the fluorescence properties of complexes due to theirselves big π-conjugate system. Hence, we designed and prepared four Cd(II) 1,2,4-trimellitic/2-sulfoterephthalate complexes containing N-donor ligands (bpp, 2,2’-bipy, 2,4-bipy) hydrothermally, and investigated crystal structures, thermal stabilities and fluorescence properties.

2. Experimental

2.1. Materials and methods

All chemicals were commercially available and used as received without further purification. Elemental analyses (CHN) were performed using an Vario EL elemental analyzer. FT-IR spectra were recorded from KBr pellets in the range of 4000–400 cm −1 on a Nicolet Avatar 360 FT-IR spectrometer. Fluorescence measurements were carried out with a SHIMADZU RT5301PC spectrofluorophotometer. Thermogravimetric experiments were measured on a Netzsch STA449 F3 thermogravimetric analyzer.

2.2. Synthesis

[Cd2(Htma)2(dpp)2(H2O)] (1). A mixture of Cd(NO3)2·4H2O (0.013 g, 0.05 mmol), H3tma (0.016 g, 0.05 mmol) and dpp (0.013 g, 0.05 mmol) in 3 mL H2O was stirred for 15 min, then the mixture was placed in a 23-mL Teflon-lined autoclave and heated at 140 °C for 96 h. The autoclave was cooled over a period of 10 h by natural cooling. The colorless block-like crystals of 1 were collected by filtration, washed with ethanol, and dried in air (yield: 41 mg, ca. 33% based on Cd). Anal. calcd for C54H30Cd2N8O13 (1): C, 53.00; H, 2.47; N, 9.16%; Found C, 52.76; H, 2.69; N, 8.86%. IR data for 1 (KBr, cm−1): 3435 (m), 2928 (w), 2365 (w), 1680 (s), 1577 (vs), 1557 (vs), 1489 (s), 1416 (s), 1357 (s), 1286 (m), 1074 (m), 1044 (w), 812 (w),757 (m),733 (m).

[Cd3(tma)2(2,4-bipy)4(H2O)2] (2). A mixture of Cd(NO3)2·4H2O (0.013 g, 0.05 mmol), H3tma (0.016 g, 0.05 mmol) and 2,4-bipy (0.016 g, 0.05 mmol) in 3 mL H2O was stirred for 30 min, then the mixture was placed in a 23-mL Teflon-lined autoclave and heated at 140 °C for 96 h. The autoclave was cooled over a period of 10 h by natural cooling. The colorless block-like crystals of 2 were collected by filtration, washed with ethanol, and dried in air (yield: 52 mg, ca. 25% based on Cd). Anal. calcd for C62H42Cd3N3O14 (2): C, 53.56; H, 3.04; N, 3.02%; Found C, 53.14; H, 2.88; N, 3.42%. The mixture was stirred for 10 min and was placed in a 23 mL Teflon-lined stainless steel autoclave and heated at 140 °C for 72 h. The autoclave was cooled over a period of 16 h in air. The yellow lath-like crystals of 4 were collected by filtration, washed with ethanol, and dried in air (yield: 14 mg, 51% based on Cd). 2.3. Crystal structure determination

Single crystal X-ray diffraction analyses of the two complexes were carried out on a Bruker SMART APEX CCD diffractometer equipped with a graphite monochromated Mo-Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å). Raw data were integrated with the SAINT program [12]. The structures were solved by direct methods with SHELXS-97 and refined by full-matrix least-squares on F2 using SHELXS-97 [13]. An empirical absorption correction was applied with the program SADABS [14]. All non-hydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically. The hydrogen atoms were set in calculated positions and refined by a riding mode. The crystallographic details of complexes 1–4 are provided in Table 1, while the selected bond distances and angles are listed in Tables S1–S4.

3. Results and discussion

3.1. Crystal structures

X-ray single-crystal diffraction analysis of 1 show there are two Cd2+ ions, two Htma2− anions, two dpp ligands and one coordinated water molecule in the asymmetric unit. The Cd1 ion lies in a hexa-coordinated distorted triangle prism environment, surrounded by four oxygen atoms (O3A, O7, O10 and O10B) from 1- and 2-site carboxyl groups of three Htma2− anions, as well as two nitrogen atoms (N1 and N2) from one dpp ligand (Fig. 1a). While for Cd2 ion, three oxygen atoms (O1, O2 and O8) from 1- and 2-site carboxyl groups of two Htma2− anions, two nitrogen atoms (N5 and N6) from one dpp ligand and one coordinated water molecule are coordinated to the Cd2 ion also into a hexa-coordinated distorted triangle prism geometry (Fig. 1b). The bond lengths of CdO range from 2.196 (4) to 2.529 (4) Å, and the CdN bond lengths are 2.313 (4) and 2.378 (4) Å.

Two adjacent Cd1 ions are bridged by two μ2-oxgen atoms (O10) from 1-site carboxyl groups of Htma2− anions forming a four-member cycle with the Cd ⋯ Cd distance of 3.8788 (6) Å and the MnOMn bond angles of 110.263 (2)°. The four-member cycle units are further extended via two 4-site carboxyl groups of Htma2− anions and Cd2 ions into an infinite one-dimensional chain featuring the left- and right-handed stranded chains interweaved each other along b axis (Fig. 2). In 1D chain, the Htma2− anion adopts two coordination modes to join the metal ions: (i) η1:η1:η1:μ2 and (ii) η2:η1:η1:μ3 (Scheme 1a and 1b). The 1D chains extend further into 2D supramolecular network by a pair of H-bonds (O11H11W⋯O6i: 2.625 Å and O5H5W⋯O12i: 2.601 Å. symmetrical codes(i): −x, 3−y, 4−z) between two 4-site carboxyl groups of Htma2− anions (Fig. 3). The dpp ligands parallel to the phenyl rings of Htma2− anions and array on two sides of 1D chains, their parts of phenyl rings weakly interacts each other from the adjacent networks by the face-to-face π···π packing interactions. Similar to the others reported complexes containing dpp ligand, π···π packing interactions usually are observed between the outer pyridine rings of the tpy ligands. However, the π packing interactions between tpy ligands in complex 1 play an important role to link the 2D supramolecular networks into 3D supramolecular architecture (Fig. 4).

Complex 2 consists of a two-dimensional network based on Cd2+ ions and tma3− anions, and two crystallographically independent Cd(II) ions. The Cd1 ion is hexa-coordinated and surrounded with one nitrogen atom (N4) from one 2,4-bipy ligand and five oxygen atoms from three tma3− anions, including three oxygen atoms (O2, O16 and O16B) from 2-site carboxyl groups, one oxygen atom (O3) from 1-site carboxyl group and one oxygen atom (O4) from 4-site carboxyl group (Fig. 5a). Cd2 ion lies in a hexa-coordinated tetragonal bipyramidal configuration and coordinated with two nitrogen atoms (N6 and N6A) from two 2,4-bipy ligands, two oxygen atoms (O7 and O7A) from 1-site carboxyl group of two tma3− anions and two oxygen atoms (O1 and O1A)from two coordinated water molecules. The bond lengths of CdO range from 2.196 (4) to 2.529 (4) Å, and the CdN bond lengths are 2.313 (4) and 2.378 (4) Å.

Two Cd1 ions are linked by four tma3− anions into a dimer with the Cd1 ⋯ Cd1 metallic distance of 4.9089 (2) Å. The tma3− anion adopts a coordination mode of η1:η1:η2:η1:η1:η1:μ4 (Scheme 1c) connecting four Cd(II) ions. Along b axis, the metallic dimers come into being a one-dimensional ladder-like chain structure assisted by tma3− anions as shown in Fig. 6. The one-dimensional chains extend into two-dimensional network based on Cd2 ions bridged by 1-site carboxyl group of tma3− anions. Then the 2,4-bipy ligands stretching from the adjacent networks interact through the face-to-face π···π packing interactions to assemble the 2D networks into 3D supramolecular structure (Fig. 7).

In the asymmetric unit of 3, there are one Cd(II) ion, one 2-Hstp2- anion, two 2,2'-bipy ligands and two lattice water molecules. Each Cd(II) ion is hexa-coordinated to two oxygen atoms (O1 and O4) from the 1-site carboxyl and 2-site sulfonate groups of one 2-Hstp2- anion and four nitrogen atoms from two 2,2'-bipy ligands forming a distorted tetragonal bipyramidal geometry (Fig. 8). The bond lengths of CdO range from 2.196 (4) to 2.529 (4) Å, and the CdN bond lengths are 2.313 (4) and 2.378 (4) Å.

In complex 3, the 2-Hstp2- anion adopts a η1:η1 chelated coordination mode to one Cd(II) ion (Scheme 1d), which limites the structural extension to one-dimensional structure. However, the H-bonds (O7H7⋯ O5i: 2.555 Å) between the oxygen atoms from 1-site and 4-site carboxyl groups of 2-Hstp2- anion, link the isolated complex molecules into one-dimensional supramolecular chain along b axis (Fig. 9). Then two one-dimensional supramolecular chains interact each other by the face-to-face π···π packing interactions between two 2,2'-bipy ligands into a two-dimensional supramolecular layer in ab plane (Fig. 10). There is a reported zinc(II) complex isostructural to complex 3 [15].

The structure of complex 4 is also a discrete molecule based on a metallic dimer. There are two Cd(II) ions, two 2-Hstp2- anions, two 2,4-bipy ligands and four coordinated water molecules in the asymmetric unit of 4. Each Cd(II) ion lies in a hexa-coordinated tetragonal bipyramidal environment, coordinated with two oxygen atoms (O1, O4 and O7) from the sulfonate and carboxyl groups of two 2-Hstp2- anions, one nitrogen atom (N1) from one 2,4-bipy ligand, and two oxygen atoms (O1W and O2W) from two coordinated water molecules (Fig. 11). The bond lengths of CdO range from 2.196 (4) to 2.529 (4) Å, and the CdN bond lengths are 2.313 (4) and 2.378 (4) Å.

The 2-Hstp2- anion in 4 uses a η1:η1η1:μ2 coordination fashion (Scheme 1e) to bridge two Cd(II) ions into a metallic dimer (Fig. 12). The metallic dimers firstly form a one-dimensional supramolecular chain through a pair of H-bonds (O1WH1WB···O2i: 2.765 Å and O2W H2WA···O3i: 2.813 Å). Symmetrical codes (i): x − 1, y, z) between the oxygen atoms (O2 and O3) from the sulfonate group of 2-Hstp2- anion and the oxygen atoms (O1W and O2W) from the two coordinated water molecules (Fig. 13). Another pair of H-bonds (O6H6⋯O5i:2.508 Å and O1WH1WA···O5ii: 2.750 Å. Symmetrical codes (i): x, 1+y, z; (ii)) between the oxygen atoms (O5 and O6) from the carboxyl group of 2-Hstp2- anion and the oxygen atom (O1W) from the coordinated water molecule result in the formation of two-dimensional supramolecular layer. The monodentate coordinated 2,4-bipy ligands also are stretching out from the two sides of two-dimensional layer and interacting each other by the face-to-face π···π packing interactions into three-dimensional supramolecular architecture, as well as a H-bond (O2WH2WB···N2i: 2.775 Å. Symmetrical codes (i): −x, −y, 1−z) between the uncoordinated nitrogen atom (N2) from 2,4-bipy ligand and the coordinated water molecule (O2W).

3.2. Comparisons of the structures

Four Cd(II) complexes with H3tma/H3stp ligands have been prepared under hydrothermal condition. The two ligands are similar except for 2-site substituting group, however, four complexes exhibit completely different structural features as described above. Firstly, although all the coordination environments of Cd2+ ions in four complexes are hexa-coordinated, the coordination geometry of Cd2+ ion in 1 is triangle prism, while in the others complexes it is tetragonal bipyramid. Secondly, the coordination modes of two carboxylate ligands (H3tma/H3stp) are different. 1,2,4-trimellitic acid adopts (a) η1:η1:η1:μ2 and (b) η2:η1:η1:μ3 coordination modes in complex 1 and (c) η1:η1:η2:η1:η1:η1:μ4 mode in 2, which cause 1D or 2D coordination polymers. However, 2-sulfoterephthalate ligand uses two fashions: (d) η1:η1 (3) and (e) η1:η1η1:μ2 (4) fashions, resulting in two discrete complexes 3 and 4. In most of complexes based on 2-sulfoterephthalate, the ligand is apt to construct multi-dimensional coordination polymers for its diversiform coordination modes [16], however, several discrete Mn(II) complexes with 2-sulfoterephthalate have been discovered for its 4-site carboxyl group is not deprotonated [17]. For instance, one Mn(II) mononuclear complex from 2-sulfoterephthalate and 4,4'-bpyridine exhibits discrete structural feature, in which 2-sulfoterephthalate adopts a η1:η1 chelate mode with the protonated 4-site carboxyl group [17a]. Complexes 3 and 4 might be the discrete examples of Cd(II) complexes of 2-sulfoterephthalate for the first time. Finally, in the auxiliary N-donor ligands, the dpp and 2,2'-bipy ligands are chelate coordinated to Cd(II) ions, but 2,4-bipy ligand is monodentate and its 2-site nitrogen atom doesn't take part in coordination. The N-donor co-ligands act as the same role of interacting by the face-to-face π···π packing interactions to assemble three-dimensional supramolecular structures.

3.3. Thermal stabilities

The thermal stability experiments of complexes 1–4 have been carried out from 30 to 900 °C in air atmosphere with the heating rate of 10 °C· min−1 (Figs. S1–S4). The thermal decomposition of 1 can be divided into three stages: firstly, the dehydration process occurs in the beginning of TG experiment until 95 °C with the residue weight of 98.5%, corresponding to the loss of one coordinated water molecule (Calcd. 98.8%). Secondly, the next weight loss begins from 166 °C to 431 °C with residue weight is 67.0% (Calcd. 52.7%), which could be attributed to the loss of dpp molecules, then the complex is broken down with the final residue weight is 14.9%, which seems that the residue might be CdO (Calcd. 20.99% based on CdO) (Fig. S1). The TG curve of complex 2 shows that the first step is dehydration of two coordination water molecules with the weight loss of 2.46% (Calcd. 2.58%) from 110 to 162 °C, and the supramolecular structure of 2 was destroyed gradually as the temperature increasing until 546 °C into CdO with the residue weight of 26.82% (Calcd. 27.58%) (Fig. S2). The thermal decomposition of 3 is similar to that of 2, which exists dehydration and decomposition process as shown in Fig. S3. The first step could be attributed to the leaving of one lattice water molecule with the weight loss of 97.31% (Calcd. 97.44%) from 46 to 101 °C, then the complex is decomposed. For 4, there are two decomposed steps: the first step is the loss of four coordinated water molecules with the residue weight of 93.5% (the calculated value is 93.4%) from 30 to 234 °C, the second step is the decomposition of the framework in the temperature range of 235–570 °C and the final residue weight is 32.9% (the calculated value is 23.4% based on CdO) (Fig. S4).

3.4. Fluorescence properties

The fluorescence properties of complexes 1–4 are investigated in solid state at room temperature as shown in Fig. 14. Its emission spectrum of complex 1 with excitation at 325 nm exhibit one weak bond at 394 nm, belonging to the π - π* transition of Htma2− ligand because a similar single peak has been observed at 361 nm for H3tma ligand in solid state (λex = 320 nm) [18]. Differed from complex 1, one stronger emission band centered at 515 nm and the other weaker emission peak at 389 nm in the spectrum of 2 excited at 345 nm, can also be assigned to the π - π* transition of 2,4-bipy and tma3− ligand, respectively. One weak emission peak at 393 nm (λex = 354 nm) has been observed for complex 3, which could be mainly attributed to the contribution of 2-sulfoterephthalate for its emission spectra at 421 nm and the emission of free 2,2’-bipy molecules can hardly be observed in the solid state [19]. There are three emission peaks in the spectra of complex 4 (λex = 343 nm): two weaker band at 395 and 420 nm could be assigned to the π - π* transition of 2-sulfoterephthalate, and the strongest one at 513 nm might come from the emission of 2,4-bipy ligand similar to complex 2. The difference of emission peaks for these complexes can be attributed to the effect of the different coordination fields of the Cd(II) ions [20].

4. Conclusions

Four Cd(II) complexes based on 2-substituted terephthalate building block (H3tma/H3stp) with N-donor co-ligands have successfully prepared by hydrothermal reactions. Structural analyses show that complex 1 is one-dimensional chain-like structure, 2 exhibits two-dimensional network, and complexes 3 and 4 are discrete structures containing a great deal of supramolecular interactions. The fluorescence properties of these complexes discover their different emission bands are from the π - π* transition of the different organic ligands due to the coordination field effect of metal ions.

  • Differential Fresnel-reflection-based fiber biochemical sensor with temperature self-compensation for high-resolution measurement of Cd<sup>2+</sup> concentration in solution

    2019, Sensors and Actuators, B: Chemical
    Citation Excerpt :

    Therefore, the precise measurement of Cd2+ ion concentration is very important to many fields such as fundamental research [8], bio-chemical analysis [9–12], environments, contamination assessment of food and chemical industry. Consequently, a variety of optical and non-optical techniques, such as inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometry (ICP-MS) [13,14], inductively coupled plasma optical emission spectrometry (ICPOES) [15], flame atomic absorption spectrometry (FAAS) [16,17], fluorescence spectrometry [18–23], and graphite furnace atomic absorption spectrometry (GFAAS) [24], have been used to measure cadmium ion concentrations. Usually, these methods are more expensive and time-consuming [25], as compared to fiber optic sensor techniques for detecting cadmium ions.

    A fiber biochemical sensor system was developed to measure cadmium (Cd2+) solution based on differential temperature self-compensation, which exhibits high sensitivity in detecting Cd2+. The device only consists of a light source, three couplers, two photodetectors, and two fiber sensing ends. Utilizing the efficient adsorption of cadmium ion by allylthiourea, the sensing head was prepared. Moreover, the differential technique of the sensor can effectively eliminate concentration errors resulting from temperature fluctuations and environment effects, and thus real-time monitoring can be obtained.

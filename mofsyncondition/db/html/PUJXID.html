Divergent layer topologies in divalent metal aliphatic dicarboxylate coordination polymers containing 3-pyridylmethylnicotinamide

Abstract

Hydrothermal reaction of the requisite metal salt, an aliphatic dicarboxylic acid, and the hydrogen-bonding capable dipyridylamide ligand 3-pyridylmethylnicotinamide (3-pmna) resulted in four coordination polymers whose connectedness and layer topology depend on the metal coordination environment and dicarboxylate binding mode. These new crystalline phases were characterized by single crystal X-ray diffraction. [Cu(ox)(3-pmna)]n (1, ox = oxalate) manifests stacked 3-connected (6,3) herringbone layer motifs. {[Cd(mal)(3-pmna)(H2O)]·3H2O}n (2, mal = malonate) shows a 4-connected (4,4) grid topology with entrained water molecule trimeric chains in the interlamellar regions. {[Cd2(suc)2(3-pmna)(H2O)2]·3H2O}n (3, suc = succinate) possesses {Cd2O2} dimer-based [Cd(suc)]n layers pillared by 3-pmna ligands into a 5-connected sandwich motif with 4862 topology. {[Cd(glu)(3-pmna)(H2O)]·3H2O}n (4, glu = glutarate) manifests a rippled (4,4) grid topology. Luminescent behavior in the cadmium complexes is ascribed to intra-ligand molecular orbital transitions. Thermal decomposition behavior is also discussed herein.

1. Introduction

The basic research focus on the synthesis and characterization of divalent metal coordination polymers has now remained intense for nearly two decades. The ability of this class of extended solid state compound to act as gas storage substrates [1], selective molecular adsorbents [2], drug-delivery agents [3], and heterogeneous catalysts [4] has provided ample impetus for continued study. More recently, it has been determined that these solid-state materials can serve as sensors for the detection of nitroaromatic explosives residue exemplars [5]. Most coordination polymer solids are based on aromatic dicarboxylate ligands, with varying donor dispositions and binding modes instilling a tremendous variety of possible structural topologies [6]. Comparatively less attention has been paid to the synthesis of coordination polymers with aliphatic straight chain α,ω-dicarboxylate ligands as the requisite dianionic component. Coordination polymers containing the conformationally restricted oxalate (ox, Scheme 1) and malonate (mal, Scheme 1) ligands, with included dipyridyl coligands, have been shown to have a large scope of different topologies [7], [8], [9], [10], [11], [12]. [Cu(ox)(bpy)]n (bpy = 4,4′-bipyridine) possesses a (4,4) grid structure with weak ferromagnetic coupling imposed by axial-equatorial bridged carboxylate groups within its [Cu(ox)]n chain substructure [7]. [Cd(ox)(dpp)]n (dpp = 1,3-di(4-pyridyl)propane) manifests a herringbone layer structure [8]. [Cu2(mal)2(H2O)2(bpy)]n contains anti-syn carboxylate bridged square [Cu4(mal)4] tetramers connected by bpy tethers into a 2D layered network, with moderate ferromagnetic coupling occurring within the tetrameric subunits [9]. The inclusion of a hydrogen-bonding capable dipyridyl ligand can alter topology in these systems. For example, {[Cu2(mal)2(dpa)(H2O)2]·H2O}n (dpa = 4,4′-dipyridylamine) possesses both anti–anti and anti-syn carboxylate bridging modes within different [Cu(mal)]n chain motifs, pillared by dpa ligands into a (6,3) herringbone layer instead of a more common (4,4) grid [10]. Few cadmium malonate phases have been reported [11], [12]. [Cd(mal)(pyridine)(H2O)]n exhibited a grid-like layered structure, while [Cd(mal)(bpt)(H2O)]n (bpt = 2,5-bis(4-pyridyl)-1,3,4-thiadiazole) shows an interdigitated layered structure with pendant, monodentate bpt ligands [12].

Due to its flexible σ-bonds, the longer dicarboxylate ligands succinate (suc, Scheme 1) and glutarate (glu, Scheme 1) can adopt different conformations in response to the incipient structural requirements imposed by metal coordination environments or the presence of neutral hydrogen-bonding capable dipyridyl ligands during self-assembly, also resulting in a wide divergence of different topologies. {[Cd(suc)(dpa)]·H2O}n possesses a dimer-based 2-fold interpenetrated 41263pcu net [13], as does [Cd(glu)(dpa)]n[14], while the dipyridylamide ligand 4-pyridylisonicotinamide (4-pina) afforded the dimer-based 446108 mab self-penetrated topology of [Cd(suc)(4-pina)]n[15].

In comparison to coordination polymers that contain the rigid-rod bpy ligand or even the hydrogen-bonding capable dpa or 4-pina ligands, there are far fewer reports of extended solid-state materials containing the simple dipyridylamide 3-pyridylmethylnicotinamide (3-pmna, Scheme 1) [16], [17], [18]. The amide functional group of 3-pmna can provide both hydrogen bonding donor and acceptor points of contact, allowing access to structure-directing supramolecular interactions not possible in bpy; the dpa ligand can only serve as a hydrogen-bonding donor in this regard. Additionally, different conformations of the 3-pmna, as defined by the N⋯C⋯C⋯N torsion angle Ψ (Scheme 2), can instill changes in the through-ligand metal–metal distance. We thus attempted to prepare a series of divalent metal coordination polymers containing both a short straight chain α,ω-dicarboxylate ligand and the potentially tethering dipyridylamide 3-pmna ligand. In this report we discuss the single-crystal structures, topology, and optical and thermal properties of four new layered coordination polymer phases: [Cu(ox)(3-pmna)]n (1), {[Cd(mal)(3-pmna)(H2O)]·3H2O}n (2), {[Cd2(suc)2(3-pmna)(H2O)2]·3H2O}n (3), and {[Cd(glu)(3-pmna)(H2O)]·3H2O}n (4, glu = glutarate).

2. Experimental section

2.1. General considerations

Metal salts and the aliphatic dicarboxylic acids were commercially obtained. Potassium copper oxalate was prepared via a literature procedure [19]. Condensation of 3-picolylamine and nicotinoyl chloride hydrochloride in dry pyridine was used to prepare 3-pyridylmethylnicotinamide (3-pmna), which was isolated via CH2Cl2 extraction and rotary evaporation [20]. Water was deionized above 3MΩ–cm in-house. IR spectra were recorded on powdered samples using a Perkin Elmer Spectrum One DRIFT instrument. The luminescence spectra were obtained with a Hitachi F-4500 Fluorescence Spectrometer on solid crystalline samples anchored to quartz microscope slides with Rexon Corporation RX-22P ultraviolet-transparent epoxy adhesive. Thermogravimetric analysis was performed on a TA Instruments Q-50 Thermogravimetric Analyzer with a heating rate of 10 °C/min up to 600 °C. Elemental Analysis was carried out using a Perkin Elmer 2400 Series II CHNS/O Analyzer.

3. X-ray crystallography

Diffraction data for 1–4 were collected on a Bruker-AXS SMART-CCD X-ray diffractometer using graphite-monochromated Mo-Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å) at 173 K. The data were processed via SAINT [21], and subjected for Lorentz and polarization effect and absorption corrections using SADABS [22]. The structures were solved using direct methods with SHELXTL [23] within the OLEX2 crystallographic software suite [24]. All non-hydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically. Hydrogen atoms were placed in calculated positions and refined isotropically with a riding model. The crystal of 2 was non-merohedrally twinned; its twin law was determined using CELL NOW [25]. Only data from the major twin component was used in the solution and refinement for 2. Crystallographic details for 1–4 are given in Table 1.

4. Results and discussion

4.1. Synthesis and spectra

Crystalline samples of 2–4 were produced by the hydrothermal reaction of the requisite metal salt, aliphatic dicarboxylic acid, and 3-pmna in the presence of aqueous base Compound 1 was prepared by hydrothermal reaction of potassium copper oxalate with 3-pmna. The infrared spectra of 1–4 were consistent with their structural components as determined by single-crystal X-ray diffraction. Intense, broadened asymmetric and symmetric C–O stretching bands within the carboxylate ligands were observed at 1599 and 1296 cm−1 for 1, at 1547 and 1374 cm−1 for 2, at 1530 and 1415 cm−1 for 3, and at 1554 and 1423 cm−1 for 4. Medium intensity bands in the range of ∼1600 cm−1 to ∼1300 cm−1were ascribed to stretching modes of the pyridyl rings of the 3-pmna ligands [26]. Features corresponding to C–H bending and ring puckering within the pyridyl moieties exist in the region between ∼900 and ∼650 cm−1. Broad, weak spectral bands in the vicinity of ∼3000–3400 cm−1 indicate the presence of bound and unbound water molecules in the spectra of 2 and 3. The CO stretching bands of the amide group of the dipyridylamide ligands appeared at 1660 cm−1, 1653 cm−1, 1653 cm−1, and 1661 cm−1 respectively for 1–4.

4.2. Structural description of [Cu(ox)(3-pmna)]n (1)

The asymmetric unit of compound 1 contains a divalent copper atom, halves of two crystallographically distinct ox ligands (ox-A, ox-B), and a 3-pmna ligand. A Jahn-Teller distorted {CuO4N2} octahedral coordination sphere is seen at copper (Fig. 1), with cis-disposed 3-pmna pyridyl nitrogen donor atoms. The remaining coordination sites are taken up by 1,2-chelating carboxylate groups from an ox-A ligand and an ox-B ligand. 4.3. Structural description of {[Cd(mal)(3-pmna)(H2O)]·3H2O}n (2)

All cadmium atoms in 2 adopt a {CdO4N2} octahedral coordination environment (Fig. 3) with the axial positions taken up by 3-pmna pyridyl nitrogen donor atoms. The nominal equatorial plane contains a 1,3-chelating mal ligand in two cis positions, a single oxygen donor atom from a second mal ligand, and an aqua ligand. The mal ligands in 2 rest in a μ2-κ3-O:O′,O'′ binding mode, conjoining adjacent cadmium atoms into [Cd(mal)(H2O)]n chains in an anti-syn fashion (Fig. 4a). These chains are aligned along the b crystal direction with a Cd⋯Cd internuclear distance of 5.050 Å. The chain patterns are stabilized by hydrogen bonding donation from the aqua ligands to bound mal carboxylate oxygen atoms (Table 3). In turn the chain motifs are pillared by dipodal 3-pmna ligands into [Cd(mal)(3-pmna)(H2O)]n coordination polymer layers that are oriented parallel to the (1 0 1) crystal planes (Fig. 4b). The 3-pmna ligands adopt an anti L-conformation with a Ψ torsion angle of 154.4°, spanning a Cd···Cd internuclear distance of 11.968 Å. Each Cd atom in 2 serves as a 4-connected node in a rectangular (4,4) grid coordination polymer motif with grid apertures of 11.771 × 14.104 Å defined by Cd···Cd through-space distances. The grid windows are somewhat pinched, with Cd⋯Cd⋯Cd angles of 75.54 and 104.46°. Adjacent [Cd(mal)(3-pmna)(H2O)]n layers stack pattern in an AAA pattern along the a crystal direction (Fig. S2), and are anchored to each other via supramolecular hydrogen bonding interactions involving 3-pmna amide N–H groups, unligated water molecule trimers, and unligated mal oxygen atoms in another layer (Table 3). The incipient solvent accessible voids in 2 comprise 12.9% of the unit cell volume according to a calculation performed with PLATON [27].

Single oxygen donor atoms from a pair of suc-B ligands construct {Cd2O2} rhomboid dimers with a Cd⋯Cd distance of 3.748(1) Å. The suc-B ligands adopt a slightly twisted anti conformation (four-C atom torsion angle = 168.4°) with an exotetradentate μ4-κ4-O,O′:O':O″,O‴:O‴ binding mode that connects adjacent {Cd2O2} dimers into [Cd2(suc-B)]n chain motifs aligned along the a crystal direction. These are linked into [Cd2(suc-A)(suc-B)]n layer motifs aligned parallel to the ab crystal planes (Fig. 6a) by bis(chelating) anti conformation suc-A ligands (four-C atom torsion angle = 167.6°), that span cadmium atoms in adjacent dimeric units with a Cd⋯Cd distance of 9.188 Å.

Pairs of [Cd2(suc-A)(suc-B)]n layers are connected into sandwich-like [Cd2(suc)2(3-pmna)(H2O)2]n slabs (Fig. 6b) by 3-pmna ligands that span a Cd⋯Cd distance of 11.430 Å. The 3-pmna ligands are curled into an anti L-shaped conformation with a Ψ torsion angle of 140.3°. The {Cd2O2} dimers can be considered to be 5-connected nodes, with a TOPOS calculation [28] revealing a 4862 layered topology (Fig. 7). Neighboring [Cd2(suc)2(3-pmna)(H2O)2]n slabs stack in an AAA pattern along the c crystal direction (Fig. S3), facilitated by hydrogen bond donating between 3-pmna amide N–H groups in one layer and water molecules of crystallization, which in turn donate hydrogen bonds to aqua ligands in another layer (Table 3). The water molecules of crystallization aggregate into discrete trimeric short chains, occupying incipient solvent accessible voids comprising 10.2% of the unit cell volume.

Bis(chelating) glu ligands with an anti–anti conformation (four C-atom torsion angles = 177.0°, 178.9°) connect cadmium atoms into [Cd(glu)(H2O)]n chain motifs (Fig. 9a) that are oriented parallel to the a crystal direction. The internuclear Cd⋯Cd distance of 10.330 Å within the chain motif defines the a lattice parameter. Adjacent [Cd(glu)(H2O)]n chain motifs are pillared into (4,4) grid [Cd(glu)(3-pmna)(H2O)]n coordination polymer layers by 3-pmna ligands (Fig. 9b), with a Cd⋯Cd through-ligand distance of 9.640 Å. The 3-pmna ligands adopt a twisted conformation partway between anti and syn conformations (Ψ torsion angle = 94.5°). The cis orientation of the 3-pmna pyridyl nitrogen donor atoms imparts a noticeable rippling to the layer motif (Fig. 9c).

Adjacent layers are held in place within the crystal structure of 4 by means of hydrogen bonding donation between the aqua ligands and ligated glu oxygen atoms (Table 3), stacking in an ABAB pattern along the b crystal direction (Fig. S4). Discrete trimeric water molecule chains are located in the interlamellar regions, anchored to the coordination polymer framework by accepting hydrogen bonds from the 3-pmna N–H moieties; these occupy 14.7% of the unit cell volume.

4.6. Luminescent properties of 2–4

Polycrystalline samples of 2–4 were irradiated with ultraviolet in order to investigate their capability for photoluminescence. Excitation spectra were first obtained in order to determine the best wavelength for photoluminescent excitation, via monitoring emission at a single wavelength of 400 nm. For 2–4, the maximum excitation was observed at 370 nm, 360 nm, and 260 nm respectively. Broad and modestly intense violet light emission profiles (Fig. 10) were observed in both cases, with maximum emission wavelengths at 425 nm, 418 nm, and 397 nm respectively for 2–4. The 3-pmna ligand showed a maximum emission at 342 nm, with excitation at 250 nm. It is most likely that the fluorescent behavior of these materials can be attributed to π–π* or π–n molecular orbital transitions within the pyridyl rings of the 3-pmna ligands [29], with the redshift caused by complexation to cadmium. Unfortunately, the luminescence of 2–4 in ethanol suspension was insufficient to attempt assaying of potential nitroaromatic sensor behavior.

4.7. Thermal properties

The mass of compound 1 remained stable until 185 °C, with a 25.5% mass loss between this temperature and 200 °C indicative of a loss of oxalate ligands (24.1% calc'd). The 3-pmna ligands were ejected between 200 and 250 °C. The mass remnant of 22.2% at 440 °C matches well with a putative deposition of CuO (21.8% calc'd). For 2, two-stage mass loss totaling 14.3%, with the first stage occurring between 50 and 75 °C, and the second stage occurring between 75 and 115 °C. This corresponds extremely well with the loss of all bound and unbound water (14.4% calc'd). The mass remained stable until 185 °C, whereupon the organic ligands were combusted. The final mass remnant of 26% at 600 °C matches well with a likely deposition of CdO (25.7% calc'd). Compound 3 underwent dehydration between 50 and 155 °C, with a mass of loss of 11.8% matching exactly with that predicted for loss of five molar equivalents of water. Its mass remained stable until 275 °C, whereupon ligand combustion occurred. Compound 4 underwent dehydration between 50 and 100 °C, with a 13.4% mass loss matching well with elimination of four molar equivalents of water. The mass then remained largely stable until 225 °C, whereupon ligand combustion occurred. The TGA traces for 1–4 are shown in Figs. S5–S8, respectively.

5. Conclusions

The kinked, conformationally flexible, and hydrogen-bonding capable 3-pmna ligand continues to show promise as an ancillary tethering ligand in the design and construction of divalent metal coordination polymers with varied topologies. Complex synergistic interactions between metal coordination preferences, cis versus trans donor dispositions, and aliphatic dicarboxylate ligand length, conformation, and binding mode operate during self-assembly to instill a variety of layered topologies in this system. The aromatic rings of the 3-pmna ligands impart a measure of thermal stability and also molecular orbital mechanisms for modest luminescent behavior. The higher connectivity of the sandwich slab structure of the cadmium succinate derivative appears to impart the most thermal stability across this series of coordination polymers.

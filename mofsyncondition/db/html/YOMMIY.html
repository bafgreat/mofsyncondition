Abstract

Hydrothermal synthesis has yielded four divalent metal coordination polymers containing both bis(4-pyridylformyl)piperazine (4-bpfp) and pyromellitate (pyro, 1,2,4,5-benzenetetracarboxylate) ligands, which were structurally characterized by single-crystal X-ray diffraction. Various topologies were obtained depending on specific metal used and level of base in the reaction mixture. {[Cd(pyroH2)(4-bpfp)]·2H2O}n (1) displays an uncommon 3-D binodal fsc network, while {[Zn2(pyro)(4-bpfp)(H2O)8]·4H2O}n (2) and {[Co2(pyro)(4-bpfp)(H2O)8]·4H2O}n (3) manifest aggregated 1-D coordination polymer chains. A decrease in amount of added base from that used in the preparation of 3 resulted in the generation of {[Co(pyroH2)(4-bpfp)]·2H2O}n (4), which has the same 3-D fsc topology as 1. The closed shell complexes 1 and 2 emit blue-violet and blue light, respectively, upon ultraviolet irradiation. Dehydration and decomposition behavior of these four new materials is also presented.

1. Introduction

Throughout the last fifteen years, there has been a worldwide intense basic research focus on the preparation, molecular structure, and properties of crystalline coordination polymer solids. While numerous studies have focused on these materials’ potential role as gas storage substrates [1], as selective absorbents [2], in ion exchange [3], in heterogeneous catalysis [4], and as phosphorescent sensors [5], interest is also piqued by the often beautiful three-dimensional networks and diverse topologies [6]. Among all the myriad possible choices for the organic linking components, aromatic dicarboxylates have proven the most beneficial for the construction of coordination polymer solids [7], [8], [9], [10]. As of this writing, a priori deliberate prediction and control of underlying coordination polymer structure and topology has been achieved only in a few limited scope systems [11], [12], because the combination of possible metal coordination geometries with different carboxylate dispositions and binding modes creates an enormous parameter space.

The structure directing capability for the pyromellitate ligand (pyro, 1,2,4,5-benzenetetracarboxylate, Scheme 1) is even less predictable, with its eight oxygen atoms able to conjoin from two up to ten divalent metal ions [13]. Adjustment of pH levels during coordination polymer self-assembly can afford tetraanionic pyro [13], trianionic pyroH [14], dianionic pyroH2 [15], or monoanionic pyroH3 ligands [16]. Charge balance across these phases can be provided either by adjustment of metal:ligand stoichiometric ratio or by the inclusion of cationic species [17], [18]. Incorporation of neutral dipyridyl coligands can allow access to even greater structural diversity and some functional properties [19]. For example, [Cu(pyroH2)(2,2′-bipyridine)]n can catalyze cyclohexene oxidation [19a], while {[Ni2(pyro)(4,4′-bipyridine)3]·3DMF}n can adsorb N2 after removal of co-crystallized solvent [19c]. Even in pyromellitate/dipyridyl coordination polymers with more limited functional properties, some intriguing and diverse topologies have been reported. {[Zn2(Hpyro)2(H2O)2(H2-3-bpmp)]·H2O}n (3-bpmp = bis(3-pyridylmethyl)piperazine) possesses a rare 4,5-connected binodal net with (4462)(4466) tcs topology. A related phase with the isomeric bis(4-pyridylmethyl)piperazine (4-bpmp) ligand, {[Zn2(Hpyro)2(H2-4-bpmp)]·4H2O}n, manifested an unprecedented yet extremely simple 3,4-connected binodal (4.82)(4.82103) self-penetrated topology [20]. In the case of the cobalt derivative {[H2-4-bpmp][Co(H2O)4(pyro)]·2H2O}n, protonated 4-bpmp molecules provide charge balance by co-crystallizing with anionic coordination polymer chain motifs [21].

In this study we have aimed to extend the scope of divalent metal pyromellitate coordination polymers by employing bis(4-pyridylformyl)piperazine (4-bpfp) as a coligand. Although quite structurally similar to 4-bpmp, 4-bpfp possesses carbonyl groups that can serve both as hydrogen bonding acceptors and as potential ligands. The well-known amide resonance mechanism will also substantially reduce lone pair character at the piperazinyl nitrogen atoms within the 4-bpfp ligands in comparison to 4-bpmp; the expected lack of ability of this site to be protonated will in turn affect the requisite anionic charge balance and pyromellitate protonation levels. In this contribution, we report the synthesis, crystallography, and preliminary physical properties of {[Cd(pyroH2)(4-bpfp)]·2H2O}n (1), {[Zn2(pyro)(4-bpfp)(H2O)8]·4H2O}n (2), {[Co2(pyro)(4-bpfp)(H2O)8]·4H2O}n (3), and {[Co(pyroH2)(4-bpfp)]·2H2O}n (4).

2. Experimental

2.1. General considerations

Metal salts and pyromellitic acid were commercially obtained. Bis(4-pyridylformyl)piperazine was prepared by literature procedure [22]. Water was deionized above 3 MΩ-cm in-house. Thermogravimetric analysis was performed on a TA Instruments Q-50 Thermogravimetric Analyzer with a heating rate of 10 °C/min up to 600 °C. IR spectra were recorded on powdered samples using a Perkin Elmer Spectrum One instrument. The luminescence spectra were obtained with a Hitachi F-4500 Fluorescence Spectrometer on solid crystalline samples anchored to quartz microscope slides with RexonCorporation RX-22P ultraviolet-transparent epoxy adhesive.

3. X-ray crystallography

Diffraction data for 1–4 were collected on a Bruker-AXS SMART-CCD X-ray diffractometer using graphite-monochromated Mo Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å). The crystal of 3 was non-merohedrally twinned; its twin law was determined using cell now [23]. The data were processed via saint [24], and subjected to Lorentz and polarization effect and absorption corrections using sadabs (1, 2, 4) [25] or twinabs (3) [26]. The structures were solved using direct methods with shelxtl [27]. All non-hydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically. Hydrogen atoms bound to carbon were placed in calculated positions and refined isotropically with a riding model. For 1, 2, and 4, hydrogen atoms bound to oxygen atoms were located by Fourier difference map, and restrained with isotropic displacement parameters. For 3, hydrogen atom positions were calculated for the water molecules. Crystallographic details for 1–4 are given in Table 1.

4. Results and discussion

4.1. Synthesis and infrared spectroscopy

Compounds 1–4 were prepared by hydrothermal reaction of a metal nitrate, pyromellitic acid, 4-bpfp, and some added aqueous base. Infrared spectra for 1–4 were consistent with the structural elements and functional groups as determined by X-ray diffraction. Broad, weak bands at ∼3500 cm−1 can be attributed to the O–H bonds in the water molecules of crystallization and amide or pyridyl N–H bonds. Weaker, high energy bands at ∼3000–3200 cm−1 are attributed to C–H bond stretching modes in all cases. Asymmetric and symmetric C–O stretching modes of the pyromellitate ligands are indicated by slightly broadened, strong bands at 1581 and 1381 cm−1 (1), 1575 and 1370 cm−1 (2), 1576 and 1370 cm−1 (3), and 1581 and 1378 cm−1 (4). Sharp, medium intensity bands in the range of ∼1600 to ∼1200 cm−1 are ascribed to stretching modes of the pyridyl rings of the 4-bpfp ligands. Features corresponding to pyridyl ring puckering are observed in the region between 800 and 600 cm−1. Sharp, moderate intensity bands at 1705 cm−1 (1), 1669 cm−1 (2), 1698 cm−1 (3), and 1702 cm−1 (4) indicate the presence of the amide CO carbonyl stretching modes within the 4-bpfp ligands. The ligation of the carboxyl groups of the 4-bpfp ligands in 1 and 4 results in a shift of the CO stretching band to higher wavenumber.

4.2. Structural description of {[Cd(pyroH2)(4-bpfp)]·2H2O}n (1)

The coordination environment at cadmium is a {CdN2O4} octahedron (Fig. 1) with trans 4-bpfp nitrogen donors in equatorial positions. Oxygen donors from two other 4-bpfp ligands and two pyro ligands complete the coordination sphere. Bond lengths and angles within the coordination environment are listed in Table 2.

Bis(monodentate) pyroH2 ligands with a μ2-κ2-O:O′ binding mode and the 4-bpfp ligands link divalent cadmium atoms into 2-D [Cd(pyroH2)(4-bpfp)]n coordination polymer layers (Fig. 2). Here, the pyroH2 ligands construct [Cd(pyroH2)]n chain motifs with a Cd⋯Cd distance of 11.640 Å, with their protonated carboxylate groups remaining unligated. These are bridged through 4-bpfp ligands, which span a Cd⋯Cd distance of 16.586 Å. If the cadmium atoms are considered connecting nodes and the pyroH2 and 4-bpfp ligands are considered linkers, the [Cd(pyroH2)(4-bpfp)]n grid patterns can be construed as simple 4-connected regular (4,4) nets. The Cd⋯Cd through-space distance within the layer submotif is 15.694 Å.

The carbonyl CO groups within the 4-bpfp ligands project above and below the [Cd(pyroH2)(4-bpfp)]n layers, and ligate to cadmium atoms in neighboring layers, thus forming a non-interpenetrated [Cd(pyroH2)(4-bpfp)]n 3-D coordination polymer network (Fig. 3). The cadmium atoms of 1 can be thus be construed as 6-connected nodes. The 4-bpfp ligands act as 4-connected nodes, connecting to two cadmium atoms within a single [Cd(pyroH2)(4-bpfp)]n layer submotif, and to cadmium atoms in two adjacent [Cd(pyroH2)(4-bpfp)]n layers via carboxyl tethers. Topological analysis with TOPOS [28] reveals an underlying non-interpenetrated 4,6-connected binodal fsc net (Fig. 4) with a Schläfli symbol of (4462)(446108). This fsc net is seldom encountered in coordination polymer chemistry, with [(CuI)7(DABCO)2.5]n [29] and pillared sheet coordination polymer [Cd(μ4-SO4)(4,4′-bipyridine)]n [30] being among the few reported examples.

The water molecules of crystallization in 1 are anchored to the 3-D coordination polymer network by hydrogen bonding acceptance from the protonated carboxylate termini of the pyroH2 ligands, and by hydrogen bonding donation to the unligated oxygen atoms belonging to the monodentate carboxylate groups of the pyroH2 ligands. The isolated water molecules of crystallization occupy small pockets within the 3-D network of 1. Geometrical parameters for these hydrogen bonding interactions are listed in Table 3.

The [Zn(H2O)4]2+ square planar fragments are linked into 1-D [Zn(H2O)4(pyro)Zn(H2O)4(4-bpfp)]n chains (Fig. 6) by means of alternating 4-bpfp and bis(monodentate) pyro ligands. 4.6. Luminescent properties of 1 and 2

Excitation of polycrystalline samples of 1 and 2 with ultraviolet light with a wavelength of 240 nm resulted in a moderately intense blue-violet emission in the case of 1, and moderately intense blue emission for 2. The maximum emission wavelength of the cadmium compound 1 was 398 nm, while that of the zinc congener 2 was 450 nm. The broadened emission profiles are shown in Fig. 7. Luminescent behavior for 1 and 2 is ascribed to ligand-centered π–π∗ molecular orbital transitions of the aromatic rings of the pyromellitate ligands and the pyridyl rings of the 4-bpfp ligands, as the d10 closed shell nature of the cadmium and zinc ions precludes low energy ligand-to-metal charge transfer behavior [34]. Although both the cadmium ions in 1 and the zinc ions in 2 manifest a similar octahedral coordination environment, the stronger ligand binding in 2 promotes a red-shift of the emission maximum.

4.7. Thermal decomposition

According to thermogravimetric analysis, the 3-D coordination polymer 1 underwent complete dehydration in two steps between 25 and 200 °C, with a mass loss of 5.7% matching reasonably well with the 5.2% value calculated for loss of two molar equivalents of water molecules of crystallization. The organic components were ejected above 230 °C. At 600 °C, the final mass remnant of 27.1% corresponds to a mixture of CdCO3 along with some uncombusted organic char (24.7% calc’d for CdCO3). Dehydration of the chain coordination polymer 3 was largely complete by 140 °C, as evidenced by a mass loss of 19.5%. The predicted value for elimination of twelve molar equivalents of water is 24.5%, possibly indicating that some of the water molecules of crystallization had effloresced at ambient temperature on long-term storage (∼90 d). Ligand ejection occurred above 250 °C, with the final mass remnant of 15.1% at 600 °C corresponding roughly to a deposition of CoO (17.0% calc’d). Thermogravimetric analysis plots for 1 and 3 are shown in Figs. S7 and S8, respectively.

5. Conclusions

Hydrothermal reaction of divalent metal salts with pyromellitic acid and the long-spanning dipyridine 4-bpfp has afforded a series of four coordination polymers containing both ligands. The preference of cadmium to bind to the carbonyl oxygen atoms of the 4-bpfp in lieu of aqua ligands resulted in a 3-D coordination polymer net with a rare fcs topology. In the zinc case, the preference for aqua ligands resulted in a simple coordination polymer chain topology, with unligated 4-bpfp carbonyl groups. Depending on pH, cobalt derivatives with both 3-D fsc and chain topology could be obtained. A higher level of added base promotes the complete deprotonation of pyromellitic acid, necessitating a 2:1 cobalt:pyro ratio and formation of the 1-D chain structure. The topology of the zinc derivative 2 varies dramatically from its related congener with 4-bpmp, as the absence of lone pair character on the piperazinyl nitrogen atoms in 4-bpfp prevents their protonation. Similarly, the neutral cobalt chain complex 4 differs from the anionic chain motif seen in its related 4-bpmp analog, which possessed protonated 4-bpmp counterions. The 3-D cobalt phase 3 can be obtained due to the ligation of the 4-bpfp carbonyl groups, which allows the ligand to serve as a 4-connected node instead of a simple linker; a lower amount of base in this case afforded doubly protonated pyro ligands that act as simple linkers within the uncommon fsc topology. Thus, variation of metal cation and pH of the reaction mixture result in preference of one of the energetically similar 1-D or 3-D coordination polymer topologies in this system.

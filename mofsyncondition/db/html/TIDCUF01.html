Syntheses, characterizations and topology analyses of two 3D copper–organic frameworks from Cu(pyzca)2 (pyzca = pyrazine-2-carboxylate) building block

Author links open overlay panel
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.inoche.2008.06.021Get rights and content

Abstract

The solvothermal reactions of CuX (X = CN, SCN) with Cu(pyzca)2 (pyzca = pyrazine-2-carboxylate) afforded compounds Cu2(CN)(pyzca) (1) and CuI(SCN)Cu0.5II(pyzca) (2), respectively. They are both characterized by infrared spectroscopy, elemental analysis and X-ray single-crystal analysis. The structure of 1 exhibits a (728)2(7383) network which has not been reported for the (3, 4)-connected nets, while that of 2 displays a (63)(658) network which belongs to the ins topology.

Graphical abstract

Two copper–organic frameworks, namely, Cu2(CN)(pyzca) (1) and CuI(SCN)Cu0.5II(pyzca) (2), have been isolated from the reaction of CuX (X = CN, SCN) with Cu(pyzca)2 (pyzca = pyrazine-2-carboxylate) under solvothermal conditions, respectively. They are both characterized by infrared spectroscopy, elemental analysis and X-ray single-crystal analysis. The framework of 1 exhibits an unreported (728)2(7383) network for the (3, 4)-connected nets, while that of 2 can be attributed to the ins topology.

Over the past decade, the field of metal–organic coordination networks has witnessed tremendous success in the synthesis and structural characterization of numerous metal–organic frameworks (MOFs), many of which exhibit novel topological structures and potential applications as functional materials [1], [2], [3], [4], [5]. Generally, the multidentate ligands containing rich coordination sites (N- or/and O-donors) are widely utilized as molecular building blocks in the construction of MOFs, because of their versatile coordination modes, structural diversity and thermodynamic stability. Among these, the aromatic polycarboxylates, e.g. benzene dicarboxylate, benzene tricarboxylate and 4-carboxylate pyridine and the polypyridyl based ligands, such as 4,4′-bipy (4,4′-bipydine), bpp (1,3-bi(4-pyridyl)propane) and bix (1,4-bis(imidazol-1-ylmethyl)-benzene), have been extensively investigated for the design and synthesis of MOFs [6], [7], [8]. The structures and topologies of the resultant MOFs are greatly influenced by the nature of these ligands, e.g. the flexibility, the size of spacer groups and the ligand conformations. Meanwhile, some metal complexes can also be used instead of simple multifunctional organic ligands in the construction of supramolecular arrays [9]. These metal complexes, usually called metalloligands, contain uncoordinated or/and coordination unsaturated donor atoms which are capable to combine with other metal ions under certain reaction conditions to create extended architectures [10]. The use of metalloligands as linkers in principle allows for the incorporation of a wide variety of spacer types that have no organic analogue, and this may offer the possibility of rational design and better control over the structures of the resulting assemblies. It also provides a useful methodology for the preparation of heterometallic metal–organic frameworks [11].

The Cu(pyzca)2 (pyzca = pyrazine-2-carboxylate) complex (Chart 1) should be a good metalloligand for the construction of new metal–organic frameworks. Surprisingly, this complex has seldom been used for the construction of MOFs, despite the fact that several mixed-metal coordination polymers have been successfully synthesized by using Cu(pyzca)2 as building blocks [12]. This could be due to the poor solubility of Cu(pyzca)2 that limits its use as a metal-containing ligand. We thought that such problem might be overcome by carrying out the self-assembly reactions in more polar solvents such as DMF at high temperature. In the present work, the solvothermal reactions of Cu(pyzca)2 with CuCN or CuSCN were investigated which led to the isolation of two 3D polymeric compounds Cu2(CN)(pyzca) (1) and CuI(SCN)Cu0.5II(pyzca) (2). In the former compound, the Cu(II) ion in Cu(pyzca)2 was reduced into Cu(I) with the loss of one pyzca− ligand, whereas in the later compound the Cu(pyzca)2 remains unchanged and serves as a bitopic linker.

The Cu(pyzca)2 complex is commercially available and used without further purification. Compound 1 has been obtained from the solvothermal reaction of Cu(pyzca)2 with CuCN in DMF at 160 °C [13]. The preparation of 2 is similar to that of 1 except that CuSCN was used instead of CuCN [13]. The IR spectra of 1 (Fig. S1) and 2 (Fig. S2) exhibit bands at 2138.1 and 2115.8 cm−1, respectively, which can be assigned to the vibrations of the bridging cyanide groups [14].

Single-crystal structure study reveals that 1 crystallizes in the monoclinic space group P21/n. The asymmetrical unit of 1 contains two crystallographically independent univalent Cu ions, one cyanide group and one pyzca− ligand. It should be pointed out that the assignment for C and N in the bridging cyanide groups in 1 is uncertain, a common characteristic of such structure types [14](b), [15], [16]. As shown in Fig. 1a, the Cu1 atom is coordinated by one N atom and one O atom from two different pyzca− ligands (Cu1–N3 = 2.177(2), Cu1–O1 = 1.9193(19) Å) and one X atom belonged to the cyanide group (Cu1–X2 = 1.850(2) Å). The coordination environment of Cu2 is similar to that of Cu1. It is coordinated to one N atom and one O atom from a pyzca− ligand (Cu2–N4 = 1.966(2), Cu2–O2 = 2.234(2) Å) and one X atom from the cyanide group (Cu2–X1 = 1.863(2) Å). The distances of Cu1⋯Cu2 (2.9913(8) Å) is a little longer than twice of the van der Waals radius of Cu (2.8 Å), indicating a non-negligible copper–copper interactions [17]. The 1D wavelike chains based on Cu ions and pyzca− ligands are linked by such weak copper–copper interactions to form a 2D layer (Fig. 1b). These 2D layers are further connected by symmetry-related disordered cyanide groups (X1X1B and X2X2C) leading to the formation of a 3D microporous framework (Fig. S3).

The pyzca− ligand in 1 can be viewed as a three-connected node when it is simplified as a pellet. Taking the copper–copper interactions into account, the Cu1 and Cu2 atoms can be regarded as four- and three-connected nodes, respectively. In this way, the structure of 1 can be rationalized as a (3, 4)-connected net. The molar ratio of these three kinds of nodes is 1:1:1 (pyzca/Cu2/Cu1). Thus, the framework of 1 is symbolized as a (728)(728)(7383) network (Fig. 1c). When further analyzed by OLEX program [18], the vertex symbols of the three nodes are 7·7·83, 7·7·83 and 7·7·7·82·8·8, respectively. Therefore, the Schäfli symbol of 1 can be simply regarded as (728)2(7383).

Structure determination shows that 2 crystallizes in the monoclinic space group P21/c. The asymmetrical unit of 2 consists of one univalent Cu ion, half divalent Cu ion, one SCN− group and one pyzca− ligand. The local coordination environment of Cu is shown in Fig. 2a. The Cu1 center is surrounded by one N atom from pyzca− ligand (Cu1–N2 = 2.0735(17) Å), one N atom from SCN− group (Cu1–N1 = 1.9231(17) Å) and two symmetry-related S atoms from SCN− groups (Cu1–S1 A = 2.3475(6), Cu1–S1B = 2.3655(6) Å). The bond angles around Cu1 center vary from 101.70(5)° to 116.40(8)°, which are a little deviated from that of the ideal tetrahedron. The coordination geometry of Cu2 center is very different from that of Cu1. It is located in the center of a parallelogram defined by O1 and N3 from pyzca− ligand (Cu2–O1 = 1.9268(15), Cu2–N3 = 1.9731(17) Å) as well as their corresponding symmetry-related atoms. The SCN groups act as triply-bridged ligands in the architecture of 2, linking Cu1 atoms to produce a 2D {CuSCN} substructure with (6, 3) topology (Fig. 2b). While most of the {CuSCN} substructures reported so far are 1D single or staircase chains [19], the 2D {CuSCN} substructures in 2 have been relatively rarely observed for coordination polymers with bridging bidentate ligands [20]. The {CuSCN} substructures are further bridged by the bidentate Cu(pyzca)2 metalloligands to generate a 3D network, as illustrated in Fig. S4. The framework of 2 can be described as a (3, 4)-connected network when Cu(pyzca)2 and SCN− groups are viewed as connectors. Thus we can get the overall topology of 2 defined as (S1)(Cu1) with a Schäfli symbol of (63)(658), as illustrated in Fig. 2c. The topological analysis of 2 has been performed on OLEX program [18] giving the vertex symbols of the two nodes as 6·6·62 and 6·6·6·6·6·106, respectively.

Reticular chemistry is the basis for materials with interesting and useful properties. A large number of 3D high symmetry networks have been enumerated within the RCSR, EPINET and Topos database [21], [22], [23]. Among the known (3, 4)-connected nets in coordination polymers [24], the most symmetrical and important examples are boracite, Pt3O4 [25], cubic-C3N3 [26] and the twisted boracite [27]. To further study the topology of 1 and 2, various (3, 4)-connected binodal nets have been carefully examined in the resources of RCSR, EPINET and Topos [21], [22], [23]. In all the found (3, 4)-connected binodal nets, however, none of them is the same as that of 1. Therefore, the structure of 1 provides another valuable prototype of (3, 4)-connected nets which would be important for the design of the metal–organic frameworks. According to O’Keeffe [21] and Wells [28], the net with a Schäfli symbol of (63)(658) displayed by 2 has been assigned as a ins notation. The coordination sequences of ins net are 3, 9, 17, 31, 51, 72, 93, 129, 165, 189 for a 3-connected node and 4, 9, 19, 35, 50, 70, 102, 127, 155, 205 for a 4-connected node [21]. In the ins net, the S atom is triconnected and In is tetraconnected [29], whereas in the topological net of 2, the triconnected node and tetraconnected node is corresponding to S and Cu atoms, respectively.

In summary, we have successfully isolated two 3D microporous compounds from CuX (X = CN or SCN) and Cu(pyzca)2 via solvothermal reactions. They have been characterized by elemental analysis, FT-IR spectra and single-crystal X-ray diffraction studies. They both exhibit 3D complicated topology networks. Compound 1 can be described as a (728)2(7383) network which has not been observed in the reported (3, 4)-connected nets, while compound 2 display a (63)(658) net which has been assigned as a ins notation.

  • Single crystal to single crystal transition in (10, 3)-d framework with pyrazine-2-carboxylate ligand: Synthesis, structures and magnetism

    2012, Journal of Solid State Chemistry

    Assembling of pyrazine-2-carboxylate (Pzc) acid with nickel chlorine under solvothermal condition with MeOH as solvent gave a porous complex 1 {[Ni(Pzc)ClH2O]·MeOH}n with 1D channels. In 1 the ligands and metal ions are connected by three of each other and a rare (10,3)-d topology net is gained. The MeOH molecules filled in the 1D channels as guests.

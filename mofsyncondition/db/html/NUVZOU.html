Introduction

The synthesis and characterization of coordination polymers remains a fertile area of research, as these materials show capability in gas storage,1 shape-selective absorption,2 ion exchange,3 catalysis,4 non-linear optics,5 and luminescence.6 Many also show intriguing magnetic properties at low temperatures.7Dicarboxylate dianions have been the most prominent choice for the design and generation of coordination polymer solids;8–10 the tremendous structural diversity accessible in this genre of materials is hinged on the coordination geometry preferences of the metal ions, the spatial orientation of the carboxylate donor sites, and the specific binding mode (e.g.bis(monodentate), monodentate/chelating, etc.). Aromatic dicarboxylates have been used to impart structural rigidity to the resulting coordination polymer frameworks, often in the presence of nitrogen-based co-ligands such as 4,4′-bipyridine (4,4′-bpy).11,12 Aliphatic α,ω-dicarboxylate ligands with a higher degree of conformational flexibility have also been successfully used in the formation of interpenetrated three-dimensional (3-D) phases.13–15

Dissimilar to the ubiquitous rigid rod tether 4,4′-bpy, 4,4′-dipyridylamine (dpa) has a kinked disposition of its nitrogen donors, along with hydrogen-bonding donation ability. This ligand has proven especially fruitful in the synthesis of aliphatic α,ω-dicarboxylate divalent metal coordination polymers, some with novel structural topologies.16[Co(succinate)(dpa)]n exhibited a hydrogen-bonded system of interdigitated homochiral helices while {[Ni(dpa)2(succinate)0.5]Cl} possesses an unprecedented self-penetrated 5-connected 610 (rld-z) 3-D network topology.17 {M2(malonate)2(dpa)(H2O)2]·2H2O}n (M = Co, Ni) presented isostructural 4466sqp 3-D topologies, but manifested divergent magnetic properties.18 {[Cu2(malonate)2(dpa)(H2O)2]·H2O}n displayed alternating syn–anti and anti–anti bridged copper carboxylate chains linked into 2-D (6,3) herringbone layers.19

Use of the substituted aliphatic dicarboxylate 2,2-dimethylsuccinate (dms) afforded coordination polymers whose topologies differ dramatically from their unsubstituted succinate congeners.20[Co(dms)(dpa)]n had a 4-fold interpenetrated 42638 sra topology in contrast to helical chains. [Ni(dms)(dpa)]n exhibited a simple (4,4) rectangular grid structure, albeit with {Ni–O–}n chains that show antiferromagnetic superexchange and possible spin canting below 5 K. This prior work inspired our efforts to probe the effect of methylmalonate (mmal) and dimethylmalonate (dmmal) ligands on the topology and physical properties of dpa-containing divalent metal coordination polymers, especially in relationship to the previously reported unsubstituted malonate phases. Four new complexes with diverse underlying dimensionalities and topologies were obtained as a result of these synthetic investigations: {[Cu(mmal)(Hmmal)(Hdpa)]·H2O}n (1), {[Cu3(dmmal)2(dpa)3](ClO4)2·2H2O}n (2), {[Cd2(dmmal)2(dpa)(Hdpa)](ClO4)·2H2O·CH3CH2OH}n (3), and {[Co(dmmal)2(Hdpa)2]·6H2O} (4). Variable temperature magnetic properties of 1 and 2, and luminescent properties of 3 are also discussed herein.

Results and discussion

Synthesis and spectral characterization

Compounds 1–4 were prepared cleanly as crystalline products at room temperature by slow diffusion of an ethanolic solution of dpa with an aqueous solution of the relevant metal perchlorate and dicarboxylic acid. The infrared spectra of all compounds corresponded with their single crystal structures. Broad yet weak bands in the region of ∼3400 cm−1 to ∼3200 cm−1 in all cases represent N–H stretching modes within the dpa and Hdpa+ ligands and O–H bonds within water molecules of crystallization. The broadness of these higher energy spectral features is caused by the hydrogen-bonding present in all cases (see below). Medium intensity bands in the range of ∼1600 cm−1 to ∼1200 cm−1 can be ascribed to stretching modes of the pyridyl rings of the dpa ligands.21 A moderate intensity band at 1724 cm−1 for 1 denotes the presence of a protonated mmal carboxylategroup. Asymmetric and symmetric C–O stretching modes of the dicarboxylate ligands correspond to the strong, broadened features at ∼1509 cm−1 and ∼1358/1336 cm−1 (for 1), ∼1526 cm−1 and ∼1362 cm−1 (for 2), ∼1517 cm−1 and ∼1342 cm−1 (for 3) and ∼1514 cm−1 and ∼1416 cm−1 (for 4). The differences in Δν between the carboxylate bands reflect differences in the binding mode of the mmal or dmmal ligands across the series 1–4. Perchlorate Cl–O stretching bands were observed in the vicinity of ∼1090 cm−1 in the spectra of 2 and 3. Puckering modes of the pyridyl rings are evident in the region between 820 cm−1 and 600 cm−1.

Structural description of {[Cu(mmal)(Hmmal)(Hdpa)]·H2O}n (1)

Compound 1 crystallizes in the acentric orthorhombic space groupPca21, with a Flack parameter22 of 0.020(15) indicating enantiomeric purity within the crystal. Its asymmetric unit (Fig. 1) contains a divalent copper atom, one fully deprotonated mmal ligand, one singly protonated Hmmal ligand, and one protonated Hdpa+ ligand. The copper coordination environment is best described as a distorted {CuO4N} square pyramid, with an Addison τ factor23 of 0.142. Two of the coordination sites in the basal plane are occupied by carboxylateoxygen atoms from the deprotonated mmal ligand, which binds to copper in a 1,3-chelating fashion. A carboxylateoxygen atom from a protonated Hmmal ligand and a dpanitrogen donor atom are oriented in a cis fashion in the remaining two basal plane sites. The apical position is occupied by an oxygen donor atom from a second Hmmal ligand. Bond lengths and angles are consistent with a singly chelated Jahn–Teller distorted copper coordination sphere (Table S1†). A long-range Cu⋯O interaction of 2.824 Å with one of the dmmal oxygen atoms provides a “5 + 1” environment at copper.

Extension of the structure through the Hmmal ligands reveals a {[Cu(mmal)(Hmmal)(Hdpa)]}n coordination polymer chain motif (Fig. 2), in which adjacent copper atoms are bridged in an anti–syn axial–equatorial manner by one carboxylate terminus of a single Hmmal ligand in an embedded {Cu(OCO)}n chain. The Cu⋯Cu distance along the chain is 4.773 Å. As the chain pattern propagates along the b crystal direction, this distance marks the b lattice parameter. Neither oxygen atom of the other carboxylategroup of the Hmmal ligand binds to copper. The 1,3-chelating deprotonated mmal ligands do not bridge neighboring copper atoms, nor do the pendant, protonated Hdpa ligands.

Hydrogen-bonding interactions between the central amine groups of the Hdpa+ ligands and unligated oxygen atoms of the 1,3-chelating mmal ligands connect the {[Cu(mmal)(Hmmal)(Hdpa)]}n chains along the a crystal direction to construct a supramolecular layer (Fig. S1†). In turn these layers aggregate into a supramolecular network via strong charge-assisted N+–H⋯Ohydrogen-bonding24 between the protonated pendant pyridyl groups of the Hdpa+ ligands and other unligated carboxylateoxygen atoms belonging to mmal ligands. Hydrogen-bonding geometric parameters are given in Table S2†. If all of the hydrogen-bonding interactions are considered covalent bonds, the supramolecular topology of 1 is a 6-connected primitive cubic network.

Structural description of {[Cu3(dmmal)2(dpa)3](ClO4)2·2H2O}n (2)

The asymmetric unit of compound 2 (Fig. 3) contains two crystallographically distinct divalent copper atoms, one of which (Cu2) rests on a crystallographic inversion center. In addition the asymmetric unit contains one fully deprotonated dmmal ligand, along with one-and-one-half dpa ligands (dpa-A, N1–N3; dpa-B, N4–N5). The N–H amine functional group of dpa-B lies on the crystallographic 2-fold rotation axis. There are also one unligated perchlorate counteranion (disordered over two positions in a 70 : 30 ratio) and one water molecule of crystallization within the asymmetric unit.

The coordination environment at Cu1 (blue in Fig. 3) is a distorted {CuO3N2} square pyramid (τ = 0.190), in which the basal plane contains two transnitrogen donors from two dpa-A ligands and one oxygen atom from a carboxylategroup of a dmmal ligand. The remaining basal position and the apical site are spanned by a dmmal ligand in a 1,3-chelating binding mode. The special position Cu2 atom (green in Fig. 3) has {CuN2O2} square planar coordination, with transnitrogen donor atoms from two dpa-B ligands and transoxygen donors from two dmmal ligands. Bond lengths and angles about Cu1 and Cu2 are given in Table S3†.

Each Cu2 atom is bridged by dmmal carboxylategroups (O3, O4) to two Cu1 atoms, creating a {Cu3} linear trimeric kernel (Fig. 4). The Cu1⋯Cu2 distance within the trimer is 4.450 Å. The crystallographically distinct copper atoms are joined by anti–syn bridging carboxylates that connect the equatorial positions of each Cu1 coordination sphere through the square planar coordinated Cu2 atom. The Cu1 atoms are connected to two other Cu1 atoms (Cu1⋯Cu1 distance = 4.888 Å) through dmmal carboxylates (O1, O2), also in an anti–syn bridging mode. Here, each dmmal carboxylategroup bridges the axial position of one Cu1 square pyramid to the equatorial position of another. Thus, each dmmal ligand is exotridentate, chelating to one Cu1 atom and bridging to one Cu2 and one Cu1 atom. Through this binding mode every trimer is connected to four other trimers, constructing a 2-D cationic [Cu3(dmmal)2]n2n+ layer (Fig. 5). Water molecules of crystallization lie within the layer apertures.

Neighboring [Cu3(dmmal)2]n2n+ layers are pillared by the dipodal dpa tethers, with Cu1 atoms bridged by dpa-A and Cu2 atoms bridged by dpa-B, thereby forming a 3-D [Cu3(dmmal)2(dpa)3]n2n+ cationic network (Fig. 6). The through-dpaCu1⋯Cu1 and Cu2⋯Cu2 distances are 11.631 and 11.616 Å. This slight variance is imparted by subtle alterations in the inter-ring torsion within the dpa ligands (10.5° in dpa-A, 23.8° in dpa-B). If the anti–syncarboxylate bridges and dpa tethers are treated as spacers, Cu1 can be considered a 5-connected node (connecting four Cu1 and one Cu2 atom), with Cu2 a 4-connected node (connecting two Cu1 atoms and two Cu2 atoms). Therefore the overall [Cu3(dmmal)2(dpa)3]n2n+ cationic network can be considered to be a (4,5)-connected binodal lattice (Fig. 7), which visually resembles a pillared graphite-layer structure. According to TOPOS,25 this lattice possesses an unprecedented (4462)(4664)2 topology. The unligated perchlorate counteranions and water molecules of crystallization occupy extra-framework space comprising 19.5% of the unit cell volume, as determined by PLATON.26

Structural description of {[Cd2(dmmal)2(dpa)(Hdpa)](ClO4)·2H2O·CH3CH2OH}n (3)

The asymmetric unit of compound 3 (Fig. 8) contains two divalent cadmium atoms, two dmmal dianions, one dpa molecule, one protonated Hdpa+ cation, one unligated perchlorate anion, two water molecules of crystallization, and a co-crystallized ethanol molecule. The coordination environment at Cd1 is a distorted {CdO5N} octahedron, defined by four oxygen donors from two 1,1-chelating dmmal carboxylategroups, a single oxygen atom donor from a third dmmal ligand, and a nitrogen donor from a dpa ligand. In contrast the coordination environment at Cd2 is a {CdO5N2} distorted pentagonal bipyramid. Here, the nitrogen donor atoms are oriented in a trans fashion, belonging to a dpa and Hdpa+ ligand, respectively. The equatorial plane of the pentagonal bipyramid is occupied by a 1,1-chelating dmmal carboxylategroup, a 1,3-chelating dmmal carboxylategroup, and a single oxygen atom donor from a third dmmal ligand. Relevant bond data for 3 are listed in Table S4†.

One type of dmmal ligand binds to one Cd2 atom in a 1,3-chelating mode, another Cd2 atom with a 1,1-chelating binding mode, and to a Cd1 atom via a 1,1-chelating binding mode at its other terminus. The other type of dmmal ligand connects Cd1 and Cd2 atoms by bridging across one carboxylategroup, and binds to another Cd1 atom via 1,1-chelation. Thus, a [Cd2(dmmal)2]n chain motif is formed through both exotridentate types of dmmal ligands (Fig. 9). This chain contains an alternating pattern of 4-membered {CdOCdO} rings, 6-membered {CdOCdOCO} rings, and 12-membered {Cd(OC3O)Cd(OC3O)} rings, across which the respective Cd⋯Cd contact distances measure 3.776, 3.838, and 5.847 Å.

Neighboring [Cd2(dmmal)2]n chains are connected into a layered motif through pillaring dpa ligands, constructing (6,3) herringbone grid [Cd2(dmmal)2(dpa)(Hdpa)]nn+ cationic layers (Fig. 10). The pendant Hdpa+ ligands stretch across the grid spaces and hydrogen bond to dmmal carboxylateoxygen atoms via protonated pyridyl groups. Water molecule dimers, unligated ethanol molecules, and perchlorate counterions are situated between the layers (Fig. S2†), linked to the coordination polymer backbone by various hydrogen-bonding mechanisms (Table S2†). The unligated species occupy 22.7% of the unit cell volume, according to PLATON.

Structural description of {[Co(dmmal)2(Hdpa)2]·6H2O} (4)

The asymmetric unit of compound 4 consists of a cobalt atom on a crystallographic 2-fold rotation axis, one dimethylmalonate ligand, and one dpa molecule protonated at its pyridylnitrogen atom, along with three water molecules of crystallization. Operation of the 2-fold rotation axis generates a complete [Co(dmmal)2(Hdpa)2] neutral molecular species (Fig. 11). The coordination environment at cobalt is a {CoN2O4} octahedron, with cis disposed pyridylnitrogen donor atoms from two different Hdpa+ ligands. The remaining four coordination sites are taken up by two dmmal ligands, attached in a 1,3-chelating binding mode. Hydrogen-bonded to the individual molecular units are aggregations of six hydrogen-bonded water molecules, consisting of a central rhomboid tetramer unit decorated by a water molecule on opposite sides. The classification term for this water molecule aggregation is R4A2, according to the nomenclature of Motherwell and Infantes.27 Neighboring [Co(dmmal)2(Hdpa)2] molecules construct supramolecular ribbons (Fig. S3†) by hydrogen-bonding between the protonated pyridyl groups of the Hdpa+ ligands and ligated dmmal carboxylateoxygen atoms. In turn the ribbon motifs organize into a supramolecular layer through π–π interactions between pendant pyridyl groups of the Hdpa+ moieties (ring-centroid-to-ring-centroid distance = 3.5612(9) Å as determined by PLATON). Adjacent supramolecular layers are held together by hydrogen-bonding mechanisms involving the R4A2 water molecule aggregations. Metrical parameters regarding the hydrogen-bonding mechanisms in 4 are given in Table S2†.

Magnetic properties of 1 and 2

Variable temperature magnetic studies were carried out to investigate spin communication along the anti–syn{Cu(OCO)}n chains in 1 and within the equatorial–equatorial bridged {Cu3O2} linear trimers in 2. The data for 1 followed the Curie–Weiss law (Fig. S4†) over the entire temperature range (2–300 K), with C = 0.40 cm3K mol−1 and Θ = −0.41 K, signifying weak antiferromagnetic coupling. The χmT value for 1 at 300 K was 0.40 cm3K mol−1, consistent with that expected for an uncoupled S = 1/2 ion. As temperature decreased, χmT remained largely constant down to 25 K, dropping to 0.35 cm3K mol−1 at 7 K, and reaching a value of 0.24 cm3K mol−1 at 2 K. The χmT data (Fig. 12) were modeled using Baker's expression28 for a chain of S = 1/2 spins (eqn (1)), with the best fit values of g = 2.07(1) and J = −0.46(7) cm−1, with R = 1.9 × 10−3 = {Σ[(χmT)obs − (χmT)calc]2/Σ[(χmT)obs]2}. The small negative J value corroborates the presence of weak antiferromagnetic coupling along the {Cu(OCO)}n chains in 1. The weakness of the coupling is ascribed to the axial–equatorial bridging mode of the carboxylategroups of the Hmmal ligands, which causes orthogonality of the magnetic dx2−y2 orbitals on adjacent copper ions. Distortion from idealized square pyramidal geometry prevents complete orthogonality of these orbitals, permitting a modicum of antiferromagnetic superexchange. (1) with A = 1 + 5.7979916x + 16.902653x2 + 29.376885x3 + 29.832959x4 + 14.036918x5 and B = 1 + 2.7979916x + 7.0086780x2 + 8.6538644x3 + 4.5743114x4 where x = J/2kT.

The magnetic data for 2 also obeyed the Curie–Weiss law (Fig. S5†) over the interval 3–300 K, with C = 1.30 cm3 K mol trimer−1 and Θ = −2.0 K, indicating likely antiferromagnetic coupling within the equatorial–equatorial bridged {Cu3O2} linear trimers. The χmT value for 2 at 300 K was 1.27 cm3 K mol trimer−1, near that expected for three uncoupled S = 1/2 ions. This value remained relatively constant upon cooling from 300 to 40 K, then slowly decreased to 1.23 cm3K mol−1 at 25 K, 0.92 cm3K mol−1 at 10 K, and finally 0.35 cm3K mol−1 at 3 K. The magnetic data were fit (Fig. 13) to Hatfield's expression29 for a linear trimer of S = 1/2 ions (eqn (2)), giving g = 2.22(1) and J = −3.5(1) cm−1, with R = 9.5 × 10−3, where J denotes the coupling between the central copper ion and those on the periphery of the trimer. Incorporating a mean-field term30 into the expression to account for any inter-trimer coupling resulted in much poorer fits. Antiferromagnetic superexchange within the {Cu3O2} linear trimers would result in an inability to cancel all spins. The χmT value at 3 K, 0.35 cm3K mol−1, is close to that expected for a remnant S = 1/2 spin ground state. (2)

Luminescent properties of 3

Irradiation of complex 3 with ultraviolet light (λ = 300 nm) in the solid state resulted in moderately intense blue-violet visible light emission with a λmax of 375 nm (Fig. 14). By comparison with other d10 metal coordination polymers containing aromatic ligands,31 this property is likely a result of ligand-centered π–π* electronic transitions within the molecular orbital manifolds of the aromatic ring systems of the dpa and Hdpa+ ligands.

Conclusions

Room temperature layering of aqueous solutions of metal perchlorates and substituted malonic acids with ethanolic solutions of 4,4′-dipyridylamine generated a series of coordination complexes whose covalent dimensionalities depend critically on the metal coordination environment and the steric bulk of the dicarboxylate component. While the monomethyl-substituted complex 1 was a relatively simple carboxylate-bridged 1-D coordination polymer, its dimethyl-substituted analogue 2 manifested a cationic lattice with a unique (4,5)-connected 3-D binodal topology. The structures of both 1 and 2 differed substantially from the (6,3) herringbone 2-D grid lattice seen in the unsubstituted malonate complex {[Cu2(malonate)2(dpa)(H2O)2]·H2O}n. The different levels of steric hindrance appear to play a synergistic structure-directing role in this system. The presence of a single methyl group alters the local coordination environment at copper, causing 1,1-chelation in addition to 1,3-chelation of the carboxylate ligands. Thus, the herringbone grid pattern seen in {[Cu2(malonate)2(dpa)(H2O)2]·H2O}n is avoided, and the observed 1-D chain motif of 1 is seen instead. Superficially, compound 2 presents a similar 1,3-chelating/bridging binding mode as in its unsubstituted congener. However, the steric bulk caused by the gem-dimethyl substituents in 2 apparently forces the formation of {Cu3O2} linear trimers, which aggregate into a cationic [Cu3(dmmal)2]n2n+ layer motif. The nitrogen donor ligands project above and below this layer, resulting in a topologically unique 3-D lattice. In addition, the variable levels of steric bulk at the 2-position of the malonate ligands promote a wide divergence in the antiferromagnetic submotifs of 1 and 2, namely, 1-D copper carboxylate chains and linear trimers, respectively.

On the other hand, the (6,3) grid structure of compound 3 was largely similar to that seen in {[Cd2(2,2-dimethylsuccinate)2(dpa)(Hdpa)](ClO4)·6H2O}n,32 showing that shortening the α,ω-dicarboxylate length does not appreciably alter coordination polymer topology in this particular system. Unfortunately no cadmium/dpa coordination polymer containing the unsubstituted malonate ligand has been made to date, precluding a direct structural comparison. The steric bulk imparted by the gem-dimethyl substituents on the dicarboxylate ligand in the cobalt derivative 4 appears to obviate completely the formation of an extended network, as this complex's unsubstituted malonate analogue possesses a 5-connected 3-D coordination polymer net.

Experimental section

General considerations

Metal perchlorates and the substituted malonic acids were purchased from Aldrich. 4,4′-Dipyridylamine (dpa) was prepared via a published procedure.33Water was deionized above 3 MΩ cm in-house. IR spectra were recorded on powdered samples using a Perkin Elmer Spectrum One instrument. The luminescence spectrum of 3 was obtained with a Hitachi F-4500 Fluorescence Spectrometer on solid crystalline samples anchored to quartzmicroscope slides with Rexon Corporation RX-22P ultraviolet-transparent epoxy adhesive. Variable temperature magnetic susceptibility data (2 K to 300 K) were collected on a Quantum Design MPMS SQUID magnetometer at an applied field of 0.1 T. After each temperature change the sample was kept at the new temperature for 5 min before magnetization measurement to ensure thermal equilibrium. The susceptibility data were corrected for diamagnetism using Pascal's constants.30

Powder X-ray diffraction experiments, to check phase purity, were performed on a Rigaku Rotaflex instrument using Θ − 2Θ scans. The powder XRD spectra for 1–3 are shown in Fig. S6–S8†, respectively.

X-Ray crystallography

Single crystal X-ray diffraction was performed with a Bruker-AXS SMART 1k CCD instrument at 173 K. Reflection data were acquired using graphite-monochromated Mo-Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å). The data were integrated viaSAINT.34 Lorentz and polarization effect and empirical absorption corrections were applied with SADABS.35 The structures were solved using direct methods and refined on F2 using SHELXTL.36 All non-hydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically. Hydrogen atoms bound to carbon atoms were placed in calculated positions and refined isotropically with a riding model. The hydrogen atoms bound to the central amine and/or pyridylnitrogen atoms of the dpa and Hdpa+ moieties were found via Fourier difference maps, then restrained at fixed positions and refined isotropically. Distended thermal ellipsoids on some of the pendant Hdpa carbon atoms in 3 likely indicate some level of disorder, however, this could not be successfully modeled.

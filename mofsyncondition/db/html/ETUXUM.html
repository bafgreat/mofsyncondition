Organically templated zinc selenites: MIL-86 or [H2N(CH2)2NH2]2·Zn4(SeO3)4 and MIL-87 or [H3N(CH2)3NH3]4·Zn4(SeO3)8

https://doi.org/10.1016/j.solidstatesciences.2003.12.011Get rights and content

Abstract

Two new organically templated zinc selenites MIL-86 or [H2N(CH2)2NH2]2·Zn4(SeO3)4 and MIL-87 or [H3N(CH2)3NH3]4·Zn4(SeO3)8 have been prepared by the reaction of ZnO with SeO2 under hydrothermal conditions in the presence of organic amines. Crystal data: MIL-86, monoclinic, space group P21/n, a=6.7712(4) Å, b=9.4520(5) Å, c=8.0295(4) Å, β=113.601(1)°, V=470.91(4) Å3; MIL-87, orthorhombic, space group Pbcm, a=8.8311(3) Å, b=7.8391(2) Å, c=16.2992(4) Å, V=1128.36(6) Å3. MIL-86 combines the structural features of templated networks and coordination polymers. Its structure is built up from ZnO3N tetrahedra and SeO3 pseudo-pyramids as the polyhedral building units. The ZnN bond corresponds to a direct link between zinc and the ethylenediamine template. In the other hand, MIL-87 is built up from chains of vertex-linked ZnO4 and SeO3 building units crosslinked by 1,3-diammonium propane cations. These structures are understood by comparison with the already known organically templated zinc phosphites.

1. Introduction

A remarkable variety of inorganic networks templated by organic moieties have been reported over the last 10 years [1]. The search for novel microporous materials continues to be very active because of the wideness of their potential applications in catalysis, shape-selective absorption, nonlinear optical (NLO) devices or molecular-based magnetism and more recently in hydrogen storage. Although a majority of the inorganic open-framework structures reported in the literature are silicates [2], phosphates [1], or carboxylates [3], [4], [5], there have been recently some efforts to prepare open-framework structures involving the arsenates [6], [7], germanates [8], [9], phosphites [10], [11], sulfates [12] and selenites. In the case of selenite, the first organically templated zinc selenite with a layered structure was reported by Harrison et al. [13]. In this compound, (CN3H6)4[Zn3(SeO3)5], one of the ZnO4 tetrahedra is highly distorted with an OZnO bond angle of greater than 130°, although the ZnO bond lengths are normal. Later, Choudhury et al. [14] prepared an organically templated three-dimensional iron selenite of the formula [PIPH2]0.5[Fe2F3(SeO3)2]. More recently, four organically templated selenites, [DABCOH2]0.5[(VIVO)(HSeO3)(SeO3)]·H2O [15], [enH2][(VIVO)2(VVO)O2(SeO3)3]· 1.25H2O [15], [enH2][(VO)(SeO3)2] [16], [enH2][CdCl2(HSeO3)2] [17] have been reported in the literature. Our interest in the selenite-based frameworks was concerned with the possible role of the lone pair of electrons as an invisible structure-directing agent. Thus, the structure of inorganic solids containing the [SeIVO3]2− selenite ion are of interest because of the asymmetric coordination polyhedron adopted by this species [18]. The stereochemically active lone pair of electrons possessed by SeIV generally leads to a pyramidal coordination for the [SeIVO3]2− species. It was hoped that this may cause the selenites to crystallize in noncentrosymmetric structures with consequent interesting physical properties, such as nonlinear optical second harmonic generation (SHG) [19]. Herein, we describe the synthesis as well as the structure solution from X-ray crystal data of two new organically templated zinc selenites [H2N(CH2)2NH2]2·Zn4(SeO3)4 or MIL-86 and [H3N(CH2)3NH3]4·Zn4(SeO3)8 or MIL-87.

2. Experimental

2.1. Synthesis

MIL-86 and MIL-87 were prepared under hydrothermal conditions in the presence of organic amines (en) and (dap). In a typical synthesis, 0.0814 g of ZnO was dispersed in 5 ml of deionised water. To this solution, 0.2219 g of SeO2 was added followed by the addition of 0.1200 g of ethylenediamine (en) for MIL-86 and 0.1497 g of diaminopropane (dap) for MIL-87 under continuous stirring. The resulting mixtures of pH 8, with the final molar ratios of 1ZnO:2SeO2:2 diamine:180H2O were transferred to a polypropylene bottle and heated at 100 °C for 3 days. The reaction mixtures in both cases after the above treatment did not show any appreciable change in the pH, remaining at 8. The solution, after cooling over 24 h, yielded a good crop of colourless crystals. The crystals were filtered, washed with deionised water and dried in ambient conditions.

2.2. Elemental analysis

MIL-86 gave the following results: Zn, 30.0%; Se, 35.1%; C, 5.2%; N, 6.6%; H, 2.2%. These values compare well with those calculated from the proposed formula [H2N(CH2)2NH2]2·Zn4(SeO3)4: Zn, 29.4%; Se, 35.5%; C, 5.4%; N, 6.3%; H, 1.8%. Similar results were obtained for MIL-87. Moreover, X-ray electron diffraction was performed to determine the semiquantitative zinc to selenium ratio using a JEOL 5800LV apparatus; five different analyses gave a Zn:Se ratio to be 1:1 for MIL-86 and 1:2 for MIL-87, consistent with the formula derived from single crystal data.

2.3. Infrared spectroscopy

The infrared spectra of MIL-86 and MIL-87 were recorded as a KBr pellet in the frequency range 400–4000 cm−1. The two spectra clearly show characteristic bands of the CH and NH vibrators due to the amine (peaks between 3050 and 3350 cm−1 due to υ(NH), peaks between 2800 and 3000 cm−1 due to υ(CH), peaks between 1500 and 1650 cm−1 due to δ(NH), peaks between 1300 and 1400 cm−1 due to δ(CH) and peaks between 1000 and 1250 cm−1 due to δ(CN). A large band due to the stretching mode of selenite between 700 and 850 cm−1 has also been observed. Peaks around 500 cm−1 could be assigned for bending modes of the selenite.

2.4. X-ray diffraction

Single crystals of both MIL-86 and MIL-87 suitable for data collection were glued on a glass fiber. Intensity data were collected at room temperature on a Bruker–Siemens SMART three-circle diffractometer equipped with a CCD bidimensional detector, using the Mo-Kα radiation (λ=0.71073 Å). Data reduction was performed with the SAINT software and absorption corrections were applied using the SADABS program [20]. The structure was solved by direct methods and refined by full-matrix, least squares based on F2 using the SHELXTL software package [21].

3. Results and discussion

3.1. Structure solution

MIL-86 crystallizes with a monoclinic cell [a=6.7712(4) Å, b=9.4520(5) Å, c=8.0295(4) Å, β=113.601(1)°] in the P21/n (No. 14) space group [22] while MIL-87 crystallizes with an orthorhombic cell [a=8.8311(3) Å, b=7.8391(2) Å, c=16.2992(4) Å] in the Pbcm (No. 57) space group [22]. The first E-map revealed the location of almost all the atoms. Successive difference Fourier maps allowed complete location of the organic moieties. All nonhydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically. Hydrogens atoms from the organic molecules were placed in calculated positions and treated as riding (CH=0.97 Å, NH=0.90 Å). Details of the structure determination are given in Table 1, Table 2. The oxidation state (+II) of zinc has been confirmed by bond valence calculations [23]. Finally, the formula deduced from the structure determination is [H2N(CH2)2NH2]2·Zn4(SeO3)4 for MIL-86 and [H3N(CH2)3NH3]4·Zn4(SeO3)8 for MIL-87, respectively.

3.2. Structure description

The asymmetric unit of the zinc selenite, [H2N(CH2)2NH2]2·Zn4(SeO3)4 or MIL-86, consists of 7 independent nonhydrogen atoms, 5 of which belong to the inorganic framework (one Zn, one Se and three oxygens) and 2 to the diamine (one N and one C atom) as shown in Fig. 1. The zinc atom is tetrahedrally coordinated by three oxygens and one nitrogen atoms (ZnO3N). The selenium atom, bound to three oxygen atoms, forms a pyramid characteristic of the SeIVO3 group, with the stereochemically active lone pair of electrons occupying the remaining tetrahedral site. The inorganic component of the MIL-86 structure is, therefore, built up from ZnO3N tetrahedra and SeO3 pseudo-pyramids as the building units. The connectivity of the strictly alternating ZnO3N and SeO3 units in [H2N(CH2)2NH2]2·Zn4(SeO3)4 results in infinite layers propagating normal to [10-1] (Fig. 2). Polyhedral 4-rings and 8-rings are apparent in these formally neutral sheets, and this topology can be described in terms of 4.82 network (i.e., each nodal Zn or Se atom participates in one 4-ring and two 8-rings). This topology has been previously observed in various aluminosilicate zeolite structures [24] (see, for example, the KAlSi3O8 structure) and in an organically ethylenediamine zinc hydrogen phosphite recently reported by Harrison et al. [10]. It should be noted that [H2N(CH2)2NH2]2·Zn4(SeO3)4 crystallizes in two allotropic forms: MIL-86 which can be described by polyhedral 4-rings and 8-rings and another form which can be described by only polyhedral 6-rings [14]. The ZnN bond in MIL-86 corresponds to a direct link between zinc and the en template [d(ZnN)=2.030(1) Å]. The Zn species makes three ZnOSe links to nearby Se atoms [dav(ZnO)=1.998(1) Å]. The ZnOSe bond angles of the three bridging O atoms are ranging from 104.84° to 125.97°. The geometrical parameters of the organic moiety of MIL-86 are typical. Crystallographic symmetry generates the complete en template, which is, therefore, bonding to Zn from both its teminal N atoms, as ZnenZn. This linkage forms the connection between the next nearest neighbor layers to form a 3D structure (Fig. 3). This structure is closely related to the [H2N(CH2)2NH2]2·Zn4(HPO3)4 structure [10]. The atom-to-atom dimensions of [H2N(CH2)2NH2]2·Zn4(SeO3)4 8-rings are of 5.57 and 7.48 Å for ZnZn and 6.47 and 7.48 Å for SeSe whilst the atom-to-atom dimensions of [H2N(CH2)2NH2]2·Zn4(HPO3)4 8-rings are of 8.05 and 9.04 Å for ZnZn and 8.27 and 8.33 Å for PP. Then, the 8-ring maybe subject to various degrees of distortion. Although, in both structures, the en molecules act as pillars through end-to-end (ZnNCCNZn) connectivity, an unusual templating effect occurs in [H2N(CH2)2NH2]2·Zn4(HPO3)4 with the sheet-to-sheet ZnenZn chain sticking through an 8-ring in an intermediate layer while a usual direct templating effect (if the term is appropriate) is observed between the two nearest sheets in [H2N(CH2)2NH2]2· Zn4(SeO3)4 (Fig. 3). Finally, hydrogen bonds are also important in MIL-86.

The asymmetric unit of the zinc selenite, [H3N(CH2)3NH3]4·Zn4(SeO3)8 or MIL-87, consists of 8 independent nonhydrogen atoms, 5 of which belong to the inorganic framework (one Zn, one Se and three oxygens) and 3 to the diamine (one N and two C atoms). The zinc atom is tetrahedrally coordinated by four oxygens (ZnO4) while the selenium, bound to three oxygen atoms, adopts its characteristic pseudo-pyramid (SeO3). Therefore, the inorganic component of the MIL-87 is built up from four rings of ZnO4 tetrahedra and SeO3 pseudo-pyramids, sharing vertices, as ZnOSe (Fig. 4). The Zn nodes serve to fuse the four rings together resulting in a 1:2 Zn:Se ratio. The Zn species makes four ZnOSe links to nearby Se atoms [dav(ZnO)=1.956(1) Å], whilst each Se species is bonded to two Zn neighbors [dav(SeO)=1.683(1) Å]. Of the three oxygen atoms, two serve as ZnOSe bridges (121.13° and 126.12°) and one is terminal to the selenite group. The connectivity of the strictly alternating ZnO4 and SeO3 buildings units in [H3N(CH2)3NH3]4·Zn4(SeO3)8 results in infinite chains propagating normal to [001]. The diaminopropane molecules lie between the resulting chains of the [Zn(SeO3)2]2− and are protonated twice to balance the two negative charges of the inorganic chain (Fig. 5). The geometrical parameters of the 1,3-diammonium propane cation are similar to those of the same species when it serves as a template to gallophosphate networks [25], [26], [27], [28]. An extensive network of hydrogen bonds thus contributes to the cohesion of the MIL-87 structure through template–chain interactions. The topology of the chains in [H3N(CH2)3NH3]4·Zn4(SeO3)8 is identical to that previously observed in an organically diaminopropane zinc hydrogen phosphite [H3N(CH2)3NH3]4·Zn4(HPO3)8 [11]. Also, the crystal structure of MIL-87 resembles some organically templated zincophosphates such as (N2C6H14)·Zn(HPO4)2·H2O [29] and (NC2H8)·Zn(HPO4)(H2PO4)·0.5H2O [30], which show the same corner-sharing 4-ring chain architecture with di(hydrogen) phosphate tetrahedra replacing the selenite pseudo-pyramids.

4. Conclusion

The use of mild hydrothermal reaction conditions has allowed the synthesis of two new examples of organically templated zincophosphite materials. The first one, MIL-86 or [H2N(CH2)2NH2]2·Zn4(SeO3)4, combines the structural features of templated networks and coordination polymers whilst the second one, MIL-87 or [H3N(CH2)3NH3]4·Zn4(SeO3)8, possess a 1D inorganic chain structure crosslinked by 1,3-diammonium propane cations. The present study suggests there is the possibility of building up other novel open-framework structures using selenite as the buildings units.

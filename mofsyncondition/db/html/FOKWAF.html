A CuII–GdIII–CuII heterometallic coordination polymer constructed by gadolinium(III) ion and copper(II) Schiff-base building block: Structure and magnetic property

Abstract

A new 3d–4f heterometallic coordination polymer [Gd(H2O)3(NO3−)(CuL)2] H2O (1) (H3L = glycylglycine, N-[1-(2-hydroxy, phenyl)-propylidene]) has been synthesized and characterized by IR, elemental analysis, TGA and X-ray single-crystal diffraction. In the coordination polymer, two metallo-ligand CuLs are connected with Gd3 + ion to form a 1-D chain via carboxylate and carbonyl oxygen. The neighboring Cu–Gd–Cu chains are further connected by hydrogen bonds to form a two-dimensional structure. Magnetic studies for 1 reveal that ferromagnetic interactions (JCuGd = 1.30 cm− 1) between Cu(II) and Gd(III) centers are operating within the CuII–GdIII–CuII systems.

The design and synthesis of lanthanide (Ln) transition metal (3d–4f) heterometallic coordination polymers (CPs) have attracted a great deal of interest due to their excellent properties and potential applications in magnetic materials [1], molecular adsorption [2] and bimetallic catalysis [3]. However, it is quite difficult to assemble lanthanide and transition metal ions together because the competitive reactions between the lanthanide and transition metal ions to coordinate to the same ligand often result in homometallic CPs rather than heterometallic ones. Based on the hard–soft acid base theory [4], the Ln ions prefer oxygen donors to nitrogen donors, while transition metal ions have a strong tendency to coordinate to both nitrogen and oxygen donors. Therefore, the usual synthetic strategy for preparing 3d–4f heterometallic CPs is to employ N/O mixed-donor-bridging ligands to conquer the competitive reactions between the lanthanide and transition metal ions [5]. Several different synthetic strategies have been employed to fabricate 3d–4f heterometallic CPs. Among the reported strategies, the metallo-ligands (ML) approach has been shown to be effective [6], [7] to prepare heterometallic coordination polymers. Contrary to organic ligands, metallo-ligands act as bridging ligands incorporating several advantages; e.g., multi-coordination sites, flexible geometric control and the ability to assemble a discrete unit into a polymeric frame giving rise to polymeric heterometallic frameworks [8]. Our interest has been directed toward metal-mediated molecular self-assemblies based on metal complexes with Schiff bases, which serve as a ML toward various metal ions. As a building block for furnishing a coordination polymer, CuL (H3L = glycylglycine, N-[1-(2-hydroxy, phenyl)-propylidene]) has several advantages. First, it can furnish various coordination modes because of the presence of a multifunctional group for metal coordination. Second, it has a rigid planar structure that allows the formation of a multi-dimensional framework. Third, it has a strong ability to form hydrogen bonds that play an important role in the assembly of supramolecular architectures. Fourth, it easily builds heterometallic assembly systems [8a,b]. Using the strategy mentioned above, we succeeded in assembly heterometallic CPs using CuL as a building block, and reported several examples of alkaline-earth transition metal heterometallic CPs and lanthanide (Ln) transition metal heterometallic CPs based on rigid planar CuL and its analog [9]. In this contribution, a 3d–4f heterometallic CP, [Gd(H2O)3(NO3−)(CuL)2] H2O (1) has been successfully obtained and structurally studied in detail. Magnetic studies have been performed as well.

X-ray structural analyses revealed that the coordination polymer 1 crystallized in the Monoclinic system, space group P21 [10], [11], [12]. As shown in Fig. 1a, the unit cell consists of trinuclear unit [Gd(H2O)3(NO3−)(CuL)2], and lattice water molecule. Each Cu(II) ion adopts an approximately square-planar {CuO2N2} coordination geometry with two oxygen atoms (O1, O2) and two nitrogen atoms (N1, N2) from L3 −. While Gd(III) ion is coordinated by three oxygen atoms (O3, O4 and O8) from two ligands, two oxygen atoms (O4s, O5s) from nitrate anion and three oxygen atoms (O1s, O2s and O3s) from three water molecules to form a distorted trigonal-dodecahedron {GdO8} coordination geometry (Fig. 1b). The bond lengths of CuN are in the range of 1.874(6)–1.932(7) Å and that of CuO are in the range of 1.881(5)–1.982(5) Å, which are in the range compatible with the values of the previously reported copper complexes [9], [13]. The bond lengths of GdO are in the range of 2.282(6)–2.520(7) Å. The deprotonated Schiff base ligand is a triple negatively-charged and coordinates to the Cu(II) atom to form [CuL]−. The best-fit least-square plane through the four basal and Cu-atoms shows that these atoms are nearly coplanar. The O(1)–Cu(1)–N(2) angle of 178.1(3)° is nearly linear. The phenyl ring [C(1)–C(6)] and the C(1), C(6), C(7), N(1), O(1), Cu(1) chelate rings are almost coplanar with a small dihedral angle of 1.6°. The coordinate environment of Cu(2) is similar to that of Cu(1). The bond length and bond angle are in good agreement with these similar Schiff base ligands coordinated to Cu(II) center [9], [14]. The two metallo-ligands [CuL]− in the asymmetric unit of 1 are in two different coordination modes. One is a bidentate ligand and uses the two oxygen atoms (O3, O4) to bridge two adjacent Gd(III) ions in the 1-D chain running along the b-axis (Figs. 1c and S1a, ESI). The other as a monodentate ligand uses the carbonyl oxygen atom (O8) to link with Gd(III) ion and acts as the ridge part of the 1-D chain motif. The ridges are interdigitated with those of neighboring 1-D chains with nearest Cu…Cu distance of 3.4996(5) Å (Fig. S1b and c ESI). The ability of the [Gd(H2O)3(NO3−)(CuL)2] unit to form hydrogen bonds plays an important role in the formation of the final assembly [15]. As shown in Fig. 2, the coordinated water molecules (O1s, O2s O3s) act as bridges through hydrogen bonds with oxygen atoms in the ridge part of the adjacent chains (O5, O6, O7) to generate 2-D structure. The uncoordinated water molecule (O1w) formed hydrogen bond with oxygen atoms of the bidentate metallo-ligand (O1,O2) and the coordinated water molecule to reinforce the stability of the whole structure (Figs. 2, S2 and S3, Table S3 ESI).

To confirm the crystalline phase purity, the PXRD experiments have been carried out at room temperature. The diffraction peaks of bulk samples are consistent with the simulated patterns in terms of the single crystal data, indicating the presence of mainly one crystalline phase in the corresponding sample of 1 (Fig. S4, ESI). To confirm the thermal stabilities, the TGA has been also studied under an N2 atmosphere (Fig. S5, ESI). The TGA curves show that the weight loss of 6.3% below 300 °C is due to the loss of three water molecules (calcd. 6.2%). Further weight loss from ca. 340 °C is attributed to the decomposition of the framework. The remaining residue (37.2%) is likely to be Cu2O and Gd2O3(calcd. 36.7%) (Fig. S6, ESI).

Diamagnetic corrections were made with Pascal's constants for all the constituent atoms [16]. The cryomagnetic properties for 1 were measured in a magnetic field of 100 Oe in the temperature range of 2–300 K, as plotted in Fig. 3. The χMT is 8.19 cm3 K mol− 1 at 300 K, which is in good agreement with the expected value for the three uncoupled metal ions (two CuII, one GdIII: 0.375 cm3 K mol− 1 per Cu(II), 7.875 cm3 K mol− 1 per Gd(III)). At lower temperatures, the χMT increased and reached a maximum of 8.52 cm3 K mol− 1 at 7.4 K. This indicates that the copper(II) gadolinium(III) interaction is ferromagnetic. The abrupt decrease of χMT below 7.4 K may be attributable to antiferromagnetic interaction between the neighboring molecules.

The data for trinuclear system 1 were analyzed on the basis of a spin-only expression. In this analysis, the two copper(II) ions are assumed to be identical for the sake of simplicity. The coupling of the spin momentum of gadolinium and the two neighboring copper ions is described by the spin Hamiltonian H = − JCuGdSCu1 SGd–JCuGdSCu2 SGd–JCuCuSCu1 SCu2, where JCuGd and JCuCu are the CuII GdIII and CuII CuII interaction parameters, respectively. The experimental data were analyzed using the expression (1):χM=Ng2β24kT−θAB+NαA=165+84exp−45JCuGd/kT+84exp−3.5JCuGd+JCuCu/kT+35exp−8JCuGd/kTB=5+exp−4.5JCuGd/kT+4exp−3.5JCuGd+JCuCu/kT+3exp−8JCuGd/kT.

In this expression, N is Avogadro's number, β is the Bohr magneton, k is the Boltzmann constant, J is the exchange integral, T is the absolute temperature, Nα is the temperature-independent paramagnetism and g is the Landé g factor. θ is included as a correction term for the interaction between the terminal Cu2 + ions and interchain interactions. In CP 1, the interaction between Cu1 and Cu2 (JCuCu) is assumed to be zero because the two copper(II) ions are well separated (7.13 Å). A satisfactory theoretical curve fit to the experimental data was obtained for the following set of parameters: JCuGd = 1.30(3) cm− 1, g = 1.96(1), θ = − 0.87 K and Nα = 270 × 10− 6 emu mol− 1 (R = ∑[(χMT)obs − (χMT)calc]2/∑[(χMT)obs]2 = 2.0 × 10− 3). Positive J values for CuII GdIII interaction for 1 indicate a ferromagnetic coupling. The estimated J value is comparable with that of the reported complex [17].

In conclusion, a new heteronuclear coordination polymer [Gd(H2O)3(NO3−) (CuL)2] H2O (1) using Schiff base containing glycylglycine as ligand was synthesized and structurally characterized by X-ray single-crystal diffraction. The rigid planar metallo-ligand [CuL]− assembly with Gd3 + to construct a 1-D chain [Gd(H2O)3(NO3−) (CuL)2], through oxygens form the ligand. Hydrogen bonds play an important role in the formation of the final 2D structure assembly. Magnetic studies for 1 reveal that ferromagnetic interactions between copper(II) and gadolinium(III) centers are operating within the reported CuII–GdIII–CuII systems.

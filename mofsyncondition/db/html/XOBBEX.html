Synthesis and X-ray crystal structures of silver complexes of 2,6-dimethylpyridine N-oxide: Steric factors override electronic effects

Abstract

2,6-Dimethylpyridine N-oxide consistently reacts with various silver(I) salts to generate coordination polymers in which the N-oxide oxygen bridges two silver atoms. The recently reported μ3-O,O,O bridging mode observed with pyridine N-oxide is suppressed due to the greater steric demand of the 2,6-dimethyl derivative.

1. Introduction

Pyridine N-oxides have a long history of use as ligands in coordination chemistry [1] and have been incorporated into ligands for the construction of many metallosupramolecular frameworks [2]. Silver(I) has proved to be a popular metal for use in metallosupramolecular chemistry due to its promiscuous coordination behaviour [3]. However, the study of silver(I) complexes of N-heterocyclic N-oxides has been curiously ignored, with only a handful of complexes having been crystallographically characterised [4]. Recently, we showed that pyridine N-oxide (1) has a special affinity for silver(I) and that the oxygen atom can bind to one, two or three silver atoms (Scheme 1) [5]. Although pyridine N-oxides are well-known to act as bridges to two metal centres, the μ3-O,O,O triply-bridging mode has not been observed with other metals and we introduced the term “hyperdentate” to describe such bonding.

We were interested to know what factors would favour this mode of coordination, having previously established that it is encouraged by a high M:L stoichiometric ratio of the reactants [5]. We now show that the introduction of methyl groups adjacent to the N–O groups suppresses this mode of coordination, despite the fact that the oxygen ought to be a better donor due to the electron donating nature of the methyl groups. Specifically, we report that 2,6-dimethylpyridine N-oxide (2) consistently acts as a bridge to only two silver atoms, presumably due to the greater steric constraints at the donor site (Scheme 1).

2. Experimental

2.1. General

Melting points were determined using an Electrothermal melting point apparatus and are uncorrected. Elemental analyses were performed by the Campbell microanalytical laboratory at the University of Otago. Solvents were purified according to standard procedures. Other reagents were obtained from commercial sources and used as supplied. Ligand 2 was prepared by a literature procedure [6] in 90% yield. M.p. 49 °C [Lit [6]: m.p. 52 °C]. 1H NMR (400 MHz, CDCl3) /ppm: 7.16 (s, 3H, H3 H4, H5), 2.55 (s, 6H, H6). 13C NMR (100 MHz, CDCl3) δ/ppm: 149.71 (C2), 126.42 (C3), 124.10 (C4), 18.32 (C6). IR (KBr) νmax/cm−1: 3414, 1950, 1711, 1574, 1499, 1460, 1376, 1271, 1101, 828, 783. ESI-MS m/z: Found [L+H]+ 124.0760, C7H10NO requires [L+H]+ 124.0757.

2.2. Preparation of silver(I) trifluoroacetate complex 3

To a solution of ligand 2 (13 mg, 0.10 mmol) in methanol was added silver(I) trifluoroacetate (24 mg, 0.10 mmol) in methanol (1 ml). The resulting solution was allowed to evaporate slowly at room temperature in darkness. Colourless crystals suitable for X-ray crystallography were thus obtained. Yield 25 mg (72%), m.p. 148 °C. IR (KBr) νmax/cm−1: 1722, 1679, 1574, 1410, 1245, 1207, 1131, 1109, 836, 802, 772. Elemental Anal. Calc. for C7H9NO·AgOCOCF3: C, 31.42; H, 2.64; N, 4.07. Found: C, 31.40; H, 2.53; N, 3.96%.

2.3. Preparation of silver(I) triflate complex 4

Silver(I) triflate (28 mg, 0.10 mmol) dissolved in methanol (1 ml) was added to a methanol (2 ml) solution of ligand 2 (13 mg, 0.10 mmol). The solution was left in darkness at room temperature and allowed to evaporate slowly to give crystals suitable for X-ray crystallography. Yield 22 mg (34%), m.p. 129 °C. IR (KBr) νmax/cm−1: 3414, 2023, 1638, 1615, 1430, 1380, 1177, 1131, 838, 582. ESI-MS m/z: Found [L+Ag]+ 229.9727, C7H9AgNO requires [L+Ag]+ 229.9730; Found [2L+Ag]+ 353.0412, C14H18AgN2O2 requires [2L+Ag]+ 353.0414. Elemental Anal. Calc. for C7H9NO·2(AgSO3CF3)·CH3OH: C, 17.95; H, 1.96; N, 2.09. Found: C, 17.81; H, 1.48; N, 2.35%.

2.4. Preparation of silver(I) nitrate complex 5

A solution of silver(I) nitrate (24 mg, 0.13 mmol) in water (1 ml) was dropped slowly into solution of ligand 2 (17 mg, 0.13 mmol) in water (3 ml). The mixture was then evaporated slowly over a period of a few days in darkness at room temperature. Colourless crystals suitable for X-ray crystallography were obtained. Yield 28 mg (73%), m.p. 162 °C. IR (KBr) νmax/cm−1: 2400, 1761, 1574, 1496, 1460, 1380, 1232, 1099, 826, 789. ESI-MS m/z: Found [L+Ag]+ 229.9729, C7H9AgNO requires [L+Ag]+ 229.9730. Elemental Anal. Calc. for C7H9NO·AgNO3·½H2O: C, 27.84; H, 3.34; N, 9.27. Found: C, 27.44; H, 2.86; N, 9.36%.

2.5. X-ray crystallography

The crystal data and details of the data collections and refinements for the X-ray structures are listed in Table 1. Measurements were made with a Bruker APEX II (for 3 and 5) or Agilent Supernova (for 4) instrument using Mo Kα (λ = 0.71073 Å) radiation. The structures were solved by direct methods using shelxs [7] and refined on F2 using all data by full-matrix least-squares procedures with shelxl [7]. Non-hydrogen atoms were refined with anisotropic displacement parameters. Hydrogen atoms were included in calculated positions with isotropic displacement parameters 1.2 times the isotropic equivalent of their carrier atoms.

3. Results and discussion

2,6-Dimethylpyridine N-oxide (2) was reacted with a variety of silver(I) salts, three of which produced crystalline products suitable for X-ray structure determination. The silver(I) trifluoroacetate complex 3 crystallises in the monoclinic space group P21/c, with the asymmetric unit containing one molecule of 2, a silver atom and a trifluoroacetate anion with a disordered CF3 group having a 77:23 occupancy ratio (Fig. 1). The N-oxide ligand adopts a μ2-O,O bidentate bridging coordination mode with Ag1 and Ag1′ separated at a distance is 3.8614(3) Å. The silver atom is five-coordinate being bonded to two oxygens supplied by different trifluoroacetate anions, two N-oxide oxygens and a symmetry related silver atom (Ag1”) at a distance of 2.9820(3) Å. The geometry at silver is closer to square pyramidal than trigonal bipyramidal with a τ5 [8] value of 0.38.

Complex 3 is a 1D linear coordination polymer that propagates along the c-axis with adjacent silver(I) atoms bridged by alternating pairs of trifluoroacetate anions and N-oxide ligand molecules, which are orthogonally oriented as shown in Fig. 2. Along the chain adjacent pyridine rings are separated by 6.729 Å, which nicely allows adjacent chains to interdigitate with face-to-face π–π stacking interactions having a centroid–centroid distance of 3.559 Å.

The silver(I) triflate complex 4 crystallises in the triclinic space group P1¯ and has a 1:2 ligand to silver(I) ratio. The asymmetric unit is shown in Fig. 3 and has two independent silver atoms bridged by a μ2 N-oxide and a triflate counterion, along with another triflate anion. Both silver atoms are four-coordinate with four coordinated oxygens and have τ4 values [9] of 0.38 for Ag1 and 0.55 for Ag2.

The asymmetric unit extends into a 2D polymeric network in the ab-plane, as shown in Fig. 4a. One of the two independent triflate anions bridges two silver atoms while the other bridges four silvers. As shown in Fig. 4b the silver atoms all lie approximately in the plane of the 2D sheet, the surfaces of which are decorated with the bridging N-oxides and triflates.

The silver(I) nitrate complex 5 crystallises in the monoclinic space group P21/n and has a 1:1 M:L ratio. The asymmetric unit (Fig. 5) contains two independent silver atoms bridged by two independent μ2 N-oxide ligands, along with two nitrate anions that bridge symmetry related silvers. The planes of the bridging pyridine N-oxide rings are orthogonal to the plane of the central Ag2O2 rectangle. The silver atoms are both four-coordinate with four oxygen donors from two N-oxides and two nitrates, but have slightly different τ4 values [0.81 for Ag1 and 0.73 for Ag2].

The asymmetric unit grows into a 2D polymeric network that extends in the ab-plane, as shown in the space-filling representation in Fig. 6.

Closer inspection reveals that the structure consists of large 20-membered rings formed from six silver atoms, four N-oxide ligands and four bridging nitrates (Fig. 7a). The silver(I) atoms bridged by two molecules of 2 (Ag1 and Ag2) are separated by a distance of 3.654 Å. The two independent bridging nitrates each use two different oxygens to coordinate to two silvers, but do so in a different manner. One of these has a symmetrical W-shaped coordination mode (Fig. 7b), whereas the other bridges in a less symmetrical S-shaped mode (Fig. 7c). As a result the distances between the silvers differ for the two modes. Specifically, the W-shaped coordination mode bridges silvers (Ag2 and Ag2″′) separated by 5.542 Å, whereas the S-shaped mode leads to a separation (Ag1 to Ag1′) of 5.245 Å.

Once again, adjacent 2D polymeric sheets are interdigitated with face-to-face π–π stacking interactions with centroid–centroid distances of 3.856 Å, as shown in Fig. 8.

Thus, in all three complexes ligand 2 faithfully bridges two silver atoms. In our previous report [5] we showed that pyridine N-oxide (1) was regularly found to bridge three silver atoms in a rare μ3-bridging mode. The introduction of the two methyl groups appears to suppress this mode of coordination. The electron donating methyl groups in 2 might be expected to enhance the coordination ability of the oxygen atom, but appear to suppress this coordination mode. Clearly the steric factors associated with the size of the methyl groups overrides the electronic effects. We also showed [5] that it was possible to generate very different coordination polymers of pyridine N-oxide (1) simply by changing the stoichiometry of the reactants, with a higher ratio of silver favouring the μ3-bridging mode. Thus, we examined the reaction of 2 with silver nitrate in three different (1:1, 1:2 and 1:4) L:M ratios, but in all cases this led to the same complex (5), as shown by powder diffraction measurements.

4. Conclusion

Whereas pyridine N-oxide (1) was shown [5] to bind to one, two or three silver atoms, depending on the stoichiometry and the counteranion, 2,6-dimethylpyridine N-oxide (2) consistently bridges two silver atoms in three complexes with different anions. The rare μ3-O,O,O bridging mode is suppressed by the steric size of the adjacent methyl groups.

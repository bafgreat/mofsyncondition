Novel supramolecular compounds based on tetrathiafulvalene tetracarboxylate with the cationic chains and anionic chains: Syntheses, charactarization and magnetic propertises

Abstract

Seven supramolecular framework, [M(bipy)2(H2O)4][M(TC–TTF)(H2O)2] (M = Mn 1, Co 2, Fe 3, Cu 4, Zn 5 and Cd 6; H4TC–TTF = tetrathiafulvalene tetracarboxylate; bipy = 4,4′-bipyridine) and [Fe4(μ2-Cl)6(OH2)12][H2.5(TC–TTF)]4·4H2O 7 have been prepared by using a diffusion method. 1–6 are isomorphous and crystallize in monoclinic crystal system, space group P21/c (1–5), and C2/m (6), respectively. {[M(TC–TTF)(H2O)2]2 −}n is a 1D polymer anionic chain consisting of MII bridged by TC–TTF4 −. {[M(bipy)2(H2O)4]2 +}n cation forms 1D cationic chain by π···π stacks, and then are linked through O–H···O (N) hydrogen-bonding and π···π interactions to construct a 2D supramolecular net. Furthermore, the 2D nets are connected through O–H···O hydrogen-bonding interactions and form a 3D supramolecular framework. 7 displays a 3D supramolecular framework by O–H···O hydrogen-bonding and S···S interactions with a 1D open channel. Variable–temperature magnetic susceptibility measurements for 1 and 2 show antiferromagnetic coupling interactions at 1.8–300 K.

Interest in supramolecular frameworks based on metal ions and organic ligands has been rapidly expanding for the fascinating structural diversity and potential application in catalysis, sensors, nonlinear optics, and porosity [1], [2], [3], [4]. Presently, the use of multicarboxylate ligands containing a functional organic unit to construct well-ordered multidimensional supramolecular architectures is an appealing field because it affords a viable method to design and construct functional supramolecular frameworks with electronic or magnetic units [5].

Tetrathiafulvalene–tetracarboxylate (H4(TC–TTF)) (see Chart 1), is an excellent ligand for constructing supramolecular frameworks [6]. The reasons can be listed as follows: a) The carboxyl groups may completely or partially lose hydrogen atoms, inducing various coordination modes and interesting high-dimensional structures; b) the high symmetry may be advantageous to the crystal growth; and c) the ligand that includes the TTF unit may demonstrate electronic activity. For example, Mercier et al. reported that (Bu4N)2[H2(TC–TTF)] as supporting electrolyte affords wholly TTF organic materials [6]; Rosseinsky et al. reported microporous hydrogen-bonding framework [Co(H2O)6]H2(TC–TTF) [6]; and Dai group, Férey group et al. synthesized a series of coordination polymer based on H4(TC–TTF) [6]. Meanwhile, two N atoms of the ligand 4,4′-bipyridine (bipy) with a 180° angle makes it very attractive for designing fascinating supramolecular frameworks [7].

Earlier, we also synthesized the crystal structure of Zn(TC–TTF)0.5(bipy)2(CH3OH) showing 3D coordination network [8]. Here, we report the syntheses and characterization of seven supramolecular compounds based on H4(TC–TTF): [M(bipy)2(H2O)4][M(TC–TTF)(H2O)2] (M = Mn 1, Co 2, Fe 3, Cu 4, Zn 5 and Cd 6) and [Fe4(μ2-Cl)6(OH2)12][H2.5(TC–TTF)]4·4H2O 7. (see Electronic Supplementary Information).

The detailed crystallographic data and structural refinement parameters for 1–7 are summarized in Table S1 and the selected bond lengths and angles for 1–7 are listed in Table S2 (see Electronic Supplementary Information). Single X-ray diffraction analyses reveal that 1–6 have the same architectures. So the structure of 1 is taken as an example that we will describe in detail. The X-ray structure analyses reveal that 1 crystallizes in a monoclinic crystal system with the space group P21/c (Fig. 1). There are one [Mn(bipy)2(H2O)4]2 + cation and one [Mn(TC–TTF)(H2O)2]2 − anion per symmetric unit.

For the [Mn(bipy)2(H2O)4]2 + cation, the Mn2 atom is bonded to two nitrogen atoms and four oxygen atoms with the slightly distorted octahedral coordination environment. The pyridyl rings of all the bipy molecules are noncoplanar and twisted by 21.20(1)° and 21.14(4)° for the coordinated and uncoordinated ones, respectively. The interplanar separation of approximately 3.71 Å (centroid-to-centroid separation: 4.27 Å) is similar to that of the optimized structure for the parallel displaced stacking pyridyl ring dimer, indicating weak π···π stacking interactions [9]. Through the π···π stacking interactions, the cations form the extended 1D chains (Fig. 2).

{[M(TC–TTF)(H2O)2]2 −}n is a 1D polymer anionic chain consisting of MnII bridged by TC–TTF4 − and two coordinated water molecules (Fig. 3). Each MnII is bonded to six oxygen atoms from four carboxyl groups and two water molecules. The Mn–OH2 distances are 2.196(3) Å, similar to Mn–O(carboxyl) bond lengths of 2.121(5) and 2.163(5) Å, all are consistent with the literature [10]. The chelate angle O1–Mn–O3 is 86.05(10)°, while the angles O1–Mn–O3A, O3–Mn–OH2, and O3A–Mn–OH2 are 93.95(10)°, 92.28(11)°, and 87.72(11)°, respectively. The Mn···Mn distance of the Mn–bipy···bipy–Mn segment is 13.210 Å in the cationic chains, which is exactly equal to the length of Mn–(TC–TTF)–Mn (Mn···Mn = 13.210 Å) in the anionic chains. However, the Mn···Mn separation of Mn–OH2···O–Mn is 5.782 Å between the neighboring anionic and cationic chains. The alternating anionic and cationic chains are connected into a 2D sheet by O7–H···O1 and O5A–H···N2 hydrogen-bonding and π···π interactions (Fig. 4). Furthermore, the neighboring sheets form a 3D network through O–H···O hydrogen-bonding interactions (Fig. 1S).

7 possesses one [Fe4(μ2–Cl)6(OH2)12]6 + cation and four [H2.5(TC–TTF)]1.5 − anions per symmetric unit (Fig. 5). The [Fe4(μ2-Cl)6(OH2)12]6 + cation is a tetramer. The distance between the adjacent FeIII ions along the chain is 3.680 Å. Each FeIII is at the center of a distorted octahedron, which is made up of two different types of octahedra.

Hydrogen-bonding interactions play an important role for constructing microporous framework 7. Three different types of hydrogen-bonding interactions are present in 7. a) Within the anionic organic chains: the uncoordinated O13 water molecules act as donors and the noncoordinated O4 carboxyl–oxygen atoms and the O4A oxygen of the unidentate carboxyl groups are also involved as acceptors. Each anionic unit is linked to a 2D organic layer through S···S interactions (S···S distances are 3.8 Å, slightly longer than reported in the references (Fig. 6). The [H2.5(TC–TTF)]1.5 − anions are tilted with respect to the inorganic layer by an angle of 35°. The layers of [H2.5(TC–TTF)]1.5 − comprise only one crystallographically independent [H2.5(TC–TTF)]1.5 − molecule located on the central carbon–carbon double bond at the center. b) Within the cationic inorganic chains: hydrogen-bonding interactions between water molecules, i.e., Fe–O···H2O–Fe, join the tetrameric octahedra along the b axis into a chain (Fig. 7). The distance between the tetrameric nuclei is 15.214 Å. c) Between the anionic organic layers and cationic inorganic chains: apart from the electrostatic interactions between the anionic layers and cationic chains, extensive hydrogen-bonding interactions also involve the noncoordinated carboxyl oxygen and the water molecules coordinated to FeIII. Moreover, O–H···Cl hydrogen-bonding interactions exist in the coordinated chlorine atoms and the noncoordinated carboxyl oxygen. Through hydrogen-bonding interactions and S···S interactions, a 3D microporous supramolecular framework is formed with a 1D open channel (4 × 8 Å along the b axis) (Fig. 8).

The strong bands of IR spectra at 1598 and 1422 cm− 1 are assigned to the asymmetric and symmetric stretching vibrations of the carboxyl groups, respectively. The bonds at 3060, 1548, 1369, 797, and 612 cm− 1 show the aromatic skeletal vibration of the pyridyl rings.

The temperature variation of susceptibility for 1 was investigated within the temperature range 1.8–300 K. The χMT value is 8.54 cm3 K mol− 1 at room temperature (Fig. 9), which is slightly lower than the spin-only value of 8.75 cm3 K mol− 1 (g = 2.0) expected for the two MnII ions (S = 5/2). Over the entire temperature range, the χMT value remains almost constant from 8.54 cm3 K mol− 1 at room temperature to 8.48 cm3 K mol− 1, indicating paramagnetic behavior in 1 [11].

The temperature variation of susceptibility for 2 is displayed in Fig. 10. Because the structures of 1 and 2 are isomorphous, the paramagnetic properties of 1 indicate that there are no remarkable coupling interactions between the metal ions. Thus, the magnetic properties of 2 are mainly attributable to the contribution of the single-ion behavior of the CoII ion. Upon lowering the temperature, the χMT value smoothly decreases, which is mainly attributable to the splitting of the 4T1 term into six Kramers doublets because of the effect of spin-orbit coupling. Therefore, according to the structure, the magnetic behavior can be treated as two isolated high-spin CoII ions, with a correction for the mean field theory and the spin Hamiltonian H^=DS^z2−13S^S^+1+g||βHzS^z+g⊥βHxS^x+HyS^y [12]. Assuming g|| = g⊥ = g, the best least-squares fitting calculation of the χM vs. T plots from 300 to 2.5 K leads to g = 2.27 and D = 13.62 cm− 1 (R = 5.1 × 10− 4). This relatively large value of D can be understood by keeping in mind the relatively large value of the spin-orbit parameter of the CoII ion, and it is comparable with the reported CoII complexes [12].

In conclusion, we have successfully synthesized seven supramolecular frameworks constructed by hydrogen-bonding, π···π, or S···S interactions with the cationic and anionic chains. The results show that weak interactions are an excellent tool for constructing supramolecular frameworks. Further, the emphasis is on designing and constructing the weak interactions of supramolecular frameworks by multicarboxyl or other more extended ligands.

Introduction

The constructing of coordination framework through crystal engineering has been of great interest not only due to their fascinating structure topologies1 but also due to their potential application as functional materials.2 Until now, the most useful method for the synthesis of these compounds is the ‘node and spacer’ approach raise by Robson and co-workers.3 Exo-bidentate ligands such as 4,4′-bipyridine and its analogue are widely used as spacer and metal ions as node in designing coordination frameworks. A number of network structures have been reported, including 1D chain or ladder,4 2D layer,5 and 3D diamond6 or NbO7 or α-Po frameworks.8 When preparing coordination frameworks, there are some factors, such as the choice of metal ions (cation) or anions, the ligands/metal ratios, temperature, solvent and the pH value of the solution should be considered.9 Among them, the choice of metal ions (cation) and anion is the key factor governing the topology of coordination framework. The coordination geometry and the size of the cation are the two key effects upon the structure of the final product. These two effects will affect the geometry and the connectivity of the node in the frameworks and thus their topology.5,6 The anion has two main effects on the final framework topology. Firstly, the coordinative ability of the anion can clearly influence the coordination sphere of the metal cation used and hence framework connectivity.10 Secondly, the size of the anion can influence the long-range order and degree of interpenetration.11

It has been demonstrated that 4,4′-bipyridine-N,N′-dioxide (dpdo) and its derivatives show some unique features in the construction of coordination framework.11c, 12,13Dpdo and its derivatives have been shown to have extremely versatile coordination modes compared with 4,4′-bipyridine and its derivatives.12 In addition, they have a strong capacity for forming hydrogen bonds, which plays an important role in the assembly of supramolecular compounds.13 The coordinative versatility and the capability of forming hydrogen bond, together with the coordination nature of central metal ions, have allowed the possibility for the construction of different architectures.11c, 12,13 In our previous work we have successfully introduced 1,2-bis(4-pyridyl)ethane-N,N′-dioxide (bpeado) into the binary system of M(dca)2 or Mn(tcm)2 (dca = dicyanamide, tcm = tricyanomethanide) and obtained some fascinating framework topology formed by both bpeado and dca or tcm.14 Here, we want to explore the ability of bpeado itself in constructing coordination framework. Compared with dpdo, bpeado has the following salient feature: (a) bpeado is a longer spacer; (b) bpeado is more flexible and some new coordination or bridging modes could be expected. In this work, we describe the synthesis and structures of a series of coordination frameworks containing metal perchlorate or metal tetraphenylborate and bpeado. We demonstrate that the size of the anion influences the bridging mode of bpeado as well as the topology of the coordination framework.

Experimental

Synthesis

1,2-bis(4-pyridyl)ethane-N,N′-dioxide (bpeado) was prepared from 1,2-bis(4-pyridyl)ethane by a literature method.15 Other reagents were commercially available and used without further purification.

Caution

Metal perchlorates are potentially explosive. Only small amount of material should be prepared and should be handled with caution.

[Mn(bpeado)3][ClO4]21

0.25 mmol (90 mg) Mn(ClO4)2·6H2O and 0.5 mmol (108 mg) bpeado were mixed in 15 mL methanol with continuous stirring. A light yellow precipitate formed immediately. H2O was added dropwise to the solution to give a clear yellow solution. The solution was filtered and allowed to slowly evaporate at room temperature. After a few days, block yellow single crystals suitable for X-ray diffraction appeared. The crystals were collected, washed with methanol, and dried in air (yield 50% based on bpeado). Anal., calcd. for C36H36Cl2MnN6O14: C, 47.91; H, 4.02; N, 9.31%. Found: C, 47.93; H, 3.89; N, 9.27%.

[Cu(bpeado)2(H2O)2][ClO4]2·bpeado 2

A procedure similar to that for preparing compound 1 with Cu(ClO4)2·6H2O instead of Mn(ClO4)2·6H2O was used to prepare compound 3. Green block crystals were obtained after crystallization of about one week (yield 75% based on bpeado). Anal., calcd. for C36H40Cl2CuN6O16: C, 45.65; H, 4.26; N, 8.87%. Found: C, 45.80; H, 4.09; N, 8.96%.

[M(bpeado)3][B(Ph)4]2 [M = Mn3 and Co4]

Compound 3 and 4 were prepared by the diffusion method in an H tube. In one arm of the H tube, a 5 mL methanol solution of 0.25 mmol metal chloride (58 mg MnCl2·6H2O for 3 or 59 mg CoCl2·6H2O for 4) and 0.5 mmol (171 mg) soldium tetraphenylborate was added. And in the other arm, 0.5 mmol (108 mg) bpeado in 5 mL methanol was added. Finally, methanol was added to link the two solutions to allow slow diffusion. After two weeks, yellow (3) or pink (4) crystals were harvested (yield 80% for 3 and 85% for 4, respectively based on bpeado). Anal., calcd. for 3 C84H76B2MnN6O6: C, 75.14; H, 5.71; N, 6.26%. Found: C, 75.26; H, 5.63; N, 6.32%. Anal., calcd. for 4 C84H76B2CoN6O6: C, 74.91; H, 5.69; N, 6.24%. Found: C, 75.02; H, 5.63; N, 6.28%.

Crystallography

The intensity data for crystals of 1–4 were collected on a Nonius Kappa CCD diffractometer with graphite-monochromated Mo Kα radiation (0.71073 Å) at 293K.16 Empirical absorption corrections were applied using the Sortav program.17 The structures were solved by direct methods and refined by a full-matrix least-squares technique based on F2 using the SHELXL 97 program.18 All non-hydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically. The hydrogen atoms of the water molecules were located by a difference Fourier map and refined with distance restrains for O–H and H⋯H (0.96 and 1.52 Å, respectively) and one variable isotropic U for all. Other hydrogen atoms were placed by calculation positions. The details of the crystal data and selected bond lengths and angles for compounds 1–4 are listed in Tables 1 and 2, respectively. Crystallographic data for the structures reported in this paper have been deposited with the Cambridge Crystallographic Data Centre as CCDC 691371–691374.

Results and discussion

Description of the structures

Complex [Mn(bpeado)3][ClO4]21

The structure is made of Mn(bpeado)3 3D cationic 3-fold interpenetrated frameworks incorporating ClO4− anions (Fig. 1, 2 and 3). The unique octahedral manganese ion lies on the position (Wyckoff 1b). It is coordinated by six oxygen atoms from six separate bpeado ligands (Mn1–O1 = 2.175 Å) (Fig. 1). It is clear that the less stereo hindrance of N-oxide allows each metal ion to have six bpeado ligands coordinated, like dpdo,11c while for 4,4′-bipy or its derivatives such a case has never been observed because of the clashes of the α-hydrogen atoms. Each bpeado ligand lying on an inversion center (Wyckoff 6g) bridges two manganese ions in an anti-mode and each manganese ion is connected to six manganese ions via bpeado ligands, generating 3D network with the topology of α-Po (Fig. 2)8 with the Mn⋯Mn separations of 14.969 Å. Frameworks of the same topology have been reported for the similar N-oxideligand dpdo, such as Sc(dpdo)3(NO3)2, in which the α-Po nets are 2-fold interpenetrated.11c The longer bpeado ligands not only results in larger Mn⋯Mn separations, but also the framework with larger open windows, thus allowing a higher degree of interpenetration to occur. Actually, in 1, each net is penetrated by two other independent nets and 3-fold interpenetration was observed (Fig. 3a). The independent networks are related to each other by unit cell transitions in the c direction. The top-view of the 3-fold interpenetration framework is presented in Fig. 3b. The perchlorate anions lying on the threefold axis (Wyckoff 2d) occupy the channel of the triangle cross-section that runs parallel to the c-axis, held there by weak C–H⋯O hydrogen bonds with the cationic framework (C2a⋯O2 = 3.20 Å, C2a–H2a⋯O2 = 119.5; C5b⋯O2 = 3.25 Å, C5b–H5b⋯O2 = 122.3°; symmetry codes: a 1 + x − y, 1 + x, 1 − z; b 1 − y, 1 + x − y, z).19

Complex [Cu(bpeado)2(H2O)2][ClO4]2·bpeado 2

The structure of 2 is a laminar type, consisting of 2D (4,4) sheets of Cu(bpeado)22+, lattice bpeado molecule and perchlorate anions. Within the Cu(bpeado)22+ sheet, the Cu(II) ion lies at the inversion center (Wyckoff 1a) and is coordinated by four oxygen atoms in the equatorial positions (Cu–O = 1.954 or 1.975 Å), and has a weak bonding interaction with two water molecules in the axial sites (Cu–O = 2.513 Å), giving a 4 + 2 environment (Fig. 4). The bpeado molecules on the inversion center (Wyckoff 2i) bridge Cu(II) ions in an anti-mode along both [0 0 1] and [1 1 0] directions, forming a 2D framework with a parallelogram cavity of 14.0 × 14.6 Å based on the Cu⋯Cu distance (Fig. 5). The lattice bpeado molecules residing on inversion center (Wyckoff 2i) occupy these channels, forming strong O–H⋯O hydrogen bonding with the coordination water molecules (O4⋯O3a = 2.717 Å, O4–H1W⋯O3a = 173.98°; symmetry codes: a −x, −y, −z) and weak C–H⋯O hydrogen bonding with anti-bpeado bridges (C12b⋯O3 = 3.14 Å, C12b–H12Ab⋯O3 = 163.6°; symmetry codes: b x − 1, y, z).19

The structure of 2 can be regarded as 3D α-Po network when taking the interlayer hydrogen bonding into account. As shown in Fig. 6, the 2D layers bridged by bpeado were further connected by hydrogen bonding between the lattice bpeado and coordination water molecules, resulting in a 3D network with the topology of α-Po constructed by both anti-bpeado bridges and hydrogen bonding. The large angular deviation of the lattice bpeado molecule from perpendicular to the sheets is due mainly to the large angle between the lattice bpeado molecule and the coordination water (N3–O3⋯O4 = 124.9°). This also has an effect of bridging the (4,4) sheets closer together, making the net less spacious, and thus comparing with compound 1, only two independent such networks interpenetrate (Fig. 7a). In addition, the structure is stabilised by extensive hydrogen bonding between the coordination water and the bridging bpeado molecules from the interpenetrating nets (O4⋯O1f = 3.201 Å, O4–H2W⋯O1f = 173.21°; O4⋯O2g = 3.158 Å, O4–H2W⋯O2g = 120.12°; symmetry codes: f −x + 1, −y, −z; g x + 1, y, z).19 The independent networks are related to each other by unit cell transitions along the a axis and form approximate triangle cross-section channels that runs parallel to the a-axis (Fig. 7b). These channels are occupied by the perchlorate anions by forming weak C–H⋯O hydrogen bonding with anti-bridged and lattice bpeado molecules (C⋯O = 3.00–3.30 Å, C–H⋯O = 127.8–141.2°).19

Complex [Mn(bpeado)3][B(Ph)4]23

X-Ray single crystal structure analysis reveals that compound 3 is comprised of a non-interpenetrating Mn(bpeado)32+ layer constructed by both anti- and gauche-bpeado bridges, in which anti- and gauche-bpeado molecules lie on an inversion center and general position (Wyckoff 4e), respectively. Within the Mn(bpeado)32+ layer, the metal ions lie on inversion center (Wyckoff 2c) and are octahedrally coordinated by four oxygen atoms from gauche-bpeado in the equatorial positions (Mn–O = 2.115 or 2.149 Å) and another two oxygen atoms from anti-bpeado in the axial sites (Mn–O = 2.215 Å) (Fig. 8a). The metal ions are bridged by double gauche-bpeado bridges in [1 0 0] direction and single anti-bpeado bridge along [0 0 1] direction, forming a 2D framework with a rectangular grids of 15.2 × 10.0 Å2 (Fig. 8b). The crystal packing diagram of 3 is shown in Fig. 8c. 2D rectangular layers are stacked in parallel in ABAB alternations along the crystallographic b-axis. The BPh4− counter anions lie between the layers with one of their phenyl rings plugged in the cavities, which prohibit further interpenetration. In addition, another phenyl ring of BPh4− forms C–H⋯π weak interaction with the gauche-bpeado bridge from an adjacent layer (H11Aa⋯R1 = 2.54 Å, C11a⋯R1 = 3.39 Å, C11a-H11Aa⋯R1 = 151.6°; R1 = C37/C38/C39/C40/C41/C42; symmetry codes: a x − 0.5, −y + 1.5, z − 0.5).19 These weak interactions between tetraphenylborate anion and cationic Mn(bpeado)32+ layer stabilize the 3D framework of 3.

Complex [Co(bpeado)3][B(Ph)4]24

The crystal structure of 4 also contains rectangular (4,4) grids of 14.6 × 9.7 Å2 bridged by both anti- and gauche-bpeado bridges, which is similar to that found in 3. However, there is some difference between them. Compared with 3, the BPh4− counter anions in 4 residing between the layers form the more extensive C–H⋯π interaction with the cationic layer. The phenyl rings also plug in the cavities, prohibiting interpenetration. While the other two phenyl rings of BPh4− form two kinds of C–H⋯π interaction with the gauche-bpeado bridge from the adjacent layer (H4Aa ⋯R1 = 2.70 Å, C4a ⋯R1 = 3.53 Å, C4a–H4Aa⋯R1 = 128.3°; H7Ab ⋯R1 = 2.70 Å, C7b⋯R2 = 3.53 Å, C7b–H7Ab⋯R2 = 144.3°; R1 = C19/C20/C21/C22/C23/C24, R2 = C31/C32/C33/C34/C35/C36; symmetry codes: a x + 0.5, −y + 0.5, z − 0.5 b x − 0.5, −y + 0.5, z − 0.5).19

Feature of bpeado

Bpeado, an analogue of 4,4′-bipyridine-N,N′-dioxide (dpdo), shows some unique features in constructing supramolecular structures.12,13 Compared with 1,2-bis(4-pyridyl)ethane (bpea), bpeado can adopt an angular coordination mode or act as multidentate hydrogen-bonding acceptor, which makes it possible to construct high connect network and some interesting supramolecular structure. In addition, compared with dpdo, the introduction of two methylene groups between the two pyridine rings makes bpeado a longer and flexible spacer. Meanwhile, the increase of flexibility makes it possible to find some new bridge modes. In fact, in this series of compounds comprised of bpeado, 3D 3-fold (1) or 2-fold (2) interpenetrated α-Po network, non-interpenetrated (4,4) (3 and 4) layer was found, which is not found in dpdo containing complexes.13a, 13e Furthermore, two kinds of bridge modes are found in these compounds including unique gauche-mode, which is another remarkable feature of bpeado compared with dpdo.

Influence of the anion

Compounds 1 and 3 containing the same metal center but different counter anions display a different structure, which provides the choice of exploring the influence of the counter anion on the formation of the final structure. In 1 and 3, the radius of Mn2+ is large enough to adopt six bpeado molecules. Furthermore, the perchlorate anion is small (59 Å3) in 1 and bpeado has enough space to expand, resulting in an anti-mode bridge. Thus, compound 1 has 6-connected α-Po nets. The small size of perchlorate takes a small space, which allows the occurrence of interpenetration. And compound 1 is a 3D 3-fold interpenetrated α-Po network and perchlorate takes the channel along the c direction, while in 3, big BPh4− anions (385 Å3) take more space than perchlorate and there is no space for bpeado to expand. So, four bpeado molecules employ gauche-mode, resulting in 2D (4,4) layer with a cavity. The layers have enough space for interpenetration; however, the big BPh4− anions take the void space by plugging in the cavities, and thus precluding interpenetration. For 4, compared with the reported compound of [Co(bpeado)2.5(H2O)2][ClO4]2·0.5bpeado,10b the size effect of cation also works. The possible reason is the repulsion between bpeado and BPh4− is stronger than that between six bpeado around the Co2+ ion, resulting in the stable structure found in 4.

Conclusions

The series of first-row transition metal compounds with bpeado and perchlorate or tetraphenylborate have been prepared and structurally characterized. They show 3D 3-fold or 2-fold interpenetrated α-Po network, non-interpenetrated (4,4) layer, respectively. We have shown that by variation of the size of metal ion (cation) or anion we are able to influence their structure together with the topology. Furthermore, the features of bpeado ligand—angular coordination mode, strong hydrogen bonding ability, flexible bridging mode (anti- or gauche-) — have been fully embodied in these structures.

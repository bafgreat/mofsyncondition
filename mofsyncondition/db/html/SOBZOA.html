Each ligand binds three or four metal ions. High temperature synthesis tends to give ligands with larger coordination numbers. 1â€“5 are all 2D double-layer coordination polymers. Complex 2 and 3 are isostructural. The ligands in 1â€“5 exist in 4(1H)-pyridinone form rather than commonly presented 4-oxypyridinium forms. Introduction

In recent years, many of researchers have been interested in the assembly (via coordination) and crystal engineering of metalâ€“organic frameworks and coordination polymers thanks to their fascinating structural motifs and potential applications in porous, magnetic, luminescent, and catalytic materials [1], [2], [3]. Much effort has been focused on the rational design and controlled synthesis of polynuclear complexes through multidentate organic ligands such as polycarboxylate and N-heterocyclic ligands [4], [5], [6]. In particular, N-containing polycarboxylate ligands such as pyrazine- and pyridine-carboxylates are efficient building blocks for constructing coordination polymers [7], [8], [9], [10]. Since they have diverse coordination modes to chelate and bridge metal atoms through the carboxylate oxygen and heterocyclic nitrogen donors, their coordination frameworks are highly influenced by factors such as metal ions, pH value, co-ligands, and counter ions [11], [12], [13], [14]. Although various aromatic carboxylates have been used in the construction of thermal stable coordination polymers, cationic pyridinum-containing ligands are still uncommon [15], [16], [17]. Due to their cationic moiety, these compounds are expected different in properties from those without pyridinum moiety. Inspired by previous work [18], [19], H2L (4-(4-oxypyridinium-1-yl) phthalic acid) was chosen for the following reasons [20], [21]: (1) this organic ligand is a cation-type ligand as presented in literature (Scheme 1, I), which has a good water-solubility and therefore it is beneficial to investigate the nature of this ligand in hydro(solvo)thermal conditions [22], [23]; (2) viologens are bis-pyridinium compounds, they can change color upon heating, irradiation or via electrochemical processes, and were used as displays (dichromic material); [24], [25] the pyridinium compounds are quite similar to viologen compounds, whether they can act as a dichromic material is still unclear; (3) the pyridine ring and phenyl ring are twisted across the Câˆ’N single bond and the skew coordination orientation of the carboxyl groups provides the potential of the formation of helical segments; (4) the ligandâ€™s lacks of symmetry in the positioning of carboxylate groups, which is beneficial for structural tenability; (5) only rare-earth coordination polymers constructed from H2L have been reported [22], [23], and much more work is still necessary to understand the coordination chemistry of H2L.

With the aim of further understanding the coordination chemistry as well as is dichromic properties of H2L and preparing new materials with interesting architecture and excellent physical properties, we have recently engaged in the research of coordination polymers based on this ligand and transition metal ions in the absence and presence of bases. Ligand structures and its coordination modes are summarized in Scheme 1.

Results and discussion

Synthetic conditions and IR spectra

Solvent, pH, and temperatures have a significant influence on the reaction products, which has been extensively studied in literature [11], [12], [13], [14], [29], [30]. However, H2L is still quite new to the study of coordination compounds and only two papers dealing with it have appeared in literature [22], [23]. Temperature is critical in many coordination reactions as it influences activation energy and the reaction thermodynamics. Compound 1, without any coordination and crystalline water molecules, was assembled in aqueous solution in the presence of triethylamine at 160 Â°C. No crystal can be isolated in the same condition if temperature decreased to 85 Â°C. Complex 2, 3 and 4 were obtained in DMA-H2O (1:1) solution at 85 Â°C. 1 and 2 differ in coordination mode of ligand and water molecules. Lower temperatures (85 Â°C) result in water coordination in 2â€“5. IR spectra of 1 and 2 are quite similar except the water signal in 1 at 3453 cmâˆ’1 (KBr), while in 2, this signal moved to 3406 cmâˆ’1. It should be mentioned that 2 and 4 can be synthesized simultaneously in DMA-H2O (1:1) solution at 85 Â°C without triethylamine. Crystal shapes and sizes of 2 and 4 are different and they can be separated manually. When 10 Î¼l of triethylamine was added to the reaction solvent, only 4 can be obtained. There is a bridging water molecule in 4, while 2 only has a monodentate water molecule. Except for the metal ion difference, complex 2 and 3 are isostructural and were synthesized in the same conditions. However, their IR spectra are different, possibly due to different coordination abilities between Cd(II) and Co(II). Acicular crystal 5 was synthesized in DMA:H2O (1:3) solution in the presence of triethylamine. DMA:H2O (1:1) solvent failed to synthesize 5. From these results, we can conclude that (1) high temperatures disfavor water coordination (complex 1 and 2), this can easily be explained by high flexibility of water molecule compare with the bulky ligand; (2) base favors bidentate water (complex 2 and 4); (3) the ligand is deprotonated in all the complexes, whether in the presence or absence (in DMA containing solvent) of base. This indicates that DMA can be used as weak base to deprotonate ligand.

Structure comparison

Important synthetic condition and structure data is listed in Table 2. The ligand can provide five of its oxygen atoms to form up to six coordination bonds (Scheme 1). Ligands in all the complexes, whether in the absence or presence of triethylamine, are fully deprotonated. As indicated by the name (hydr)oxypyridinium, the ligand in literature was represented as inner salt [35]. In pyridinium compounds, the NC distance is the shortest amongst the aromatic rings [16], [36]. On the other hand, phenol CO distances is âˆ¼1.35 Ã… [37], while CO is âˆ¼1.25 Ã…. In 3-Hydroxy-1-(3-imidazolylpropyl)-2-methyl-4-pyridinone dihydrochloride dihydrate, which is quite similar to our ligand, the CO (phenol) distances are around 1.32â€“1.33 Ã… [38], although they name it 4-pyridinone, but this should be pyridinium compounds in actuality. The C11O5 bond distances increase from 1.27 in 1â€“4 to 1.29 in copper complex 5. These figures are obviously close to CO double bound at 1.25 Ã…. The indisputably short C11O5 distances in our ligand shows that the ligand is in neutral ketone form (Scheme 1, II) rather than the inner salt pyridinium form (Scheme I). However, the nitrogen atom and three adjacent carbon atoms are coplanar, this indicates that the N containing ring is still aromatic. The pz orbital of N atom is conjugated with the ring. Another piece evidence for the ligand being in pyridinone rather than pyridinium form is that all the complexes do not change color upon heating to 150 Â°C in air or sunlight or UV irradiation. Pyridinium form is quite similar to that of viologen compounds (dichromic material), 1â€“5 are non-dichromic material which is an indication that the ligand exist in ketone form.

Thermal analysis and PXRD pattern

Powder X-ray diffractions (PXRD) for complex 1â€“5 were performed to characterize their purity (Fig. S2 in Supporting information). All the diffraction peaks are in good agreement with the simulated results, which confirm the phase purity of the complexes. To estimate the stability of the coordination polymers 1â€“5, their thermogravimetric analyses were studied by TGAâ€“DSC (Fig. 5 and S3). The experiments were performed in the temperature range of 23â€“600 Â°C under N2 atmosphere with a heating rate of 10 Â°C minâˆ’1. For complex 1, there is no obvious weight loss from 23 to 400 Â°C in the TGA curve (Fig. 5). Above 410 Â°C, rapid weight loss occurred for 1 owing to the decomposition of the ligand with a fastest exothermic peak at 490 Â°C (Fig. S3a). Clearly, there is a phase transfer at 140 Â°C. This is the most thermally stable complex among 1â€“5 (Fig. 5). Complex 2, containing 2 coordinate water molecules, remained stable up to 118 Â°C. The weight loss in 118â€“330 Â°C is attributed to the removal of two coordination water molecules with an endothermic peak at 176 Â°C (obsd: 8.4%, calcd: 8.8%), and the anhydrous complex begins to decompose at 346 Â°C (Fig. S3b). 3 shows weight loss of 10.6% in the range of 156â€“236 Â°C, which is attributed to the release of two coordinated water molecules (calcd: 10.2%) with an endothermic peak at 224 Â°C. The decomposition of the frameworks occurred at about 237 Â°C, which is 79 Â°C lower than its isostructural 2, indicating Co(II) complex is less thermally stable than that of Cd(II) complexes. This is possibly due to Cd(II) being a non-redox active ion compared with Co(II) (Fig. S3c). For 4, the weight loss between 165 and 303 Â°C is consistent with the removal of two and a half coordinated water molecules (obsd: 10.6%, calcd: 10.9%). Above 217 Â°C, rapid weight loss assigned to the release of two coordinated water molecules and the ligand decomposition process. 5 is the most thermal unstable complex in all the complexes as its framework decomposed at 217 Â°C. This is due to the strong oxidation ability of Cu(II) compared with Co(II) and Cd(II). The weight losses in 1â€“4 are all endothermic in nature. Luminescence

The fluorescent behaviors of compounds 1, 2, and 4 were investigated in the solid state at room temperature (Fig. 6). 1, 2, and 4 exhibited emission peaks at 380 nm and 428 nm for 1, 380 nm and 432 nm for 2, 386 nm and 425 nm for 4 upon excitation at 354 nm. Free H2L ligands fluoresced in the solid state with its emission peaks at 386 nm and 436 nm. The emission wavelengths of 1, 2, and 4 is quite similar to that of free H2L ligand, thus the luminescent of 1, 2, and 4 are attributed to ligand-centered emission rather than ligand to metal charge transfer (LMCT). These emissions are quite similar to the Sm complex reported by Feng et al. [22] Compared with the H2L ligand, these complexes exhibit enhancement in luminescence intensity. Usually upon complexation with metal, enhancements in the intensity and also quantum efficiencies have been observed as the metal cluster effectively increases the rigidity of the ligand and reduces the loss of energy through non-radioactive decay [39].

Conclusions

In summary, five 2D coordination polymer bases on 4-(4-oxypyridinium-1-yl) phthalic acid (more accurately, 4-(4(1H)-pyridinone-1-yl) phthalic acid) were synthesized under hydrothermal conditions. X-ray analysis revealed that ligands in these complexes are fully deprotonated. All carboxylates participated in coordination. Of all the oxygen atoms in ligand, carbonyl has the shortest bond lengths. The ligand exists in ketone form rather than phenolate inner salt form as indicated by bond lengths. The coordination number of ligand varies from 4 to 6. Higher synthetic temperature tends to form ligands with higher coordination numbers. The two carboxylate in all these complexes are almost perpendicular each other. Ï€â‹¯Ï€ interaction between 4(1H)-pyridinone moieties in adjacent layer polymers is much stronger than that between benzene rings. H-bond exists in water containing the coordination polymer. Of all the coordination polymers, 1 is the most thermally stable while 5 is the most unstable. 1, 2, and 4 have ligand emission 430 and 480 nm, the emission intensity increased compared with free ligands. The carbonyl oxygen in the ligand either form the strongest coordination bonds or the strongest H-bonds with water molecules.

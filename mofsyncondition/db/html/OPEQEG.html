Metal-controlled assembly of coordination polymers: Structural variation from 2D parallel interpenetration to 3D CsCl-type topology

Abstract

Two novel metal-organic frameworks [Cd2(hdb)2(1,4-ndc)2]n (1) and [Ni3(Hhdb)2(1,4-ndc)4(H2O)2]n (2) have been synthesized under the similar hydrothermal conditions (hdb = 1,1′-(1,6-hexanediyl)bis(imidazole) and 1,4-ndc = 1,4-naphthalenedicarboxylate). The polymers (1) and (2) show 2D parallel interpenetration and 3D CsCl-type topology, respectively, which are mainly affected by the differences of the metal centers.

The design and synthesis of metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) are attracting increasing attention, not only for their interesting molecular topologies, but also for their potential applications as functional materials [1]. Thus, in this aspect, considerable progress has been made on the theoretical prediction and network-based approaches for controlling the topology and geometries of the networks to produce useful functional materials [2]. However, there still remains great challenge for directional construction of functional MOFs with predictable molecular structures and expected properties because of many subtle factors related with the crystallization process, and therefore, systematic research on this topic is still very necessary for understanding the roles of the factors in the formation of metal coordination frameworks [3]. Generally, the diversity in the framework structures greatly depends on the selection of the metal, the inorganic counterion, the solvent system, and sometimes the metal-to-ligand ratio [4]. In this regard, the selection of the metal is an important factor that may be utilized in determining the framework topology, which has not been widely employed to date [5].

On the other hand, bis(imidazole) ligands, as an important family of flexible N-donor ligands, have attracted great interest [6]. In our previous work, we have reported a series of fascinating archetypal structures based on 1,1′-(1,6-hexanediyl)bis(imidazole) (hdb) ligand [7]. Herein two novel MOFs [Cd2(hdb)2(1,4-ndc)2]n (1) and [Ni3(Hhdb)2(1,4-ndc)4(H2O)2]n (2) were synthesized under the similar reaction conditions (1,4-ndc = 1,4-naphthalenedicarboxylate). The polymers (1) and (2) show 2D parallel interpenetration and 3D CsCl-type topology, respectively, which are mainly affected by the differences of the metal centers.

Crystals of (1) and (2) were hydrothermally synthesized by reacting metal chloride with 1,4-ndc and hdb in molar ratio 1:1:1 in water at 140 °C for 2 days [8], [9]. The asymmetric unit of (1) contains two unique Cd(II) cations, two unique 1,4-ndc anions and two unique hdb molecules (Fig. S1). Each Cd(II) atom is six-coordinated by four carboxylate oxygen atoms (Cd(1)–O(1) = 2.370(7), Cd(1)–O(2) = 2.307(7), Cd(1)–O(5) = 2.347(8), Cd(1)–O(6) = 2.396(7), Cd(2)–O(7) = 2.433(10), Cd(2)–O(8) = 2.270(9), Cd(2)–O(4A) = 2.293(8) and Cd(2)–O(3A) = 2.452(7) Å) from two different 1,4-ndc ligands, and two nitrogen atoms (Cd(1)–N(1) = 2.282(8), Cd(1)–N(4A) = 2.293(8), Cd(2)–N(5) = 2.275(12) and Cd(2)–N(8A) = 2.251(9) Å) from two different hdb ligands (Fig. S1). The average Cd–O and Cd–N distances are comparable to those observed in [Cd2L4(H2O)] (L = 4-(pyridin-3-ylmethoxy)benzoate) [10]. As depicted in Fig. 1, the 1,4-ndc dianions act as bidentate bridging ligands, jointing the Cd(II) centers together to result in a 1D polymeric [Cd(1,4-ndc)]n chain with the Cd1⋯Cd2 separation of 11.168 Å. The hdb ligands further extend these chains into the final two-dimensional (4, 4) network. Interestingly, the two (4, 4) networks are interpenetrated in a parallel mode (Fig. 2). The two interpenetrating networks are individually generated by translation and they are related by inversion.

To study the influence of the metals on their complex structures, the nickel chloride was selected to react with 1,4-ndc and the hdb ligand under the similar synthetic conditions, and a structurally-different complex [Ni3(Hhdb)2(1,4-ndc)4(H2O)2]n (2) was obtained. As shown in Fig. S1, the asymmetric unit of (2) consists of one and a half unique Ni(II) atoms, two unique 1,4-ndc ligands, one unique protonated Hhdb ligand. The Ni1 atom is six-coordinated by four carboxylate oxygen atoms (Ni(1)–O(6) = 2.053(2), Ni(1)–O(7A) = 2.0443(19), Ni(1)–O(6A) = 2.053(2) and Ni(1)–O(7B) = 2.0443(19) Å) from four different 1,4-ndc ligands, and two water oxygen atoms in a octahedral coordination sphere. The Ni2 atom is also six-coordinated by one nitrogen atom (Ni(2)–N(1) = 2.065(3) Å) from one protonated Hhdb ligand, four carboxylate oxygen atoms (Ni(2)–O(1) = 2.026(2), Ni(2)–O(5) = 2.002(2), Ni(2)–O(4A) = 2.016(2) and Ni(2)–O(8A) = 2.025(2) Å) from four different 1,4-ndc ligands, and one water oxygen atom in a octahedral coordination sphere. The three adjacent Ni(II) atoms are connected by carboxylates and the water molecules to form a trinuclear Ni(II) unit (Fig. 3). The protonated Hhdb ligands are attached on both sides of the trinuclear Ni(II) unit in an unusual monodentate mode. The trinuclear Ni(II) units are further linked by eight backbones of the 1,4-ndc anions to generate a 3D framework structure (Fig. 4 and Fig. S2). If each trinuclear Ni(II) unit acts as a node, the coordination network in 2 can be simplified as CsCl-type topology with the 1,4-ndc anions as linkers (Fig. 5). So far, although the CsCl-type net is commonly described in textbooks, such topologies with eight-connected nodes have only appeared in a few examples [11].

In conclusion, we have successfully synthesized two new MOFs by combining a long flexible hdb ligand and a dicarboxylate ligand. The two polymers (1) and (2) show 2D parallel interpenetration and 3D CsCl-type topology, respectively. Clearly, the different framework structures of the two complexes are mainly affected by the differences of the metal centers. Further systematic studies for the design and synthesis are under way in our laboratory.

(1) Synthesis of 1,1′-(1,6-hexanediyl)bis(imidazole) (hdb). A mixture of imidazole (3.4 g, 50 mmol) and NaOH (2.0 g, 50 mmol) in DMSO (10 mL) was stirred at 60 °C for 1 h, and then 1,6-dichlorodecane (25 mmol) was added. The mixture was cooled to room temperature after stirring at 70 °C for 2 h, and then poured into the water (150 mL). Colorless powder of hdb was obtained after evaporation of the solvent. Yield: 56%.

(2) Syntheses of [Cd2(hdb)2(1,4-ndc)2]n (1) and [Ni3(Hhdb)2(1,4-ndc)4(H2O)2]n (2): A mixture of CdCl2·2H2O (0.50 mmol), 1,4-H2ndc (0.50 mmol), hdb (0.10 mmol), and water (12 mL) was sealed in a Teflon reactor (15 mL), which was heated at 140 °C for 48 h and then gradually cooled to room temperature. Colorless crystals of 1 were collected in a 51% yield based on Cd(II). The preparation of 2 was similar to that of 1 except that NiCl2·6H2O (0.5 mmol) was used instead of CdCl2·2H2O as the metal. Block crystals were obtained in a 39% yield based on Ni(II).

Introduction

Despite a large number of coordination polymers (CPs) that have been rationally assembled recently, the construction of entangled systems is a longstanding fascination for chemists due to their intriguing aesthetic structures and promising applications as functional materials.1,2 Entangled systems, such as interpenetration, polycatenane, polyrotaxane, polythreading and polyknotting (self-penetrating networks), are a major subject in the area of coordination polymer frameworks, which has been well discussed in several comprehensive reviews by Robson, Batten, Ciani and co-workers.3 Among many types of entanglements, the most frequently encountered are the interpenetrating networks,4 in which two or more independent networks pass through each other either in parallel or inclined mode. Polythreading5 is characterized by the existence of closed loops as well as of rod or string elements that can thread through the loops. Self-penetration,6 as an important phenomenon in the realm of entangled networks, is a single network having the peculiarity that the smallest topological rings are catenated by other shorter rings belonging to the same net. In contrast to the aforementioned types of entanglements, self-threading7 is a new threading mode found in single nets, in which the closed loops are threaded by the components from the network itself.

To date, a variety of entangled coordination polymers with complicated structures have been synthesized and documented.8 Of these syntheses, the most effective and representative synthetic approach for generating new entangled networks is the elaborate choice of the well-designed organic ligands.9 The structural change of the organic ligand such as the angle, spacer, functional groups and relative position of the donor can control and vary the topology of the final structures. Therefore, utilizing similar or isomeric ligands to construct CPs has turned out to be an effective way to explore the impact of organic ligands on the formation of structures. In our previous report,10 we employed a rigid bidentate linear ligand fumaric acid to react with Zn(ii) atoms in the presence of auxiliary N-donor ligands to obtain five entangled coordination polymers. The result suggests that the chemical nature of N-donor ligands play a significant role in tuning the entangled modes of the coordination networks. Following this synthetic strategy, herein, we chose four kinds of similar aliphatic dicarboxylic acids (fumaric acid, succinate acid, glutaric acid, adipic acid) in combination with Cd(ii) centers and a flexible ligand 1,3-bis(4-pyridyl)propane (bpp) to investigate the influence of the configurations of dicarboxylic acids on the tuning of entangled networks. Furthermore, compared with rigid fumaric acid, the flexible bpp ligand provides advantages in constructing entangled networks because it can exist in anti–anti (TT), anti–gauche (TG), and two different gauche–gauche (GG and GG′) conformations11 to meet the geometry requirements of metal centers. Moreover, the influence of molar ratios on the self-assembly process is also investigated. As a result, we successfully isolated six new CPs with distinct entanglements, {[Cd(fum)(bpp)(H2O)]·(H2O)}n (1) with 2D → 2D interpenetration, {[Cd(fum)(bpp)2]·(H2O)5}n (2) with 2D → 3D polythreading, {[Cd2(suc)1.5(bpp)2(NO3)(H2O)2]·6H2O}n (3) with 2D self-penetrating, {[Cd(suc)(bpp)2]·(H2O)1.5}n (4) with 2D self-threading, {[Cd2(glu)2(bpp)3]·10H2O}n (5) and {Cd(adp)(bpp)(H2O)}n (6) with 3D self-penetrating. Their syntheses, crystal structures, topologies, thermal stabilities, and photoluminescence properties are reported in this paper.

Results and discussion

Syntheses

The six coordination polymers were all synthesized under hydrothermal conditions. As we know, hydrothermal synthesis is a relatively complicated process and the final products can be affected by a variety of reaction variables (pH values, reaction time, solvents, template, reactant ratio, etc.). In order to investigate the influence of the ligand flexibility on the entangled coordination polymers, we chose four kinds of aliphatic dicarboxylic acids with different molecular backbones as secondary ligands incorporated into the Cd–bpp system. In the meantime, the effects of the reactant ratio on structural construction were also explored in this system. Inspired by our previous work,14 we firstly introduced the rigid linear ligand (H2fum) into the Cd–bpp system in a molar ratio of 1 : 1 : 1, yielding the crystal product of compound 1. With the change of the Cd–bpp–fum ratio to 1 : 2 : 1 under uniform reaction conditions, a structurally different compound, 2, was formed. Encouraged by the successful isolation of compounds 1 and 2, the four-carbon dicarboxylate ligand with a flexible C–C σ bond (H2suc) was selected instead of H2fum. Similarly, when using different metal–ligand ratios, two structurally distinct compounds 3 and 4 were obtained. The results indicate that the reactant ratio plays an important role in the formation of coordination polymers. To further verify the influence of the ligand flexibility, the five-carbon and six-carbon aliphatic dicarboxylate ligands (H2glu/H2adp) were employed, and two new 3D complicated architectures, compounds 5 and 6, were isolated, respectively. However, while further adjusting the reactant ratio, we only obtained either the same compounds or unidentified white powder products. Therefore, our synthetic route is schematically depicted in Scheme 1. Based on the aforementioned observations, the reaction conditions such as the ligand flexibility and reactant ratio exert a critical effect on the formation of entangled coordination polymers. Additionally, in order to study whether the temperature impacts the final structures, numerous parallel experiments have been repeated in the range of 80–120 °C, which shows that the effect of temperature in this system was small and same structural compounds were obtained in this range.

Crystal structure of 1

Crystallographic analysis reveals that compound 1 crystallizes in the monoclinic group P21/c and features a 2-fold parallelly interpenetrated 2D → 2D network motif. As depicted in Fig. 1a, the asymmetric unit contains one Cd(ii) ion, one fum2− anion, one bpp ligand, one aqua ligand and one lattice water molecule. Each Cd(ii) ion is hepta-coordinated and exhibits a pentagonal-bipyramidal geometry, with five oxygen atoms from two chelating carboxylate groups and one nitrogen atom of the bpp ligand occupying the basal plane, one pyridyl nitrogen and one aqua donor at the apical sites. The two carboxylate groups of one fum ligand show a bisdentate chelating mode. Each fum2− ligand acts as a bi-node and bridges two Cd(ii) atoms with the Cd⋯Cd distance of 9.44 Å. Each bpp ligand shows the TT conformation. The dihedral angle between the two pyridine ring planes is 82.1°. Each bpp acts as a bidentate bridging ligand and joins two Cd(ii) atoms with the Cd⋯Cd distance of 12.98 Å. Four Cd atoms are interlinked by two fum2− and two bpp ligands, thus affording a square grid with a large window of 12.98 × 9.44 Å2, which are further connected into a 2D puckered (4,4) net (Fig. 1b). The large square windows and the corrugation of the single sheets enable another identical network to interpenetrate the square windows in a parallel mode, thus giving a 2-fold (2D → 2D) interpenetrating network (Fig. 1c and d).

Crystal structure of 2

Single-crystal X-ray diffraction measurement reveals that compound 2 crystallizes in the triclinic system with the P space group. The asymmetric unit contains one Cd(ii) atom, two half fum2− ligands, two bpp ligands and five lattice water molecules. As shown in Fig. 2a, the Cd(ii) cation adopts a distorted pentagonal bipyramidal geometry, coordinating to four carboxylic O atoms from two fum2− anions and three N atoms from three different bpp ligands. The Cd–O bond distances are in the range of 2.385(8)–2.462(9) Å and the Cd–N bond distances are in the range of 2.338(10)–2.369(12) Å. For convenience, the fum2− ligands containing O1 and O3 are designated as fum-A and fum-B, and the bpp ligands containing N1 and N3 are designed as bpp-A and bpp-B, respectively. All Cd(ii) cations are connected through fum2− and bpp-A ligands to give a typical 2D square grid sheet, which contains two kinds of rectangular windows [Cd2(fum-A)2(bpp-A)2] and [Cd2(fum-B)2(bpp-A)2] with the dimensions of 9.31 × 12.02 Å and 9.29 × 12.02 Å, respectively. However, the one-end coordinated bpp-B ligands, grafted up and down each 2D network, resemble lateral arms protruding from both sides of the 2D grid sheet alternately (Fig. 2b). All the sheets are stacked in a parallel manner and arranged in a staggered fashion, and the distance between the two adjacent sheets is approximately 8.02 Å. Each dangling bpp-B ligand is disposed in a mutual anti orientation with respect to the layer plane with an effective length of about 11.64 Å (from one Cd(ii) center to the uncoordinated terminal nitrogen of bpp) which paves the way for the generation of mutual polythreading. As a result, the dangling lateral bpp-B ligands projected from both sides of the 2D layer are intercalated into the rectangular windows of the two adjacent layers above and below. Only the [Cd2(fum-A)2(bpp-A)2] rectangular windows of each sheet are threaded by two dangling arms coming from two opposite directions (Fig. 2c and d). Therefore, these entangled modes result in an interesting 2D → 3D polypseudo-threading framework.

Crystal structure of 3

Single-crystal X-ray structural analysis reveals that compound 3 crystallizes in the monoclinic group P21/n and exhibits a fascinating 2D self-penetrating network. As depicted in Fig. 3a, the asymmetric unit of 3 contains two crystallographically independent Cd centers, one and a half suc2− ligands (suc-A(O1–O3), suc-B(O5–O6)), two bpp ligands (bpp-A(N1–N2), bpp-B(N3–N4)), two coordinated water molecules, one nitrate ion and six lattice water molecules. The Cd1 atom is located in a distorted {CdN2O5} pentagonal bipyramidal coordination environment with oxygen donors from the coordinated water molecules and bpp pyridine nitrogen donors in the axial position. The equatorial plane of each coordination sphere contains two oxygen atoms from one suc2− ligand, two oxygen donors from the coordinated nitrate ion and one nitrogen atom from one bpp ligand while the six-coordinated Cd2 atom adopts a distorted octahedral coordination geometry which bonds to three oxygen donors from two suc2− ligands, two nitrogen donors from two different bpp ligands and one oxygen donor from the coordinated water molecule. Each bpp-B ligand binds to the Cd2 atoms using the TG conformation to form a 1D crankshaft shaped chain along the b axis. The neighboring 1D crankshaft shaped chains are interconnected by the suc-B molecules into a (6,3) undulated sheet (Fig. 3b and c). Similarly, the bpp-A ligands exhibit the TG conformation and coordinate the Cd1 atoms to generate infinite crankshaft shaped [Cd(bpp-A)]n chains (Fig. 3d). Significantly, these 1D [Cd(bpp-A)]n chains are threaded through the 2D 63 sheet to yield a (1D)n + 2D → 2D polythreading aggregation (Fig. 3e and f). Furthermore, these 1D [Cd(bpp-A)]n chains and the 2D 63 sheet are subsequently connected via the suc-A ligands to generate an unusual 2D self-penetrating network (Fig. 3g and h).

So far, only a few examples of 2D self-penetrating networks have been obtained. Among these examples, most of the 2D self-penetrating structures are derived from co-parallelly interpenetrating sheets15 or the passage of the rod through the circuit in the 2D sheet.16 However, the structure of 3 is composed by the 1D chains and a 2D layer which are entangled without increasing the dimensionality of the overall framework, and it represents an unique self-penetrating type. Topologically, if Cd1 and Cd2 cations are viewed as 3-connected and 4-connected nodes, respectively, and the suc2− and bpp ligands are simplified to be linear connector, the structure of 3 can be described as a 2-nodal (3,4)-connected 2D network with the Schläfli notation of (42·63·8)(42·6) (Fig. 3h). According to electronic databases, EPINET, RCSR, and TOPOS TTD,17 it is a new topology. In addition, strong hydrogen bonds are observed among the adjacent 2D sheets. As depicted in Fig. 4a, the coordinated O1w molecules form hydrogen-bonds with the carboxylate oxygen O1 atoms of suc2− anions (O1w–H1wa⋯O1d = 2.754 Å; symmetry codes: d = −x, −y, −z), which extend the 2D sheets into a 3D supramolecular architecture (Fig. 4b). From the topological view point, if the hydrogen bonds are taken into account, the Cd1 atoms can be considered as 4-connected nodes. Therefore, the whole 3D supramolecular architecture exhibits a uninodal 4-connected sxa network with the Schläfli symbol of (42·63·8) (Fig. 4c).

Crystal structure of 4

As shown in Fig. 5a, the asymmetric unit consists of one crystallographically independent Cd(ii) atom, one suc2− ligand, two bpp ligands (bpp-A(N1–N2), bpp-B(N3–N4)), and one and a half water molecules. Each seven-coordinated Cd(ii) atom sits in a distorted pentagonal bipyramidal geometry, which is defined by four oxygen atoms (O1, O2, O3a and O4a) of two suc2− ligands and one nitrogen atom (N1) from one bpp ligand at the equatorial plane, and two nitrogen atoms (N3 and N2a) from two different bpp ligands in the axial positions. The Cd(ii) atoms are connected by the suc2− ligands in the chelating mode into [Cd(suc)] helical chains with a pitch of 11.22 Å along the crystallographic b axis. And the adjacent opposite handed chains are arranged alternately and further extended into an achiral 2D layer via the bpp-A ligands which adopt the TG conformation (Fig. 5b). From the topological view point, each Cd atom can be assigned to four-connected nodes, the bpp ligands and suc2− anions are used as linkers, the 2D sheet can be simplified into a 4-connected network with the Schläfli symbol of {42·63·8}, representing a new 2D topological prototype17 (Fig. 5c). One outstanding feature of the structure of 4 is that there is one type of dangling bpp ligand (bpp-B) in the TT conformation, located up and down each 2D sheet, inserting into the parallelogrammic voids of the 2D layer to produce an unusual 2D self-threading motif (Fig. 5d and e). As far as we know, this kind of 2D self-threading structure is quite rare in coordination polymers, and only a few examples have been reported which are all originated from the 44-sql nets.18

Crystal structure of 5

When the longer aliphatic dicarboxylate glu2− ligand was introduced in the Cd–bpp system, compound 5 was isolated. It crystallizes in the monoclinic C2221 non-centro space group. There are one Cd(ii) atom, two halves of two crystallographically distinct glu2− ligands (glu-A(O1–O2), glu-B(O3–O4)), and one and a half bpp ligands (bpp-A(N1–N2), bpp-B(N3)) in the asymmetric unit of 5 as shown in Fig. 6a. Each Cd(ii) shows a distorted {CdN3O3} octahedral coordination environment, in which the equatorial plane contains O1, O2, and O3 from two distinct glu2− and N2a from one bpp ligand. The axial positions are occupied by two nitrogen atoms from two bpp ligands with an N1–Cd1–N3 angle of 177.17(19) Å. The Cd–O bond distances fall in the region of 2.272–2.488 Å, and the Cd–N bond distances averaging 2.362 Å are in the normal range (Table S5†). A distance of 2.744(5) Å between Cd1 and O4 indicates the existence of a weak interaction between them.

The bpp-A molecules adopt the TT conformation and coordinate the Cd(ii) centers to afford infinite 1D chains. The chains span two directions in the ab plane and can be divided into two sets which are separated by about 7.842 Å. And the two sets of arrays are bound by the bpp-B bridges in a GG conformation resulting in a 2D T-shaped bilayered structure (Fig. 6b and c). In addition, the glu-A ligands act as the pillars to connect the Cd(ii) atoms with the Cd⋯Cd separation of 8.581 Å which consolidates the 2D structure (Fig. 6d and e). As is usually found, the large distances between the two planes result in large cavities within the network. In order to stabilize the network, each bilayer net interlocks with two adjacent bilayer nets into a 2D → 3D parallel interpenetrating array (Fig. 6f). These parallel interpenetrating bilayers are further connected by the glu-B ligands as linkers to generate a 3D self-penetrating framework (Fig. 6g). Topological analysis of compound 5 reveals that it is a 5-connected uninodal htp network with a point symbol {42·67·8} (Fig. 6h). By carefully looking into this structure, we notice that five kinds of distinct helical chains coexist in the 3D network running along a, b and c directions with the pitch of 20.627, 13.667, 20.683 Å, respectively (Fig. 7).

Crystal structure of 6

When glu2− is replaced with a longer adp2− ligand, a particularly fascinating 3D self-penetrating structure of 6 has been obtained. Single-crystal X-ray structural analysis reveals that the asymmetric unit of 6 contains two crystallographically independent Cd(ii) atoms, four adp2− anions (adp-A(O1–O2), adp-B(O3–O4), adp-C(O5–O6), adp-D(O7–O8)), two bpp ligands (bpp-A(N1–N2), bpp-B(N3–N4)), and two coordinated water molecules (Fig. 8). Both Cd(ii) atoms possess a distorted {CdN2O5} pentagonal bipyramidal coordination environment, with bpp pyridyl nitrogen donors in the axial positions. The equatorial plane of each coordination sphere contains two chelating carboxylate groups from two adp2− ligands, and an additional oxygen donor from the coordinated water molecule.

The bpp-A molecules present the TG coordination conformation and the bpp-B molecules adopt the TT coordination conformation, respectively. Two kinds of bpp ligands coordinate Cd(ii) atoms in an alternate manner to generate fascinating left- and right-handed helical chains with a pitch of 27.58 Å corresponding to the length of the c axis (Fig. 9a). The neighboring Cd(ii) atoms are separated by the distances of 14.683 Å and 14.152 Å. The left- and right-handed helical chains are associated with the adp-A ligands to build a 1D ladder of [Cd2(bpp)2(adp)]n with the rectangular windows of 9.80 × 27.58 Å2 (Fig. 9b and c). The presence of the large windows paves the way for the onset of intertwinement. As shown in Fig. 10a and b, two sets of ladder chains are interlaced to give rise to an interesting 1D + 1D → 1D interpenetrating network, exhibiting a clearly defined polycatenation arrangement. Meanwhile, the adp-B ligands connect two sets of interpenetrating ladders into a 1D unique self-penetrating chain (Fig. 10c and d). So far, although a large number of 2D or 3D self-penetrating structures have been documented, the 1D self-penetrating chains are rare. Furthermore, these self-penetrating chains are combined by the adp-D ligands to form a 3D open framework with 1-D channels running along the c axis (Fig. 10e and f). As anticipated, in order to minimize the big void cavities and stabilize the framework, the potential voids formed by a single 3D network show incorporation of another identical network, thus giving a 2-fold 3D + 3D → 3D interpenetrating network (Fig. 11a and b). Finally, the interpenetrating 3D networks are further interconnected by adp-C ligands, resulting in an unusual self-penetrating framework (Fig. 11c and d). As shown above, the unprecedented structure of 6 contains two distinct levels of self-penetration derived from 1D + 1D → 1D polycatenation and 3D + 3D → 3D interpenetration. To date, although many exotic self-penetrating structures are being documented each year, the coexistence of different entangled motifs, especially the simultaneous occurrence of two levels of self-penetration in the same crystal structure is extremely rare.

A better insight into the nature of the involved framework can be achieved by application of a topological approach. To illustrate the unique structure of 6, each Cd(ii) center, being connected by two adp2− and two bpp ligands, can be simplified as a 4-connected node, and the adp2− and bpp ligands can be simplified into a linear linker. Accordingly, the whole network can be extended to a 4-connected sxa net with a point symbol of 66.

Structural comparison and the factors influencing the network

According to the comparison of the synthetic method and the structures of compounds 1–6, it is clear that the spacer length and flexibility of auxiliary ligands and the reactant molar ratio play important roles in the formation of the entangled coordination polymers.

On the one hand, we introduced four different aliphatic dicarboxylate ligands (H2fum, H2suc, H2glu, H2adp) into the Cd–bpp system, aiming at exploring the influence of aliphatic dicarboxylate on the assembly and structure of MOFs. The geometrical parameters of aliphatic dicarboxylate ligands in compound 1–6 are listed in Table 2. Analyzing the structural descriptions above, compounds 1, 3, 5, and 6 are obtained from the same metal center and neutral ligand (bpp) under uniform reaction conditions, and all dicarboxylate ligands are linear linkers, which differ in the spacer length and flexibility of the aliphatic dicarboxylate ligands. The spacer groups of the aforementioned aliphatic dicarboxylate ligands are –CHCH–, –(CH2)2–, –(CH2)3–, –(CH2)4–, respectively. Different spacer groups of dicarboxylate ligands can change the length of the ligand to various extents, which in turn varies the separation between the metal atoms. As shown in Table 2, in compound 1, rigid linear fum2− ligands link Cd(ii) atoms with the Cd⋯Cd separation of 9.44 Å. When the π-bond (–CHCH–) spacer is replaced by the flexible σ-bond (–CH2CH2–) spacer, the suc2− ligands in compound 3 display two kinds of conformations and bridge the Cd(ii) atoms with the Cd⋯Cd separation of 7.05 and 9.50 Å. Obviously, as the same four carbon aliphatic dicarboxylate ligands, the suc2− ligand is more flexible than the fum2− ligand. As a result, compound 3 features a complicated 2D self-penetrating network, whereas compound 1 exhibits a 2D → 2D interpenetrating framework comprised of simple 2D 44-sql structures. When extending the spacer length into –(CH2)3–, the more flexible glu2− ligand binds the Cd(ii) atoms with the Cd⋯Cd separation of 7.52 and 8.58 Å. The changeable backbone of glu2− is in favor of the occurrence of the helix. Consequently, five kinds of distinct helical chains coexist in the 3D self-penetrating network, which induce the chirality of compound 5. Compared with the glu2− ligand, the adp2− ligand has an additional –CH2– group. In compound 6, the adp2− ligands exhibit four types of conformations with the Cd⋯Cd separation of 7.52, 7.64, 9.80, and 10.54, respectively. The intricate conformations result in an unprecedented self-penetrating framework derived from the two-level hierarchical entanglement. As we know, in the dual ligand system, the conformational changes in one of the ligands are strongly affected by the other ligand. By changing the spacer length of dicarboxylate ligands, the dipyridyl ligands (bpp) present different conformations and dihedral angles between the pyridyl rings, which in turn affect the formation of the final structure (Table 2). From the above discussion, compounds 1, 3, 5, and 6 represent a classical example in which the spacer length and flexibility of auxiliary ligands show significant effects on modulating the formation of metal–organic frameworks with various types of entanglements.

On the other hand, a comparison of the two reactant systems (Cd–bpp–fum, Cd–bpp–suc) demonstrates that the reactant molar ratio remarkably affects the resulting structures. Each system is prepared by the same metal centers and identical linkers under a uniform hydrothermal procedure except using different reactant ratios. For a Cd–bpp–fum system, when the metal–ligand ratios are changed from 1 : 1 : 1 to 1 : 2 : 1, two different entangled frameworks ranging from the 2-fold 2D → 2D interpenetrating sheet (1) to the 2D → 3D polypseudo-threading framework (2) are obtained. The structural difference caused by the reactant ratio can also be supported by compounds 3 and 4. Compound 3 was obtained in a Cd–bpp–suc molar ratio of 1 : 1 : 1, which features a complicated 2D self-penetrating framework consisting of (1D)n + 2D → 2D polythreading aggregation. With the change of the Cd–bpp–suc ratio into 1 : 2 : 1, an unusual 2D self-threading motif, 4, was isolated. Clearly, the different reactant ratios led to the structural discrepancy.

Powder X-ray diffraction and thermal properties

The powder X-ray diffraction patterns for compounds 1–6 are presented in Fig. S1 (ESI†). The diffraction peaks of both simulated and experimental patterns are in agreement with each other, demonstrating the phase purities of 1–6. The difference in the intensity of some diffraction peaks may be due to the preferred orientation of the crystalline powder samples.

Thermogravimetric (TG) experiments were performed on compounds 1–6 in the temperature range of 30–800 °C (Fig. S2 in the ESI†). For compound 1, a gradual weight loss in the range of 90 to 150 °C is attributed to the release of coordinated water molecules and lattice water molecules (obsd 7.51%, calcd 7.81%). Decomposition of the anhydrous residue was observed at 245 °C and above 320 °C. The weight loss corresponds to the loss of bpp ligands (obsd 43.23%, calcd 42.97%). Upon further heating, the coordinated ligands were lost until the residue was CdO (obsd 26.84%, calcd 27.88%). The TG curve of compound 2 reveals a steady weight loss between room temperature and 100 °C corresponding to the departure of lattice water molecules (obsd 12.46%, calcd 12.62%), and then a plateau region follows until 160 °C. The further weight losses represent the decomposition of the framework. A total loss of 11.17% is observed for 3 in the temperature range of 30–120 °C, which can be attributed to the loss of lattice water molecules (calcd 10.78%), and the anhydrous compound begins to decompose at 230 °C forming an unidentified product. Compound 4 first loses lattice water in the 30–100 °C region (obsd 4.53%, calcd 4.14%), and the second step in the temperature range of 200–300 °C corresponds to the loss of the bpp ligand (obsd 57.88%, calcd 60.74%). The remaining weight of 18.61% is consistent with the deposition of CdO (calcd 19.69%). For 5, the loss of uncoordinated water molecules (obsd 13.72%, calcd 14.29%) is observed before 75 °C. The abrupt weight loss corresponding to the release of organic ligands starts at 150 °C and leads to the formation of CdO as the residue (obsd 21.15%, calcd 20.39%). For 6, the mass remained largely stable until 90 °C, at which point a series of mass losses ensued. The weight loss of coordinated water molecules took place in the range of 90–140 °C (obsd 3.56%, calcd 3.81%), and the anhydrous component began to decompose at 210 °C.

Photoluminescence properties

Recently, coordination polymers based on d10 transition metal ions have received more and more attention owing to their interesting luminescent properties. Therefore, the luminescent properties of these six compounds were investigated at room temperature in the solid state. As shown in Fig. 12, upon excitation at 360 nm, these six Cd(ii) compounds are luminescent with the emission bands at 458 nm for 1, 437 and 506 nm for 2, 430 nm for 3 and 4, 416 nm for 5, and 408 nm for 6, respectively. In order to understand the nature of these emission bands for these six Cd(ii) compounds, the luminescent properties of the free aliphatic dicarboxylate and bpp ligands were also studied under the same conditions. Notably, the aliphatic dicarboxylate ligands are nonfluorescent in the visible light range, and the bpp ligand is luminescent with the maxima emission at 468 nm (λex = 360 nm). According to the literature, the Cd(ii) ion is difficult to oxidize or reduce because of the d10 configuration. As a result, the luminescent emission bands of 1, 3, 4, 5 and 6 can be tentatively assigned to intraligand charge transfer, the lower energy emission band (437 nm) of 2 can be assigned to intraligand charge transfer and the higher energy emission band (506 nm) of 2 can be assigned to ligand-to-metal charge transfer, as reported for other Cd(ii) coordination polymers constructed from mixed N-donor and O-donor ligands.19

Conclusions

Six new entangled MOFs with interesting architectures have been successfully synthesized by introduction of different aliphatic dicarboxylates into a Cd–bpp system. These compounds display various entangled structural motifs, including the 2D → 2D interpenetrating sheet (1), 2D → 3D polythreading array (2), 2D self-penetrating net (3), 2D self-threading net (4), and 3D self-penetrating nets (5 and 6). The structural differences of the compounds demonstrate that the variations of the spacer length and flexibility of auxiliary ligands and reactant ratios are critical to the assemblies of MOFs in some particular systems. Additionally, the bpp ligands exhibit a versatile conformation, which will be responsible for structural diversity of the resulting coordination polymers. Thus, the present work not only shows an aesthetic diversity of coordinative network chemistry, but can also represent a fine example for constructing functional MOFs by the rational choice of organic ligands with specific functional groups and geometries.

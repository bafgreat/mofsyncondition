Mixed-ligand coordination polymers constructed from flexible 2,2′-biphenyldicarboxylate and rigid isomeric bipyridines

Author links open overlay panel
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.poly.2009.07.043Get rights and content

Abstract

Six coordination polymers have been synthesized and characterized based on dpa and isomeric bipyridine mixed ligands with various metal ions. Complex 1 exhibits a two-dimensional layer structure. Complex 2 is isomorphic to complex 1, with only small differences in bond lengths and bond angles. Complex 3 shows a one-dimensional chain structure composed of interesting pentanuclear Co building units. Complex 4 possesses a two-dimensional (4, 4) layer network, which crystallizes in a homochiral space group. During the synthesis process of complex 4, the dpa ligands decompose and form 2-bpa ligands, which connect with Ni atoms and produce the two-dimensional complex 5. Complex 6 exhibits a three-dimensional supramolecular structural feature constructed from discrete building units with π–π interactions. Furthermore, the magnetic properties of complex 2 are studied.

Graphical abstract

In this article, we describe the synthesis of six new coordination polymers, among these complexes, complex 6 exhibits an interesting supramolecular network.

1. Introduction

Over the past few decades, coordination polymers have attracted considerable attention because of their interesting topologies and crystal packing modes, alongside their potential applications in many areas including gas storage, catalysis, magnetism, optics, as well as conductivity [1], [2], [3], [4], [5], [6], [7]. Studies in this field have focused on the design and construction of novel coordination polymers and the relationships between their structures and properties [8], [9]. Recently, many studies have been carried out based on flexible ligands to build coordination polymers [10], [11], [12]. The connection of these kinds of ligands with the appropriate metal ions can lead to a lot of interesting structures [13], [14], [15]. The biphenyldicarboxylate ligands, bearing structural flexibility and conformation freedom, are a good choice of flexible ligand [16], [17]. Previous investigations indicate that 2,2′-biphenyldicarboxylate (dpa) has a strong capability of affording novel coordination polymers for several reasons [18], [19]: (a) it has four potential donor carboxylate oxygen atoms which can coordinate to metal ions with different connection modes [20], [21], (b) the skew coordination orientation of the carboxylate groups, the hindrance between the 2,2′-positioned carboxylate groups, and the non-linear flexibility around the two benzene groups make it quite a versatile ligand for constructing novel structures [22], [23], and (c) it displays strong reactivity with d and f block metal ions [24], [25].

Recently, studies have revealed that coordination polymers constructed from mixed ligands of carboxylate groups and pyridyl groups not only are more adjustable through changing one of the above two organic ligands but also incorporate interesting properties of the different functional groups [26], [27], [28]. Although many coordination polymers have been synthesized from the dpa ligand, complexes constructed from dpa and nitrogen-containing ligands are still largely unexplored [29], [30], [31], [32], [33]. We can conclude that the coordination polymers constructed from these two kinds of ligands may exhibit interesting structural and physical properties. To synthesize new coordination polymers based on dpa and isomeric bipyridine mixed ligands, as well as to enrich the coordination chemistry of the dpa ligand, herein we introduce two types of isomeric bipyridine ligands: 4,4′-bipy and 2,2′-bipy to the metal-dpa system. Interestingly, six new complexes with various structural features are synthesized under similar reaction conditions: [Fe(dpa)(4,4′-bipy)]n (1), [Co(dpa)(4,4′-bipy)]n (2), {[Co4(dpa)2(CH3COO)3(OH)(H2O)2]·3H2O}n (3), [Ni2(dpa)2(4,4′-bipy)2(H2O)]n (4), [Ni2(2-bpa)4(4,4′-bipy)2]n (5) and [Cd2(dpa)2(2,2′-bipy)2(H2O)2]n (6) (dpa = 1,1′-biphenyl-2,2′-dicarboxylate acid; 2-bpa = 1,1-biphenyl-2-carboxylate acid; 4,4′-bipy = 4,4′-bipyridine; 2,2′-bipy = 2,2′-bipyridine).

2. Experimental

2.1. Materials and methods

All chemicals purchased were reagent grade and used without further purification. Elemental analyses (C, N and H) were performed on a Perkin–Elmer 2400 CHN elemental analyzer. FT/IR spectra were recorded in the range 4000–400 cm−1 on an Alpha Centaut FTIR spectrophotometer using a KBr pellet. TG analyses were performed on a Perkin–Elmer TGA7 instrument in flowing N2 with a heating rate of 10 °C min−1. Variable temperature magnetic susceptibility was measured at 1000 Oe from 1.99 to 300 K (SQUID Quantum Design).

2.2. Synthesis of the complexes

2.2.1. Synthesis of 1

A mixture of FeCl2·4H2O (0.080 g, 0.40 mmol), dpa (0.192 g, 0.80 mmol), 4,4′-bipy (0.125 g, 0.80 mmol) and 8 ml H2O was stirred for 30 min, and then the pH value was adjusted to 5 with 1 M NaOH. After stirring for another 30 min, the mixture was transferred to a 23 ml Teflon-lined stainless steel bomb and kept at 170 °C under autogenously pressure for 4 days. The reaction system was cooled to room temperature over 24 h. A large amount of green block crystals of 1 were obtained. Yield: 63% (based on Fe). Anal. Calc. for C24H16N2O4Fe: C, 63.74; H, 3.57; N, 6.19. Found: C, 63.69; H, 3.41; N, 6.33%. The mixture was stirred for 25 min, and the pH value was then adjusted to 5 with 1 M NaOH. After this, the mixture was transferred to a 23 mL Teflon-lined stainless steel bomb and it was kept at 165 °C under autogenously pressure for 4 days. 2.2.4. Synthesis of 6

Complex 6 was prepared from a mixture of Cd(OAc)2·2H2O (0.107 g, 0.40 mmol), dpa (0.192 g, 0.80 mmol) and 2,2′-bipy (0.125 g, 0.80 mmol) in 8 ml H2O. The mixture was stirred for 15 min and sealed in a 23 mL Teflon-lined stainless steel bomb. The bomb was kept at 170 °C under autogenously pressure for 5 days. The reaction system was then cooled to room temperature over 24 h, and yellow block crystals of 6 were obtained. Yield: 71% (based on Cd). Anal. Calc. for C48H36N4O10Cd2: C, 54.67; H, 3.44; N, 5.32. 2.3. X-ray crystallography study

Crystallographic data for 1 to 6 were all collected at 298 K with a Bruker AXS SMART APEX II CCD diffractometer. The structures were solved by the direct method and refined by the full-matrix least-squares method on F2 using the shelxtl 97 crystallographic software package [34], [35]. Carbon-bound hydrogen atoms were placed in calculated positions, with C–H = 0.93 Å, and treated as riding atoms in the final cycles of refinement. Oxygen-bound hydrogen atoms were located in the difference Fourier maps, refined with isotropic temperature factors and kept in that position. 3. Result and discussion

3.1. Structural description

3.1.1. [Fe(dpa)(4,4′-bipy)]n (1) and [Co(dpa)(4,4′-bipy)]n (2)

Complex 1 is composed of a two-dimensional layer of Fe(dpa)(4,4′-bipy). The asymmetric unit of complex 1 consists of one crystallographic independent Fe atom, as shown in Fig. 1a. Fe(1) adopts a distorted octahedral coordination mode and connects with four oxygen atoms from three dpa ligands and two nitrogen atoms from two 4,4′-bipy ligands. The Fe–O distances range from 2.094–2.129 Å, the O–Fe–O bond angles are in the range 87.09 to 176.58°, and the average Fe–N bond distance is 2.260 Å. In this complex, the dpa ligand adopts only one kind of coordination mode, two carboxylate groups of the dpa ligand are deprotonated and both adopt bridging coordination modes as shown in Fig. S1. The carboxylate oxygen atoms of the dpa ligands connect the adjacent Fe atoms and form a one-dimensional chain-like structure. Furthermore, the 4,4′-bipy ligands link such neighboring chains and form a two-dimensional layer structure, as shown in Fig. 1b.

When FeCl2·4H2O is replaced by Co(OAc)2·4H2O, complex 2 is successfully synthesized. The crystal structure of complex 2 is isomorphic to complex 1, with little difference.

3.1.2. {[Co4(dpa)2(CH3COO)3(OH)(H2O)2]·3H2O}n (3)

To our interest, during the synthesis of complex 2, we obtained complex 3 accidentally. The single crystallographic analysis reveals that complex 3 shows a one-dimensional chain-like structure. There exist five crystallographic independent Co atoms in the fundamental unit, as shown in Fig. 2a. Co1 connects with six oxygen atoms, three from dpa ligands, two from OAc− ligands, and one from an OH− group. The Co–O bond distances range from 2.012 to 2.214 Å and the O–Co–O bond angles are in the range 79.00 to 171.05°. This results in distorted octahedral coordination geometry. Similar to Co1, Co3, Co4, and Co5 also adopt the same coordination manners, but with different bond lengths and bond angles. Different to the above four Co atoms, Co2 adopts a square pyramidal coordination mode and connects with five oxygen atoms. The two carboxylate groups in the dpa ligand both adopt bridging coordination modes, as shown in Fig. S2, the OAc− ligand adopts two kinds of coordination modes, as shown in Fig. S3, and with such connection modes, five crystallographic independent Co atoms are connected together and form an interesting pentanuclear Co building unit. To our knowledge, such a kind of building unit has seldom been synthesized in previous works. Adjacent pentanuclear Co clusters are further connected by dpa ligands and OAc− to form a one-dimensional chain-like structure, as shown in Fig. 2b.

3.1.3. [Ni2(dpa)2(4,4′-bipy)2(H2O)]n (4)

Single crystal X-ray diffraction analysis reveals that complex 4 is also a two-dimensional covalent network, derived from Ni2(dpa)2(4,4′-bipy)2(H2O). There exist three crystallographic independent Ni atoms in the fundamental unit, as shown in Fig. 3a. Ni(1) adopts an octahedral coordination mode and connects with four oxygen atoms from two different dpa ligands and two nitrogen atoms from two different 4,4′-bipy ligands. The Ni(1)–O bond distances are in the range 2.051–2.152 Å, and the O–Ni–O bond angles range from 78.28 to 176.33°. The average Ni–N bond distance is 2.105 Å. Ni(2) also adopts an octahedral coordination mode and connects with two oxygen atoms from dpa ligands, two nitrogen atoms from 4,4′-bipy ligands and the other two coordination sites are occupied by two water molecules. The Ni–O distances are in the range 2.040–2.076 Å, and the bond angles range from 88.42 to 175.18°. The average Ni–N distance is 2.099 Å. Ni(3) adopts the same coordination mode as Ni(2), but with different bond lengths and angles. In this complex, two carboxylate groups in dpa ligand adopt two kinds of coordination modes. One is bridging, and the other is monodentate, as shown in Fig. S4. The adjacent Ni atoms are linked by dpa ligands to form a one-dimensional chain-like structure. The 4,4′-bipy ligands link neighboring chains and form a two-dimensional layer structure, as shown in Fig. 3b.

To our interest, complex 4 crystallizes in a chiral space group. Although some complexes with helical structures have been synthesized from dpa ligands, to date homochiral complexes have never been synthesized from the dpa ligand.

3.1.4. [Ni2(2-bpa)4(4,4′-bipy)2]n (5)

During the synthesis of complex 4, we obtained complex 5. Like complex 4, complex 5 is also a two-dimensional layer network. There exist two crystallographic independent Ni atoms in this complex, as shown in Fig. 4a. Ni(1) adopts an octahedral coordination mode, connected with four oxygen atoms from four different carboxylate groups and two nitrogen atoms from different 4,4′-bipy ligands. The Ni(1)–O bond distances range from 2.063 to 2.086 Å and the O–Ni–O bond angles are in the range 88.35–172.67°. Ni(2) adopts the same coordination mode, but with different bond angles and bond lengths. The carboxylate group adopts a bridging coordination mode, as shown in Fig. S5, and connects the above two kinds of Ni atoms to form a one-dimensional chain-like structure. Such chains are further connected by 4,4′-bipy ligands and form a two-dimensional layer structure, as shown in Fig. 4b.

It is surprising that one of the two carboxylate groups of the dpa ligand decomposes during the reaction process and the dpa ligand turns into 1,1′-biphenyl-2-carboxylate acid (2-bpa). To our knowledge, this phenomenon has been reported in previous studies [36].

3.1.5. [Cd2(dpa)2(2,2′-bipy)2(H2O)2]n (6)

Single crystal X-ray diffraction study reveals that there exists only one crystallographic independent Cd atom in complex 6, which adopts an octahedral coordination mode. It connects with three oxygen atoms from two dpa ligands, two nitrogen atoms from 2,2′-bipy ligands, and the other coordination mode is occupied by a water molecule, as shown in Fig. 5a. The Cd(1)–O bond distances range from 2.192 to 2.583 Å and the O–Cd–O bond angles are in the range 53.38–123.79°. The carboxylate groups in the dpa ligand adopt two kinds of coordination modes, one is chelating and the other is monochelating, as shown in Fig. S6. Based on these two modes, an 18-membered ring is formed. Furthermore, the rings are extended to form an interesting three-dimensional supramolecular network through π–π interactions between the phenyl rings from the dpa ligands and 2,2′-bipy ligands. The distances of the adjacent phenyl rings range from 3.50 to 3.57 Å, as shown in Fig. 5b.

3.2. Thermal properties

The thermal stability properties have been determined on single crystalline samples in a nitrogen atmosphere by thermo gravimetric analysis (TGA). For complex 1, the TG curve shows that chemical decomposition starts at 369 °C and ends at 458 °C, with a weight loss of 87.69% (Calculated: 87.74%), which is equivalent to the loss of the dpa and 4,4′-bipy ligands. For complex 3, the TG curve exhibits two weight losses: the first (10.56%), from 90 to 170 °C, represents the loss of water molecules (Calculated: 10.70%), the second (65.82%), from 360 to 481 °C, represents the loss of organic ligands (Calculated: 65.68%). For complex 4, a weight loss of 1.82% is observed from 167 to 198 °C, equivalent to the loss of coordinated water molecules (Calculated: 1.90%). The second weight loss of 83.60%, from 408 to 506 °C, corresponds to the loss of the dpa and 4,4′-bipy ligands (Calculated: 83.70%). For complex 5, the weight loss of 90.38%, from 391 to 474 °C, corresponds to the loss of organic ligands (Calculated: 90.27%). For complex 6, the first weight loss (3.51%), from 134 to 166 °C, corresponds to the loss of water (Calculated: 3.42%); the second (75.29%), from 376 to 457 °C, represents the loss of organic ligands (Calculated: 75.17%).

3.3. Magnetic properties

For complex 2, the magnetic susceptibility data was studied in the temperature range 1.99 to 300 K, as shown in Fig. 6. The inverse susceptibility plot as a function of temperature is linear in the temperature range 50 to 300 K, closely following the Curie–Weiss law with C = 0.2904 emu K mol−1 and Θ = −6.30 K. This suggests typical antiferromagnetic interactions in above 50 K. The χmT versus T plot shows a value of 0.28 emu mol−1 K at 300 K, and continuously decreases on cooling to a value of 0.22 emu mol−1 K at 15 K. However, an abruptly increase at around 13 K was observed and χmT reaches 0.40 emu mol−1 K at 1.99 K. This indicates the occurrence of ferromagnetic behavior [37].

4. Conclusions

In this work, a series of coordination polymers generated from mixed-ligand systems of dpa and isomeric bipyridine mixed ligands have been successfully synthesized under hydrothermal conditions. It is anticipated that more coordination polymers containing the flexible dpa ligand with interesting structures as well as physical properties will be synthesized.

  • Preparation of a novel 1D Co(II) coordination polymer Co(dpa)(2,2″-bipy)(H<inf>2</inf>O)<inf>2</inf> catalyst for the allylic oxidation of cyclohexene

    2018, Catalysis Communications

    A Co(II) coordination polymer, Co(dpa)(2,2′-bipy)(H2O)2 (H2dpa = 2,2′-diphenic acid, 2,2′-bipy = 2,2′-bipyridine), was prepared by a hydrothermal method and characterized in detail. The dpa2 − ligand offered two carboxylate groups to construct two monodentate configurations that connected two Co(II) centers to form a 1D chain. Multiple 1D chains stacked on each other to generate a 3D framework via the weak CH ⋯ π interaction. Co(dpa)(2,2′-bipy)(H2O)2 exhibited an efficient catalytic performance for solvent-free allylic oxidation of cyclohexene by oxygen. The highest turn-over frequency (TOF) of 979 h− 1 was obtained at the molar ratio of Co(dpa)(2,2′-bipy)(H2O)2 to cyclohexene of 1:16000.

Construction of a novel Cd(II) coordination polymer based on a flexible tripodal carboxylic acid and bimid coligands

Abstract

A new coordination polymer was solvothermally prepared based on a semi-rigid multidentate carboxylic acid ligand and benzimidazole (bimid) ligand (H3L = 4,4′,4″-(((2,4,6-trimethylbenzene-1,3,5-triyl)tris(methylene))tris(oxy))tribenzoic acid): [Cd2(L) (bimid) (DMF)2]·(DMF)3·(H2O)2 (1). The complex 1 was characterized by single crystal X-ray diffraction, infrared spectroscopy, thermogravimetry measurement, elemental analysis and X-ray powder diffraction. Structure analysis reveals that complex 1 displays a rare doubly-interpenetrating (3, 5)-connected hms network with a point symbol {63}{69.8}. Moreover, the fluorescence and gas adsorption properties of the compound 1 were also explored.

In the past decade, coordination polymers or metal–organic frameworks (MOFs) as functional materials have garnered an attractive prospect of chemistry for their intriguing structural diversities and their potential applications in catalysis, chemical sensors, luminescent materials, magnetism etc [1], [2], [3], [4], [5], [6]. In order to obtain coordination polymers, it is the most typical synthetic strategy that the use of judiciously designed multidentate organic ligands with abundant coordination modes to metal ions or clusters. Among various organic ligands reported, multicarboxylic acids have been proven to be good candidate for ligand in the preparation of MOFs. Moreover, the combination of different ligands in constructing MOFs may offer greater adjustability of structural frameworks compared with using a single ligand [7], [8]. It's commonly known that interpenetrating networks are one important complicated structure in a large number of coordination polymers, and it is noteworthy that they have recently become a tremendous attractive prospect because of their applications in many fields, such as photochemical, photophysical properties, etc [9], [10], [11]. A reasonable strategy for exploring entangled systems or the interpenetrating networks is to use large and flexible multicarboxylate building blocks, since they can promote looped structural patterns by threading or penetrating with other components [12]. As a building block to construct MOFs, H3L has a –OCH2– linker between two phenyl rings, it has the capability of adopting desired structures according to different coordination modes during the self-assembly process. Up to now, a number of complexes with rich structural topologies have been successfully reported by using this ligand [13], [14], [15], [16], [17], [18], [19], [20].

In this work, we employed the H3L and bimid as ligands to construct a new Cd(II) coordination polymer, namely, [Cd2(L) (bimid) (DMF)2]·(DMF)3·(H2O)2 (1). The title complex 1 features a rare 2-fold interpenetrating (3, 5)-connected three dimensional (3D) framework structure with hms topology. Moreover, the luminescent and gas adsorption properties of 1 are also explored.

The experimental section is given in the ESI†. The detailed information for complex 1 is summarized in Tables S1 and S2†.

Compound 1 crystallizes in monoclinic system, P21/n space group. The asymmetric unit of 1 contains two independent Cd(II) cations, one L3 − anion ligand, one bimid ligand, two coordinated DMF molecules, and two free DMF molecules, As depicted in Fig. 1(a) (b), the neighbouring Cd(II) ions are both six-coordinated, but have the different coordination modes, the Cd1 ion is coordinated by two oxygen atoms from two DMF molecules at the equatorial positions and three oxygen atoms from three different L ligands, and one nitrogen atom from bimid ligand at the axial positions, to form a slightly distorted octahedral coordination geometry, with the Cd1-N3 distance of 2.172(3) Å and the Cd1-O distances of 2.216(3)–2.339(3) Å. Among which, two O atoms are derived from two different μ3-bridging carboxylate groups, one O atom is derived from one monodentate bridging L ligand, respectively. The Cd2 ion is in a distorted trigonal prism coordination geometry, coordinated by five oxygen atoms from three different L ligands, and one nitrogen atom from bimid ligand, with the Cd2–N4#4 distance of 2.178(3) Å and the Cd2–O distances of 2.228(3)–2.392(3) Å. Which are comparable with other Cd complex in the literature [21]. Of which, four O atoms are derived from two different μ3-bridging carboxylate groups, one O atom is derived from one monodentate bridging L ligand, respectively. The Cd1 and Cd2 ions are linked together by two carboxylic μ3–O and one carboxylic μ2–O to form a binuclear unit. The Cd1…Cd2 distances within the cluster are 3.574(5) Å. The crystallographically independent L3 − ligand adopt a cis, cis, trans-conformation, and between three benzoate rings and the center phenyl ring are nearly perpendicular. Each ligand bonds to six Cd ions in chelating and monodentate coordination modes, respectively. Each dinuclear cluster is connected by two bimid ligands, forming an infinite chain along the [010] and [001] directions (Fig. 2(a) (b)). Then L3 − linkers connect the chains, and adopt a distorted trigonal coordination geometry, with the angles between the three benzoate arms ranging from 100.636(32)° to 126.704(42)°.

Interestingly, the coordination interactions between the Cd(II) and L3 − ligand form a two-dimensional (2D) honeycomb-like (hcb) network with topological representation of 63 in the bc plane with Cd…Cd separations of 18.1867(12) Å (Cd1…Cd1) and 20.1503(9), 20.9797(9) Å (Cd1…Cd2). The adjacent layers are further linked by bimid ligands to form a three-dimensional (3D) framework, as illustrated in Figs. 2(b) and 3(a). It is notable that there are one-dimensional (1D) open channels with size about 18.1 × 16.6 Å2 along the a-axis direction, and the large window within this single framework are filled via mutual interpenetration of the other identical framework, generating to the formation of 2-fold interpenetrating 3D architecture Fig. 3(b). PLATON [22], [23] calculations show that the guest accessible void comprises 18.7% of the unit cell volume. The channels are occupied by DMF molecules.

Compound 1 is constructed from dinuclear Cd(II) clusters, bimid ligand and L3 − ligands as secondary building units (SBUs), As shown in Fig. 2(a), each triangular ligand L3 − coordinates to three Cd(II) units, each bimid ligand connects two dinuclear Cd(II) clusters, while each Cd(II) unit connects two bimid ligand and three ligands L3 −. This gives rise to a rare doubly-interpenetrating (3,5)-connected 3D network with a {63}{69.8} hms topology [24], [25]. One feature of this framework is a self-dual net [26].

To investigate the thermal stability of compound 1, thermogravimetric analysis (TGA) was carried out in N2 atomsphere. As shown in Fig. S2 (ESI†), the first weight loss occurred at 50–150 °C with loss of 8.3%, corresponding to the loss of the guest water and DMF molecules (obs. 7.9%). Then the weight loss occurred within the range of 150–330 °C compound lost ca. 21.98% of its weight, which could be attributed to the loss of two coordinated DMF molecules and one bimid ligand (obs. 20.99%). Above ca. 330 °C, compound 1 starts to lose its ligand L as a result of thermal decomposition with the final residue unknown.

Considering that coordination polymers with d10 metal centers and conjugated organic ligands usually exhibit excellent photoluminescent properties [27], [28], [29], the photoluminescent properties of complex 1 as well as the free ligands were investigated in the solid state at room temperature. As depicted in Fig.4, The photoluminescent spectra of the free H3L and bimid ligands show the main peaks at 363 and 362 nm, respectively, which are probably attributable to the π* → π transitions. Compound 1 exhibits a strong fluorescent emission peak with a maximum at 362 nm upon excitation at 300 nm. Obviously, it is similar to the emission bands of the free ligand, assigned to the intraligand fluorescent emissions [30].

The X-ray structure analyse showed that accessible volum is 18.7% for 1 of its respective total crystal volume without the guest molecules in the pores. We thus carried out gas adsorption measurements over the activated 1. The result showed that compound 1 could maintain permanent porosity. In order to remove guest solvent molecules in the channel, the freshly prepared sample of 1 was soaked in CH2Cl2, and every 8 h for a fresh CH2Cl2, then degassed at 100 °C under dynamic vacuum for 24 h.The framework thermal stability of compound 1 after activation was evaluated by X-ray powder diffraction. The results showed that main XRD peaks of the activated 1 coincided these of the as-synthesized and the simulated ones using PXRD studies, (Fig. S1 in the ESI†). The compound 1 can maintain its porous framework after activated, so we concentrated on the gas adsorption properties of 1. The sorption measurements, using N2, CO2, and CH4 as the adsorptive gases, were carried out on the activated compound 1. The activated framework 1 showed no adsorption of N2. The CO2 sorption isotherm of 1 measured at 273 K is represented in Fig. 5.

As shown in Fig. 5, the sorption amount of CO2 is 18 cm3 g− 1 for 1, which are higher than that of CH4 (3 cm3 g− 1) at 273 K and 760 Torr. It is worthwhile to note that compound 1 is potential material for the selective separation of CO2/CH4. The selective adsorption of CO2 over CH4 can be attributed to the smaller kinetic diameter of CO2 (3.3 Å) than CH4 (3.80 Å), and larger quadrupole moment of CO2 (− 1.4 × 10− 39 cm2) [31], [32]. Compared to other highly selective MOF adsorbents, the adsorption capacity and separation selectivity are relatively low [33], [34], [35], [36], [37].

In summary, a new Cd(II) coordination polymer with a rare doubly-interpenetrating (3,5)-connected hms network was solvothermally synthesized by using a flexible tripod carboxylic acid ligand(H3L) and rigid bidentate linker (bimid). Compound 1 exhibits strong fluorescent emission upon excitation at room temperature. The gas adsorption result indicated that compound 1 is potential material for the storage and separation of CO2 and CH4. The results show that H3L is a good candidate for construction of coordination polymers with intriguing structures and interesting properties.

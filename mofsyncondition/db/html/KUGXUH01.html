Bistable spin crossover (SCO) complexes of 3d4–3d7 transition metal ions exhibiting spin transition between the low-spin (LS) state and the high-spin (HS)1 state, under relatively minor external stimuli such as temperature, pressure, or light2 have potential applications in designing molecular switches, and display and memory devices.3 Generally, SCO compounds forming substantial intermolecular interactions, such as hydrogen bonding, π⋯π stacking or interchain interactions, show abrupt spin transitions with hysteresis loops, and are very useful in memory devices or chemosensors.4,5 It has been suggested theoretically and confirmed experimentally that cooperativity may be increased by designing polymeric structures in which the active sites are linked to each other via chemical bridges.6 Most SCO materials investigated so far involve Fe(ii)7,8 and Fe(iii)9 complexes and to a lesser extent compounds of Co(ii).10–14 The 3d7 Co(ii) ion experiences a change in spin between the 2E (t2g6 eg1) (S = 1/2) and 4T1 (t2g5 eg2) (S = 3/2) states involving the transfer of just one electron. Discrete mono-, di-, tri-10,11 and tetranuclear12 species as well as a few 1D polymeric10,13 Co(ii) SCO complexes have been reported so far.

In an earlier endeavour we reported13 the synthesis, structures, and thermal and magnetic behaviours of a 1D coordination polymer of Co(ii), [Co(enbzpy)(μ1,5-dca)]n(ClO4)n (1·ClO4), [enbzpy = N,N′-(bis(pyridine-2-yl)benzylidene)ethane-1,2-diamine; dca = dicyanamide] exhibiting a symmetry breaking abrupt reversible spin transition with a small hysteresis loop. To extend such work, herein we present the isolation, structures (room and low) and magnetic properties of another 1D Co(ii) coordination polymer, [Co(enbzpy)(μ1,5-dca)]n(PF6)n (1·PF6), which shows a gradual SCO behaviour reflecting a remarkable change in properties just by changing magnetically innocent counter anions. Single crystals of 1·PF6 suitable for X-ray diffraction measurements were isolated using self-assembly of a 1 : 1 : 1 : 1 molar ratio of CoCl2·6H2O, enbzpy, Na(dca) and KPF6 in MeOH at room temperature.†

X-ray structural analyses‡ of the new compound were successfully carried out at 298 K (1·PF6RT) and 100 K (1·PF6LT). 1·PF6 crystallizes‡ in a monoclinic system with the P21/c space group in contrast to our earlier report on 1·ClO4 where a symmetry breaking crystallographic phase transition from RT (monoclinic P21/c) to LT (triclinic P)13 was observed. Each Co(ii) ion in 1·PF6RT and 1·PF6LT has a tetragonally elongated [CoN6] octahedron consisting of four equatorial N atoms (N1, N2, N3, N4) of the Schiff base (enbzpy) and two axial nitrile N atoms (N5 and N7) of two different μ1,5-dca bridges that connect adjacent metal centres forming an infinite linear 1D chain (Fig. 1 and S1†) along the b-axis. The coordination environment of the Co(ii) ion in 1·ClO4RT is a distorted octahedron with a CoN6 chromophore, consisting of four equatorial N atoms of the Schiff base and two axial nitrile N atoms of two different μ1,5-dca bridges. On the other hand, the asymmetric unit in 1·ClO4LT contains four crystallographically different Co(ii) centres (Co1A, Co1B, Co2A and Co2B) with coordination environment of each metal(ii) centre analogous13 to the Co(ii) centre in 1·ClO4RT. The average equatorial Co–N(enbzpy) bond distances [2.072 Å in 1·PF6RT and 1.98 Å in 1·PF6LT, Table S1†] are markedly shorter than the axial Co–N(dca) bond length [2.13 Å in 1·PF6RT and 2.172 Å in 1·PF6LT, Table S1†]. The average Co–N bond distances are 2.092 Å for 1·PF6RT and 2.044 Å for 1·PF6LT reflecting a HS Co(ii)14 in 1·PF6RT and a LS Co(ii)14 in 1·PF6LT. Interestingly, the equatorial Co–N distances decrease while cooling in the 298–100 K region by ca. 0.092 Å on an average. Another very striking observation is that in contrast to our expectation, the axial Co–N distances increase to some extent (0.042 Å on an average) affecting the magnitude of average bond length contraction. In the perchlorate analogue 1·ClO4, the Co–N distances are in the 2.023–2.136 Å range with an average value of dav(Co–N) 2.08 Å at RT, which is characteristic14 of a Co(ii) HS system. In contrast, the four crystallographically different Co(ii) centres (Co1A, Co1B, Co2A and Co2B) have average Co–N distances dav(Co1A–N) 2.108 Å, dav(Co2A–N) 2.015 Å, dav(Co1B–N) 2.111 Å and dav(Co2B–N) 2.017 Å, which strongly suggest that Co1A and Co1B are in the HS state, whereas Co2A and Co2B, are in the LS state. The μ1,5-dca bridge (N5–N6–N7) forms an angle of 138.9° in 1·PF6RT and of 130.74° (Table S1†) in 1·PF6LT. The intra-chain Co⋯Co separations in 1·PF6RT and 1·PF6LT through the μ1,5-dca bridge are 8.699 Å and 8.718 Å, respectively. Strikingly, one intra-chain Co⋯Co separation (8.57 Å) through the μ1,5-dca bridge in 1·ClO4RT arises from the presence of only one type HS Co(ii) centre whereas four intra-chain separations (8.673 Å, 8.573 Å, 8.639 Å and 8.581 Å) in 1·ClO4LT through the μ1,5-dca bridge are seen due to the presence of two HS and two LS Co(ii) centres.

In the crystal packing, one hydrogen atom (H4) of a pyridine ring of enbzpy and a central N6 atom of the dca bridge are connected through C–H⋯N hydrogen bonds (Table S2†) to associate two linear 1D chains (Fig. S2†) in 1·PF6RT. Strikingly, this occurs only between two 1D chains and does not extend further via C–H⋯N hydrogen bonds. These chains then propagate through C–H⋯π interactions involving H8 and H21 atoms of the phenyl ring and π-cloud of the neighbouring pyridine rings [Cg(4) and Cg(5), Table S2†] of the two closest enbzpy ligands leading to a 2D sheet structure (Fig. S3†). Such 2D sheets in turn afford a 3D network structure (Fig. S4†) through π⋯π interactions involving the phenyl rings [Cg(6)–Cg(6) and Cg(7)–Cg(7), Table S3†] of two enbzpy units in 1·PF6RT. The hexafluorophosphate counter anion does not involve in any supramolecular contact in 1·PF6RT. On the other hand in 1·PF6LT, the C–H⋯N hydrogen bonds (Table S4†) involving H2 and H23 atoms of different phenyl rings and N6 and N5 atoms of the dca bridge form a 2D sheet structure (Fig. S5†). C–H⋯π interactions (Table S4†) strengthen this 2D sheet. Interestingly, in 1·PF6LT the counter ion plays an important role in joining the 2D sheets via C–H⋯F hydrogen bonds (C1–H55⋯F3, Table S4†) affording a 3D network structure (Fig. S7†); moreover, π⋯π stacking between two phenyl rings [Cg(7)–Cg(7) and Cg(8)–Cg(8), Table S5†] of enbzpy strengthens the 3D network. Analysing the superstructures reveals that the nature of non-covalent interactions at RT in both 1·ClO4 and 1·PF6 are almost similar;13 however, at LT the degrees of hydrogen bondings as well as the C–H⋯π interactions decrease considerably in 1·PF6 compared13 to that in 1·ClO4. In the perchlorate analogue 1·ClO4, the number of weak interactions substantially increases on going from RT to LT, whereas in the present hexafluorophosphate compound, 1·PF6, the weak interactions at both the temperatures remain almost invariant. It is worth mentioning that the anion⋯π interaction involving the O atom of ClO4− and π-cloud of the dca unit, which is most likely responsible for the small hysteresis loop13 in 1·ClO4, is absent in 1·PF6.

The magnetic susceptibilities for 1·PF6 were determined over the temperature range 2–300 K applying a constant field of 0.5 T (Fig. 2). The room temperature χMT value of 2.67 cm3 mol−1 K is greater than that expected for a d7 mononuclear HS Co(ii) (S = 3/2, χMT = 1.87 cm3 mol−1 K) presumably due to a large first-order orbital angular momentum contribution to the magnetic moment. On cooling, χMT decreases gradually until it reaches a low temperature, approximately at 50 K to attain an almost constant value of 0.65 cm3 mol−1 K which may be reasonably ascribed to one Co(ii) LS atom (S = 1/2, χMT = 0.37 cm3 mol−1 K). It is interesting and important to note that in contrast to abrupt symmetry breaking SCO behaviour (black line, Fig. 2) of the perchlorate analogue (1·ClO4) reported13 earlier, a very gradual (blue line, Fig. 2) spin transition occurs in 1·PF6. The value of χMT below 190 K with the abrupt drop in 1·ClO4 can be reasonably ascribed to two Co(ii) HS centres and two Co(ii) LS centres (expected = 1.4 cm3 mol−1 K), which is in accordance with structural parameters.13 Such behaviour suggests an efficient cooperative effect among adjacent Co(ii) centres along the 1D supramolecular chains and between the chains through interchain interactions as well. The behaviour in 1·PF6 is in consonance with a non-efficient cooperative effect among the spin-active Co(ii) centres of 1D coordination polymers in the broader array of supramolecular architectures in 1·PF6 compared to that of 1·ClO4 in which the anion⋯π interaction13 plays an important role in its characteristic SCO behaviour.

In summary, the 1D coordination polymer 1·PF6 shows a gradual spin transition primarily due to the presence of the non-coordinating bulky counter anion PF6− as compared to the smaller ClO4−, with effective diminutions of the cooperative interactions among the Co(ii) centres in the crystal lattice. This reveals that magnetic innocent counter anions play an effective role in SCO behaviour in the polycationic coordination polymer [Co(enbzpy)(μ1,5-dca)]nn+. Currently, the exhaustive investigation on the structures and SCO properties of [Co(enbzpy)(μ1,5-dca)]nn+ with relatively small counter anions (CF3SO3−, NO3−, BF4−etc.) is underway.

Syntheses, structures, and photoluminescence of two Cd(II) coordination polymers constructed from analogue (pyridyl)imidazole derivative and polycarboxylate acid

Author links open overlay panel
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.inoche.2009.07.013Get rights and content

Abstract

Two novel Cd(II) coordination frameworks based on a semirigid asymmetric ligand L and aromatic multi-carboxylate ligands, {[Cd2L(btc)(H2O)2]·5H2O}n (1) and [Cd2L2(bdc)]n (2) (L = (4-((2-(pyridine-2-yl)-1H-imidazol-1-yl)methyl)benzoic acid, H3btc = 1,3,5-benzenetricarboxylic acid, H2bdc = 1,3-benzenedicarboxylic acid), have been hydrothermally synthesized, and structurally characterized by elemental analysis, IR spectroscopy, single-crystal X-ray crystallography. Due to various coordination modes and conformations of the semirigid asymmetric ligand, and carboxylate containing co-ligands, therefore, the two complexes exhibit structural and dimensional diversity. Complex 1 exhibits a novel 2D (3, 5)-connected network structure with (3·52)(32·53·64·7) topology. Complex 2 exhibits a novel 3D CdSO4 topology, which is formed from different dinuclear cadmium units. In addition, the photoluminescence properties of the free ligand L, and complexes 1 and 2 were studied in the solid state at room temperature.

The design and synthesis of metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) constructed from metal salts and bridging ligands have attracted great attention in recent years, not only because of their intriguing variety of architectures and topologies [1], such as rectangular grids, herringbones, boxes, ladders, brick walls, rings, diamondoids, and honeycombs, but also because of their potential applications [2] in ion-exchange, nonlinear optics, molecular sieves, gas storage, catalysis, magnetism, and molecular sensing. Consequently, a variety of MOFs with interesting compositions and topologies have been prepared through taking some certain factors into account, such as the coordination nature of the metal ion and the shape, functionality, flexibility, symmetry of organic ligand and so on [3], [4]. From the viewpoint of crystal engineering, the most effective approach may be the appropriate choice of the well-designed organic bridging ligands containing modifiable backbones and connectivity information, together with the metal centers with various coordination preferences [5], [6], [7], [8]. Multidentate N-donor ligands have been investigated extensively as organic spacers in the construction of MOFs. For example, the use of 4,4′-bipyridine [9], and its analogues [10], as neutral N-donor ligands, are excellent organic ligands towards the syntheses of extended multidimensional coordination polymers due to their simple bridging fashion and strong coordination ability to transition metal ions. Moreover, it is well-known that carboxylate ligands play important roles to construct novel MOFs in coordination chemistry and can adopt various binding modes such as terminal monodentate, chelating to one metal center, bridging bidentate in a syn–syn, syn–anti and anti–anti configuration to two metal centers, and bridging tridentate to two metal centers [11].

According to the above considerations, we have recently designed and synthesized a novel ligand, namely, 4-((2-(pyridine-2-yl)-1H-imidazol-1-yl)methyl)benzoic acid (L), as shown in Fig. 1. From the structural point of view, it should be pointed out that (1) this ligand possesses a certain flexibility owing to the presence of a –CH2– spacer between the pyridine ring and benzene ring, and rigid imidazole ring and pyridine ring of the ligand are connected by a rotatable C–C bond, the rigid/flexible nature of the ligand backbone may produce unexpected structure; (2) the L ligand containing both N- and O-donor sites remarkable coordination abilities may exhibit more varied coordinating patterns in the construction of coordination frameworks, as shown in Fig. 1.

In addition, 1D, 2D, or 3D Cd(II) coordination complexes have received much attention because of their potential applications in catalysis, luminescent materials, NLO materials, phase transformation, and host–guest chemistry [12]. Here, we will report the synthesis, crystal structures of two novel Cd(II) coordination polymers with diverse network structures by carefully selecting auxiliary ligands (Supporting Information Fig. S1): {[Cd2L(btc)(H2O)2]·5H2O}n (1) and [Cd2L2(bdc)]n (2) In addition, their luminescent properties were also investigated.

Complex 1, which is synthesized through hydrothermal reaction [14], exhibits an interesting 2D network structure. The single-crystal X-ray analysis [15] shows that the asymmetrical unit of complex 1 contains two kinds of Cd(II) cations, one L ligand, one btc3− ligand and two coordinated water molecules (Fig. 2a). Cd1 atom is in a distorted octahedral coordination environment, in which four carboxylic oxygen atoms (Cd1–O = 2.186(3)–2.422(4) Å) from two different btc3− ligands and one L ligand, and two nitrogen atoms (Cd1–N = 2.245(4)–2.410(4) Å) from one chelate L ligand; whereas Cd2 atom shows a seven-coordinated geometry, being ligated by five carboxylic oxygen atoms (Cd2–O = 2.291(4)–2.541(3) Å) from two distinct btc3− ligands and one L ligand, and two oxygen atoms (Cd2–O = 2.268(4)–2.343 (5) Å) from two coordinated water molecules. The Cd–O/Cd–N bond distances are in the normal range of those observed in other Cd(II) complexes [16]. The L ligand binds to three cadmium ions through two carboxylic oxygen atoms in tridentate-bridging fashion and two nitrogen atoms in chelate coordination fashion (Supporting Information Fig. S2a). One carboxylate group of the btc3− ligand adopts bidentate coordination fashion to link Cd1 and Cd2, and another two carboxylate groups both adopt chelate-bridging modes to link Cd1 and Cd2 (Supporting Information Fig. S3a). The neighboring Cd(II) centers are interlinked by one carboxylate group of btc3− in bidentate and the carboxylate group of L in tridentate-bridging fashion to generate a dinuclear cluster with the Cd⋯Cd distance of 4.021 Å (Supporting Information Fig. S4). As a result, the L and btc3− ligands connect with the adjacent dinuclear metal cluster to form a 2D framework (Fig. 2b). From the topological point of view, when we take the dinuclear cadmium cluster as a single node and the btc3− ligand is regarded as a three-connected node, therefore, the network can be simplified to a 2D (3, 5)-connected network with (3·52)(32·53·64·7) topology (Fig. 2c). To the best of our knowledge, MOFs possessing (3, 5)-connected net are relatively rare, to date, few examples have been reported in literatures [17].

When H2bdc ligands were replaced by H3btc ligands, and react with cadmium ions and L ligands, complex 2 was obtained [14]. X-ray crystallographic analysis [15] reveals that complex 2 exhibits an interesting 3D metal-organic framework. The fundamental unit is shown in Fig. 3a. There are two crystallographically unique Cd(II) atoms in the asymmetric unit, both displaying distorted octahedral coordination geometry, which are surrounded by four carboxylic oxygen atoms (Cd1–O = 2.218(7)–2.551(7) and Cd2–O = 2.210(7)–2.567(8) Å) from two different L ligands and one bdc2− ligand, and two nitrogen atoms (Cd1–N = 2.241(8)–2.429(8) and Cd2–N = 2.255(8)–2.466(8) Å) from one L ligand, respectively. The bond lengths around Cd1 are somewhat different from those around Cd2. Compared with complex 1, the L ligand adopts another coordination mode in 2. That is, the L ligand binds to two cadmium ions through one carboxylic oxygen atom in monodentate-bridging fashion and two nitrogen atoms in chelate coordination fashion (Supporting Information Fig. S2b). All carboxylate groups of bdc2− in chelate-bridging mode to connect with two Cd atoms (Supporting Information Fig. S3b). From the topological point of view, each dinuclear Cd1/Cd2 cluster linking four L ligands and two bdc2− ligands forms a four-connected node, green node is dinuclear Cd1 cluster, pink node is dinuclear Cd2 cluster, in which the distance of Cd1⋯Cd1 is 4.218 Å and the distance of Cd2⋯Cd2 is 4.144 Å, respectively (Fig. 3b). Each L/bdc2− ligand acts as a two-connected linker, respectively, so the whole framework can be described as a four-connected network with the Schläli symbol of (63·83)(65·8).

It is obvious from the above descriptions that the auxiliary ligand (1,3,5-benzenetricarboxylic acid, 1,3-benzenedicarboxylic acid) has significant effects on the final structures of the resulting complexes. Through varying the auxiliary ligands under similar synthesis conditions, two related Cd(II) complexes were successfully isolated and they exhibit obvious differences in their architectures. As demonstrated by a comparison of complexes 1 and 2, the second ligand causes the distinctness of the coordination modes of L ligands. The L ligands in complex 1 adopt tridentate–chelate coordination fashions, while the L ligands adopt monodentate–chelate coordination fashions in complex 2. Simultaneously, the influence of auxiliary aromatic acid ligands on the complex structures is also revealed by the structural differences of two complexes. Different carboxylate groups of the auxiliary ligands and different angles between two carboxylate groups result in the auxiliary ligands produce various coordination fashions. Carboxylate groups of the auxiliary ligands in complex 1 adopt bidentate and chelate-bridging coordination fashion to link cadmium ions, whereas all the carboxylate groups of auxiliary ligand in chelate-bridging mode connect with cadmium ions in complex 2. The structural differences between 1 and 2 can be attributed to the differences in the coordination modes in their organic-acid ligands.

The thermal stability of complexes 1 and 2 have been studied using thermal gravimetric analysis (TGA) from room temperature to 800 °C under flowing nitrogen at a heating rate of 10 °C/min. TGA curve of 1 exhibits two significant weight losses (Supporting Information Fig. S5a). The first step varies from room temperature to 98 °C, which corresponds to the release of water molecules with the weight loss of 10.29% that is close to the calculated value (10.76%). The second step covers from 365 to 482 °C, which can be attributed to the combustion of the organic groups. TGA curve of 2 shows one main step of weight loss corresponding to the combustion of the organic groups (Supporting Information Fig. S5b).

In addition, taking into account of the excellent luminescent properties of d10 metal complexes, the luminescence of complexes 1 and 2 was investigated (Supporting Information Fig. S6). It can be observed that an emission occurs at 382 nm (λex = 350 nm) for 1 and 392 nm (λex = 344 nm) for 2, respectively. To understand the nature of emission band, the luminescent properties of free ligand L was analyzed. It was found that the intense emission could be observed for free ligand L at 404 nm (λex = 344 nm). As previously reported [18], solid-state benzene–dicarboxylate ligands (1,3,5-btc, 1,3-bdc), can also exhibit fluorescence at room temperature, and the emission bands of these ligands can be assigned to the π∗ → n transition. Fluorescent emission of benzene–dicarboxylate ligands resulting from the π∗ → n transition is very weak compared with that of the π∗ → π transition of the L ligand, so the two emissions of two compounds were not involved with contributions from the auxiliary ligands. Accordingly, the main emission bands of complexes 1 and 2 may be attributable to an intraligand transition π → π∗ [19] or a ligand-to-metal charge transfer (LMCT) transition [20].

In conclusion, two novel cadmium coordination polymers based on analogue (pyridyl)imidazole derivative and polycarboxylate acid have been successfully synthesized. Complex 1 possesses a rare (3, 5)-connected network structure, in which btc3− ligands act as three-connected nodes and the dinuclear cadmium SBUs act as five-connected nodes. While complex 2 exhibits a novel 3D CdSO4 topology, in which each dinuclear Cd1/Cd2 cluster acts as a four-connected node and each L/bdc2− ligand acts as a two-connected linker. The results revealed that the second ligand plays an important role in modulating the architecture of coordination complexes. In addition, complexes 1 and 2 showed fluorescence in the solid state at room temperature. Further work on the properties of this ligand and its metal compounds are still explored in our laboratory.

  • The versatile coordination chemistry of 1,3-benzenedicarboxylate in the last 20 years: An investigation from the coordination modes to spectroscopic insights

    2021, Polyhedron

    Aromatic carboxylates have been vastly applied as building blocks for the assembly of robust molecular-based materials owing to their potential application in various fields. In this family, 1,3-benzenedicarboxylate (1,3-bdc), which possesses a “V”-type configuration, has been distinguished. Over the past 20 years, the number of compounds based on 1,3-bdc rapidly expanded due to its multifaceted coordination chemistry and the possibility to generate materials with diversified applications. Therefore, the main purpose of this study is to explore the structural aspects, diversity, and the factors that influence coordination modes of 1,3-bdc by comparison of a series of structurally related compounds. In this sense, we highlight some aspects that can assist in a more rational design of coordination networks based on 1,3-bdc.

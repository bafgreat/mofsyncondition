The use of trivalent lanthanide cations for constructing lanthanide metal-organic frameworks (LMOFs) has been a rapidly developing area in recent years because of the interesting properties such as porosity, luminescence, magnetism, and catalytic activity due to their fâ€“f electronic transitions [1], [2]. In contrast to the fruitful production of MOFs with d-block transition-metal ions, the design and control of high-dimensional lanthanide-based frameworks is currently a formidable task owing to their high coordination numbers and flexible coordination environments [3]. Taking advantage of the strong affinity of lanthanide ions for oxygen, dicarboxylate ligands have been employed previously to synthesize a number of LMOFs with fascinating architecture [4], [5], [6]. Of these organic dicarboxylate ligands, however, 2,4â€²-biphenyldicarboxylic acid has received the least attention up to now [7].

In this work, 2,4â€²-biphenylcarboxylic acid (2,4â€²-H2bpdc) (Scheme 1) was utilized as a multifunctional bridging ligand according to the following considerations: (i) the multidentate 2,4â€²-H2bpdc is easy to bridge or chelate the Ln center to form 1D chains, 2D layers or 3D networks; (ii) it has two ionizable hydrogen atoms and can be successively deprotonated to generate the uninegative 2,4â€²-Hbpdcâˆ’ and dinegative 2,4â€²-bpdc2âˆ’ ions depending on the pH of the solution; as such, this ligand can provide two anions with different electrovalancies for charge-neutrality and to support various acidity-dependent coordination modes; therefore, H2bpdc has the ability to serve as a bridge between metal ions; (iii) rotation of the Câ€“C single bond between the two phenyl rings gives rise to the skew coordination orientation of the carboxylate groups which is favorable for the formation of the helical structure and high coordination numbers for lanthanide ions.

In this paper, we present the synthesis and crystal structure of the new 2D samarium(III) compound [Sm(2,4â€²-bpdc)(2,4â€²-Hbpdc)H2O]n (1) with single 2,4â€²-biphenyldicarboxylic acid ligand, which shows a two-dimensional layer-like structure with 44 topology and high thermal stability. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first example of the lanthanide coordination polymer containing single 2,4â€²-H2bpdc ligand.

In summary, a new 2D samarium-organic framework was successfully constructed from single 2,4â€²-biphenyldicarboxylic acid (2,4â€²-H2bpdc) under hydrothermal condition for the first time. The 2,4â€²-H2bpdc shows two new types of special coordination patterns in the compound. In addition, the luminescence property of the compound was also measured. Finally, it would be interesting to extend this work to other trivalent lanthanide ions, and our current research efforts are indeed in that direction.

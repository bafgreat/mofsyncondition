Solvent-induced assembly of two supramolecular isomers of MnII thiophenedicarboxylate coordination polymers

Author links open overlay panel
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.inoche.2009.06.007Get rights and content

Abstract

Two supramolecular isomers of MnII thiophenedicarboxylate coordination polymers, namely, [Mn2(tdc)2(dmf)2]n (1) and [Mn3(tdc)3(dmf)3]n (2) (H2tdc = 2,5-thiophenedicarboxylic acid, dmf = N,N-dimethylformamide), were synthesized with solvent-induced assembly method and the antiferromagnetic interactions between the MnII ions in their solid phase were measured.

Graphical abstract

The assembly of coordination polymers by design is one aim of crystal engineering because the certain bulk physical and chemical properties of the complexes are intimately related to their structures [1], [2], [3], [4], [5], [6]. Significant effort has been expended towards this goal, resulting in important developments in recent years [7], [8], [9], [10], [11], [12]. The deliberate design of such materials, however, presents considerable challenges. In particular, the final structure obtained can be influenced by many factors, including metal and ligand geometries, synthetic approaches (e.g. room temperature versus solvothermal reaction conditions), solvent effects, temptation by guests or counterions, and other weaker interactions [13], [14]. Different approaches can lead to supramolecular isomerism [15] which still represents a fundamental scientific challenge because there is presently very little understanding concerning on how to predict, direct or control these phenomena [15].

In this paper, we report the syntheses [16], crystal structures [17] and magnetic properties of two supramolecular isomers of MnII thiophenedicarboxylate coordination polymers, namely, [Mn2(tdc)2(dmf)2]n (1) and [Mn3(tdc)3(dmf)3]n (2) (H2tdc = 2,5-thiophenedicarboxylic acid, dmf = N,N-dimethylformamide). The two different structures are arrived at by the introduction of ethanol into the reaction mixture (despite it not appearing in the product) [16].

In the crystal structure of 1, there are two MnII cations and two tdc dianions in the asymmetric unit. Mn1 is coordinated to six oxygen atoms, five of which are from tdc, and one from DMF. In addition, there is a long and weak seventh coordination bond to a carboxylate oxygen (O8) bound to an adjoining metal (Mn2), with distance of 2.561 Å. Mn2 is also coordinated to six oxygen atoms, five from tdc and one from DMF (Fig. 1). The coordination modes of the two tdc dianions are different: one adopts a (κ1 − κ1)-(κ1 − κ1)-μ4 tetradentate mode; the other adopts a {(κ1 − μ2)-(κ1 − μ2)}-(κ1 − κ1)-μ5-tdc pentadentate mode. According to a search of the Cambridge Structural Database [18], the latter coordination mode of tdc has not been reported before.

In 1, four MnII cations are bridged by μ2 oxygen atoms from tdc to form a subunit containing two edge-sharing polyhedra in addition to two corner-sharing polyhedra (Fig. 2a and b). The subunits are further bridged by pairs of carboxyl groups to form a 1D rod, which is further connected to other chains by tdc, resulting in the formation of the 3D structure (Fig. 2c). The rods can be thought of as infinite rod-shaped secondary building units (SBUs) [19]. Each rod is directly connected to four neighbouring rods through the tdc ligands. A recent paper by Yaghi and O’Keeffe discussed possible infinite rod packings, and applied these to metal carboxylate structures. Under this nomenclature, the structure of 1 displays rod packing type 2 (pcu) [19].

In the crystal structure of 2, there are three MnII cations and three tdc dianions in the asymmetric unit (Fig. 3). The coordination environments of the three MnII cations are quite different from each other: Mn1 is five coordinated to three oxygen atoms from tdc and two oxygen atoms from DMF; Mn2 is coordinated to six oxygen atoms from tdc; Mn3 is coordinated to five oxygen atoms from tdc and one oxygen atom from DMF. The tdc dianions can be divided into two kinds: one adopts a (κ1 − κ1)-(κ1 − κ1)-μ4 tetradentate mode, the other two adopt (κ1 − κ1)-{(κ1 − μ2)-(κ1)}-μ4-tdctetradentate modes. The latter coordination mode of tdc is also reported for the first time in this work.

In 2, three MnII cations are bridged by μ2 oxygen atoms from tdc to form a subunit containing three corner-sharing polyhedra which is further bridged to the next subunit by a carboxyl to form a 1D rod (Fig. 4a and b). As seen in 1, the rods are then connected to other rods by tdc to form the 3D structure of 2 (Fig. 4c). Each rod is connected to six others, leading to a type 4 (hex) packing mode, using the nomenclature described above [19].

Although 1 and 2 are synthesized with the same reactants, with the only difference being the presence of ethanol in the synthesis of 2, the structures of 1 and 2 are distinctly different. X-ray powder diffraction measurements show that pure phases of 1 or 2 are obtained, respectively (S1). The structures of 1 and 2 are obviously determined by whether or not ethanol is present in the solvent, but the effect of the ethanol is difficult to discern as it is not included in the product. This consideration highlights the difficulties inherent in trying to deliberately and definitively design these materials.

The magnetic susceptibility of 1 was measured throughout the temperature range 2–300 K by using a SQUID magnetometer (S2). At 300 K, the magnetic moment value is 8.33 BM, which is similar to that expected for two uncoupled high spin MnII ions (8.36 BM). The μeff value continuously decreases upon cooling and reaches a value of 0.75 BM at 2 K, which is a characteristic of an antiferromagnetic interaction between the MnII ions [20].

The magnetic susceptibility of 2 was measured at the same condition as 1. At 300 K, the magnetic moment value is 12.69 BM, which is somewhat smaller than that expected for three uncoupled high spin MnII ions (13.125 BM). The μeff value continuously decreases upon cooling and reaches a value of 0.85 BM at 2 K, which is also characteristic of an antiferromagnetic interaction between the MnII ions [21].

In summary, we have successfully obtained two pure products of the two isomers of coordination polymers by solvent-tuned strategy, namely, [Mn2(tdc)2(dmf)2]n and [Mn3(tdc)3(dmf)3]n. Magnetic susceptibility measurements show antiferromagnetic interactions between the MnII ions.

  • Coordination steric effect of N,N-dimethylformamide, N,N-dimethylacetamide and N-methyl-2-pyrrolidone on the assembly of coordination polymers

    2010, Polyhedron

    Three coordination polymers 1, 2 and 3 have been synthesized in DMF (N,N-dimethylformamide), DMA (N,N-dimethylacetamide) and NMP (N-methyl-2-pyrrolidone), respectively. In 1, DMF solvent molecule coordinates to zinc ion as an ancillary ligand, and 1D chain structure is obtained. 2 and 3 are isostructural, in which solvent molecules, DMA and NMP, do not coordinate to zinc ions, and 1D double stranded chain structures are formed.

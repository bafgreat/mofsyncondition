The last decade witnessed great success in the research of molecule-based magnetic materials.1 Significant progress has been made not only in the fundamental research of magnetic interactions and magneto-structural correlations, but also in the research of functional molecule-based materials.2 The main emphasis on the principle of design is selecting suitable bridging ligands that can effectively mediate magnetic coupling between the local spin carriers. It is found that pseudohalogens, such as SCN−,3 CN−,4,5 and N3−,6,7 are widely used in assembling molecule-based magnets, because they can efficiently conduct magnetic couplings. As one of the most versatile short ligands, azide is used for the construction of enormous magnetic systems.8,9 Its rich bridging modes and ability to transfer different magnetic couplings lead to abundant magnetic behaviours.10

As another excellent magnetic mediator, formate, the smallest and simplest carboxylate, has been extensively employed in creating materials with interesting magnetic properties.11–14 And furthermore, formate is similar to azide in terms of coordination modes and magnetic transmitting, which can also be viewed as a pseudohalogen.15 However, isostructural azido/formato-bridged complexes have rarely been reported.16,17 In the past, we studied metal azide/formate complexes with N-heterocycle carboxylic acid as co-ligands.18,19 The structure of these metal azide/formate complexes is quite different, and isostructural azide/formate complexes with such co-ligands were not found. In this work, we reported two isomorphous azide/formate MnII complexes with pyrazine-2-carboxylate (L) as a co-ligand. In the structure, azide and formate take the same role in constructing the complexes, which form new screw chains to enhance the (10,3)-a pyrazine-2-carboxylate Mn(ii) framework and to give a β-SnF2 topology net finally.20 In 1, strong AF interactions were found between the MnII ions, while 2 was a canted antiferromagnet with a Néel temperature of TN = 38.5 K.

Single-crystal X-ray diffraction reveals that these two complexes are isostructural 3D coordination polymers.21 Complex 1 crystallizes in an orthorhombic space group P212121 with an asymmetric unit containing one MnII ion, one solvated methanol molecule, one azide anion, and one L ligand (Fig. 1a). The MnII ion lies in a general position with a distorted octahedral geometry constructed from two nitrogen atoms from two azide anions, and two oxygen atoms and two nitrogen atoms from L [Mn1–N1 = 2.177 (7) Å, Mn1–N3A = 2.177 (7) Å, Mn1–O2C = 2.131 (5) Å, Mn1–O1 = 2.196 (5) Å, Mn1–N5 = 2.344 (5) Å, and Mn1–N4B = 2.337 (4) Å]. The L ligand adopts a μ3 coordination mode to bridge Mn1, Mn1A, and Mn1F ions. The L ligand chelates to the Mn1 ion via the 1-position N5 atom and a carboxylate O1 atom, and coordinates to Mn1A and Mn1F via the 4-position N4 atom and a carboxylate O2 atom. The pyrazine rings and manganese network exhibit a (10,3)-a net with the L ligands and MnII ions as 3-connected nodes. In the b direction, the azide anions take a μ-1,3 bridging mode linking two MnII ions, which form helical chains (Fig. 2d). The azide bridging intermolecular Mn1–Nazido–Mn1B distance is 6.193 Å in 1. Thus, with the connection of the azide anions, the (10,3)-a net is enhanced. And a 3D network having a rare β-SnF2 topology can be obtained (Fig. 2c) with the methanol molecules mainly filling in the 1D channel of the structure (Fig. 2a).

2 has the same topology net with 1 but with the anti–anti formate bridging ligands replacing the azide ligands (Fig. 1b and 2b–d). The Mn1–OHCOO–Mn1B distance is 6.129 Å, which varies slightly compared with that of 1.

The magnetic susceptibility of 1 and 2 was collected under an external field of 2000 Oe in the temperature range of 2–300 K (Fig. 3). Upon cooling, the χmT values of 1 and 2 decreased smoothly, which suggested global antiferromagnetic (AF) interactions conducted by the L ligands and μ-1,3 azide or anti–anti formate anion bridges. The Curie–Weiss fit of the susceptibility data above 50 K gave the Curie constants C = 4.71 and 4.76 cm3 K mol−1 and Weiss constants θ = −47.63 and −5.76 K for complexes 1 and 2, respectively. The negative Weiss constants also suggested dominance of AF interactions between the metal ions. The Weiss constant in 1 was more negative than that of 2, indicating that the magnetic interaction conducted by azide was stronger than that of formate. At lower temperatures, the χmT value of 1 dropped quickly in an unremarkable fashion. This was the typical plot for a simple AF system without any other important features. For 2, the χmT value decreased continuously and reached the minimum, then rose sharply to the maximum, and after that it went down on further cooling. This is due to long range magnetic order trigged by spin canting in 2 at low temperatures.

The long rang order of 2 was confirmed by zero-field-cooled and field-cooled magnetization (ZCFM and FCM) (Fig. 4a) under an external field of 20 Oe, and the curves were deviated at about 45 K. The 3D structure nature made three-dimensional magnetic interactions, in which 3D long range magnetic ordering was found usually. To further probe the long-range ordering in 2, the ac magnetic susceptibility measurements were performed with different ac frequencies (Fig. 4b). The ac susceptibility measurements of 2 in an oscillating field of 3.5 Oe at 100 and 997 Hz were performed. The in and out phase ac susceptibility data showed frequency independent peaks at 38.5 K (inset Fig. 4b). This indicated long-range magnetic ordering in the 3D network of 2 under 38.5 K.

Furthermore, the plot of the reduced magnetization at 2 K M(H) indicates antiferromagnetic coupling clearly (Fig. S2†). The magnetization of 1 and 2 did not follow the Brillouin curves. The magnetization of 1 increased almost linear with the field and reached 0.8Nβ at 5 T per MnII unit, much less than the expected saturation value of 5Nβ for a MnII ion with S = 5/2 and g = 2, which was consistent with its observed strong AF behavior. While for 2, the magnetization increased with the applied field appearing as an inflexion at about 1.5 T, and then it increased linearly to 1.44Nβ at 50 kOe. This result indicated that spin canting and a canting angle of 9° can be estimated roughly on the basis of the equation θ = tan−1 (Mr/Ms),21,22 in which Mr was the intercept by the line fitting of the M vs. H plots in a high field range. The experimental values indicated that the AF coupling between Mn1 and Mn1B occurs through HCOO− and N3− bridging ligands, and μ-1,3 N3 mediates the stronger AF coupling than anti–anti HCOO−.

There are two mechanisms for spin-canting: (1) antisymmetric exchange (origin of DM interactions) and (2) single-ion anisotropy,23,24 which all needed non-symmetry relation of the nearest neighbour magnetic carrier.251 and 2 are crystallized in a chiral apace group; thus, it satisfied the symmetry requirement for spin canting. To deeply analyse the magnetism of 1 and 2, magnetic anisotropy (Fig. S3† and Fig. 5) and isotropic exchange coupling constants J were calculated (for details, see the ESI†). The calculated zero-field splitting parameters D(E) (cm−1) and g (gx, gy, gz) tensors of the lowest spin–orbit state of complexes 1 and 2 using NEVPT226 with Orca 4.027 were D(E) = 0.04 (0.01) cm−1, g = 2.0 for 1 and D(E) = −0.15 (−0.01) cm−1, g = 2.0 for 2 (listed in Table S3†). It is found that the calculated D value of 1 is positive, and that of 2 is negative. Thus, the single-ion MnII energy barrier ΔA of 1 is almost close to zero, but that of 2 has a small value of 0.9 cm−1. From Table S4,† the calculated isotropic MnII–MnII coupling constants Jα (α = 1, 2 and 3, J1, J2 and J3 conducted by azide/formate, pyrazine ring and syn,anti carboxylate, respectively) (Fig. S4†) using B3LYP proposed by Becke28,29 and Lee et al.30 are all negative. This suggests that all of the MnII–MnII couplings in 1 and 2 are antiferromagnetic. What's more, the antiferromagnetic coupling constant J1 (conducted by azide anions) in 1 is much stronger than that (conducted by formate) in 2. According to Barbara and co-workers’ theory,31 sharp domain walls will be present where ΔA > 1/3Δξ. For complex 1, it is not a spin-canted system due to the very weak single-ion MnII energy barrier ΔA and the larger MnII–MnII couplings. But for 2, although the energy barrier ΔA is only 0.9 cm−1, it is larger than all of 1/3Δξ (see Table S4†), and thus sharp domain walls will be present. Complex 2 can be approximately regarded as a spin-canted system.

In summary, two isostructural azido/formato-bridged 3D complexes were synthesized in solvothermal reactions. The former has normal AF coupling, while the latter displays an unexpected long range magnetic order originated from spin-canting. Calculation suggested that the magnetic anisotropy MnII ions in 1 and 2 and the magnetic interactions conducted by azide and formate are quite different, which are responsible for the distinct magnetism of the two complexes. Our results show that azide and formate could be used as equal bricks in constructing the structure, but in terms of magnetism, their behaviors are quite different.

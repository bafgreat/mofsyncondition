Synthesis, structures, and luminescence of lanthanide coordination polymers constructed from benzimidazole-5,6-dicarboxylate and oxalate ligands

Author links open overlay panel
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.inoche.2010.01.013Get rights and content

Abstract

Three novel coordination polymers [(Eu2(Hbidc)2(ox)2·(H2O)3] (1), [Tb2(Hbidc)2(ox)(H2O)2]·4H2O (2), [Er2(Hbidc)2(ox)] (3) have been synthesized with hydrothermal reaction (H3bidc = Benzimidazole-5,6-dicarboxylic acid, H2ox = Oxalic acid) and characterized by elemental analysis, IR and X-ray single crystal diffraction. 1, 2 and 3 have the same crystal system of triclinic, but they exhibit different kinds of metal-organic framework structures. The luminescent properties of 1 and 2 were studied at room temperature.

In recent years, metal-organic frameworks (MOF) based on supramolecular chemistry and crystal engineering have attracted extensive attention not only due to their diverse topologies and intriguing structures but also owing to their interesting physical and chemical properties, such as photoluminescence, magnetism, ferroelectricity, gas storage, ion exchange, catalysis, etc. [1], [2], [3], [4]. N-Heterocyclic multicarboxylic acids have been widely used to construct MOF for their potential application [5], [6], [7], [8], [9]. Among the reported network lanthanide N-Heterocyclic carboxylates, Benzimidazole-5,6-dicarboxylic acid(H3bidc), as the derivatives of 4, 5-imidazoledicarboxylic acid (H2imdc), which remains largely unexplored, H3bidc exhibits several interesting characteristics: (i) The multifunctional coordination sites provide a high likelihood for construction of high-dimension a land high-connected frameworks. (ii) It can be partially or completely deprotonated to generate H2bidc–, Hbidc2–, and bidc3– by controlling the pH carefully, which allows Benzimidazole-5,6-dicarboxylate anions to display various coordination modes [10], [11]. Furthermore, compared to MOFs that contain only one carboxylate ligands, reports on MOF built from two different carboxylate bridging ligands are relatively rare [12], [13]. This prompted us to investigate addition of an auxiliary organic ligand (Oxalic acid) to the reaction mixture to construct MOF containing lanthanide metal. In this paper we report three novel MOF, namely [(Eu2(Hbidc)2(ox)·(H2O)3] (1), [Tb2(Hbidc)2(ox)(H2O)2]·4H2O (2) and [Er2(Hbidc)2(ox)] (3). The luminescent studies reveal that compounds 1 and 2 have better fluorescence properties at room temperature.

All chemicals were of regent-grade quality and were obtained from commercial sources and used without further purification. A mixture of Eu(NO)3·6H2O(0.15 mmol, 0.067 g), H3bidc (0.18 mmol, 0.037 g), H2ox (0.18 mmol, 0.023 g), and water (10 mL) were dissolved in a 25 mL Teflon reactor and mixed with stirring, the KOH solution (0.2 mol L−1) was added to adjust the pH at 7.4. The reaction vessel were heated to 160 °C for 3 days under autogenous pressure, then cooled to room temperature at a rate of 10 °C/h. The colorless crystals of 1 were obtained with yields of 66%. Complexes 1, 2 and 3 were analyzed by X-ray crystallography analysis [14].

The IR spectra of complexes 1–3 have in common a strong and broad absorption around 3360 cm−1 could be attributed to the O–H stretching vibration of hydrogen bonds in their supramolecular structure. Peaks at 1359 m(1363 m in 2 and 1357 m in 3) [νs(CO)] and 802 m cm−1(808 m in 2 and 806 m in 3)[δ(OCO] support the presence of a bischelating oxalic acid group in three complexes [15]. In addition, peaks at 1635 s (1637 s in 2 and 1639 s in 3) and 1465(s) (1470(s) in 2 and 1466(s) in 3) correspond to the asymmetric and symmetric stretching vibrations of the carboxylate group, respectively [16], [17]. All these spectral suggestions have been confirmed by the X-ray structures of 1–3.

The X-ray structural analysis of [(Eu2(Hbidc)2(ox)2·(H2O)3] (1) reveals that each Eu(III) is coordinated by two oxygen atoms from one chelating ox ligands(O5, O6B), three monodentate carboxylate groups of three additional Hbidc2– ligands (O1, O4 and O3A), and the remaining three coordination sites are occupied by three oxygen atoms of three coordination water molecules(O7w, O8w and O9w), respectively, as shown in Fig. S1. Two adjacent Eu(III) were bridged by ox to form a one-dimensional chain structure (Fig. 1a). Two oxygen atoms from two monodentate carboxylate groups of Hbidc2– ligands connected one-dimension chain to construct a two-dimensional coordination network, as shown in (Fig. 1b). The two-dimensional network further extended into three-dimensional supramolecular structure through N–H···O hydrogen bonds (∠N2–H2···O5 177.17°, N2–O5 2.831 Ǻ) and O–H···O hydrogen bonds (∠O2–H7A···O7 170.98°, O2–O7 2.797 Ǻ) from two adjacent layers (Fig. 1c).

The X-ray structural analysis of [Tb2(Hbidc)2(ox)(H2O)2]·4H2O (2) reveals that each Tb(III) is coordinated by two oxygen atoms from one chelating carboxylate group of ox ligands(O6B, O5) and five oxygen atoms from three carboxylate groups of three Hbidc2– ligands (O1, O2, O3B, O4B, O1A). The remaining two coordination sites are occupied by the oxygen atoms of two water molecules (O7w, O8w), as shown in (Fig. S2). Two neighbouring Tb(III) were connected by ox ligands to form one-dimension chain (Fig. 2a). Two adjacent chains have been bridged by two carboxylate groups of two Hbidc2– ligands, to form a two-dimensional network (Fig. 2b). Two-dimensional network through N-H···O and O–H···O hydrogen bonds (Table S1) from adjacent ligands further extended into three-dimensional supramolecular structure (Fig. 2c).

The X-ray structural analysis of [Er2(Hbidc)2(ox)] (3) reveals that each Er(III) was coordinated by two oxygen atoms from one chelating ox ligands(O5, O6), and five oxygen atoms from three crystallographically independent Hbidc2– ligands (O1A, O1B, O2A, O4A, O3A), and the remaining coordination site is occupied by nitrogen atoms of other crystallographically independent Hbidc2– ligands, respectively, as shown in (Fig. S3). Er1 and Er1A are linked together to form a binuclear unit by two μ2-O bridges. The distance between two Er atoms is 3.867 Å. The binuclear units are linked by two O–C–O bridges to form a one-dimensional chain (Fig. 3a), the chains are connected by ox ligands to construct a two-dimensional network (Fig. 3b). Furthermore, the two-dimensional network further extend into three-dimensional framework (Fig. 3c).

1, 2 and 3 have the same triclinic system, but they exhibit different kinds of crystal structures. The structural difference between 1, 2 and 3 is caused by two reasons: One is the difference coordination motifs of Hbidc2– ligands [(i) The ligand Hbidc2– in 1 adopts monodentate μ3–η1: η1: η1 mode (Scheme S1a); (ii) The ligand Hbidc2– in 2 adopts μ3–η1: η1: η1: η2 mode (Scheme S1b); (iii) The ligand Hbidc2– in 3 adopts μ4–η1: η1: η1: η2: η1 mode (Scheme S1c)]. The other is the reduced coordination water of rare earth ions.

The luminescence of lanthanide complexes has also attracted intensive research for their high color purity and potentially high internal quantum efficiency [18], [19]. It is well-known that the luminescence of lanthanide complexes from the f–f transitions are usually generated via the “antenna effect”, since the f–f transition is spin- and parity-forbidden. So researcher introduced suitable chromophores in the lanthanide complexes, which absorb light and transfer excitation energy to the lanthanide ions [20]. The room temperature solid-state luminescent spectra are in Figs. 7 and 8. For compound 1, upon excitation of 1 at 397 nm, the following transitions are observed the emission peaks of 1 at 5D1 → 7F1(536 nm), 5D0 → 7F1(594 nm), 5D0 → 7F2(617 nm), 5D0 → 7F3(651 nm), 5D0 → 7F4(695 nm) transitions of the Eu(III) ion, respectively. Notably, the 5D0 → 7F2 transition is much more intense than the 5D0 → 7F1 transition, which reflects a low symmetry of the Eu(III) site, consistent to the distorted dodecahedral coordination environment and the so-called hypersensitive transition responsible for the brilliant-red emission of 1 [21]. For compound 2, upon excitation at 377 nm, four lines of Tb(III) emission can be detected in the visible spectrum, corresponding to transitions from the 5D4 state: 5D4 → 7F6 (490 nm), 5D4 → 7F5 (548 nm), 5D4 → 7F4 (590 nm), and 5D4 → 7F3 (620 nm). The most prominent line was observed at 548 nm.

In summary, we have synthesized and structurally characterized three different lanthanide coordination polymers with H3bidc and ox ligands: Eu(III) is eight coordinated and Hbidc2− ligands adopt μ3-bridging coordination mode in 1 but Tb(III) is nine coordinated and Hbidc2− ligands adopt μ3-bridging coordination mode in 2. Moreover, Er(III) has the same coordinated number as Eu(III) but Hbidc2− ligands in 3 adopt μ4-bridging coordination mode. The solid-state photoluminescent properties indicate that complexes 1 and 2 may be candidate for potential luminescent materials.

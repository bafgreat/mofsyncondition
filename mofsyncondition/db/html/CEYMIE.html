Due to the unique excitation and electrochemistry properties of the UO22+ unit, uranyl-organic compounds show interesting characters under the excitation of light. Earlier reports indicated that uranyl-organic compounds have wide applications in photoluminescence, photocatalysis, photocurrent and photovoltaic response.1 For example, a uranium containing compound that could be applied to photoelectric conversion as an n-type semiconductor has been reported.1a Three-dimensional (3D) frameworks of uranyl-organic compounds are considered more desirable than their one- and two-dimensional (1D and 2D) counterparts owing to their superior stability.1e However, the synthesis of 3D uranyl-organic frameworks is still a great challenge because of the strong UO bonds and considerably constrained coordination geometry of the linear uranyl ions.1e To obtain uranyl-organic frameworks, organic polycarboxylate ligands are preferred due to their various connectivity modes.1e,2 As a typical carboxylate ligand, 2,5-furandicarboxylic acid (H2FDA), which could be prepared from untreated lignocellulosic biomass, has been listed by the US Department of Energy biomass programme as one of the 12 chemicals that can be obtained as feedstock from biorefineries in the future.3 Therefore, using H2FDA is of enormous significance rather than other non-renewable substances in the construction of functional uranyl-organic compounds.

On the other hand, chiral solid materials are of great interest owing to their potential applications in enantioselective separation and asymmetric catalysis.4 Most of the reported homochiral inorganic–organic hybrid complexes were obtained with chiral organic ligands, which often require laborious synthesis and are expensive.5 However, chiral complexes could also be synthesized with achiral precursors via spontaneous resolution or asymmetric induction.6 Some chiral uranyl-organic compounds assembled with chiral ligands have been reported,7 while the examples using achiral precursors are still limited.8 Herein, we report the successful synthesis of 3D chiral uranyl-organic frameworks with achiral H2FDA via spontaneous resolution. Besides the investigation of the nature of spontaneous resolution, the photocatalytic property of the resulting compound has also been studied.

At first, [(NH4)2(H2O)4][(UO2)2(FDA)3] (UOC-NH4) was obtained with H2FDA, NH3·H2O and UO2(NO3)2·6H2O.‡UOC-NH4 crystallizes in an achiral space group C2/c, and the asymmetric unit contains one UO22+ ion, one and a half FDA2−ligands, one NH4+ ion, and two free H2O molecules. Each UO22+ ion coordinates with three carboxylate groups from three different FDA2−ligands in chelating mode resulting in a usual hexagonal bipyramidal geometry. For FDA2−, there are two different conformations depending on the dihedral angle between the two carboxyl groups in the same FDA2−ligand (2.3° and 30.0°, respectively) (Fig. S1, ESI†). It is noteworthy that, owing to the nonplanar conformations of FDA2−ligands, the different ligands alternately bridge UO22+ ions to form 1D helical infinite chains (right-handed chains or left-handed chains) (Fig. 1a and c). The 1D helical chains share the common edge to form a homochiral layer. However, the neighbouring layers show opposite chirality (Fig. 1b), so the overall compound is achiral.

We noticed that NH4+ ions as counterions were located in the helical chains. The need for positive counterions for charge balance in this compound provides an opportunity to introduce heterometal ions. It has been reported that the incorporation of heterometal ions with a strong cross-linking ability into the reaction system is beneficial to the construction of 3D uranyl-organic compounds.1e,9 Then we tried to introduce Ca2+ ions into the reaction system and obtained [(UO2)2Ca(FDA)3(H2O)5]·2H2O (UOC-Ca). Similar to UOC-NH4, UOC-Ca is constructed with helical chain based homochiral layered structures (Fig. S2, ESI†), while Ca2+ ions replace NH4+ ions to serve as counterions. Each Ca2+ ion in UOC-Ca coordinates with two O atoms from two carboxyl groups and five O atoms from five water molecules (Fig. S3, ESI†). Although Ca2+ ions are incorporated successfully, they just locate inside the layered structure rather than link layered structures. The neighbouring layers have opposite chirality as UOC-NH4 (Fig. S2, ESI†), so UOC-Ca is also an achiral compound.

When Na+ was introduced into the reaction system, a 3D framework compound, [Na(H2O)4][(UO2)2Na(FDA)3(H2O)2] (UOC-Na), was obtained. UOC-Na crystallizes in a chiral space group C2221, which indicates the chirality of the structure. Fortunately, we successfully separated the enantiomers, and both of them were characterized by single crystal X-ray diffraction. In order to ascertain the spontaneous resolution of this compound, 10 crystals chosen randomly were refined using single crystal X-ray diffraction data with the same set of atomic coordinates. The Flack parameters of 5 crystals are close to zero, whereas those of the other 5 are close to one (Table S1, ESI†). When the atomic coordinates of the latter 5 crystals are inverted, the Flack parameters are close to zero (Table S2, ESI†). These results prove the homochirality of the single crystal of UOC-Na.

The asymmetric unit of UOC-Na contains one UO22+ ion, one and a half FDA2−ligands, two independent one-half Na+ ions, and three coordinated H2O molecules. Similar to the case of UOC-NH4 and UOC-Ca, the FDA2−ligands and UO22+ ions of UOC-Na assembled into layered structures, with a small difference in the dihedral angle between the two carboxyl groups (–COO) of the ligand (3.3° and 25.3°) (Fig. S4, ESI†). On the other hand, two types of Na+ ions exist in UOC-Na depending on their roles in the formation of the framework structure. Na1 is coordinated by two O atoms from two H2O molecules and four O atoms of four different FDA2−ligands from adjacent layers and shows a distorted octahedral geometry (Fig. 2a). Na2 is coordinated by four water molecules to form a [Na(H2O)4]+ counterion and locates in the layered structure. Via the bridge of Na1, adjacent 2D layers are cross-linked to generate a 3D framework structure. From the topological viewpoint, one sodium polyhedron and two connected uranium polyhedrons with shared edges define a trinuclear heterometal cluster (U2Na), which could be considered as a six-connected secondary building unit (SBU) (Fig. S5 and S6, ESI†). Then the network of UOC-Na can be rationalized as a 3D pcu net (Fig. 2b). It should be noted that the neighboring layers bridged by Na1 have the same chirality, which makes the whole 3D framework homochiral. The assembly of the layers with the same chirality might be attributed to the fact that the cis-octahedral coordination conformation (Δ configuration) of Na1 only allows the coordination of chiral matched structures.10 Based on the above discussion, we conclude that the spontaneous resolution occurs during the assembly process of enantiomers.

The solid-state circular dichroism (CD) spectra obtained from KCl pellets using a single crystal further confirmed the optical activity and enantiomeric nature of UOC-Na. As shown in Fig. S7 (ESI†), the CD spectra of enantiomers are nearly mirror images of each other, indicating the enantiomeric nature of the compound. An obvious Cotton effect is observed in the spectra, further corroborating the homochirality of the single crystal. But the bulk sample shows no obvious Cotton effect, demonstrating that it is racemic (Fig. S8, ESI†).

So far, the search for photocatalytic materials has been stimulated largely by the demand for solving the pollution problems in consideration of their applications in the green degradation of organic pollutants.11 Since uranyl units have the potential to photocatalytically oxidize organic substrates,12 we selected methyl orange (MO), a typical azo dye, as the model substrate to evaluate the photocatalytic activity of UOC-Na. The concentration change of MO was characterized by the variation of the absorbance at 464 nm.11b As illustrated in Fig. 3, the addition of UOC-Na can greatly accelerate the degradation of MO under UV irradiation. Approximately 21.5% of MO was decomposed after 1.5 h of irradiation. These results show that UOC-Na is active in the decomposition of MO under UV light irradiation. After photocatalysis, the XRPD pattern of UOC-Na was found to be nearly identical to that of the original compound (Fig. S9, ESI†), indicating that UOC-Na is stable in the photocatalytic process.

In summary, a 3D chiral uranyl-organic compound has been synthesized by the reaction of 2,5-furandicarboxylic acid with UO22+ and NaOH under mild conditions. It is demonstrated that the spontaneous resolution is induced by the cis-octahedral geometry of the Na+ ion, which makes the neighboring layers to have the same chirality for preventing it from being a mesomer. This work shows that the chirality of the compound can be gained by influencing the self-assembly of chiral units with different cations, which might be instructive in the synthesis of other chiral compounds.

Carboxylate-bridged helical-chain Cu(II) complex and zig-zag chain Ni(II) and Co(II) complexes from 8-quinolinyloxyacetate

Author links open overlay panel
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.molstruc.2005.05.031Get rights and content

Abstract

Tridentate carboxylate-containing ligand L (L=8-quinolinyloxyacetate) reacts with M(II) chloride (M=Cu, Ni and Co) to give rise to three coordination polymers [CuLCl]n (1), {[NiL(H2O)2]Cl·H2O}n (2), and {[CoL(H2O)2]Cl·H2O}n (3), respectively. Complex 1 is a helix chain polymer while 2 and 3 are two zig-zag chain polymers. The bridging carboxylate groups are in anti–anti bridging mode in complex 1 while they are within syn–anti conformation in 2 and 3. In 1, carboxylate groups link two basal positions in basal–apical, while in complexes 2 and 3, they are basal–basal positions. The structures of these complexes have been determined by X-ray crystallography. Interestingly, three chain complexes are also linked to form 3D networks through hydrogen bonds.

1. Introduction

The design and synthesis of polymeric coordination architectures is a rapidly developing field in current coordination and supramolecular chemistry [1], [2], [3], [4], [5], [6]. Polynuclear complexes containing bridging carboxylate groups are of current interest due to the applications in many biochemical systems and potential uses as functional materials [7], [8], [9], [10]. In carboxylate-bridged complexes, a carboxylate group can assume many types of bridging conformations, the most important being triatomic syn–syn, anti–anti, and syn–anti and monatomic [11], [12], [13], [14], [15]. Multidentate carboxylato-bridged ligands with acetate are one of the good multi-atom bridging ligands, and their complexes with helix and discrete polynuclear structure have been reported [16], [17], [18], [19], [20], [21], [22], [23]. However, these carboxylato-bridged ligands are limited to substituent aminoacetic acid [18], [19], carboxylate-containing Schiff base ligands and their reduced Schiff base ligands [20], [21], [22], [23]. So, the study of the complexes with the other multidentate ligands containing acetate is interesting.

In an effort to investigate the bonding nature of other multidentate carboxylato-bridged metal(II) complexes, we have turned to 8-quinolinyloxyacetic acid (L), as it has a potentially rich coordination chemistry, with a chelating capacity through N, O atoms of quinolinyloxy group and hydrogen bonding capacity through carboxylate. We have succeeded in obtaining three new coordination polymers by reacting 8-quinolinyloxyacetic acid with M(II) chloride (M=Cu, Ni and Co). In this paper, we report the synthesis and crystal structures of these complexes.

2. Experimental

2.1. Materials and methods

The ligand 8-quinolinyloxyacetic acid was prepared by general procedure reported in the literature [24]. All the other reagents for synthesis were commercially available and employed as received or purified by standard methods prior to use. Elemental analyses were performed on a Perkin–Elmer 240 C analyzer and IR spectra on a 170SX (Nicolet) FT-IR spectrometer with KBr pellets.

2.2. Preparation of complexes

2.2.1. [CuLCl]n (1)

8-Quinolinyloxyacetic acid (0.0203 g, 0.1 mmol) was dissolved in methanol (20 mL), and an aqueous solution (2 mL) of CuCl2·2H2O (0.017 g, 0.1 mmol) was added slowly with stirring to the ligand solution. The yellow green solution was stirred for 2 h at room temperature, and 10 mL methanol of NaOH (0.004 g, 0.1 mmol) was added. The resulting green solution was stirred for about 2 h and then filtered. The filtrate was allowed to evaporate slowly and obtained green crystals block-shaped single crystals 1 suitable for X-ray analysis. Yield: 71%. IR (KBr pellet cm−1): 3065w, 1609s, 1436m, 1379s, 1256m, 1115s. Anal. found: C, 43.23; H, 2.71; N, 4.69. Calcd for C11H8ClCuNO3: C, 43.87; H, 2.68; N, 4.65.

2.2.2. {[NiL(H2O)2]Cl·H2O}n (2)

A solution of 8-quinolinyloxyacetic acid (0.0203 g, 0.1 mmol) in methanol (20 mL) is stirred together with an aqueous solution (2 mL) of NiCl2·6H2O (0.0238 g, 0.1 mmol) for 1 h. The solution of NaOH (0.004 g, 0.1 mmol) in methanol (10 mL) was then added. The resulting green solution was stirred for about 2 h at room temperature and then filtered. Slow evaporation from the filtrate yielded square prismatic green crystals 2 suitable for X-ray analysis. Yield: 56%. IR (KBr pellet, cm−1): 3423vs, 1587s, 1427m, 1381m, 1263m, 1123m. Anal. Found: C, 37.29; H, 4.10; N, 4.05. Calcd for C11H14ClNNiO6: C, 37.71; H, 4.03; N, 4.00.

2.2.3. {[CoL(H2O)2]Cl·H2O}n (3)

Purple crystal of 3 suitable for X-ray analysis was obtained by the similar method described for 2. Yield: 51%. IR (KBr pellet, cm−1): 3421vs, 1586s, 1423m, 1378m, 1259m, 1121m. Anal. Found: C, 37.21; H, 3.99; N, 4.01. Calcd for C11H14ClCoNO6: C, 37.68; H, 4.02; N, 3.99.

2.3. X-ray crystallographic analysis

The diffraction data of the complexes 1–3 were collected at 193 K with a Rigaku Mercury CCD area detector, using graphite-monochromated Mo Kα radiation (λ=0.71073 Å). The structures were solved using direct methods [25] and refined by full-matrix least squares on F2 using the SHELXL-97 program [26]. All non-hydrogen atoms were refined with anisotropic thermal parameters. Water hydrogen atoms were identified from difference Fourier synthesis and were refined freely, and other hydrogen atoms were included in calculated positions refined in the riding-model. 3. Results and discussion

3.1. Complex 1

The complex 1 is 1D helical chain structure [CuLCl]n, and it results from the fact that the Cu(II) ions are bridged sequentially by anti–anti carboxylate groups. The coordination geometry around the copper atom may be described as a distorted square pyramidal, the basal plane being defined by O(1), O(3), N(1), and Cl(1) and the apical position being occupied by O(2A) from carboxylate oxygen atom of another ligand. The root-mean-square value of the best plane of O(1), O(3), N(1), and Cl(1) atoms is 0.0453(1) Å, indicating a tetrahedral distortion from planarity. As expected, the Cu(1) atom is displaced from this plane by 0.1206(1) Å toward the apical O(2A) atom. Each monodeprotonated ligand is tridentate with respect to one Cu center, but it is in fact tetradentate when the bridging bond is considered. It forms two five-membered chelate ring with the metal, producing considerable distortions in the chelate rings. Thus, the N(1)–Cu(1)–O(1) and O(1)–Cu(1)–O(3) chelate angles are 81.10(7)° and 79.40(7)°, respectively. The five-membered chelate ring defined by Cu(1), N(1), C(9), C(8) and O(1) is slightly nonplanar (maximum atomic displacement: 0.1131(1) Å), while the five-membered chelate ring defined by Cu(1), O(1), C(10), C(11) and O(3) is significantly nonplanar (atomic displacement: 0.1888(1) Å). The two fused rings systems are also folded along the common Cu(1)–O(1) axis by 10.3(1)°.

Within the coordination sphere, four short bonds are formed in the basal plane with one nitrogen atom [Cu(1)–N(1)] 1.987(2) Å, two oxygen atoms [Cu(1)–O(1) 2.0183(16) Å, Cu(1)–O(3) 1.9425(17) Å] from one ligand and one chloride atom [Cu–Cl(1) 2.2115(7) Å] from chloride anion, while the other oxygen atom forming the bridge between two copper atoms is placed at the axial position and coordinated at a longest distance [Cu(1)–O(2A) 2.2979(17) Å]. The three C–O distances in one ligand are different [O(3)–C(11) 1.280(3) Å, O(2)–C(11) 1.229(3) Å, O(1)–C(10) 1.447(3) Å], and these differences may be correlated with their involvement in bonding with copper. Other bond lengths and angles associated with L and Cl− are as expected.

As noted above, each carboxylate group in one L is in the anti–anti conformation and bridges two copper atoms via its two oxygen atoms. In complex 1, the copper ions deviate from the mean basal plane toward the axial oxygen donor. Because the three donor atoms of the ligands occupy in-plane coordination positions, the carboxylate bridges are forced to be oriented in a cis fashion and then placed in basal–apical position. Then, each anti–anti carboxylate group bridges two copper(II) ions in apical–basal positions with intrachain Cu⋯Cu distances of 5.666(1) Å. Interestingly, both the cis orientations of the anti–anti carboxylate groups and the shifts of the Cu(II) ions from the carboxylate planes lead to helicities in the chains, and this helix is generated by the operation of a 21 screw axis parallel to the b axis.

The 3-H atoms of quinoline rings from 1D Δ chain structure form C–H⋯Cl hydrogen bonds [27] with the terminal chloride atoms from adjacent 1D ∧ chain (the distances and angle: C⋯Cl 3.598(1) Å, H⋯Cl 2.835(1) Å, C–H⋯Cl 138.2(1)°), then, the quasi two-dimensional sheet is formed between alternate Δ chains and ∧ chains (Fig. 2a). The quasi two-dimensional sheets are packed through C–H⋯O hydrogen bonds [28] into a 3D networks structure (Fig. 2b), and these hydrogen bonds are formed between 5-H atoms of quinoline rings from 1D chain structure and the basal position oxygen atoms of carboxylate from adjacent chain structure (the distances and angle: C⋯O 3.201(1)Å, H⋯O 2.344(1) Å, C–H⋯O 147.3(1)°).Thus, in the lattices, Δ and ∧ helical chains alternate, resulting in optically inactive racemic crystals. A few similar helical chains with carboxylate-containing ligand have been reported by other authors [21], [23], [29].

Each monodeprotonated ligand acts as a tridentate ligand toward a metal(II) ion and as a monodentate ligand toward a neighbouring metal(II) center. Metal(II) ions exhibit MNO5 coordination environments comprised of three donor atoms of the ligand, one oxygen atom belonging to the carboxylate group of an adjacent molecule and two water molecule. As complex 1, each monodeprotonated ligand forms two five-membered chelate ring (N(1)–M(1)–O(1) and O(1)–M(1)–O(3)) with the metal. The water molecule is bonded in the apical position, whereas the other four coordination bonds occupy the basal positions. Because the three donor atoms of the ligands occupy in-plane coordination positions, the carboxylate bridges are forced to be oriented in a mutually trans fashion and then placed in basal–basal positions. Interestingly, both the trans orientations of the syn–anti carboxylate groups and the shifts of the metal(II) ions from the carboxylate groups contribute to zig-zag in the chains (Fig. 3b). In 2, each syn–anti carboxylate group bridges two nickel(II) ions in basal–basal positions with intrachain Ni⋯Ni distance of 5.309(1) Å, whereas for 3 the intrachain Co⋯Co distance is 5.243(1) Å.

The polymeric chains are held together by a 2D network of hydrogen bonds involving the coordinated water molecule, crystal lattice water molecule, chloride ions and the carboxylate O atom of the ligand. The shortest interchain separations are 6.620(1) and 6.610(1) Å for 2 and 3, respectively. Hydrogen bond parameters for 2 and 3 are given in Table 5. Perspective view of the 2D network packed from the 1D zig-zag chain through hydrogen bonds for 2 is given as example in Fig. 3c. The O–H proton of lattice water (O(6)) is involved in strong hydrogen bonding to O(5) of neighbouring coordinated water and O(2) of neighbouring carboxylate oxygen, and O(6) atom of lattice water are bonded to H–O of coordinated water (O(4)). All the atoms attached to O(6) participate in strong O–H⋯O hydrogen bonding. The O–H proton of the coordinated water are hydrogen bonded to neighbouring chloride ions to form O–H⋯Cl hydrogen bond. As complex 1, these 2D structures are packed into 3D networks (Fig. 3d) through C–H⋯O hydrogen bonds [28] existed between 6-H atoms of quinoline rings and O atoms of lattice water (the distances and angles are C⋯O 3.351(1) Å, H⋯O 2.484(1) Å, C–H⋯O 151.6(1)° for 2 and C⋯O 3.318(1) Å, H⋯O 2.417(1) Å, C–H⋯O 158.2(1)° for 3).

From above results, the reaction of tridentate carboxylate containing ligand with copper chloride, nickel chloride and cobalt chloride molecules lead to helix chain complex 1 with anti–anti carboxylate-bridges and zig-zag chain complexes 2 and 3 with syn–anti carboxylate-bridges, respectively. In 1, carboxylate groups link two basal positions in basal–apical, while in complexex 2 and 3, they are basal–basal. The structural differences in the complexes 1 and 2 and the isostructural complexes in 2 and 3 should contribute to the different of the metal ions coordination geometries and the configurations and coordination properties of the ligand [30]. Because the tridentate ligands do not saturate the coordination positions on metal ions (CuII, NiII, CoII), self-assembly reactions may occur through the deprotonated carboxylate groups, leading to carboxylate-bridged polynuclear complexes. 1D helix chain complex 1 results from the fact that the square pyramidal geometry conformation copper(II) ions are bridged sequentially by anti–anti carboxylate groups, and octahedral M(II) (NiII, CoII) ions and syn–anti carboxylate-bridged code are result in two 1D zig-zag polymers.

Interestingly, three coordination polymeric complexes are also linked to form 3D networks through hydrogen bonds. In 1, 3D network is packed from the 1D helical chain through intermolecular C–H⋯O, C–H⋯Cl hydrogen bonds. In 2 and 3, the 3D network is formed through hydrogen bonds involving coordinated water molecule, crystal lattice water molecule, chloride ions, the carboxylate O atom and H atoms of quinoline rings of ligand. In 1–3, the carboxylates of ligand participate in hydrogen bond formation.

In summary, this work demonstrates the use of 8-quinolinyloxyacetate ligand has been shown to be a good tool to build carboxylate-bridged metal(II) complexes. The carboxylate coordination sites of 8-quinolinyloxyacetate ligand also participate in hydrogen bond donor/acceptor interactions, giving novel networks. These results indicate the expected coordination polymer and hydrogen bonded networks may be obtained by the self-assembly of such well designed aryloxyacetate ligands with suitable metal ions.

  • Synthesis and structural investigation of mononuclear penta- and hexa-coordinated Co complexes of 8-hydroxyquinoline derived ligands

    2023, Journal of Molecular Structure

    This article describes the synthesis and structural property investigation of two new mononuclear Co(II) complexes of 8-hydroxyquinoline derived flexible tridentate N,O,N'- (LI) and tetradentate N,O,O',N'-ligands (LII). The reactions were carried out between CoCl2.6H2O and the respective ligands in methanol to obtain the complexes CoLICl2 and CoLIICl2 in good yields. The complexes are characterized by elemental analysis, mass, IR spectroscopy, and single-crystal X-ray diffraction. The molecular structure of CoLICl2 shows distorted trigonal bipyramidal coordination geometry with the structural index parameter τ = 0.83. The apical positions are occupied with the quinoline O- and one terminal Cl-atom, whereas the equatorial positions are held by the two N-atoms and the second Cl-atom. In contrast, CoLIICl2 has a slightly distorted octahedral structure with all the donor atoms of the chelating ligand LII holding the equatorial positions with the two-terminal Cl-atoms at the apical positions. Electronic structure calculations are used to evaluate the geometrical, electronic, and magnetic properties of these complexes. Non-periodic calculations used to simulate molecules indicate a strong dπ-pπ bonding between Co and ligands. The crystal packing from both the solid-state structures revealed further stability of the molecule via weak non-covalent interactions through π.…π stacking between quinoline rings and C–H.…Cl hydrogen bonding. A detailed comparison between the geometric parameters of CoL1Cl2 and CoLIICl2 are reported. In addition, the comparison of selected structural parameters between a range of transition metal quinolates from the literature are given. Periodic calculations to simulate crystals using B3LYP hybrid functional predicts both the crystalline structures to be magnetic insulators.

The structures were solved by direct methods using the shelxs-97 program [13] and refined by the full-matrix least-squares method based on F2 using shelxl-97 [14]. Non-hydrogen atoms were refined with anisotropic thermal parameters; hydrogen atoms were included from the difference Fourier Δρ maps and refined with isotropic thermal parameters.

    Two novel Ni(II) complexes of 5-nitroorotate dianion (the nitro-derivative of orotic acid, vitamin B13) have been synthesized and structurally characterized by single crystal X-ray diffraction and vibrational (IR and Raman) spectroscopy. In complex 1, {[Ni(5-NO2HOr)(H2O)3]·H2O}n, crystallizing in the orthorhombic space group Pna2(1), the 5-nitroorotate ligand is tridentate and binds to the nickel(II) ions through the deprotonated N1 pyrimidine nitrogen and two carboxylate oxygen atoms. Among a great number of the reported Ni(II)-orotates, 1 is the first complex where the carboxylate group coordinates to two nickel ions by the anti-syn bridge forming a polymeric structure. Three water molecules complete the octahedral coordination sphere around each Ni atom. Complex 2, [Ni(5-NO2HOr)(H2O)4]·H2O, crystallizes in the monoclinic space group P2(1)/c, and the bidentate 5-nitroorotate ligand coordinates to the Ni(II) ion by only one carboxylate oxygen O1 atom and the deprotonated N1 atom. The distorted octahedral environment of Ni atom is completed by four water molecules. The assignment of the characteristic bands in the IR and Raman spectra has provided further evidence for the nature of the bonding in these compounds. Temperature and field-dependent magnetic studies of 1 reveal the existence of a weak antiferromagnetic exchange interaction transmitted by anti-syn carboxylate bridge with J = −0.89 cm−1 (−JS1·S2 convention) superimposed on the zero-field splitting (D = 5.4 cm−1). The magnetic properties of 2 have been reproduced using a 2D quasi-quadratic lattice model with D = 5.5, J1 = −1.89 and J2 = 1.56 cm−1 and both magnetic interactions are transmitted through hydrogen bonding.

    Unusual bifurcated and trifurcated carboxylate binding to sodium ions in a one dimensional coordination polymer, [{Na2L(H2O)3}Br·H2O]n (1) (where L = 2-(quinolin-8-yloxy)propionate), was observed. The ionic coordination polymer 1, possessing bromide anions, has sodium ions in two independent coordination environments. Reaction of 1 with perchloric acid led to anion exchange to form the dinuclear complex [Na2(HL)2(ClO4)2(μ-H2O)] (2). A cadmium coordination polymer [Cd(L)Br]n (3) was prepared by reacting 1 with cadmium(II) acetate. This one dimensional cadmium coordination polymer 3 has cadmium ions in a distorted square pyramidal geometry. The uniqueness of the cadmium polymer is that the bromide ions are inside the coordination sphere and it forms parallel sheet-like structures of polymeric chains through bromide–bromide interactions. The coordination polymer 1 and the dinuclear complex 2 are weakly fluorescent, whereas complex 3 is highly fluorescent. The coordination polymers 1 and 2 are fluorescence receptors for Mg, Ca, Ba, Zn, Cd and Hg in the (+2) oxidation state.

  • Lanthanide complexes with 8-quinolinyloxyacetate (qoa): Synthesis and supramolecular assembly by hydrogen bonds

    Two new lanthanide supramolecular complexes, [Tb(qoa)2(H2O)2]⋅NO3 ⋅ H2O (1) and [Er(qoa)3] ⋅ 2H2O (2) [qoa = (8-quinolinyloxy)acetate] were obtained by treating the corresponding nitrates and (8-quinolinyloxy)acetic acid (Hqoa) under hydrothermal conditions.   • Three new coordination polymers based on 4-oxo-1(4H)quinolineacetate: Synthesis, structures and luminescent properties

    2010, Inorganic Chemistry Communications

    Reactions of 4-oxo-1(4H)-quinolineacetic acid (Hoqa) and varied metal salts of Zn(II), Cd(II) and Ag(I) resulted in the formation of three new coordination polymers, namely, [Zn(oqa)2]·H2O (1), [Cd(bpy)(oqa)2(H2O)2]·4H2O (2) and [Ag(bpy)(oqa)]·2H2O (3) (bpy = 4,4′-bipyridine).   • A highly novel dinickel complex: A hydrogen-bonded anti-skew carboxylate bridge and a 2D supramolecular structure

    2009, Inorganic Chemistry Communications

    Reaction of the tridentate Schiff base ligands obtained from 2,4-dihydroxybenzaldehyde and either l-isoleucine or l-tert-leucine with Ni(NO3)2 in methanol/water solution in the presence of base afforded dinickel complexes. The crystal structure of the product derived from l-tert-leucine has been determined by X-ray crystallography. The octahedrally coordinated two Ni centers were found to be bridged by a single carboxylate group in an extremely unusual non-planar fashion. A 2D supramolecular structure, constructed by infinite hydrogen-bonded complex sheets parallel to the ab-plane of the unit cell, arises from intermolecular O–H⋯O hydrogen bonds.

Introduction

During the past decades, the crystal engineering of AgI coordination architectures has been a dynamic, thriving field that has attracted increasing interest.1 Two major themes, namely, discrete molecular architectures and infinite coordination polymers, have been well developed based on the versatile coordination geometry of the AgI ion in combination with different organic ligands.2,3 The design of suitable organic ligands favoring a structure specific self-assembly is crucial for the construction of coordination architectures with expected structures and properties.3d,e Furthermore, the formation of such complexes is also sensitive to many factors such as the solvent system, the counteranion, and the reaction temperature.4 In addition, H-bonding, π–π stacking, and host–guest interactions also affect the results, and they may further link discrete subunits or low-dimensional entities into high-dimensional supramolecular networks.5,6 Flexible ligands, especially those containing N-donors possess rich structural information,7 among them, the triazole, imidazole, and benzimidazole-containing ligands are particularly appealing because of their versatile coordination modes and variable conformations compared with rigid ligands.8 Recently, some coordination polymers with such ligands showing interesting properties have been reported in literature.9,10

In our recent work, some flexible ligands based on triazole groups were successfully used to construct complexes with diverse structures.11 In order to further tune the structures of AgI complexes by ligand modification, six structurally related ligands: 1,2-bis(triazol-1-ylmethyl)benzene (L1), 1,3-bis(triazol-1- ylmethyl)benzene (L2), 1,4-bis(triazol-1-ylmethyl)benzene (L3), 1,3,5-tri(triazol-1-ylmethyl)-2,4,6- trimethylbenzene (L4), 1,4-bis[3-(2-pyridyl)pyrazol-1-ylmethyl]naphthalene (L5), 9,10-bis(benzimidazol-1-ylmethyl)anthracene (L6) (Chart 1), have been designed or selected, and the reactions of AgI salts with these ligands lead to the formation of six new AgI complexes with dinuclear, trinuclear, 1D, and 3D structures. Moreover, complex 2 is a spontaneously resolved chiral complex and its CD spectrum and fluorescence properties have been investigated in the solid state.

Experimental

Materials and general methods

All regents and solvents for synthesis were obtained from commercial sources and used as received. Ligands 1,2-bis(triazol-1-ylmethyl)benzene (L1) and 1,4-bis(triazol-1-ylmethyl)benzene (L3) were prepared according to literature methods.12FT-IR spectra were recorded from KBr pellets in the range 4000–400 cm−1 on a TENSOR 27 (Bruker) spectrometer. 1H NMR spectra were recorded on a Bruker AC-P500 spectrometer (300 MHz) in CDCl3 medium at 25 °C with tetramethylsilane as the internal reference. Elemental analyses of C, H, and N were performed on a Perkin-Elmer 240C analyzer. The solid state circular dichroism (CD) spectrum was recorded on a JASCO J-810 spectropolarimeter. The X-ray powder diffraction (XRPD) was recorded on a Rigaku D/Max-2500 diffractometer at 40 kV, 100 mA for a Cu-target tube and a graphite monochromator. Thermal stability (TG-DTA) studies were carried out on a Dupont thermal analyzer from room temperature to 700 °C. Solid-state fluorescence spectra were recorded on a Cary Eclipse spectrofluorometer (Varian) equipped with a xenon lamp and quartz cuvette at room temperature.

X-Ray crystallography

X-Ray diffraction measurements were carried out on a Bruker Smart 1000 CCD diffractometer (for complexes 1–3, and 5), and Rigaku-RAXIS RAPID diffractometer (for 4 and 6), equipped with a graphite crystal monochromator situated in the incident beam for data collection. The determinations of unit cell parameters and data collections were performed with Mo Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å) and the unit cell dimensions were obtained with least-squares refinements. The program SAINT13 was used for integration of the diffraction profiles. All structures were solved by direct methods using the SHELXS program of the SHELXTL package and refined with SHELXL.14Ag atoms were found from E-maps and other non-hydrogen atoms were located in successive difference Fourier syntheses. The final refinement was performed by full matrix least-squares methods with anisotropic thermal parameters for non-hydrogen atoms on F2. The hydrogen atoms were added theoretically, riding on the concerned atoms and refined with fixed thermal factors. Results and discussion

Syntheses consideration and general characterizations

Recently, we investigated the coordination chemistry of 2,3-bis(triazol-1-ylmethyl)quinoxaline and the emission properties of the complexes with this ligand.11a Simultaneously, some complexes with a related ligand 1,4-bis(triazol-1-ylmethyl)benzene and their third-order nonlinear optical properties were reported.10 In order to further explore the influence of the ligand (with different N-dornor positions or different spacers) nature on the crystal structures of their metal complexes, so as to tune the coordination architectures of metal complexes by ligands design, a series of structurally related ligands (L1–L6) have been designed and synthesized. The treatment of ligandsL1–L6 with AgI salts yielded complexes 1–6, which were characterized by elemental analyses, IR spectroscopy, and X-ray single-crystal diffraction. Although all the reactions of AgI salts with the ligands were carried out in a molar ratio of 1 : 1, the isolated compounds had a different stoichiometry. The infrared spectra of complexes (1–6) all exhibit the characteristic absorptions of ligands (L1–L6) with a slight shift due to coordination. In 1, the absorption band appeared at 1054 cm−1, characteristic of the coordinated BF4− anion. The absorption bands at ca. 1400 cm−1 indicate the existence of the NO3− anion in 2, 3, 5, and 6.

Structure descriptions of complexes 1–6

{[Ag2(L1)2](BF4)2(H2O)} (1)

The structure of 1 consists of a discrete [Ag2(L1)2]2+, BF4−, and a water molecules. The perspective view of 1 is shown in Fig. 1a, and the selected bond distances and angles are listed in Table 2. The AgI ion lies on an inversion centre and the water molecule lies on a two-fold axis in 1. The coordination geometry around AgI could be described as a dicoordinated linear fashion with a bond angle of 175.3(2)° [N(3)–Ag(1)–N(4A), symmetry code: A −x + 3/2, −y + 1/2, −z]. All the Ag–N bond distances fall into the normal ranges for such coordination complexes.15L1 adopts a cis-conformation in 1 and the triazole rings in the same ligand are almost parallel to each other and they are almost vertical to the benzene ring. The dihedral angle between the planes of the two triazole groups is 5.36° and the dihedral angles between the planes of triazole and benzene rings are 83.28 and 87.36°, respectively. It should be noted that one N-donor of the triazole group does not coordinate to AgI in fact, but there is weak interaction between them (the Ag⋯N distance is 2.8083 Å). The adjacent discrete [Ag2(L1)2]2+ units are arranged into a 1D chain by the weak interactions (Fig. 1b). Additionally, the adjacent 1D chains further link into a 2D supramolecular network (Fig. 1c).

[Ag2(L2)(NO3)2]n (2)

Complex 2 forms a 3D coordination network. As shown in Fig. 2a, each Ag(1) center features a distorted tetrahedral geometry, coordinated by two nitrogen atoms from L2ligands and two oxygen atoms from NO3− [Ag(1)–N = 2.226(3)–2.23(3) Å, N–Ag(1)–N = 154.43(13)°, O–Ag(1)–O = 111.38(10)°]. Different to that of Ag(1), each Ag(2) center lies in a linear geometry coordinated by two N atoms from distinct L2ligands [Ag(2)–N = 2.231(3)–2.260(3) Å, N–Ag(2)–N = 144.72(12)°], and L2 acts as a tetradentate bridging ligand with a N(3)⋯N(6) distance of 10.748(5) Å. The two triazole groups display trans-conformation in 2 different from that of L1 in 1. The dihedral angle between the two triazole planes is 74.18°, and the dihedral angles between the planes of triazole and benzene rings are 88.01 and 70.16°, respectively.View of (a) the coordination environment of AgI in 2 (symmetry code: A: x − 1/2, −y + 3/2, −z + 1; B: −x + 1, y + 1/2, −z + 1/2; C: x + 1/2, −y + 3/2, −z + 1; D: −x + 1, y − 1/2, −z + 1/2; E: −x + 2, y − 1/2, −z + 1/2); (b) the (10,3)-a topological net in 2 with a 4·82 projection; (c) the (10,3)-a topological net in 2 with a 4·82 projection; (c) the (10,3)-a topological net in 2. The alternating right hand helix (yellow) along the a-axis is shown.

A better insight into the nature of the involved framework can be achieved by the application of a topological approach. In 2, each AgI ion is linked by two triazole groups of two L2ligands and each triazole group bridges two AgI ions, therefore, the AgI ions and L2ligands can be regarded as two three-connected building units and the whole network can thus be represented topologically by three-coordinate nodes. View of all the three planes of 2, it showed networks projecting as 4.82 nets. The whole structure can be extended to a non-interpenetrated (10,3)-a net (Fig. 2d), and the extended Schläfli symbol of this net is 105·105·105. An interesting feature of this (10,3)-a net is the presence of parallel single helices running through the structure, as marked by a yellow arrowhead in Fig. 2d. This net is constructed by octagonal left-handed and tetragonal right-handed helices along the a-axis. Each tetragonal helix connects with four adjacent octagonal helices, whereas each octagonal helix connects four tetragonal and four octagonal helices by sharing a common edge. All the left- and right-handed helices are alternatively arranged, but the 3D coordination network is chiral (the Flack parameter is −0.02(3)).

[Ag(L3)(NO3)]n (3)

3 consists of a polymeric cationic 1D chain and NO3− anions in which each AgI center is linked by two N donors from two distinct L3ligands [Ag–N = 2.129(3) Å] in a dicoordinated linear fashion (Fig. 3). In 3, L3 lies on an inversion centre, and all ligands are equivalent adopting trans-gauche conformations, and two triazole rings are parallel to each other in the same ligand, and the dihedral angle between the planes of triazole and benzene rings is 74.93°. The nearest non-bonding Ag⋯Ag distance connected by L3 is 13.934 Å. The N–Ag–N [180.00°] angle confirms linear environment around the AgI center (Fig. 3).View of the 1D polymeric chain in 3 (symmetry code: A: x − 1, y + 1, z).

{[Ag3(L4)](SiF6)1.5(H2O)3.5}n (4)

4 has a M3L2 cage-like architecture (Fig. 4a), in which two L4ligands take cis,cis,cis-conformation and a face to face orientation, and they are linked by three AgI ions through coordination bonds to form a trinuclear structure. The AgI ion has a two-coordinated near linear geometry, and the distances of Ag–N bonds are ranging from 2.070(17) Å to 2.130(18) Å, and the N–Ag–N angles are between 162.2(9) and 175.4(7)°. The distance between the central benzene rings is 10.603 Å and the intramolecular intermetallic Ag⋯Ag distance is 5.764, 6.904, and 7.359 Å, respectively. After carefully studying the packing diagram of the structure, it can be seen that there are weak interactions between the AgI ions of two adjacent trinuclear units, and the Ag⋯Ag distance is 2.986(4) Å [For Ag(3)⋯Ag(3A)]. In addition, the adjacent discrete trinuclear units are assembled into a 1D chain by Ag⋯Ag weak interactions (Fig. 4b).

{[Ag2(L5)2](NO3)2(H2O)} (5)

The structure of 5 consists of a centrosymmetric dinuclear [Ag2(L5)2]2+ unit, two NO3−, and one water molecule. The dinuclear [Ag2(L5)2]2+cation comprises two L2ligands and two AgI ions (Fig. 5a). Different from 1, 3, and 4, the coordination geometry around the AgI center could be described as a distorted tetrahedron with bond angles ranging from 71.15(13) to 158.10(16)°. All the Ag–N bond distances [2.278(4)–2.251(4) Å] are in the normal range.15 Each AgI centre coordinates to four N donors from two L5ligands forming four five-membered chelating cycles (Ag–N–C–C–N), namely Ag(1)–N(1)–C(5)–C(6)–N(3), Ag(1)–N(11)–C(51)–C(52)–N(3), Ag(2)–N(5)–C(23)–C(24)–N(6), Ag(2)–N(7)–C(33)–C(34)–N(8) with the N–Ag–N angles of 71.15(13), 71.84(14), 71.40(17), and 72.77(15)°, respectively. It should be noted that, due to the flexibility of the methylene group in the ligand, the pyridylpyrazole groups can turn freely to offer a suitable space for coordination to the metal centers. It is interesting that the two L5ligands in 5 have different conformations, one is cis and the other is trans. It can be seen that there are weak H-bonds between the NO3− and dinuclear units, and the discrete dinuclear units form a 1D supramolecular chain by C–H⋯O weak interactions (Fig. 5b).

{[Ag2(L6)2](NO3)2(CH3OH)2} (6)

6 consists of a discrete [Ag2(L6)2]2+ unit, two NO3−, and two methanol molecules. Different from that of 1, 3, and 4, each AgI center in 6 takes a slightly distorted trigonal planar geometry formed by two N donors and one O atom, two N donors from the benzimidazole rings of two L6, and O atom from the NO3− (Fig. 6a). The distances of Ag–N and Ag–O are 2.130(5) and 2.471(12) Å, respectively, and the N–Ag–N angle is 161.5(4)°. They are all in the normal range for such complexes.15 The conformation of L6 in 6 is cis, and the dihedral angles between the planes of the benzimidazole and anthracene rings are 86.93 and 87.88°, respectively. In addition, the adjacent dinuclear units are linked into a 1D chain by weak π–π interactions, and further form a 2D network (Fig. 6b).

The rational design and synthesis of metallosupramolecules in crystal engineering have drawn much attention in recent years, but it is still a long term challenge. As we know, the most common approach to build metal–organic frameworks is the rational combination of an organic ligand with appropriate coordination donors and metal ions with specific coordination geometry. In this work, six N-donor organic ligands (L1–L6) have been synthesized and six AgI complexes with these ligands have been obtained and structurally characterized. For L1, it forms a dinuclear complex with AgI, which further form a 2D supramolecular network by Ag⋯N weak interactions. Similar dinuclear structures have also been obtained by self-assembly of 1,2-bis(1-imidazol-ylmethyl)benzene (or other N-donor ligands) with AgI salts.16a,b Different from L1, the triazole groups are at 1,3- positions in L2, and all the N-donors coordinated to AgI ions in 2, which is a 3D chiral coordination polymer. While a similar bis-imidazole ligand [1-bromo-3,5-bis(imidazol-1-ylmethyl)benzene] reacts with AgNO3, to form a 1D coordination network, which is very different from that of complex 2.16c The triazole groups of L3 locate at 1,4- positions, and its AgI complex 3 is a 1D coordination polymer; if the triazole groups are replaced by imidazole groups, the AgI complex of 1,4-bis(imidazol-1-ylmethyl)benzene displays an infinite 2D structure.16dL4 was used as a three-connected ligand in 4, which forms a cage-like structure, similar to some reported AgI complexes because the coordination mode of L4 is similar to the organic ligands with three imidazole groups.16eL5 is one of bis-prazolylpyridine ligands, which often used to construct cage complexes.17a,b5 takes a dinuclear structure, when the prazolylpyridine locates at 1,8-positions of the naphthalene ring (comparing with 1,4-position in L5), a mononuclear complex was obtained in the literature.17cL6 contains the bulk anthracene spacer and two benzimidazole groups, when reacted with AgNO3, a dinuclear complex 6 was obtained. Similar discrete molecules have also been obtained by using a self-assembly of bis-benzimidazole ligands with AgI ion.17d These results show that the structures of the organic ligands play an important role in constructing metal–organic architectures.

Supramolecular isomerism often occurs in coordination networks, such as conformation isomerism,18 structural isomerism,19 topological isomerism,20 and so on. Supramolecular isomerism occurred between the finite ring and the polymeric chain has been observed, and was termed as ring opening isomerism.21 Recently, a flexible bis-bidentate ligand1,4-bis(8-thioquinolyl)butane (C4TQ) and its AgI complexes have been reported, ring-opening isomers (demeric M2L2 metallacycles and polymeric MnLn chains) were obtained, and the assembly mechanism was also studied.21a However in our present work, no ring opening isomerism of compounds 1 and 4–6 was obtained under our experimental conditions. More detailed studies of ligands (L1, L4–L6) and their complexes are still under way.

CD spectrum of the chiral complex 2

Chiral coordination polymers can be constructed by using chiral or achiral building blocks.22,23 In most cases, right-handed and left handed helices will be obtained in equal amounts as racemates when achiral building blocks are used. In some cases, however, spontaneous resolution into enantiomeric chiral crystals occurs.24 To identify whether the crystallization of the bulk crystals of 2 is racemic or enantiomeric excess, solid state CD measurements of single crystal and bulk crystals of 2 were carried out. The results indicate that a single crystal displays a strong dichroic signal but almost no signal is displayed for the bulk crystals of 2 (Fig. S3 in the ESI).† This indicates that the crystallization of the chiral complex 2 is racemic.

XRPD result, thermal, and luminescent properties of complex 2

In order to confirm the phase purity of the bulk materials, X-ray powder diffraction (XRPD) was carried out for 2. Although the experimental patterns (Fig. S1 in the ESI)† have a few un-indexed diffraction lines and some are slightly broadened in comparison with those simulated from the single-crystal model, it can still be well considered that the bulk synthesized material and the as-grown crystals are homogeneous for 2. The TGA curve of 2 showed that it is stable up to 200 °C, and begins to decompose upon heating up to about 520 °C (Fig. S2 in the ESI).†

Coordination polymers containing d10 metal ions have been found to be promising luminescent materials.25 The emission spectra of 2, and the free ligandL2 in the solid state at room temperature were investigated (Fig. 7). When excited at 278 nm, the main emission band of 2 was located at 383 nm, which may be assigned to the intra-ligand (n–π* or π–π*) emission because similar emission is observed at 378 nm for the free ligandL2 when excited at 276 nm.26

In summary, six new AgI coordination compounds with six well designed ligands were synthesized and structurally characterized. Single crystal structure analysis shows that 1, 5, and 6 possess dinuclear structures, and 4 takes a trinuclear cage, while 2 is a 3D coordination polymer, and 3 exhibits a 1D chain structure. These results show that the structures of AgI complexes could be well tuned by ligand modifications.

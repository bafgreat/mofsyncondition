Construction and optical properties of infinite Cd and finite Cu molecules stairs

Highlights

Two similar stairs shape metal organic frameworks have been synthesized.

Under the same conditions of auxiliary ligands, the distance between the “staircase” in the complex increases with the increase of the plane of the main ligand.

The luminescent properties of the reported MOFs have been investigated.

Abstract

Two coordination complexes, namely [(hpdq)(pta)Cd]n (1) and [(pptp)(pta)Cu2Cl] (2) have been synthesized by solvothermal method based on two polypyridyl ligands, 2,3,6,7,10,11-hexakis- (2-pyridyl)dipyrazino[2,3-f:2′,3′-h]quinoxaline) (hpdq), 4′-(4- (3H-pyrrol-3-yl)phenyl)- 2,2′:6′,2″- terpyridine (pptp) and auxiliary ligand p-phthalic acid (pta), respectively. Single crystal x-ray diffraction analyses reveal that complexes 1 and 2 assembled based on distinct asymmetric unit comprising one and two respective polypyridyl ligands but one Cd(II) and two Cu(I)ions, respectively. Among them, The asymmetric units in 1 was extended to one dimensional chain via the link of auxiliary ligand pta, just like infinite layers of stairs that connected by cadmium ions as the node. While that in 2 to Zero dimensional tetranuclear structure via the link of auxiliary ligand pta, just like finite four layers of stairs that Copper ion as the node connection. Furthermore, solid fluorescence spectra properties of two complexes were also investigated, and the result shows the fluorescence intensity of complex 1 is stronger than that of the hpdq ligand, but the fluorescence intensity of complex 2 is weaker than that of the pptp ligand. CCDC number of 1and 2 are 1483301 and 1483302.

1. Introduction

Because of the novel structures, intriguing topologies and potential applications in luminescence, electrochemical, magnetism, gas or liquid storage and separation, ion exchange, catalysis and so on [1], [2], [3], [4], [5], [6], [7]. The metal - organic frameworks (MOFs) have received considerable attention over the past twenty years. And many excellent results have been reported by researchers in many research fields such as medicinal chemistry, biochemistry, and materials chemistry etc [8], [9], [10], [11]. As we know, the crystal structure will be affected by many factors in the process of formation, such as solvent, pH, temperature and pressure etc [12], [13], [14], [15], [16]. In order to get the ideal structure of complexes, many attempts have been done by many excellent researchers under the crystal engineering principles [17], [18], [19], [20].

In our continuing efforts to design and synthesize new metal complexes with interesting structures and potential functions [21], [22], [23], we report our success construction of two new coordination complexes based on two polypyridyl ligands (hpdq and pptp) with Cd(II) and Cu(II).

2. Experimental

2.1. Materials and methods

All the starting materials for the synthesis were of reagent grade and used as received. All the solvents used for titration measurements were purified by standard procedures. Elemental analyses (C, H and N) were carried out on a Perkin-Elmer 240C analyzer. IR spectra in the 4000–400 cm−1 range were measured on a TENSOR 27 OPUS FT-IR spectrometer using KBr disks dispersed with sample powders. Fluorescence spectra were recorded at room temperature on a Varian Cary Eclipse fluorescence spectrometer.

2.2. Synthesis of compounds

2.2.1. Synthesis of (C50H38N12O9Cd)n (1)

In a beaker, 0.25 mmol hpdq dissolves in 10 mL dichloromethane, over which, a solution of Cd(NO3)2·4H2O (0.75 mmol) in CH3CN (10 mL) was carefully added, the mixed solution stirring at room temperature and yellow precipitate appeared after about 1 h; Yellow precipitate, pta (0.25 mmol), DMF (5 mL) and water (5 mL) were added in a Teflon-lined reactor; the reactor was heated at 120 °C for 72 h and then cooled to room temperature at a rate of 5 °C h−1. Light yellow crystals of complex 1 were obtained. Yield: 20% (based on hpdq), Anal. Calcd for (C50H38N12O9Cd)n: C, 56.45; H, 3.60; N, 15.81%. Found: C, 56.13; H, 3.15; N, 15.98. FT-IR (KBr pellets, cm−1): 2863m, 2776w, 2481w, 1557s, 1470w, 1405m, 1384m,1357m, 1024m, 892w, 788s, 764s.

2.2.2. Synthesis of (C126H104Cl2N18O20Cu4) (2)

A mixture of CuCl2 (0.5 mmol), pptp (0.25 mmol), pta (0.25 mmol) and 10 mL DMF were sealed in a Tef lon-lined reactor, the reactor was heated at 120 °C for 72 h and then cooled to room temperature at a rate of 5 °C h−1. Dark green crystals of complex 2 were obtained. Yield: 23% (based on pptp), Anal. Calcd for C126H104Cl2N18O20Cu4: C, 60.16; H, 4.17; N, 10.02%. Found: C, 60.55; H, 4.56; N, 10.48%. FT-IR (KBr pellets, cm−1): 1666w, 1604s, 1507w, 1390s, 1249s, 1170m, 1089w, 769w, 685w, 625w.

2.3. X-ray crystallography

Single-crystal X-ray diffraction measurements for 1 was carried out on a Rigaku SCX-mini diffractometer at 293 (2) K with Mo-Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å) by ω scan mode. The structures were solved by direct methods using the SHELXS program of the SHELXTL package and refined with SHELXL [24] (semi-empirical absorption corrections were applied by using the SADABS program). The non-hydrogen atoms were located in successive difference Fourier syntheses and refined using anisotropic thermal para-meters on F2. All hydrogen atoms of ligands were generated theoretically at the specific atoms and refined isotropically using fixed thermal factors. Complex 2 was collected on a Bruker SMART APEX-II CCD diffractometer equipped with a graphite-monochromated Mo-Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å) at 296 (2) K using an ω-φ scan mode. Absorption correction was applied by using the SADABS [25]. The structure was solved by direct methods and refined by full-matrix least-squares techniques on F2 with SHELX-97 package [26]. All non-hydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically and hydrogen atoms isotropically by full-matrix least-squares refinement. The detailed crystallographic data and structure refinement parameters of 1 and 2 are summarized in Table 1.

3. Results and discussion

3.1. Structural description

The result of X-ray diffraction analysis revealed that complex 1 crystallizes in triclinic system, space group P-1. The asymmetric unit consists of one Cd atoms, one hpdq ligand and a pta ligand. As depicted in Fig. 1a, Cd atom is surrounded by four N atoms (N1, N2, N3 and N4) from hpdq ligand, two O atoms (O2 and O3) from pta, and then an distorted octahedron configuration is formed. The N-Cd-N angles are in the range of 66.24 (19) to 133.26 (19)°, O-Cd-N angles are in the range of 76.6 (2) to 134.1 (2)°, O-Cd-O angles is 122.8 (3)°. The CdO bond lengths are in the range of 2.249(6) −2.271(6)Å, CdN bond lengths are in the range of 2.415(6)–2.585(6) Å. bond lengths are within the normal range. The adjacent asymmetric units are connected by auxiliary ligand pta, and then a one-dimensional chain was formed (see Fig. 1b). As the hpdq ligand we use has a good rigid plane, just like a stair rest platform; the auxiliary ligands pta are tilted in space, like a flight of stairs, and the two kinds of ligands are connected by Cd, then a infinite layers of molecular stairs was formed. Furthermore, we got the distance between the stairs is about 9.4152 Å by means of measurement.

X-ray diffraction study reveals that compound 2 crystallizes in the triclinic space group P-1. The asymmetric unit consists of two Cu atoms, one pptp ligand and one and a half pta ligand. As is shown in Fig. 2a, Cu1 atom is surrounded by three N atoms (N2, N3 and N4) from pptp ligand, one O atom (O4 and O5) from pta ligand, then an distorted tetragonal pyramid configuration is formed; Cu2 atom is surrounded by three N atoms N5, N6 and N7 from pptp ligand, two O atoms (O1) from pta ligand and one chlorine atom, then an distorted tetragonal pyramid configuration is formed. The N-Cu-N angles are in the range of 78.5 (3)°to 159.8 (3)°, O-Cu-N angles are in the range of 91.9 (3) to 170.1 (3)°, O-Cu-O angle is 93.7 (3)°, N-Cu-Cl angles are in the range of 95.3 (2) to 103.9 (2)°, O-Cu-Cl angle is 99.1 (2)°. The CuO bond lengths are in the range of 1.911(5)–2.188(6) Å, CuN bond lengths are in the range of 1.911(6)–2.015(8) Å, CuCl bond length is 2.481 (3) Å. bond lengths are within the normal range. Furthermore, the adjacent the adjacent asymmetric are connected by ligand pta, and then a 0-dimensional tetranuclear structure was formed (see Fig. 2b). Just like a stair rest platform the pptp ligand has a strong rigid plane and the auxiliary ligands pta like a flight of stairs that tilted in space, two kinds of ligands are connected by Cu, and then a finite four layers of molecular stairs was formed. Furthermore, we got the distance between the stairs is about 5.0426 Å by means of measurement.

3.2. Luminescent property

Emission behaviours of complexes 1, 2 and free ligand were investigated in the solid–state on a Varian Cary Eclipse fluorescence spectrometer at room temperature. As is shown in Fig. 3a, hpdq ligand exhibits a broad fluorescent emission centred on 289 nm with an excitation maximum at 220 nm. Under the same excitation, maximum emission peak of complex 1 was also occurred at 289 nm, this phenomenon is attributed to intraligand (n-π* or π-π*) because similar emission is observed for the free ligand hpdq [27], [28]. Furthermore, compare with free ligand hpdq the fluorescence intensity of complex 1 is stronger than that of the ligand, this can be due to the coordination interactions, which effectively increase the rigidity of the ligand and reduce the loss of energy by radiationless decay [29]. As depicted in Fig. 3b pptp ligand exhibits a broad fluorescent emission centred on 375 nm with an excitation maximum at 320 nm, and the complex 2 exhibits a broad fluorescent emission centred on 355 nm with an excitation maximum at 329 nm, However, compared with the pptp ligand the intensity of complex 2 is weaker than that of the ligand, and the reason is mainly due to the coordination interactions, heavy atom effect and metal ions with different reaction conditions etc.

4. Conclusion

Two novel infinite Cd and finite Cu MOF were obtained by solvothermal reaction. Complex 1 shows a one-dimensional chain which is like an infinite layers of Cd molecular stairs. And Complex 2 shows a 0-dimensional tetranuclear structure which is like a four layers of molecular stairs Cu molecular stairs. Fluorescence results show fluorescence intensity of complex 1 is enhancement but complex 2 is decreased than that of the ligand.

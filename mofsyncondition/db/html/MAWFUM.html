Syntheses, crystal structures and anion-exchange properties of novel coordination polymers with imidazole-containing tripodal ligands

Abstract

Six novel coordination polymers, [Cu(tib)2(N3)2] · 2H2O (1), [Cd(tib)2(H2O)2](NO3)2 (2), [Cu(dien)(tib)](ClO4)2 · tib · CH3CN (3), [Mn(ibimb)2(H2O)2]Cl2 · 2H2O (4), [Zn(titmb)2](NO3)2 · 2MeOH (5) and [Cd3(titmb)2Br6] (6), where tib = 1,3,5-tris(1-imidazolyl)benzene, dien = diethylenetriamine, ibimb = 1-(1-imidazolyl)-3,5-bis(imidazol-1-ylmethyl)benzene, titmb = 1,3,5-tris(imidazol-1-ylmethyl)-2,4,6-trimethylbenzene, were obtained by self-assembly of tripodal ligands with the corresponding metal salts, and their structures were determined by single crystal X-ray diffraction analyses. Complexes 1 and 2 have similar 2D network structure in which only two of three imidazole groups of each tib ligand coordinated to the metal atoms and the additional one did not participate in the coordination. Complex 3 has a 1D chain structure in which there are two kinds of tib ligands, one kind of tib serves as a two-connecting (bridging) ligand using its two arms. The other kind of tib acts as guest molecule filled in the void of cationic 1D chains. Complex 4 has an infinite 1D hinged chain structure and ligand ibimb serves as a two-connecting (bridging) ligand using its two flexible arms. Complex 5 has a 2D honeycomb network structure with (6,3) topology in which titmb acts as a three-connecting ligand. Complex 6 has an infinite 1D chain structure in which each titmb ligand connects three metal atoms. Ligand titmb has cis, trans, trans-conformation in 6, which is different from that in complex 5(cis, cis, cis). The anion-exchange properties of complex 5 were investigated.

1. Introduction

In recent years, there are great interests in construction of coordination architectures with novel structures and topologies due to their possible chemical and physical properties [1], [2]. Coordination architectures with specific topologies such as cage-like structures, honeycomb and interpenetrating networks have been obtained by self-assembly of suitable metal salts with rigid or flexible tripodal ligands, for example 1,3,5-tricyanobenzene [3], 2,4,6-tris(4-pyridyl)-1,3,5-triazine [4], 1,3,5-benzenetribenzoate [5], 1,3,5-tris(4-pyridylmethyl)benzene [6], 1,3,5-tris(benzimidazole-1-ylmethyl)-2,4,6-trimethylbenzene [7]. Accordingly, tripodal ligands with N donors, especially arene core-based tripodal ligands like mentioned above, are good candidates for the construction of coordination architecture [8]. In contrast to the rigid ligands with small or no conformational changes when they interact with metal salts, the flexible tripodal ligands have much more possible coordination modes due to their flexibility, and they can adopt different conformations according to the geometric requirements of different metal ions. Supramolecular architectures have attracted much attention not only because of their various structures, but also because of their potential properties [9], [10], [11], [12], [13]. For example, in our previous studies, we reported two tripodal ligands, 1,3,5-tris(1-imidazolyl)benzene (tib, Scheme 1) and 1,3-bis(1-imidazolyl)-5-(imidazol-1-ylmethyl)benzene (biimb, Scheme 1), and demonstrated that they can form organic–inorganic hybridized coordination complexes with reversible anion-exchange properties [10a].

We are interested in the reactions of tripodal ligands with various metal salts to investigate the construction, structure and property of coordination architecture [10]. However, at present, the self-assembly process depends on many and/or even subtle factors and is often difficult to predict exactly [11]. This encouraged us to undertake further studies on the reactions of metal salts with rigid or flexible tripodal ligands. As shown in Scheme 1, three tripodal ligands 1,3,5-tris(1-imidazolyl)benzene (tib), 1-(1-imidazolyl)-3,5-bis(imidazol-1-ylmethyl)benzene (ibimb) and 1,3,5-tris(imidazol-1-ylmethyl)-2,4,6-trimethylbenzene (titmb) were employed in this study. Six non-interpenetrating organic-inorganic hybridized coordination complexes, [Cu(tib)2(N3)2] · 2H2O (1), [Cd(tib)2(H2O)2] (NO3)2 (2), [Cu(dien)(tib)](ClO4)2 · tib · CH3CN (3), [Mn(ibimb)2 (H2O)2]Cl2 · 2H2O (4), [Zn(titmb)2](NO3)2 · 2MeOH (5) and [Cd3(titmb)2Br6] (6) were successfully isolated, their structures and anion-exchange behavior of complex 5 were investigated.

2. Experimental section

2.1. Materials and measurements

All commercially available chemicals are of reagent grade and obtained from commercial suppliers. De-ionized water and DMSO are used as received without further purification. Methanol and ethanol were dried and purified by distillation before use [14]. C, H and N analyses were made on a Perkin–Elmer 240C elemental analyzer at the analysis center of Nanjing University. Infrared (IR) spectra were recorded on a Bruker Vector22 FT-IR spectrophotometer by using KBr discs. X-ray powder diffraction patterns were taken on a Rigaku D/max-RA rotating anode X-ray diffractometer with graphite-monochromatic Cu–Kα (λ = 1.542 Å) radiation at room temperature.

2.2. Synthesis

2.2.1. [Cu(tib)2(N3)2] · 2H2O (1)

An aqueous solution (5 ml) of [Cu(en)2]SO4 · 2H2O (22.2 mg, 0.05 mmol, en = NH2CH2CH2NH2) and an aqueous solution (5 ml) of NaN3 (6.5 mg, 0.1 mmol) were combined, refluxed and stirred for about 8 h. The aqueous solution was cooled down to room temperature and filtrated, then a solution of tib (13.8 mg, 0.05 mmol) in methanol (10 ml) was carefully layered over the aqueous solution at room temperature. About two weeks later, green block crystals suitable for X-ray analysis were obtained in 40% yield. Anal. calcd. for C30H28CuN18O2 (736.24): C, 48.94; H, 3.83; N, 34.24%. Found: C, 49.00; H, 3.75; N, 34.11%.

2.2.2. [Cd(tib)2(H2O)2](NO3)2 (2)

This complex was prepared by slow diffusion between two layers of an aqueous solution (10 ml) of Cd(NO3)2 · 4H2O (15.4 mg, 0.05 mmol) and tib (13.8 mg, 0.05 mmol) in ethanol (10 ml) at room temperature. About three weeks later, pale-yellow block crystals suitable for X-ray analysis were obtained in 50% yield. Anal. calcd. for C30H28CdN14O8 (825.06): C, 43.67; H, 3.42; N, 23.77%. Found: C, 43.60; H, 3.37; N, 23.65%.

2.2.3. [Cu(dien)(tib)](ClO4)2 · CH3CN (3)

In a typical synthesis, an aqueous solution (5 ml) of [Cu(dien)im](ClO4)2 (43.4 mg, 0.1 mmol, dien = NH2CH2CH2NHCH2CH2NH2, im = imidazole) was added slowly to a solution of tib (55.2 mg, 0.2 mmol) in methanol (20 ml) and the mixture was stirred for 5 h at room temperature to give a blue powder with ≈70% yield. Single crystals suitable for X-ray analysis were obtained by re-crystallization from acetonitrile solution. Anal. calcd. for C36H40Cl2CuN16O8 (959.28): C, 45.08; H, 4.20; N, 23.36%. Found: C, 45.35; H, 4.25; N, 23.29%.

2.2.4. [Mn(ibimb)2(H2O)2]Cl2 · 2H2O (4)

An aqueous solution (5 ml) of MnCl2 · 4H2O (9.9 mg, 0.05 mmol) and an aqueous solution (5 ml) of ibimb (15.2 mg, 0.05 mmol) were combined, refluxed and stirred for about 8 h. The aqueous solution was cooled down to room temperature and filtered. About 7 days later, colorless platelet crystals suitable for X-ray analysis were obtained in 60% yield. Anal. calcd. for C34H44Cl2MnN12O6 (842.65): C, 48.46; H, 5.26; N, 19.95%. Found: C, 48.82; H, 5.30; N, 19.90%.

2.2.5. [Zn(titmb)2](NO3)2 · 2MeOH (5)

The complex was prepared by slow diffusion between two layers of a solution of titmb (18.0 mg, 0.05 mmol) in DMSO (10 ml) and Zn(NO3)2 · 6H2O (14.9 mg, 0.05 mmol) in methanol (10 ml) at room temperature. About 5 days later, colorless platelet crystals suitable for X-ray analysis were obtained in 40% yield. Anal. calcd. for C44H56ZnN14O8 (974.40): C, 54.24; H, 5.79; N, 20.12%. Found: C, 54.35; H, 5.78; N, 20.05%.

2.2.6. [Cd3(titmb)2Br6] (6)

The title complex was also prepared by slow diffusion between two layers of an aqueous solution (10 ml) of CdBr2 · 4H2O (17.2 mg, 0.05 mmol) and titmb (18.0 mg, 0.05 mmol) in methanol (10 ml) at room temperature. Single crystals suitable for X-ray analysis were obtained in 60% yield three weeks later. Anal. calcd. for C42H48Br6Cd3N12 (1537.58): C, 32.81; H, 3.15; N, 10.93%. Found: C, 32.92; H, 3.10; N, 10.84%.

Safety note: Perchlorate and azide salts of metal complexes with organic ligands are potentially explosive and should be handled with care.

2.3. General procedures for anion exchange

Well-ground powder of [Zn(titmb)2](NO3)2 · 2MeOH (5, 200.0 mg) was suspended in water (20 ml), then NaClO4 (1.5 g) was added. The mixture was stirred for one day at room temperature, then filtered, washed with water several times, and dried. Anal. calcd. for the anion exchanged product [Zn(titmb)2](ClO4)2 [C42H48ZnN12O8Cl2]: C, 51.20; H, 4.91; N, 16.06%. Found: C, 51.02; H, 4.96; N, 16.02%.

2.4. Crystal structure determination

The collections of crystallographic data for the complexes 1–6 were carried out on a Rigaku RAXIS-RAPID Imaging Plate diffractometer at 200 K, using graphite-monochromated Mo-Kα radiation (λ = 0.7107 Å). The structures were solved by direct methods with SIR92 [15] and expanded using Fourier technique [16]. All non-Hydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically by the full-matrix least-squares method. The hydrogen atoms except for those of water molecules were generated geometrically. In complex 5, each of two oxygen atoms of two methanol molecules disordered into two positions with site occupancy factors (SOF) of 0.542(15) for O201, 0.458(15) for O202, 0.552(13) for O203 and 0.448(13) for O204, respectively. All calculations were carried out on a SGI workstation using the teXsan crystallographic software package of Molecular Structure Corporation [17]. Details of the crystal parameters, data collection and refinement are summarized in Table 1, and selected bond lengths and angles with their estimated standard deviations are listed in Table 2.

3. Results and discussion

3.1. Crystal structure description

3.1.1. [Cu(tib)2(N3)2] · 2H2O (1)

The polymeric structure of 1 was confirmed by X-ray single crystal structure determination. The molecular structure of 1with atom numbering scheme is exhibited in Fig. 1a and the complex crystallizes in monoclinic space group P21/n. It is clear that the copper(II) center sits on the inversion center and coordinates to four N atoms from four different tib ligands with N–Cu–N bond angle of 90.29(5)°, and Cu–N bond distances of 2.0242(19) and 2.031(2) Å, respectively (Table 2). And the Cu–N bond lengths are comparable those of reported copper(II) complexes [10](a), [18](a). The copper(II) atom is on the plane composed of N12, N32, N12A and N32A without deviation. Two additional positions are occupied by two N atoms from two different azide anions with N–Cu–N bond angle of 180.0° and Cu–N bond distances of 2.50 Å. Therefore, the local coordination geometry of the Cu(II) center is a slightly distorted octahedron with a N6 donor set. Because of the rigidity of the ligand, tib cannot coordinate to the metal center comfortably to meet the geometric requirement of Cu(II). As a result, each tib ligand coordinates to two copper atoms rather than three, and the third imidazole group does not participate in the coordination. Namely, ligand tib in 1 acts as a two-connecting (bridging) ligand, which is similar to the previously reported complex [Cu(tib)2(H2O)2](CH3COO)2 · 2H2O obtained by reaction of Cu(CH3COO)2 · H2O with ligand tib[22]. The three imidazol rings of tib ligand in 1 are inclined to the central benzene ring at angles of 23.0°, 36.3° and 37.5°, respectively. As shown in Fig. 1b, four Cu(II) atoms (e.g., Cu1A, Cu1C, Cu1D, and Cu1E) and four molecules of ligand tib form a 40-membered macrocyclic ring, which is further linked by Cu–N bonds to give a 2D network structure. The intermetallic distances within one layer of Cu1A⋯Cu1C = Cu1C⋯Cu1D = Cu1D⋯Cu1E =  Cu1E⋯Cu1A, Cu1A⋯Cu1D and Cu1C⋯Cu1E are 11.16, 12.01 and 18.82 Å, respectively (Fig. 1b). The Cu⋯Cu distances between two adjacent layers is 6.78 Å. In complex 1, the lattice water molecules are located in the voids between two adjacent layers and held there by O–H⋯N (azide) hydrogen bonds to give a 3D network (Fig. 1c). 3.1.2. [Cd(tib)2(H2O)2](NO3)2 (2)

As listed in Table 1, similar cell parameters and the same space group P21/n of complexes 1 and 2 imply that they are isomorphous. The difference between 1 and 2 is that two water molecules coordinated to the cadmium(II) atom at the apical positions in 2, instead of azide anions in 1, thus the coordination environment of Cd(II) atom in 2 is a slightly distorted octahedron with a N4O2 donor set. Similar coordination mode makes complex 2 also have an infinite 2D network structure with (4,4) topology considering the tib ligands and Cd(II) atoms as two connecting rods and nodes, respectively, as illustrated in Fig. 2a. The edge and diagonal lengths of the rhombus formed by four Cd(II) atoms are 11.69 (Cd1B⋯Cd1L = Cd1L⋯Cd1E = Cd1E⋯Cd1A = Cd1A⋯ Cd1B), 11.77 (Cd1B⋯Cd1E) and 20.21 (Cd1A⋯Cd1L) Å, respectively (Fig. 2a). Similar crystal packing was found in the complexes 1 and 2 (Fig. 2b). In complex 2, the uncoordinated nitrate anions are located in the voids between two adjacent layers and held there by C–H⋯O and O–H⋯O hydrogen bonds to give a 3D network (Fig. 2b, Table 3).

3.1.3. [Cu(dien)(tib)](ClO4)2 · tib · CH3CN (3)

The crystallographic study provides direct evidence for the structure of 3. As listed in Table 1, the complex crystallizes in monoclinic space group P21/n and Fig. 3a shows the asymmetric unit of 3 with an atom numbering scheme. It is interesting that the repeat unit of 3 contains two molecules of tib ligands, one of which coordinated to the Cu(II) atoms using its imidazol N atoms (tib-B), while the other one is free of coordination (tib-A). Each copper(II) atom is coordinated by five N atoms, two of which are from two different tib ligands and the other three are from a dien molecule (Fig. 3b). Therefore, the local coordination geometry of the Cu(II) center can be described as a tetragonal pyramid with a N5 donor set. The Cu–N bonds at equatorial plane have very similar bond lengths ranging from 2.009(3) to 2.028(2) Å, which are comparable to those observed in complex 1 and other reported copper(II) complexes [10](a), [18](a), while the one at apical position shows elongated bond length of Cu1–N32 = 2.199(2) Å. The N–Cu–N coordination angles between cis N atoms range from 83.29(10) to 102.14(10) Å, respectively, and the N–Cu–N coordination angles between trans N atoms are 158.04(9)° and 166.57(9)° (Table 2). The Cu1 atom locates 0.28 Å out of the plane formed by N12, N1, N2 and N3 atoms. For ligand tib-A, the three imidazolyl rings are inclined to the phenyl ring with angles of 8.7°, 13.0° and 19.2°, respectively, and the corresponding angles are 10.0°, 20.7°, 27.4° for ligand tib-B. Two copper(II) atoms are linked by ligand tib-B to form a 1D wave-like chain structure with metal–metal separations (Cu⋯Cu) of 11.71 Å (Fig. 3b). It is noticeable that ligand tib-B has only two of three imidazole groups coordinated with metal atoms and the additional one does not participate in the coordination with the Cu(II) atoms in complex 3, therefore tib-B acted as a two-connecting ligand. The nearest centroid–centroid distance between the two benzene ring planes of ligands tib-A and tib-B is 3.78 Å with a dihedral angle of 2.3°, which reveals the existence of face-to-face π–π interactions in complex 3. Therefore ligand tib-A is accommodated in the voids between the 1D chain structure through π–π interactions and hydrogen bonds (Fig. 3c and Table 3). The perchlorate anions and the uncoordinated acetonitrile molecules are also located in the voids formed between two adjacent 1D chains. As shown in Fig. 3d, the perchlorate anions and the acetonitrile molecules are held there by O–H⋯N, C–H⋯O and N–H⋯N hydrogen bonds as tabulated in Table 3.

3.1.4. [Mn(ibimb)2(H2O)2]Cl2 · 2H2O (4)

The title complex crystallizes in the monoclinic form with P21/n space group and the coordination environment around the manganese(II) center in complex 4 is shown in Fig. 4a, where the metal atom sits on the inversion center. Each Mn1 center coordinates to four N atoms from different ibimb ligands with N–Mn–N bond angle of 86.97(4)°, and Mn–N bond distances of 2.2201(12) and 2.2622(12) Å, respectively (Table 2), which are comparable to those of other reported manganese(II) complexes [10](a), [19](b), [20]. The Mn1 atom locates within the plane defined by N32, N32A, N52 and N52A atoms without deviation. Two additional positions are occupied by two O atoms from two water molecules with Mn–O bond distance of 2.2069(10) Å (Table 2). Therefore, the local coordination geometry of the Mn(II) center is a slightly distorted octahedron with an N4O2 donor set. Each ibimb ligand in turn binds to two Mn(II) atoms using its two flexible arms to generate an infinite 1D hinged chain structure (Fig. 4b) and ligand ibimb has cis-conformation (Scheme 2) [18]. It is noteworthy that ligand ibimb in complex 4 has only two of three imidazole groups coordinated with metal atoms and the additional one keeps free of coordination. For ligand ibimb, the three imidazolyl rings are inclined to the phenyl ring with angles of 33.5°, 82.7° and 87.8°, respectively. Intra-chain metal–metal distances in complex 4 are 12.88 Å. The two benzene rings from two opposite ibimb ligands in Mn2(ibimb)2 macrocyclic ring adopt a face-to-face conformation and are strictly parallel each other with a centroid–centroid distance of 6.29 Å. The 1D chains were further connected by O–H⋯Cl, O–H⋯O, O–H⋯N, C–H⋯Cl and C–H⋯O hydrogen bonds to form a 3D structure (Fig. 4c, Table 3).

3.1.5. [Zn(titmb)2](NO3)2 · 2MeOH (5)

To further investigate the influence of the flexibility of organic ligands on the structure of coordination polymers, reactions of metal salts with an flexible tripodal ligand titmb were carried out and complexes 5 and 6 were isolated. The polymeric structure of 5 was confirmed by X-ray single crystal structure analysis. The complex crystallizes in monoclinic space group P21/c and Fig. 5a shows the asymmetric unit of 5 with atom numbering scheme. Each zinc(II) atom is coordinated by six imidazole N atoms derived from six different titmb ligands and the coordination geometry of the Zn(II) center is a slightly distorted octahedron with coordination angles (N–Zn–N) between cis N atoms ranging from 86.17(17)° to 96.07(16)° and between trans N atoms ranging from 176.67(18)° to 179.29(17)°, and the Zn–N bond lengths varying from 2.168(5) to 2.205(4) Å (Table 2). The Zn–N bond lengths are slightly longer than those of other reported zinc(II) complexes [18](b), [19](a). In addition, the asymmetric unit of 5 contains two titmb ligands (titmb-A and titmb-B in Fig. 5a). Each ligand titmb in turn connects three Zn(II) atoms, which form a triangle with edge lengths (Zn⋯Zn) of 10.08, 10.37 and 10.48 Å, respectively for ligand titmb-A, and the corresponding edge lengths of 10.08, 10.68, 10.78 Å for ligand titmb-B. The three imidazolyl rings are inclined to the phenyl ring with angles of 77.1°, 83.3° and 95.0°, respectively for titmb-A, and the corresponding angles of 81.1°, 82.5°, 93.5° for ligand titmb-B. As shown in Fig. 5b, such coordination mode makes the compound a 2D network with honeycomb structure, and a schematic drawing is shown in Fig. 5c. It is obvious that all titmb ligands in the network have cis, cis, cis-conformation with up- and down-orientations alternatively (Scheme 2) [18]. The crystal packing diagram of complex 5 is illustrated in Fig. 5d. The nitrate anions and the uncoordinated methanol molecules are located in the voids formed between two adjacent layers. As shown in Fig. 5d, the nitrate anions are held there by C–H⋯O hydrogen bonds as tabulated in Table 3.

3.1.6. [Cd3(titmb)2Br6] (6)

The title complex crystallized in orthorhombic space group Pbcn and the coordination environment around the cadmium(II) center in complex 6 is shown in Fig. 6a. The asymmetric unit of 6 contains two distinct Cd(II) centers, in which Cd1 locates at a special position (Fig. 6a). In complex 6, Cd1 atom is coordinated by two imidazolyl groups from two different titmb ligands with the N–Cd–N angle of 101.52(19)° and Cd–N bond distance of 2.234(3) Å, respectively. The Cd–N bond length is slightly shorter than those of other reported Cd(II) complexes [18](b), [19](a). Two additional positions are occupied by bromide anions with a Br–Cd–Br bond angle of 111.76(9)° and Cd–Br bond distances of 2.537(2) Å (Table 2). Therefore the coordination environment of the Cd1(II) atom can be described as a distorted tetrahedron with an N2Br2 donor set. The coordination geometry of Cd2 atom is essentially the same as that of the Cd1 atom except that Cd–N bond distances are 2.245(4) and 2.261(4) Å, Cd–Br bond distances are 2.5089(18)° and 2.550(3) Å, N–Cd–N and Br–Cd–Br bond angles are 99.06(13)° and 127.70(2)°, respectively. In addition, the ligand titmb connects three cadmium(II) atoms in cis, trans, trans-conformation [18], serving as a three-connecting ligand and the three imidazolyl rings are inclined to the phenyl ring at angles of 90.1°, 93.9° and 95.3°, respectively. Two Cd2 atoms and two titmb ligands form a macrocyclic ring with a Cd–Cd distance of 12.27 Å and the rings are further linked by Cd1 atoms to give an infinite 1D chain structure in which Cd1–Cd2 metal–metal distances are 9.77 and 13.94 Å, respectively (Fig. 6b). Infinite 1D chains are repeated and joined by C–H⋯Br hydrogen bonds with C⋯Br distances in the range of 3.630(7)–3.832(8) Å (Table 3) to give a 3D structure for complex 6 as illustrated in Fig. 6c.

3.2. Anion exchange properties of complex 5

As revealed by the crystal structure analysis of 5, the nitrate anions were located within the open structure between the 2D layers through hydrogen bonds (Fig. 5d). Since complex 5 is insoluble in common solvents, this 2D layered compound is expected to display anion-exchange properties. The FT-IR spectra of the exchanged solid and the original 5 are shown in Fig. 7b and 7a, respectively. Intense bands around 1094 cm−1 appeared, which originate from the ClO4- ion, while the intense bands ranging from 1384 to 1356 cm−1 of the NO3- ion disappeared in the spectrum of exchanged solid (Fig. 7b). Furthermore, the results of elemental analysis of the anion exchanged product also indicated a complete anion exchange, that is, [Zn(titmb)2](ClO4)2. The anion-exchange reactions were also monitored by X-ray powder diffraction (XPD) techniques (Fig. 8). The similar XPD patterns observed for the exchanged product suggest that the framework of the complex is retained. As discussed by Yip and co workers. [21], the exchange of NO3- by ClO4- is favored since the hydration energy (ΔGh) of NO3- (−300 kJ mol−1) is higher than that of ClO4- (−205 kJ mol−1).

4. Discussion

We have shown that when the ligand tib reacted with [Cu(en)2]SO4 · 2H2O and NaN3 or with Cd(NO3)2 · 4H2O, one kind of structure was generated, and a previously reported complex [Cu(tib)2(H2O)2](CH3COO)2 · 2H2O obtained by reaction of Cu(CH3COO)2 · H2 O with ligand tib is similar with complexes 1 and 2 concerning the space group, network structure, and crystal packing, despite the six-coordinated Cu(II) atoms with two additional coordination sites of each metal atom occupied by two O atoms from two water molecules in [Cu(tib)2(H2O)2](CH3COO)2 · 2H2O [22]. The results indicate that the structures of assemblies are mainly depending on the nature of the organic ligand but are not influenced significantly by the anions and reaction conditions in the system with rigid tripodal ligand. When the ligand tib reacted with [Cu(dien)im](ClO4)2, dien occupied three coordination sites of the five-coordinated Cu(II) atoms, therefore the formation of 1D chain structure in 3 is favored by the introduction of dien to the self-assembly of coordination polymers. In the case of complex 4, when ligand ibimb reacted with the six-coordinated Mn(II) atoms, 1D hinged chain structure was obtained and each ligand ibimb coordinates to two Mn(II) atoms as a bidentate ligand rather than a tridentate one, which may be due to the rigid arm of ligand ibimb. The results also reveal that the nature of organic ligand has a great influence on the structure of coordination polymers.

The difference between ligands titmb and tib or ibimb is that the former is more flexible than the latter. Flexible ligand titmb can adjust its conformations to fit with the geometrical needs of metal ions. For example, in complex 5, ligand titmb adopts cis, cis, cis-conformation to give a 2D honeycomb network, while in complexes 1, 2, 3 and 4 obtained by reactions of corresponding metal salts with ligand tib or ibimb, each ligand has only two of three imidazole groups coordinated with metal atoms and the additional one keeps free of coordination. Such a difference was caused by the introduction of methylene groups between the imidazole and central benzene groups in titmb. In addition to the role of organic ligands, counter anions with different shape, and coordination ability also have remarkable impact on the structure of coordination polymers, e.g., in complex 6, bromide anions occupied two coordination sites of the four-coordinated Cd(II) atoms, which showed that the introduction of the halogen atom to the reaction may, to a certain extent, affect the resulting structure, which provides opportunities for the construction of architectures with novel structure and topology. It is noteworthy that in complex 6, ligand titmb has cis, trans, trans-conformation and lead to the 1D chain network structure with cyclic dinuclear rings.

  • Fe(III)-containing mesoporous poly-(p-phenylenediamine): Synthesis, characterization and magnetic properties

    2011, Microporous and Mesoporous Materials
    Citation Excerpt :

    Porous organic polymers [1] have huge possibilities when it can be coupled with semiconductor nanostructures [2] exploiting the void space and this can find novel applications in optoelectronics [3,4], light-harvesting [5,6] and so on. Introducing porosity in polymeric networks containing metal atoms on the other hand can find numerous potential applications in gas storage [7–9], ion exchange [10], sensors [11], drug delivery [12], magnetic materials [13], optoelectronics [14], etc. Compared to their inorganic analog, zeolites [15] and related mesoporous materials [16,17], the organic building blocks in porous polymers have more versatile potential to tune the pore architecture and functionality [18].

    A new mesoporous polymer constituting of Fe(III)-poly-(p-phenylenediamine) (Fe-MPPD) has been synthesized by using the supramolecular assembly of anionic surfactant sodium dodecylsulphate (SDS) as template under mild conditions. The polymeric framework resulted via simultaneous oxidative polymerization of p-phenylenediamine (PPD) and cross-linking in the presence of Fe(III) ion using water as reaction medium. Upon removal of template SDS molecules through solvent extraction this material has retained the 2D-hexagonal nanostructure. The presence of Fe(III) in the polymeric material has been confirmed by atomic absorption spectroscopy (AAS, 18.3 and 10.2 wt% for two samples) and 57Fe Mössbauer spectroscopy. The temperature dependence of zero-field cooled magnetization showed spin freezing temperature at 3.5 K, below which there is a large coercivity due to significant contribution of surface anisotropy. The porosity and the magnetic property of the material are found to be dependent on the molar ratio Fe(III):PPD and SDA content.

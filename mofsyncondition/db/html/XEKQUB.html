Silver(I)/cobalt(II) complexes with mixed V-shaped polycarboxylate and rod-like N-donor ligands: Syntheses, structures and properties

Author links open overlay panel
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ica.2012.07.008Get rights and content

Abstract

Four Ag(I)/Co(II) complexes based on a flexible V-shaped ligand 3,3′, 4,4′-diphenylsulfone tetracarboxylic acid (H4dstc), namely [Ag(bpe)2]·H3dstc (1), {[Ag2(H2dstc)(bpp)(H2O)]·H2O}n (2), {[Co2(dstc)(bpe)2(H2O)2]·2H2O}n (3) and {[Co4(dstc)2(bpa)3(H2O)6]·7H2O}n (4) have been synthesized in the presence of different rod-like N-donor ligands 1,2-di(4-pyridyl)ethylene (bpe), 1,3-bis(4-pyridyl)propane (bpp), and 1,2-bis(4-pyridyl)ethane (bpa). In complex 1, the H3dstc– ligand does not take part in coordination but balances the charge by its one deprotonated carboxylate group. Complex 2 contains two different motifs: a cationic 1D chain [Ag(bpp)]+ and an anionic 1D meso-helix [Ag(H2dstc)(H2O)]–. Interestingly, the non-covalent hydrogen bonding interactions extend such simple discrete units or 1D chains into 3D supramolecular architectures. Complex 3 represents a 3D binodal 4-connected (64·82) network, while complex 4 can be simplified as a primitive cubic (pcu) network. In addition, the photoluminescence of 1 and 2 and the magnetism of 3 and 4 are investigated, respectively.

1. Introduction

The rational design and construction of metal–organic frameworks (MOFs) with intriguing structural motifs, unique chemical and physical properties have been attracting extensive interests in supramolecular chemistry and material chemistry [1]. The silver(I) or cobalt(II) complexes with mixed ligands have been a focused research subject by virtue of their versatile structures and excellent luminescent or magnetic properties [2]. However, it is a great challenge in controlling the final architectures of the products, because the self-assembly process is frequently influenced by several factors [3]. In this process, the appropriate selection of organic ligands may be the most effective and facile approach, because the structural changes in organic building blocks such as length, flexibility, and symmetry may lead to diverse structural motifs. Currently, enormous attentions have been paid to V-shaped organic ligands with carboxylic groups, owning to their various coordination modes, longer backbones, and relative flexibility of the multifarious spacers [4], [5], [6], [7].

As a flexible V-shaped carboxylate ligand, 3,3′, 4,4′-diphenylsulfonetetracarboxylic acid (H4dstc) can be regarded as a good candidate to build new coordination structures. On the other hand, considering the varieties of topologies and properties, the employment of mixed ligands in the self-assembly process has gradually become a popular method used in crystal engineering. Up to now, in the presence of different auxiliary N-donor ligands, several complexes based on H4dstc have been isolated by us and other groups, with the structures ranging from 1D linear chains to 3D architectures [8]. Among these dstc-containing complexes, only three Ag(I) and Co(II) species of rigid/chelating neutral ligands (4,4′-bipyridine, 2,2′:6′,2″-terpyridine and 1,10-phenanthroline) have been reported. These rigid/chelating neutral ligands play the critical roles in the self-assembly via π···π staking to generate high-dimensional supramolecular architectures. It is well known that N-donor ligands with different size, conformation and degree of freedom, etc. may result in different structural motifs. Herein, we select three longer rod-like N-donor ligands, 1,2-di(4-pyridyl)ethylene (bpe), 1,3-bis(4-pyridyl)propane (bpp) and 1,2-bis(4-pyridyl)ethane (bpa), as the secondary ligands, aiming to prepare new dstc-containing complexes. Successfully, four new Ag(I)/Co(II) complexes, [Ag(bpe)2]·H3dstc (1), {[Ag2(H2dstc)(bpp)(H2O)]·H2O}n (2), {[Co2(dstc)(bpe)2(H2O)2]·2H2O}n (3) and {[Co4(dstc)2(bpa)3(H2O)6]·7H2O}n (4) were synthesized. Complex 1 is a 0D discrete unit, complex 2 contains two unique 1D chains, complex 3 displays a 3D 4-connected (64·82) network and complex 4 represents a pcu topological network. In addition, complexes 1 and 2 exhibit different luminescent properties in the solid state at room temperature. Magnetic analyses indicate that there exist predominantly antiferromagnetic interactions in 3 and 4.

2. Experimental

2.1. Reagents and physical measurements

All reagents and solvents employed were commercially available and used as received without further purification. Elemental analyses were performed on a Perkin–Elmer 240C analyzer. Infrared spectra on KBr pellets were recorded on Bruker EQUINOX-55 FT-IR spectrophotometer in the range of 4000–400 cm−1. Thermal analyses were performed on a NETZSCH STA 449C microanalyzer with a heating rate of 10 °C min−1 under N2 atmosphere. The X-ray powder diffraction patterns were recorded with a Pigaku D/Max 3III diffractometer. Luminescent spectra for the solid samples were registered on a Hitachi F-4500 fluorescence spectrophotometer at room temperature. The magnetic susceptibility of microcrystalline sample restrained in parafilm was measured on a Quantum Design MPMS-XL7 SQUID magnetometer with an applied field of 1 kOe.

2.2. Synthesis of [Ag(bpe)2]·H3dstc (1)

A mixture of AgNO3 (0.1 mmol, 17.0 mg), 3,3′,4,4′-diphenylsulfonetetracarboxylic dianhydride (DSPA) (0.025 mmol, 9.0 mg), bpe (0.025 mmol, 4.5 mg) and water (15 mL) was stirred for 20 min in air, then sealed in a 25 mL Teflon reactor, which was heated at 120 °C for 72 h. Colorless crystals were obtained when the solution was cooled to room temperature at a rate of 5 °C h−1. Yield: 55% based on Ag. Anal. Calc. for C40H29AgN4O10S (865.61): C, 55.50; H, 3.38; N, 6.47%. Found: C, 55.33; H, 3.27; N, 6.39%. IR (KBr, cm−1): 3421(s), 2926(w), 1608(s), 1493(m), 1424(m), 1022(m), 793(w).

2.3. Synthesis of {[Ag2(H2dstc)(bpp)(H2O)]·H2O}n (2)

A mixture of AgNO3 (0.1 mmol, 17.0 mg), 3,3′,4,4′-diphenylsulfonetetracarboxylic dianhydride (DSPA) (0.025 mmol, 9.0 mg), bpp (0.025 mmol, 5.0 mg) and water (15 mL) was stirred for 20 min in air, then sealed in a 25 mL Teflon reactor, which was heated at 130 °C for 72 h. The solution was cooled to room temperature at a rate of 5 °C h−1, and then filtered and stood for about one month, colorless crystals were obtained. Yield: 50% based on Ag. Anal. Calc. for C29H26Ag2N2O12S (842.32): C, 42.35; H, 3.11; N, 3.32%. Found: C, 42.15; H, 3.16; N, 3.40%. IR (KBr, cm−1): 3442(s), 3033(w), 1579(s), 1480(s), 1373(s), 1301(s), 826(s), 776(s), 735(s).

2.4. Synthesis of {[Co2(dstc)(bpe)2(H2O)2]·2H2O}n (3)

A mixture of Co(OAc)2·4H2O (0.05 mmol, 12.5 mg), 3,3′,4,4′-diphenylsulfonetetracarboxylic dianhydride (DSPA) (0.05 mmol, 17.9 mg), bpe (0.05 mmol, 9.1 mg), NaOH (0.1 mmol, 4.0 mg) and water (15 mL) was stirred for 20 min in air, then sealed in a 25 mL Teflon reactor, which was heated at 150 °C for 72 h. Noodle-shaped pink noodle crystals were obtained by cooling the solution to room temperature at a rate of 5 °C h−1. Yield: 65% based on Co. Anal. Calc. for C40H34Co2N4O14S (944.65): C, 50.86; H, 3.63; N, 5.93%. Found: C, 50.73; H, 3.77; N, 5.81%. IR (KBr, cm−1): 3452(s), 2927(w), 1606(w), 1547(w), 1320(m), 1096(m), 832(s), 668(m).

2.5. Synthesis of {[Co4(dstc)2(bpa)3(H2O)6]·7H2O}n (4)

Pink crystals of complex 4 were synthesized by a similar method to that of complex 3, using bpa (0.05 mmol, 9.2 mg) instead of bpe. Yield: 52% based on Co. Anal. Calc. for C68H74Co4N6O33S2 (1803.20): C, 45.29; H, 4.14; N, 4.66%. Found: C, 45.08; H, 4.12; N, 4.64%. IR (KBr, cm−1): 3449(s), 2926(w), 1614(w), 1475(w), 1407(m), 1310(w), 899(w), 806(m), 715(w).

2.6. X-ray crystallography

Single-crystal X-ray diffraction data of 1–4 were collected on a Bruker SMART APEX CCD diffractometer with graphite-monochromated Mo Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å) at room temperature. The structures were solved by using direct methods and successive Fourier difference synthesis (Shelxs-97) [9], and refined using the full-matrix least-squares method based on F2 with anisotropic thermal parameters for all non-hydrogen atoms (Shelxl-97) [10] except for disordered atoms. All non-hydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically. All hydrogen atoms bound to carbon were placed in calculated positions and refined isotropically. The hydrogen atoms of the lattice water molecules in 3 and 4 could not be positioned reliably. During the refinement of the structure, some restraints were applied including distance restraints (dfix) and thermal restraints (isor, eadp, delu and simu) which have been used on some unreasonable atoms in order to permit acceptable refinement of these parameters in complex 3. Crystallographic data and selected bond lengths and angles for 1–4 are listed in Table 1 and Tables S1 and S2, respectively.

3. Results and discussion

3.1. Structural descriptions

3.1.1. [Ag(bpe)2]·H3dstc (1)

Complex 1 is a 3D supramolecular architecture via the combination of coordination bonds and hydrogen bonding interactions. As shown in Fig. 1a, Ag(I) ion displays a nearly linear geometry (the angle of N–Ag–N is 171°), coordinating with two N atoms from two discrete bpe ligands, while the remaining two uncoordinated N atoms afford the hydrogen bonding acceptors. Meanwhile, the partially deprotonated H3dstc– ligand, which does not take part in the coordination but acts as the counterion to balance the charge by its one depotonated carboxylate group, affords the hydrogen bonding donor. The hydrogen bonding interactions between O4 and N2 atoms (2.598 Å, symmetry code D: −x + 1, −y + 1, −z + 1, Table S2) link the [Ag(bpe)2]+ units and the free H3dstc- ligands to form a 1D wave-like chain (Fig. S1a). Viewed from the b direction, the neighboring chains are connected by the interchain O–H···O hydrogen bonding interactions (O1···O1C 2.458 Å, symmetry code C: −x + 1, y, −z + 1/2, Table S2) to generate a 2D grid layer (Fig. S1b). In addition, as illustrated in Fig. 1b, the adjacent layers are packed in an ···ABAB··· stacking fashion along the b direction, further generating a 3D supramolecular architecture by the C–H···O hydrogen bonding interactions with the average C–H···O distance of 3.321 Å [11].

It is well known that hydrogen bonding interactions have significant influence on extending the final architectures of complexes [12], which can support the multinuclear discrete subunits or low-dimensional entities to the high-dimensional supramolecular networks, as well as firmly stabilize the resultant structures. Three types of hydrogen bonding interactions (O–H···O, O–H···N, C–H···O) are found in 1, which lead to the formation of 3D supramolecular framework.

3.1.2. {[Ag2(H2dstc)(bpp)(H2O)]·H2O}n (2)

There are two independent polymeric motifs in the crystal structure of 2. One motif is a cationic 1D infinite chain [Ag(bpp)]+ (Fig. 2a), in which the Ag(I) ion is coordinated by two N atoms of two discrete bpp ligands, with the N–Ag–N angle of 176° (Fig. S2a). All the flexible bpp ligands assume TT conformations [2 h], bridging Ag(I) ions to form a 1D wave-like chain with the Ag···Ag distance of 12.53 Å.

The other motif in 2 is an anionic 1D double-stranded meso-helix [Ag(H2dstc)(H2O)]– generated by intertwining of two single-stranded meso-helical chains. Each Ag(I) center coordinates to two carboxylate O atoms and one water molecule to complete a T-shaped coordination geometry (Fig. S2a). The neighboring Ag(I) centers are interconnected by long flexible spacers H2dstc2- in a bridging coordination mode to furnish a meso-helical chain along the c axis with the pitch of 12.55 Å (Fig. 2a). Indeed, each pair of the independent meso-helical chains intertwine into a unique double-stranded meso-helix by weak Ag···O interactions (Ag1···O1 2.693 Å) and aromatic π···π staking interactions between the phenyl rings of H2dstc2- ligands with the centroid-to-centroid distance of 3.946 Å [13] (Fig. S2b). It should be mentioned that the meso-helix is a special structure and has been rarely reported [14], and the intertwined anionic double-stranded meso-helix in 2 is unprecedented. Furthermore, the lattice water molecules unite these anionic 1D double-stranded meso-helical chains together to generate a 2D layer by two kinds of O–H···O hydrogen bonding interactions. One is between the lattice water molecules and coordinated water molecules (O2W–H···O1W 2.924 Å, Table S2), and the other is between the lattice water molecules and carboxylic groups of the H2dstc2− ligands (O2W–H···O4 2.762 Å, O2W–H···O10 2.679 Å, Table S2) (Fig. S2c).

It is also interesting that the two different motifs alternate in the crystals, stacking in ···ABAB··· arrangements to generate a 3D sandwich-like structure (Fig. 2b), which is stabilized by C–H···O hydrogen bonding interactions between the two motifs (C21–H···O4 3.396 Å, C27–H···O2W 3.356 Å, Table S2). According to the literatures, the coexistence of different motifs in the same crystal has also been observed in several complexes [3], [15], however, complex 2, which contains the 1D cationic chain and anion double-stranded meso-helix, represents a new example.

3.1.3. {[Co2(dstc)(bpe)2(H2O)2]·2H2O}n (3)

Complex 3 is essentially isostructural with complex [Mn2(dpstc)(bpe)2·(H2O)2]·2H2O [8d]. Each Co(II) center is six-coordinated by two N atoms of bpe, three O atoms of dstc4− ligand and one water molecule (Fig. S3a). Each dstc4− ligand adopts a (κ2)–(κ1)–(κ2)–(κ1)–μ4 coordination mode (Fig. S5a) to link four Co(II) ions to form a 2D wave-like layer (Fig. 3a), the bpe ligands act as the pillars to further extend the 2D layers to a 3D network (Fig. S3b). Topologically, each Co(II) center can be coordinated to two dstc4− ligands and two other Co(II) centers via bpe ligands, while each dstc4− ligand is linked to four metal centers, thus the whole structure of 3 can be simplified as a binodal 4-connected framework with the Schläfli symbol of (64·82) (Fig. 3b).

3.1.4. {[Co4(dstc)2(bpa)3(H2O)6]·7H2O}n (4)

There are two crystallographically unique Co(II) ions in the asymmetric unit of 4. Co1 shows an octahedral coordination geometry coming from two N atoms of two bpa ligands, three carboxylate O atoms and one water molecule. The octahedral sphere of Co2 is completed by three O atoms of dstc4− ligand, one N atom of bpa ligand and two water molecules (Fig. S4a). Four Co(II) centers are linked together by four carboxylate groups of two different dstc4− ligands to generate a Co4 unit with the Co···Co distances of 5.03 Å and 5.78 Å. Each dstc4− ligand adopts a (κ1)–(κ2)–(κ1)–(κ1)–(κ1)-μ5 coordination mode to link five Co(II) centers (Fig. S5b). On the basis of this connection mode, the Co4 units are connected together by dstc4− ligands to furnish a 1D chain (Fig. S4b). Furthermore, the bpa spacers adopting TG conformations [1i] extend the 1D motifs to form a 3D framework (Fig. 4a). Notably, each Co4 unit behaves as a 6-connected node to link six others through six bpa ligands and four dstc4− ligands (Fig. 4b). Therefore, the whole framework of 4 shows the most familiar 6-connected pcu net (Fig. 4c).

3.2. Discussion of the structural diversities of all Ag(I) and Co(II) complexes of dstc ligand

Up to now, several dstc-containing complexes have been synthesized by the self-assembly of Ag(I)/Co(II) salts with dstc and N-donor ligands [8a,8d], demonstrating a clear structural and dimensional diversities from 0D discrete units to 1D chains and 3D architectures. Several reasons may be responsible for these significant differences: (1) Rich coordination modes and relative flexibility of the H4dstc ligand. The H4dstc ligand adopts different coordination modes through complete or partial deprotonation of its carboxyic groups. In 1 and [Ag(bipy)]2·H2dstc·2H2O (bipy = 4,4′-bipyridine), the partially deprotonated H3dstc- and H2dstc2− ligands do not take part in coordination but behave as the counterions to balance the charges. Also, the flexibility around the sulfonyl group can allow the sway of two phenyl rings of the H4dstc ligand, which is an important factor in forming the meso-helical structure in 2. (2) The use of different auxiliary ligands is also an effective method for adjusting the resultant structures, which can be reflected by comparison of the structures of Ag(I) complexes as well as Co(II) complexes. In [Ag(bipy)]2·H2dstc·2H2O, the bipy ligands link Ag(I) ions to form 1D Ag–bipy chains. However, the longer bpe ligand with a rigid –CH = CH– spacer between two pyridyl groups act as the terminal ligand, resulting in 0D discrete unit of 1. It is interesting to find that when bpp ligand with a more flexible and longer spacer –CH–CH–CH– between two pyridyl groups is used, complex 2 with a different structure is obtained, in which the partially deprotonated H2dstc2− ligands take part in coordination, linking the neighboring Ag(I) centers to form an unprecedented anion double-stranded meso-helix. For Co(II) complexes, when the chelating trpy/phen ligands (trpy = 2,2′:6′,2″-terpyridine, phen = 1,10-phenanthroline) are introduced, the expected low-dimensional species [Co(H2dpstc)(trpy)·H2O] and [Co2(dpstc)(phen)2·4H2O]·2H2O (1D) [8d] are obtained because the chelating ligand reduce the binding sites in the available Co(II) ions and restrict the unit’s growth in that direction [16]. When the rod-like ligands bpe and bpa are introduced into the systems, the resultant structures of 3 and 4 show the 3D frameworks. However, 3 and 4 display different topological structures, and the remarkable difference between 3 and 4 is the coordination mode of dstc4− ligand. The dstc4− ligand adopts a (κ2)–(κ1)–(κ2)–(κ1)–μ4 coordination mode in 3, while each dstc4− ligand exhibits a (κ1)–(κ2)–(κ1)–(κ1)–(κ1)–μ5 coordination fashion in 4. (3) Other factors, such as the coordination geometry of metal ions, the metal-to-ligand ratio, the reaction pH and temperature, may also influence the final structures. It can be seen that the structures of Co(II) complexes are more complicated than those of Ag(I) complexes, as the Co(II) ion with larger radii always adopts an octahedral coordination geometry, while the Ag(I) ion has a low effective nuclear charge and low coordination number [3f].

3.3. XRPD measurement and thermal analyses

The X-ray powder diffraction (XRPD) patterns of 1–4 performed at room temperature are in good agreement with their corresponding simulated patterns, confirming phase purities of these complexes (Fig. S6). Furthermore, thermogravimetric analyses (TGA) of the polymers were performed to study the stability of these complexes (Fig. S7). Complex 1 is thermally stable to 180 °C. The weight loss from 180 °C to 650 °C (obsd 86.7%) corresponds to the loss of the organic ligands H3dstc- and bpe (calcd 87.5%). For 2, the first weight loss (4.5%) from 130 °C to 215 °C corresponds to evacuation of one coordinated and one lattice water molecules (calcd 4.3%), then the decomposition of H2dstc2− and bpp ligands is observed. The residues of 1 and 2 are Ag (obsd 12.1%, 26.7%, calcd 12.5%, 25.6%). For 3, the weight loss from 30 °C to 205 °C corresponds to the loss of one lattice and one coordinated water molecules (obsd 7.64%, calcd 7.62%). Complex 4 loses its lattice and coordinated water molecules from 35 °C to 160 °C (obsd 13.09%, calcd 12.99%). Further loss of the organic ligands is observed from 160 °C to 550 °C. The residues of 3 and 4 are CoO (obsd 15.4%, 16.3%, calcd 15.8%, 16.6%).

3.4. Fluorescent properties

The emission spectra of complexes 1 and 2 in the solid state were investigated at room temperature (Fig. 5). Complex 1 exhibits fluorescence emission bands at ca. 466 nm, while complex 2 shows a main peak at 501 nm with a shoulder at 472 nm upon excitation 330 nm. According to the literatures, the free bpe, bpp and H4dstc ligands exhibit the photoluminescence with maxima at 370 nm and 395 nm, 446 nm and 356 nm, which originate from the π∗ → n or π∗ → π transitions [8], [17]. It can be seen that in 1 and 2, the fluorescent emission arising from the bpe, bpp and H4dstc ligands are not observable, thus the emissions may arise from the electronic transition between p orbitals (filled orbitals) of coordinated N or O atoms and 5s orbital (empty orbital) of Ag(I) ion, i.e. ligand-to-metal charge transfer (LMCT) [18]. Compared with 1, a red shift of emission band has been observed in 2, which is probably due to the H2dstc2− ligands coordinated to Ag(I) ions.

3.5. Magnetic properties

Due to the flexibility of the V-shaped H4dstc ligands, the distances between the Co(II) ions are relatively short in complexes 3 and 4. And especially for 4, there exist the Co4 metal clusters. Therefore, magnetic studies have been performed on powder samples for 3 and 4 in the range 2–300 K. The χMT and χM versus T plots of 1 and 3 are shown in Figs. S8 and S9, respectively. For 3, the value of χMT decreases from 5.60 at 300 K to 0.42 cm3 mol−1 K at 1.8 K for 3 and from 12.54 at 300 K to 1.0 cm3 mol−1 K at 1.8 K for 4. Upon cooling, the magnetic susceptibility per mol of Co(II) ion for 3 continuously increases to reach a maximum of 0.42 cm3 mol−1 at 1.8 K. While in the case of 4, the χM value increases to reach a maximum of 0.79 cm3 mol−1 at 4.0 K and then decreases at lower temperatures. The 1/χ versus T plot shows a linear dependence with the temperature in the high temperature regime (T > 150 K) for 3 and 4, and the data follow well a Curie–Weiss law with C = 6.30 cm3 mol−1 K and θ = −9.8 K for 3 and C = 13.26 cm3 mol−1 K and θ = −15.82 K for 4. The negative θ indicate that there exist predominantly antiferromagnetic interactions in 3 and 4. According to the structures of 3 and 4, all Co(II) ions adopt octahedral coordination geometries and show 4T1g ground states, thus the orbit of angular momentum of these Co(II) ions will provide large contributions to the magnetic susceptibilities of these Co-containing complexes [19]. Thus it could be presumed that the main superexchange interactions may occur between Co(II) ions through the bridging carboxylate groups.

4. Conclusions

In summary, four new dstc-containing complexes were obtained and characterized by single crystal X-ray diffraction, elemental analysis, IR, TGA and XRD. Complex 1 is a 0D discrete unit, and complex 2 contains two different motifs: a cationic 1D chain and an anionic 1D double-stranded infinite meso-helix. Complex 3 represents a 3D binodal 4-connected (64·82) network, and complex 4 is simplified as a pcu framework. It can be concluded that the dstc ligand may be a good candidate to construct new complexes owning to its diverse coordination modes and relative flexibility, meanwhile, it can provide some intra-/intermolecular weak interactions, such as hydrogen bonding and π···π staking, which can also play crucial roles in the formation of the resultant complexes. Thus, the further research works should be carried out and continued for designing the functional dstc-containing complexes.

  • Syntheses, structures, and magnetic properties of cobalt(II) and nickel(II) coordination polymers based on a V-shaped ligand

    2017, Journal of Solid State Chemistry

    Two coordination polymers [Co2(TA)(4,4′-bipy)2(H2O)2]·H2O (1) and [Ni2(TA)(4,4′-bipy)2(H2O)4]·3H2O (2) were prepared by hydrothermal reactions of MCl2·6H2O (M = Co, Ni) with a V-shaped ligand TDPA (3,3′,4,4′-thiodiphthalic anhydride) and a I-shaped N-donor co-ligand (4,4′-bipy). They were characterized by elemental analyses, thermogravinetric analyses, and magnetic behavior. As is expected, TDPA hydrolyzes into the corresponding tetra-carboxylate acid H4TA (3,3′,4,4′-thiodiphthalic acid) during the reactions. Co2 dimer and Ni mononuclear center are connected into two-dimensional (2D) layers by H4TA and 4,4′-bipy bridge in 1 and 2, respectively. The most amazing feature is that 1 and 2 exhibit interesting spin-canting metamagnetism and weak ferromagnetic behavior, respectively, with the critical Néel temperature of TN =4 K for 1 and TN =13 K for 2, based on variable temperature magnetic susceptibility measurements. In low mono- or dinuclear metal system, such magnetic behaviors have rare been observed. Furthermore, complex 1 will be a potential metamagnet material.

  • Structural characterization of three semi-rigid tetracarboxylate-containing transition-metal coordination polymers

    2016, Polyhedron

    Under hydro(solvo)thermal conditions, the reactions between transition-metal salts and tetracarboxylic acid molecules, with the assistance of organic base molecules, created three new coordination polymers, namely [H2(bpp)]2[Zn2(sph)2]·H2O (sph = 4,4′-sulfonediphthalate, bpp = 1,3-bis(piperidyl)propane) 1, [H2(bpp)]2[Ni2(oph)2(H2O)2]·2H2O (oph = 4,4′-oxydiphthalate) 2 and [H2(bpe)]2[Ni(Hfph)2(bpe)] (bpe = 1,2-bis(pyridyl)ethylene; fph = 4,4′-(hexafluoroisopropylidene)diphthalate) 3. The quadruple-bridged sph molecules propagate the tetrahedral Zn2+ ions into a 2-D layer network in 1, where two types of circle loops are found; (ii) in 2, the bpp molecule is also diprotonated. The triple-bridged oph molecules extend the octahedrally coordinated Ni2+ ions into a 2-D layer network, in which a carboxylate-bridged Ni2+ chain is observed; (iii) in 3, the non-protonated bpe molecules link the Ni2+ ions into a 1-D linear chain. A magnetic property investigation indicates that antiferromagnetic interactions exist between the Ni2+ ions in 2.

  • Self-assembly of two chiral zinc coordination polymers based on N-benzoyl-l-glutamic acid: Influence of different N-donor ancillary ligands on structural arrangement and photoluminescent properties

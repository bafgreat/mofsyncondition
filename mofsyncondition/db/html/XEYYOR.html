Cadmium and zinc chain and cluster-based layered coordination polymers prepared from flexible-arm aromatic ortho-dicarboxylic acids and 4-pyridylnicotinamide

Abstract

Hydrothermal reaction of a d10-metal nitrate salt, a flexible-arm aromatic ortho-dicarboxylic acid, and 4-pyridylnicotinamide (4-pna) afforded four new crystalline coordination polymers, which were characterized by single-crystal X-ray diffraction. [Cd(Hhmph)(nic)(H2O)2]n (1, hmph = homophthalate, nic = nicotinate) is a 1-D coordination polymer chain compound whose nic ligands were generated in situ via 4-pna hydrolysis. Addition of base and a shorter reaction duration afforded [Cd(hmph)(4-pna)]n (2), which has dinuclear [Cd2(hmph)2] dimers linked into a 1-D ladder polymer via 4-pna ligands. A similar chain structure, albeit with a different hmph binding mode, is seen in [Zn(hmph)(4-pna)]n (3). {[Zn2(phda)2(4-pna)2(H2O)]⋅H2O}n (4, phda = 1,2-phenylenediacetate) has both anti-syn bridged [Zn2(OCO)2] ring dimers and [Zn2(OCO)4] paddlewheel dimers, linked into a layered coordination polymer by dipodal 4-pna ligands. Luminescent properties of these new materials are also presented.

1. Introduction

The investigation of the structures and properties of crystalline coordination polymer solids has remained an important research focus over the past decade because of their potential uses as gas storage substrates [1], selective molecular absorbents [2], ion-exchange substrates [3], catalysts [4], and as fluorescent materials [5]. Still, the aesthetic appeal of their underlying molecular topologies provides another impetus for research [6]. Divalent zinc and cadmium ions are commonly employed as the metal nodes in coordination polymer chemistry for two reasons, both based on their d10 closed-shell electronic configuration. The lack of any ligand field stabilization energy results in the primacy of ligand steric and conformational requirements in instituting the supramolecular crystal structure during self-assembly. Also, the impossibility of any d–d transition visible light absorption provides an important spectral window for emissive properties such as fluorescence or second harmonic generation [7].

Recent results illustrate that divalent metal phthalate (pht) coordination polymers containing neutral dipyridyl co-ligands (Scheme 1) can manifest a wide range of structural topologies, many of which are unprecedented [8], [9], [10], [11], [12], [13], [14]. Ligands such as 4,4′-bipyridine (bpy) [8], 4,4′-dipyridylamine (dpa) [9], bis(4-pyridylmethyl)piperazine (bpmp) [10], bis(4-pyridylformyl)piperazine (bpfp) [11], and 1,4-bis(1,2,4-triazol-1-yl)butane (btb) [12] can serve both as tethers and supramolecular structure-directing agents, imparting tremendous differences in final coordination polymer dimensionality and topology. For example, [Cd(Hpht)2(bpy)]n manifests a unique self-penetrated 6-connected uninodal 3-D network with 510647 topology [8a], built from a bpy-tethered 4-connected adamantoid [Cd(Hpht)2]n. {[Cd(pht)(dpa)(H2O)]⋅4H2O}n possesses a uninodal 4-connected network with an aesthetic self-penetrated 7482 yyz topology built from the interlocking of three chiral (12,3) regular subnets, themselves self-penetrated [9a]. Longer-spanning tethering ligands have afforded more complicated multi-nodal topologies, with {[Cd2(pht)(btb)(H2O)3]⋅4.5(H2O)}n showing 2-D slab motifs with a trinodal (4.72)2(42738)(42739)2 topology [12] and {[Cd2(pht)2(bpfp)(H2O)2]n displaying a very uncommon tcs topology 4,5-connected binodal net [11].

In contrast ortho-dicarboxylate ligands homophthalate (hmph) and 1,2-phenylenedicarboxylate (1,2-phda), which possess one and two flexible acetate arms respectively (Scheme 2), are employed far more seldom than rigid-arm pht ligands in coordination polymer synthesis [15], [16]. Divalent metal hmph or 1,2-phda coordination polymers can manifest striking differences in topology with respect to their pht analogues, despite the use of the same dipyridyl tethering coligand. For example, {[Cd(hmph)(dpa)]⋅H2O}n (hmph = homophthalate) possesses interdigitated herringbone grid (6,3) layers [15b], different from the self-penetrated yyz net of its pht congener. [Cu2(1,2-phda)2(H2O)2(bpmp)]n has a rare 3,4-connected binodal V2O5-type net with (426)(42638) topology [16b], while its pht congener {[Cu2(pht)2(Hbpmp)2(H2O)2](NO3)2⋅H2O}n has only a simple (4,4) rectangular grid topology [10].

Although hundreds of coordination polymers have been prepared using the rigid-rod 4,4′-bpy ligand [17], reports of related phases containing 4-pyridylnicotinamide (4-pna, Scheme 2) remain extremely scarce [18]. Herein we report the synthesis, structural characterization, and luminescent properties of the first aromatic ortho-dicarboxylate coordination polymers constructed from 4-pna ligand precursors: [Cd(Hhmph)(nic)(H2O)2]n (1, nic = nicotinate), [Cd(hmph)(4-pna)]n (2), [Zn(hmph)(4-pna)]n (3), and {[Zn2(phda)2(4-pna)2(H2O)]⋅H2O}n (4).

2. Experimental section

2.1. General considerations

Metal salts and dicarboxylic acids were commercially obtained. Condensation of 4-aminopyridine and nicotinoyl chloride hydrochloride in pyridine afforded 4-pyridylnicotinamide (4-pna) via modification of a published procedure [19], as the product did not crystallize from the preparation as indicated in the original report. Instead it was necessary to add water to the reaction mixture, followed by repeated extraction with methylene chloride. Crude 4-pna product was obtained after combination of the organic layers, drying over anhydrous sodium sulfate, and solvent evaporation in vacuo. Recrystallization from water gave white crystals of 4-pna in good yield. Water was deionized above 3 MΩ-cm in-house. IR spectra were recorded on powdered samples using a Perkin Elmer Spectrum One instrument. The luminescence spectra were obtained with a Hitachi F-4500 Fluorescence Spectrometer on solid crystalline samples anchored to quartz microscope slides with Rexon Corporation RX-22P ultraviolet-transparent epoxy adhesive.

4. Results and discussion

4.1. Synthesis and spectra

Crystalline samples of 1–4 were produced by the hydrothermal reaction of either cadmium or zinc nitrate, the requisite aromatic ortho-dicarboxylic acid, and 4-pyridylnicotinamide. Their infrared spectra were consistent with their structural components as determined by single-crystal X-ray diffraction. Intense, slightly broadened asymmetric and symmetric C–O stretching bands within the carboxylate ligands were observed at 1532 and 1400 cm–1 in 1, at 1537 and 1400 cm–1 in 2, at 1595 and 1364 cm–1 in 3, and at 1598 and 1398 cm–1 in 4. Sharp and medium intensity bands in the range of ∼1600 cm−1 to ∼1300 cm−1 were ascribed to stretching modes of the pyridyl rings of the 4-pna or nic ligands, or the phenyl rings of the aromatic dicarboxylate ligands [23]. Features corresponding to C–H bending and ring puckering within the phenyl and pyridyl moieties exist in the region between ∼900 and ∼650 cm−1. Broad, weak spectral bands in the vicinity of ∼3200–3000 cm−1 indicate the presence of bound and unbound water molecules in 1 and 4, and the 4-pna N–H bonds in 2–4. The carbonyl groups of the 4-pna ligands were indicated by sharp, narrow spectral peaks between 1670 and 1720 cm–1 for 2–4.

4.2. Structural description of [Cd(Hhmph)(nic)(H2O)2]n (1)

The asymmetric unit of 1 consists of a divalent cadmium atom, a pendant protonated Hhmph ligand, two aqua ligands, and a nic ligand. The nic ligand was evidently formed in situ via the hydrolysis of the 4-pna precursor, in a similar process to the formation of isonicotinate ligands from the hydrolysis of 4-pyridylisonicotinamide during the hydrothermal synthesis of [Cd3(1,4-phenylenediacetate)2(isonicotinate)2(H2O)2] [24]. The coordination environment of the cadmium atom is that of a highly distorted {CdNO5} octahedron (Fig. 1a), with its axial positions containing the two aqua ligands. Equatorial positions in the coordination sphere are occupied by an oxygen atom donor from the short carboxylate arm of the Hhmph ligand, a chelating carboxylate group from a nic ligand, and a pyridyl nitrogen atom donor from a second nicotinate ligand. Bond lengths and angles within the coordination sphere are given in Table 2.

Exobidentate nic ligands link cadmium atoms with a Cd⋯Cd internuclear distance of 8.034 Å, forming 1-D [Cd(Hhmph)(nic)(H2O)2]n polymeric chains that are oriented parallel to the a crystal direction (Fig. 1b). 4.3. Structural description of [Cd(hmph)(4-pna)]n (2)

The asymmetric unit of compound 2 consists of a divalent cadmium atom, a fully deprotonated hmph ligand, and a 4-pna ligand. The coordination sphere of cadmium is that of a distorted {CdN2O4} octahedron (Fig. 2a), with its equatorial positions occupied by a chelating carboxylate group from the longer arm of a hmph ligand, a nitrogen atom donor from the nicotinamide group of a 4-pna ligand, and one oxygen atom donor from a chelating carboxylate group from the shorter arm of a hmph ligand. The axial positions are taken by the other oxygen atom donor from the chelating carboxylate group of the shorter arm of the same hmph ligand and a nitrogen atom donor from the 4-pyridyl ring of a 4-pna ligand. Thus, the two nitrogen donors are disposed in a cis orientation with respect to each other. Bond lengths and angles within the coordination sphere are listed in Table 4.

Lack of protonation in 2 allows all hmph ligands to adopt a bis(chelating) binding mode, with pairs of staple-like hmph ligands linking pairs of cadmium atoms to create [Cd2(hmph)2] dimers (Fig. 2b) with a Cd⋯Cd distance of 5.440 Å. Crystallographic inversion centers are sited at the centroids of these dimers. Each dimeric [Cd2(hmph)2] secondary building unit is connected to two others by pairs of dipodal tethering 4-pna ligands, which span a Cd⋯Cd internuclear distance of 11.924 Å. Via these connections, 1-D [Cd(hmph)(4-pna)]n chains are formed along the [1 1¯ 0] crystal direction (Fig. 2c). If the [Cd2(hmph)2] dimers are treated as nodes and pairs of hmph and 4-pna ligands are treated as linkers, the [Cd(hmph)(4-pna)]n chains can be considered as simple 3-connected ladders (Fig. 2d). The cis arrangement and “L-shaped” donor disposition of the 4-pna ligands appears to enforce a 1-D coordination polymer dimensionality for 2. Additionally, hydrogen-bonding donation from the 4-pna central amide moieties to ligated longer arm hmph carboxylate oxygen atoms (Table 3) serves a supporting dimensionality inducing role. These [Cd(hmph)(4-pna)]n ladders are arranged into the supramolecular crystal structure via several non-classical C–H⋯O interactions (Fig. S2).

4.4. Structural description of [Zn(hmph)(4-pna)]n (3)

The asymmetric unit of 3 contains a divalent zinc atom, a fully deprotonated hmph ligand, and a 4-pna ligand, very similar to that of 2. Although the hmph ligands in 3 display a bis(monodentate) binding mode instead of a bis(chelating) binding mode as in 2, related [Zn2(hmph)2] dimeric clusters are formed (Fig. 3b). These have a Zn⋯Zn through-space distance of 5.092 Å, approximately 0.3 Å shorter than in the [Cd2(hmph)2] dimeric clusters in 2. Once again the “L-shaped” disposition of the 4-pna ligands connects neighboring [Zn2(hmph)2] dimers into [Zn(hmph)(4-pna)]n ladders (Fig. 3c), with a Zn⋯Zn through-ligand distance of 11.527 Å. Hydrogen-bonding donation by the 4-pna amide groups to ligated, longer hmph carboxylate arm oxygen atoms (Table 3) provides an ancillary stabilization effect to the coordinative covalent bonding within the ladder motifs. Similar to 2, neighboring [Zn(hmph)(4-pna)]n ladders interact and form the supramolecular crystal structure of 3 through several non-classical C–H⋯O interactions (Fig. S3).

4.5. Structural description of {[Zn2(phda)2(4-pna)(H2O)]⋅H2O}n (4)

The asymmetric unit of 4 consists of two crystallographically distinct divalent zinc atoms (Zn1, Zn2), two crystallographically distinct phda ligands (phda-A, phda-B), a 4-pna ligand, and an aqua ligand, along with a water molecule of crystallization. The coordination sphere at Zn1 is a {ZnNO5} distorted octahedron, with equatorial positions occupied by a chelating carboxylate group from a phda ligand, and two single oxygen atom donors from carboxylate groups belonging to two other phda ligands. The axial positions are filled by a nitrogen atom donor from a nicotinamide ring of a 4-pna ligand along with an aqua ligand. In contrast the coordination environment at Zn2 is a {ZnNO4} square pyramid, with single carboxylate oxygen atom donors from four different phda ligands in the basal plane. A 4-pyridyl nitrogen atom donor from a 4-pna ligand sits in the apical position. Bond lengths and angles within the distinct coordination environments in 4 are listed in Table 6, while a depiction is shown in Fig. 4a.

The structure of 4 is characterized by two distinct alternating [Zn(phda)]n ribbon motifs, both of which run along the a crystallographic axis, that are bonded into [Zn2(phda)2(4-pna)(H2O)2]n layers by 4-pna tethers (Fig. 4b). In one [Zn(phda)]n ribbon motif (Fig. 4c), Zn1 atoms are bridged by exotridentate phda-A ligands with a chelating/(bis)bridging μ3-κ4O,O′:O′′:O′′′ binding mode. Pairs of phda-A bridging termini within the Zn1-based ribbon motif create embedded [Zn2(OCO)2] dimers with a Zn⋯Zn through-space distance of 4.032 Å. In the other [Zn(phda)]n ribbon motif (Fig. 4d), Zn2 atoms are bridged by phda-B ligands with an exotetradentate μ4-κ4O:O′:O′′:O′′′ binding mode; every phda oxygen atom acts as a donor to a different Zn2 atom. This bridging mode creates embedded [Zn2(OCO)4] paddlewheel dimers within the second type of [Zn(phda)]n ribbon motif. The paddlewheel clusters show a much closer Zn⋯Zn through-space distance than the Zn1-based dimeric units, at 3.023 Å. Zn1 and Zn2 atoms in neighboring, different [Zn(phda)]n ribbon motifs are linked by the 4-pna ligands with a Zn⋯Zn distance of 11.368 Å.

Considering the [Zn2(OCO)4] paddlewheel dimers and [Zn2(OCO)2] dimers as four-connected nodes, and phda ligand pairs and 4-pna ligands as linkers, reveals that the underlying topology of the [Zn2(phda)2(4-pna)(H2O)2]n layers can be construed as a (4,4) rectangular grid (Fig. 5a). An alternate view of the topology is possible by treating the Zn1 atoms as four-connected nodes and the exotridentate phda-A ligands as three-connected nodes, while retaining the [Zn2(OCO)4] paddlewheel dimers as four-connected nodes. In this treatment, phda-B ligand pairs and 4-pna tethers are considered linkers. From this perspective, the layered network of 4 is a novel 3,4,4-connected trinodal net with a Schlafli symbol of (425)2(425262)2(5482) as computed by TOPOS [25] (Fig. 5b).

Water molecules of crystallization are present within the grid apertures, anchored to the coordination polymer framework by hydrogen-bonding donation to phda-A carboxylate oxygen atoms, and hydrogen-bonding acceptance from 4-pna amide groups. Adjacent [Zn2(phda)2(4-pna)(H2O)2]n layers stack in an ABAB manner along the c crystal via hydrogen-bonding between aqua ligands on Zn1 atoms in [Zn2(OCO)2] dimers and phda-B ligand carboxylate oxygen atoms bound to Zn2 atoms in [Zn2(OCO)4] paddlewheel dimers (Fig. S4).

4.6. Luminescent properties

Compounds 1–4 all exhibited weak to moderate blue–violet visible light emission upon exposure to ultraviolet radiation in the solid state. Compound 1 showed an emission maximum in the violet region at 421 nm upon excitation at 260 nm. Both compounds 2 and 3 exhibited the most emissive intensity upon excitation at 250 nm, with slight differences in the emission maximum (424 nm for 2, 404 nm for 3). Upon excitation with 300 nm ultraviolet light, compound 4 manifested a broad, rather featureless emission profile centered on 380 nm with intensity tailing off above 410 nm. In all likelihood the emissive behavior of 1–4 can be ascribed to molecular orbital transitions within the delocalized π systems of the 4-pna pyridyl rings and/or the ortho-dicarboxylate ligands, analogous to the behavior seen in dual ligand coordination polymers containing d10 configuration divalent metal atoms [5]. The difference in profile for 4 could plausibly arise from the presence of the more flexible phda ligands, which could exacerbate non-radiative energy loss due to vibrational dissipation. Spectral profiles for 1–4 are shown in Fig. S5.

5. Conclusions

A series of divalent cadmium and zinc coordination polymers with seldom used flexible-arm aromatic ortho-dicarboxylate and 4-pyridylnicotinamide ligands has been prepared and structurally characterized. The topology of these materials differs from other related compounds with shorter or longer dipyridyl coligands. Enhanced conformational degrees of freedom in the hmph or phda ligands can adjust carboxylate binding and bridging modes, thus influencing the final coordination polymer dimensionality or topology. While the closer spacing of the homophthalate carboxylate termini appears to promote 1,2-chelation and 1-D chain topologies, the phda ligand permits dimeric nodes with higher connectivity and an increase in dimensionality. Hydrogen bonding mechanisms provided by the central amide moieties of the 4-pyridylnicotinamide ligands impart a significant structure-directing role in this system. Future attempts at the synthesis of coordination polymers incorporating 4-pyridylnicotinamide should be undertaken at lower temperature, to avoid thermally induced hydrolysis and resulting in situ generation of nicotinate ligands.

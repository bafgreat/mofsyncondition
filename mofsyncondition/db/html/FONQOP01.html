A 2-D cadmium-centered polymer and its isomers

Abstract

A new two-dimensional coordination polymer, [Cd(NA)2], has been synthesized under hydrothermal reaction conditions. The two-dimensional networks show paired stacking arrangement and the 2-D networks are stabilized via interdigitation and π–π interactions.

Molecular recognition directed self-assembly process represents a rational approach in crystal engineering of new coordination polymers [1], [2], [3]. The functionality of the coordination polymers depends very much on their functional framework structures, metal centers as well as organic building blocks since the metal centers as well as organic building blocks play a key role in molecular recognition process. It has been reported from recent studies [2], [3], [4], [5], [6] that pyridylcarboxylate ligands tend to bind metal centers with nitrogen atoms and carboxylate groups to form extended networks, where carboxylate groups balance the metal charges. The metal centers display different coordination geometries with versatility. We and others have recently reported [M(NA)2] (NA = nicotinato) such as M = Cu 1[7], Zn 2[8], Zn 3[6], Cd 4[9] structures. In an effort to understand geometric preferences of the metal centers of Cu, Zn and Cd when coordinated by nicotinato ligands, we have synthesized a new complex [Cd(NA)2] 5. The compound 5 was synthesized by reacting Cd(NO3)2 · 4H2O with nicotinic acid and 4,4′-dipyridyl in the mole ratio of 1:2:1 for three days at 190 °C under hydrothermal conditions. The product was washed with water, acetone, and dried in air. The yield is 57%. The structure of 51 consists of cadmium metal centers coordinated by two pyridyl nitrogen and four carboxylato oxygen atoms (Fig. 1). Two Cd–O bonds (2.397(3) Å and 2.474(3) Å) are much longer than the other two Cd–O bonds (2.271(3) Å and 2.317(3) Å). Each tridentate nicotinato ligand bridges between two cadmium metal atoms. The nicotinato-bridged cadmium centers extend into a two-dimensional network structure (Fig. 2) in 5. The two-dimensional networks show paired stacking arrangement (Fig. 3). The interdigitation of the pyridine rings between two neighboring paired-layers intruded into each others unoccupied spaces (Fig. 4) that is different in orientation from those observed in complex 2. The distances between the two layers in the same pair are about 3.0 Å (Fig. 3), and the distances between the interdigitating paires are about 4.0 Å (Fig. 4). Apparently, the 2-D networks are stabilized via interdigitation and π–π interactions.

In the [M(NA)2] (M = Cu, Zn, Cd) series, Complex 1 has a 3-D structure that is based on the square pyramidal binuclear copper metal centers [7]. The copper(II) complexes are relatively common with square pyramidal geometry, some new examples of coordination polymers have been reported from our laboratory [2], [3].

Complex 2 consists of distorted tetrahedral zinc metal centers in the 2-D networks that are stacked via interdigitation and π–π interactions. The zinc atoms in complex 3 occupy highly distorted octahedral sites and the complex crystallizes in a chiral space group. The structure 2 favors tetrahedral zinc atoms and crystallizes in a rare chiral space group Fdd2. The same square-grid-based network resulted in structure 3 that possess octahedral zinc metal centers and P43212 space group. The differences between the complexes 2 and 3 are distinguished only by the coordination bonding modes of the nicotinato ligands.

The cadmium atom with square pyramidal geometry is rare in metal-oragnic polymers [10] while it has been stabilized in complex 4[9]. The square pyramidal cadmium geometry was stabilized by the binuclear motif in the three-dimensional complex 4, and that the non-interpenetrating open-framework structure can be sustained by the binuclear construction unit. The cadmium atoms in complex 5 have six coordinates. The two-dimensional networks in 5 with paired stacking arrangement resulted in a interdigitation packing structure. The only difference in the formation of complexes 4 and 5 is the reaction temperature. The complex 4 was formed at 170°, while complex 5 was achieved by raising the temperature to 190°. Please note that the temperature differences are crucial to complexes 4 and 5 since they are thermodynamically controlled, which may indicate that the rare square pyramidal geometry cadmium structure can be transformed to the six-coordinate cadmium metal centers thermodynamically.

While our intention to elucidating the geometric structural trend from the synthesis of [M(NA)2] (M = Cu, Zn, Cd) is inconclusive, the thermodynamic and bonding preferences discovered from the crystal structures of these complexes are noticeable.

    2017, Journal of Molecular Structure
    Citation Excerpt :

    For these purposes, design and synthesis of novel MOFs with specific structures and properties have been focused on rational choices of the favored geometry and function of metal ions as well as organic ligands [13,14]. The aromatic containing heterocyclic ligands, such as pyridine and pyrimidine, are often regarded as an effective strategy for the construction of 3d complexes [15–17]. Pyrimidine derivatives have proved to be interesting and versatile ligands and exhibit various coordination modes to furnish various structures [18,19].

    A new Cu(II) complex with the bridge bidentate ligand 4-amino-6-methoxypyrimidine, [Cu(C5H7N3O)(H2O)(NO3)2], has been prepared at room temperature and characterized by single crystal X-ray diffraction and IR spectroscopy. The compound crystallizes in the monoclinic space group C2/c with lattice parameters a = 17.783 (4), b = 11.131 (3), c = 12.594 (3) Å, β = 117.616 (3)°, V = 2209.0 (9) Å3 and Z = 8. The Cu(II) cation is hexa-coordinated, in distorted octahedral fashion, by two nitrogen atoms of two 4-amino-6-methoxypyrimidine ligands, one water oxygen atom and three oxygen atoms of two nitrate anions. In the atomic arrangement, the organic ligands and the 6-connected Cu centers are linked with each other to give a 1-D corrugated chain running along the b-axis direction. The chains are interconnected via OH⋯O, CH⋯O, NH⋯O hydrogen bonds to form a three dimensional network. The analysis of contacts on the Hirshfeld surface shows that the crystal packing is driven mainly by the electrostatic interactions: the coordination of Cu(II) by O and N as well as strong hydrogen bonds. The vibrational absorption bands were identified by infrared spectroscopy. Magnetic properties were also studied to characterize the complex.

  • A copper(II) complex with pyridine-2,6-dicarboxylic acid: Synthesis, characterization, thermal decomposition, bioactivity and interactions with herring sperm DNA

    2015, Polyhedron
    Citation Excerpt :

    Such systems can be conveniently generated by employing macrocyclic molecules with tunable properties via a metal directed self-assembling strategy [20]. The most common method for the preparation of coordination polymers and metal–organic framework compounds is through the self-assembly of metal centers with appropriate organic ligands to promote the formation of extended framework structures [21–23]. This methodology has been proven to be an advantageous way for building such framework structures, and has resulted in extensive families of compounds.

    The copper(II) complex of pyridine-2,6-dicarboxylic acid was synthesized and characterized by elemental analyses, single crystal X-ray diffraction, FTIR and UV–Vis spectra, and thermogravimetric analysis coupled with infrared spectrometry (TGA-FTIR). The results show that the crystal structure of the complex belongs to the triclinic system, and the Cu(II) ion is five-coordinated by one pyridine N atom, two carboxylate O atoms from the ligand anion and two O atoms from two water molecules, forming a distorted triangular bipyramidal geometry. Under the conditions of a physiological pH environment, the interaction of the complex with herring sperm DNA was studied by means of spectral methods using acridine orange as a fluorescence probe. The bonding mode between the complex and DNA is evidenced to be intercalative binding, and the binding of the complex to DNA is driven by entropy.

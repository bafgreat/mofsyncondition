Abstract

Hydrothermal reaction of divalent metal nitrates, pyromellitic acid (pymH4), and either 1,6-hexanediaminebis(nicotinamide) (hbn) or 1,6-hexanediaminebis(isonicotinamide) (hbin) afforded crystalline coordination polymers whose dimensionality depends synergistically on the dipyridylamide nitrogen donor disposition and metal coordination environment. These new phases were structurally characterized via single-crystal X-ray diffraction. [Cd(pymH2)(hbn)(H2O)2]n (1) and [Co(pymH2)(hbn)(H2O)2]n (2) display isostructural (4,4) grid topologies with [M(pymH2)(H2O)2]n chains pillared by hbn ligands. {[Cu2(pym)(hbn)(H2O)2]·2H2O}n (3) manifests a 3D 3,4-connected network with a rare (83)2(86) topology built from the hbn pillaring of [Cu2(pym)(H2O)]n layer motifs. {[Zn2(pym)(hbin)2(H2O)]·H2O}n (4) shows a 1D ribbon motif based on [Zn2(OCCCCO)2] 14-membered circuits. {[Ni2(pym)(hbin)(H2O)4]·3H2O}n (5) exhibits 3,4-connected layer motifs with rare {4.62}2{426282} topology. Luminescent behavior in 1 and 4 is ascribed to intra-ligand molecular orbital transitions. These phases were also surveyed for the ability to detect nitrobenzene in ethanol suspension. Thermal decomposition behavior of 1–5 is also discussed herein.

1. Introduction

The enticing utility of crystalline divalent metal coordination polymers in gas storage [1], selective absorption [2], ion exchange [3], industrially relevant catalysis [4], and explosives trace detection [5] has spurred an intense basic research focus on this class of materials over the past two decades. A key side benefit is their beautiful interlocked and interdigitated topologies, which provides an additional intangible impetus for continued investigation [6]. Aromatic dicarboxylates ligands provide both the crystal integrity and required charge balance for the construction of coordination polymers, and thus are clearly the most popular linking ligand choice [7], [8], [9], [10]. Specific coordination geometric preferences at the divalent metal centers (e.g. square planar, square pyramidal, trigonal bipyramidal, octahedral, pentagonal bipyramidal) can induce significant structural alterations across a series of related coordination polymers [11], [12], [13], accommodated by the variety of energetically similar carboxylate binding modes. As a result of the enormous parameter space defined by all the possible metal and ligand choices, deliberate tuning of coordination polymer structure and topology has only been achieved in systems of a narrow structural scope [14], [15].

The four carboxylate donor groups of the pyromellitate ligand (pym, 1,2,4,5-benzenetetracarboxylate, Scheme 1) can induce a tremendous diversity of possible dicarboxylate binding and bridging modes, and therefore a priori prediction of the final topology of an extended phase is problematic. Thus exploratory synthesis still presents the best means of expanding the scope of divalent metal pym-containing coordination polymers. Previous reports of this class of crystalline compound have illustrated that the pym ligand can connect from two to up to ten divalent metal ions [16]. Control of pH during coordination polymer self-assembly can afford completely deprotonated tetraanionic pym ligands [16], trianionic pymH ligands [17], dianionic pymH2 ligands [18], or monoanionic pymH3 ligands [19]. The tunable negative charges on the pym ligand backbone imparts different stoichiometric ratios with divalent metal ions, thereby imposing marked alterations in coordination polymer topology. At times, alkali metal cations [20] or non-ligating organoammonium cations [21] can be incorporated into voids and serve important templating roles in pym-containing coordination polymers.

Inclusion of neutral nitrogen base coligands has been efficacious in formation of functional coordination polymers containing the pym ligand and its partially protonated congeners; in nearly all cases wide diversity in topology is observed [22], [23], [24], [25]. [Cu(pymH2)(2,2′-bipyridine)]n is an excellent catalyst for cyclohexene oxidation [22a]; the 1D phase [Cu2(pym)(triethanolamine)2]n can catalytically CH activate cyclohexane in the presence of aqueous hydrogen peroxide [22b]. The rigid tetracarboxylate and dipyridyl ligands in {[Ni2(pym)(4,4′-bipyridine)3]·3DMF}n allow it to maintain a 3-D microporous structure and nitrogen absorption capacity after co-crystallized solvent removal [22c]. {[H2dpa][Cu3(pyro)2(H2O)3]·2.5H2O}n (dpa = 4,4′-dipyridylamine) displays an acentric 3-D 4,4,6-connected anionic network with a complicated topology, built from conjoined antiferromagnetic {Cu2(OCO)4} paddlewheel dimers and isolated copper ions [23]. Its lower temperature analog, {[Cu3(pyro)2(dpa)2(dpaH)2]·3H2O}n manifests a 3-D neutral network with (4264)4(6482) frl topology based on weakly ferromagnetically coupled {Cu3(OCO)2} linear trimers [23]. Alteration of nitrogen donor disposition within a isomeric dipyridyl ligands resulted in significant topological adjustment in a pair of 3D zinc/pym coordination polymers. {[Zn2(pymH)2(H2O)2(H2-3-bpmp)]·H2O}n (3-bpmp = bis(3-pyridylmethyl)piperazine) possesses a rarely encountered 4,5-connected (4462)(4466) tcs topology. {[Zn2(pymH)2(H2-4-bpmp)]·4H2O}n (4-bpmp = bis(4-pyridylmethyl)piperazine) exhibited a novel yet elegant 3,4-connected binodal (4.82)(4.82103) self-penetrated topology [24]. Use of the longer-spanning dipyridylamide ligand bis(4-pyridylformyl)piperazine (bpfp) generated 1D or 3D coordination polymers with the pym ligand and its protonated variants [25]. {Cd(pymH2)(bpfp)]·2H2O}n possesses an uncommon 3-D fsc network, while {[Zn2(pym)(bpfp)(H2O)8]·4H2O}n shows a supramolecular aggregation of 1D chain motifs. Heretofore there have been no reports of pym-containing divalent metal coordination polymers containing the extremely long-spanning isomeric dipyridylamide ligands 1,6-hexanediaminebis(nicotinamide) (hbn, Scheme 1) or 1,6-hexanediaminebis(isonicotinamide) (hbin, Scheme 1). In this contribution we report the synthesis and single-crystal structures of a series of divalent metal pyromellitate coordination polymers with the hbn or hbin ligands: [Cd(pymH2)(hbn)(H2O)2]n (1), [Co(pymH2)(hbn)(H2O)2]n (2), {[Cu2(pym)(hbn)(H2O)2]·2H2O}n (3), {[Zn2(pym)(hbin)2(H2O)]·H2O}n (4), and {[Ni2(pym)(hbin)(H2O)4]·3H2O}n (5). Thermal properties are also discussed herein, as well as luminescent properties and nitrobenzene detection capability of the d10 derivatives.

2. Experimental

2.1. General considerations

Metal nitrates and pyromellitic acid were commercially obtained. Condensation of 1,6-hexanediamine and two molar equivalents of nicotinoyl chloride hydrochloride in dry pyridine resulted in the preparation of the hbn ligand, which was isolated via CH2Cl2 extraction and rotary evaporation [26]. A similar reaction between 1,6-hexanediamine and two molar equivalents of isonicotinoyl chloride hydrochloride in dry pyridine afforded hbin after similar workup. Water was deionized above 3 MΩ–cm in-house. IR spectra were recorded on powdered samples using a Perkin Elmer Spectrum One DRIFT instrument. The luminescence spectra were obtained with a Hitachi F-4500 Fluorescence Spectrometer on solid crystalline samples anchored to quartz microscope slides with Rexon Corporation RX-22P ultraviolet-transparent epoxy adhesive. Thermogravimetric analysis was performed on a TA Instruments Q-50 Thermogravimetric Analyzer with a heating rate of 10 °C/min up to 600 °C. Elemental Analysis was carried out using a Perkin Elmer 2400 Series II CHNS/O Analyzer.

2.7. Nitrobenzene detection studies

Suspensions of 10 mg ground samples of coordination polymers 1 and 4 were made in 5 mL ethanol in volumetric flasks, with immersion in an ultrasonic bath for 60 s to ensure an even dispersion. The fluorescence spectra were recorded with an excitation wavelength of 300 nm. A stock solution of nitrobenzene (1 × 10−4 M) in dimethyl sulfoxide was prepared. Aliquots of this stock solution (10 μL) were added sequentially to ethanol suspensions of 1 and 4 with sonication for 30 s after each addition. The emission spectra were measured before any analyte addition and after each aliquot of analyte solution.

4. Results and discussion

4.1. Synthesis and spectra

Crystalline samples of 1–5 were produced by the hydrothermal reaction of metal nitrates, pyromellitic acid, and either hbn or hbin in the presence of aqueous base. The infrared spectra of 1–5 were consistent with their structural components as determined by single-crystal X-ray diffraction. Intense, broadened asymmetric and symmetric CO stretching bands within the carboxylate ligands were observed at 1550 and 1223 cm−1 for 1, at 1552 and 1230 cm−1 for 2, at 1558 and 1362 cm−1 for 3, at 1583 and 1366 cm−1 for 4, and at 1541 and 1387 cm−1 for 5. Medium intensity bands in the range of ∼1600 cm−1 to ∼1300 cm−1 were ascribed to stretching modes of the pyridyl rings of the dipyridylamide ligands and the aromatic rings of the pym ligands [31]. Features corresponding to CH bending and ring puckering within the pyridyl moieties exist in the region between ∼900 and ∼650 cm−1. Broad, weak spectral bands in the vicinity of ∼3000–3400 cm−1 indicate the presence of bound and unbound water molecules. The CO stretching bands of the amide group of the dipyridylamide ligands appeared at 1701 cm−1, 1650 cm−1, 1630 cm−1, 1651 cm−1 and 1631 cm−1 respectively for 1–5.

4.2. Structural description of [Cd(pymH2)(hbn)(H2O)2]n (1) and [Co(pymH2)(hbn)(H2O)2]n (2)

Compounds 1 and 2 are isostructural and therefore only compound 1 will be discussed in detail. Its asymmetric unit consists of a divalent cadmium situated on a crystallographic inversion center, half of a doubly protonated pymH2 ligand whose aromatic ring centroid is sited over another inversion center, half of an hbn ligand, and one bound water molecule. Operation of the inversion center at cadmium generates a {CdN2O4} coordination octahedron (Fig. 1) with trans aqua ligands, trans carboxylate oxygen donor atoms from two pymH2 ligands, and trans pyridyl nitrogen donor atoms from two hbn ligands. Bis(monodentate) pymH2 ligands connect cadmium atoms into [Cd(H2O)(pymH2)]n coordination polymer chains that are oriented parallel to the c crystal direction with a Cd⋯Cd internuclear distance of 11.696(3) Å (Fig. 2a). The protonated termini of the pymH2 ligands do not bind to cadmium. The chain motifs are in turn pillared into [Cd(H2O)(pymH2)(hbn)]n coordination polymer layers (Fig. 2b) by hbn ligands that bridge a Cd⋯Cd internuclear distance of 15.41(2) Å. All hbn ligands have an anti-anti-anti conformation of their central six-carbon atom aliphatic chain, with four-C atom torsion angles measuring 179.4°, 180.0°, and 179.4°. A very pinched (4,4) rhomboid grid topology is present within the [Cd(H2O)(pymH2)(hbn)]n coordination polymer layers, with Cd⋯Cd⋯Cd angles of 36.4° and 143.7°. The closest Cd⋯Cd distance between neighboring chain motifs, 9.159(2) Å, matches the b lattice parameter but is too short to be bridged by the long spanning hbn tethers. Within the coordination polymer layer motifs, supramolecular hydrogen bonding plays an ancillary structure direction role. Hydrogen bonding donation is provided by the bound water molecules to unligated carboxylate oxygen atoms of the pymH2 ligands, as well as from the hbn amide NH groups to the carbonyl oxygen atoms belonging to the protonated pymH2 carboxylate termini (Table S1). Adjacent [Cd(H2O)(pymH2)(hbn)]n layers stack along the a crystal direction in a simple AAA pattern, promoted by pairwise interlayer hydrogen bonding donation from the protonated carboxylic acid groups of the pymH2 ligands to unbound oxygen atoms belonging to the ligated pymH2 carboxylate groups (Fig. S1).

4.3. Structural description of {[Cu2(pym)(hbn)(H2O)2]·2H2O}n (3)

A square planar {CuO3N} coordination environment exists at copper, with trans carboxylate oxygen atom donors from two pym ligands. An aqua ligand and a pyridyl nitrogen donor atom from an hbn ligand occupy the remaining two trans coordination positions (Fig. 3a). Each pym ligand donates a single oxygen atom from all four of its carboxylate groups to a copper atom, resulting in an exotetradentate bridging mode. These connect copper atoms into [Cu2(pym)(H2O)2]n layers oriented parallel to the ab crystal planes (Fig. 3b). The Cu⋯Cu distance across the diagonal carboxylate groups of the pym ligands measures 10.868(1) Å. Adjacent [Cu2(pym)(H2O)2]n layers are pillared in an oblique direction by hbn ligands to form a 3D [Cu2(pym)(hbn)(H2O)2]n coordination polymer network (Fig. 4). The Cu⋯Cu internuclear distance is 19.398(2) Å through the anti-anti-anti conformation hbn ligands (torsion angles = 177.2°, 180°, 177.2°).

Treating each exotetradentate pym ligand as a 4-connected node, and each copper atom as a 3-connected node, results in the generation of a 3,4-connected binodal net with (83)2(86) topology (Fig. 5) as calculated by TOPOS [32]. This particular topology has been reported once before to the best of our knowledge, in the extended cyanide phase [Cu3Ni(CN)6(1,2-propanediamine)2] [33]. Water molecules of crystallization are held to the coordination polymer framework by hydrogen bonding donation to the hbn carbonyl groups (Table S1).

The pym ligands in 4 adopt an exotetradentate bridging mode in which one oxygen atom in each carboxylate group binds to a zinc atom, forming extended [Zn2(H2O)2(pym)]n ribbon motifs aligned parallel to the a crystal direction (Fig. 6b). The Zn⋯Zn distance across the ortho disposed carboxylate arms of the pym ligands is 5.509(9) Å, while the Zn⋯Zn distance across the meta disposed carboxylate arms of the pym ligands is 9.566(3) Å. The [Zn2(H2O)2(pym)]n ribbon motifs are decorated by pendant, monodentate hbin ligands that project off of both sides to construct [Zn2(pym)(hbin)2(H2O)2]n ribbons (Fig. 6c). Inter-ribbon hydrogen bonding between the NH groups within the hbin ligands, and CO groups belonging to other hbin ligands (Table S1), serves to aggregate neighboring ribbons into supramolecular layers (Fig. S2). Hydrogen bonding patterns involving bound and unligated water molecules and the unligated hbin pyridyl nitrogen atoms further aggregate the supramolecular layers into the 3D crystal structure of 4 (Fig. S3). Water molecules of crystallization occupy very small pockets within the supramolecular structure of 4.

4.5. Structural description of {[Ni2(pym)(hbin)(H2O)4]·3H2O}n (5)

The asymmetric unit of compound 5 contains a divalent nickel atom, half of a pym ligand whose centroid is sited over a crystallographic inversion center, half of an hbin ligand whose central CC sigma bond is positioned over another crystallographic inversion center, two aqua ligands and one-and-one-half water molecules of crystallization. The nickel atoms display a {NiO5N} distorted coordination octahedron (Fig. 7) with the nominal axial positions filled by a pair of bound water molecules; its equatorial plane is occupied by a chelating pym carboxylate group, a single oxygen atom donor from a second pym ligand, and a pyridyl nitrogen donor atom belonging to the hbin ligand. Exotetradentate pym ligands with two opposite chelating carboxylate groups and two opposite single carboxylate oxygen atom donors create [Ni2(pym)(H2O)4]n coordination polymer ribbons that are arranged parallel to the b crystal direction (Fig. 8a). 4.6. Thermal properties

Compound 1 underwent dehydration between 90 and 175 °C, with an observed mass loss of 4.7% consistent with elimination of the bound water molecules. Ligand combustion occurred above 175 °C. The final mass remnant of 14.0% at 600 °C is consistent with a putative deposition of Cd metal (15.5% calc’d). Compound 2 underwent partial dehydration between 50 and 170 °C, with a mass loss of 3.3% shy of the predicted value of 5.4% for elimination of two molar equivalents of water. Elimination of the remaining water molecules and organic ligands occurred above 170 °C. The mass remnant of 27.4% at 600 °C is consistent with a possible deposition of CoCO3 (17.7% calc’d) and uncombusted organic char. Compound 3 underwent partial dehydration between 170 and 220 °C, with a mass loss of 7.6% somewhat under the expected value of 9.2% for loss of four molar equivalents of water. Complete dehydration and ligand combustion occurred above 220 °C. The final mass remnant of 19.0% is consistent with a possible deposition of CuO (20.5% calc’d). Compound 4 underwent complete dehydration and partial decarboxylation between 135 and 175 °C, with a mass loss of 7.4% very close to the predicted value of 7.2% for elimination of two molar equivalents of water and one molar equivalent of CO2. Ligand combustion occurred above 290 °C. The final mass remnant of 25.7% corresponds to a putative deposition of ZnCO3 (22.7% calc’d) and some residual uncombusted organics. Compound 5 underwent full dehydration between 150 and 220 °C, with a mass loss of 15.4% corresponding very well with elimination of seven molar equivalents of water. Ligand combustion was observed above 270 °C. The mass remnant of 25.3% corresponds to a possible mixture of NiCO3 (28.9% calc’d) and NiO (18.2% calc’d). The TGA traces for 1–5 are shown in Figs. S5–S9, respectively.

4.7. Luminescent properties

Polycrystalline samples of 1 and 4 were irradiated with ultraviolet light in ethanol suspension in order to investigate their capability for photoluminescence and nitrobenzene detection. Excitation spectra were first obtained in order to determine the best wavelength for photoluminescent excitation, via monitoring emission at a single wavelength of 400 nm. For both 1 and 4, the maximum excitation was observed at 270 nm, respectively. Compound 1 only undergoes weak emissions at 300 nm and 374 nm (Fig. S10), while compound 2 manifested more intense luminescence and two discrete emission features centered at 318 nm and 465 nm (Fig. S11). It is most likely that the fluorescent behavior of these materials can be attributed to π-π∗ or π-n molecular orbital transitions within the pyridyl rings of the dipyridylamide ligands and the aromatic rings of the pym ligands [35]. No reduction in emission intensity was observed on exposing ethanol suspensions of 1 and 4 to aliquots of nitrobenzene dissolved in DMSO, likely due to an inability of the aromatic analyte from being absorbed into the supramolecular crystal structures with strong hydrogen bonding between the layer and chain motifs, respectively.

5. Conclusions

A series of divalent metal coordination polymers containing pyromellitate derived anionic ligands and long spanning dipyridylamide ligands was successfully prepared and structurally characterized by X-ray diffraction. Partial protonation of the pyromellitate ligands afforded 2D rhomboid grid coordination polymers, with extension to 3D provided by supramolecular interactions induced by hydrogen bonding imparted by the protonated carboxylate groups. In the copper case, full deprotonation of the pym ligands afforded coordination polymer layers with a 2:1 metal-to-ligand ratio. Pillaring by the long spanning hbn ligands afforded a rarely encountered 3D coordination polymer network. In the zinc case, a lower coordination number induced an inability to bind two pyridyl nitrogen atoms, resulting in a 1D coordination polymer chain structure featuring monodentate, pendant hbin ligands. Expansion of the coordination environment in the nickel case resulted in an uncommon 2D layer motif that does not match a standard grid topology. No clear structural trends were observed in the thermal stability of these complexes. While the cadmium derivative showed weak photoluminescence upon excitation with ultraviolet light, with the zinc congener exhibiting much stronger emission, neither phase showed suitability as a detector for nitrobenzene in ethanol suspension.

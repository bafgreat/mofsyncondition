In the past decade crystal engineering of porous metal–organic coordination networks has attracted a great deal of attention, due to their interesting structural patterns for aesthetic appeal and special functionalities for potential applications from materials science, biology, separation and catalysis to gas storage.1–6 However, the prediction of coordination network structures is still ambiguous, except for a few known systems, because the formation of a metal–organic network is sensitive to various factors, such as reaction conditions, metal coordination geometries and secondary assistant ligands, etc.7

During the course of our synthesis of porous networks, we expected to investigate the roles of the mixed ligands and the coordination preferences of different metal cations for the construction of distinct network architectures. Pyridine-2,3,5,6-tetracarboxylic acid (H4pdtc),8 which contains one pyridine and four carboxylate potential donor groups, should be an effective ligand for the construction of novel metal–organic networks.9 We expected that the pdtc4− ligand employing its four carboxylategroups and one pyridine donor might have the ability to coordinate to different metal cations to generate some interesting structural motifs, which can be further interlinked by rigid 4,4′-bipyridine (4,4′-bpy) ligands to form some interesting structural networks. Herein, we report two new 3D porous compounds, [Cu3(H2O)4(Hpdtc)2(4,4′-bpy)2]·3H2O (1) and [Zn4(H2O)6(pdtc)2(4,4′-bpy)]·5H2O (2), which provided instructive information to understand the driving forces of the coordination ligands and the metal geometries for the designed synthesis of some interesting networks.

The reaction of copper nitrate, H4pdtc and 4,4′-bpy in a mixed solvent of water and EtOH at room temperature afforded blue crystals of 1.10Single-crystal X-ray diffraction analysis has revealed that compound 1 crystallizes in the monoclinic P2/n space group.11 There are one and a half copper(ii) cations, one monoprotonated Hpdtc3− ligand, one 4,4′-bpy ligand, two aqua ligands, and one and a half water molecules in the asymmetric unit (Fig. 1). Each Hpdtc3− ligand employs its pyridinegroup and two neighbouring monodentate carboxylategroups to chelate one copper cation (Scheme 1). The third carboxylategroup bridges two copper cations in syn–anti fashion, while the remaining uncoordinated carboxylategroup is protonated and disordered. Subsequently, two neighbouring Hpdtc3− ligands bridge two neighbouring copper cations to form a dinuclear unit. Each copper cation coordinates octahedrally to one chelating Hpdtc3− ligand, one carboxylgroup from a neighbouring Hpdtc3− ligand, one 4,4′-bpy ligand and one aqua ligand. The second copper cation, which is on the two-fold axis, coordinates to two Hpdtc3−carboxylgroups, two pyridine ligands and two water molecules in an octahedral geometry. The second copper cation performs as a connecting node to join neighbouring copper dinuclear units into a linear network. Interestingly, the 4,4′-bpy ligands, which are inversion centre symmetry related, coordinate to two different copper sites linking the linear frameworks into a 3D porous structure running along three different orientations. The small cavities formed are filled with guest water molecules.

When copper nitrate was replaced by zinc nitrate in the synthesis of 1, colourless crystals of 2 were formed after one day.10Single-crystal X-ray diffraction analysis has revealed that compound 2 crystallizes in the monoclinic P21/c space group.11 There are four zinc(ii) cations, two pdtc4− ligands, one 4,4′-bpy ligand, six aqua ligands and five lattice water molecules in the asymmetric unit (Fig. 2). Each pdtc4− acts as a heptadentate ligand coordinating to four zinc atoms. The coordination mode of pdtc4− is slightly different to that in 1, as there are two additional carboxylgroups coordinating to two zinc cations in 2 (Scheme 1). According to the coordination environments, there are two types of zinc atoms in 2. The first type (Zn1 and Zn3) is chelated by the pyridinegroup and two neighbouring carboxylgroups and further coordinates to one carboxylgroup from a neighbouring pdtc4− ligand and one pyridinegroup of the 4,4′-bpy ligand to furnish the square-pyramidal coordination environment. Similar to that in 1, the second type (Zn2 and Zn4) comprises an octahedral coordination environment, coordinating to two carboxylgroups of two pdtc4− ligands, one 4,4′-bpy pyridinegroup and three water molecules. However, the slightly changed coordination mode of the pdtc4− ligand caused it to link the zinc cations into a 3D porous network structure. The 4,4′-bpy ligands occupy the cavities to coordinate to the zinc atoms.

It is interesting to note that the five lattice water molecules in 1 are hydrogen bonded to each other forming a four-water-membered watercluster with O–H⋯O distances ranging from 2.821(4)–2.897(4) Å. Furthermore, the water molecules are hydrogen bonded to aqua ligands (O–H⋯O = 2.683(4)–2.864(3) Å) and carboxyloxygen atoms (O–H⋯O = 2.796(3)–2.8209(4) Å), while the aqua ligands are hydrogen bonded to carboxyloxygen atoms (O–H⋯O = 2.667(3)–2.925(3) Å). Most interestingly, with the omission of organic ligands, the crystallizationwater molecules link the zinc octahedra and square-pyramids forming an interesting supramolecular network (Fig. 3).

There are many interesting examples of CuII or ZnII cations bridged by the pyridine carboxylates, such as pyridine 3,4-,12 2,3-,13 2,4-,14 2,5-,15 2,6-16 and 2,4,6-tricarboxylate.17 However, the dinuclear units and the linear polymeric subunits in 1 and 2 are different from previously reported CuII or ZnII compounds bridged by pyridine carboxylates. This is probably due to the multifunctional nature of the ligand to chelate and bridge the metal centres to form the distinct subunits. Firstly, it chelates a metal cation through the nitrogen atom and the two neighbouring oxygen atoms of the two carboxylic groups. The two chelated metal units are then linked into a dinuclear unit by the oxygen atoms of the third carboxylategroup. Subsequently, another oxygen atom of the third carboxylategroup coordinates to the metal centres of the neighbouring dinuclear subunit to propagate into a linear polymeric subunit both in 1 and 2. This type of coordination mode has not been observed in other pyridinecarboxylate-bridged compounds. The polymeric subunits are linked by 4,4′-bpy into the 3D network of 1, while the polymeric subunits are joined into the 3D framework of 2 through the remaining oxygen atom of the nitrogen neighboured carboxylategroups and the fourth carboxylategroup.

Thermogravimetric analyses (TGA) for the polycrystalline samples of 1 and 2 were carried out from 30 to 950 °C under a N2 atmosphere. TGA measurement of 1 reveals that a gradual weight loss of 11.4% from 30 to 110 °C corresponds to the loss of seven lattice and coordinated water molecules (calcd: 11.1%) that took place without showing distinct plateau in the curve. In the second stage, a plateau was observed between 110 and 390 °C. When the temperature was elevated to 390 °C, the product began to lose coordination ligands and decomposed gradually. TGA measurement of 2 reveals that the gradual weight loss of 17.5% from 30 to 130 °C corresponds to the loss of eleven lattice and coordinated water molecules (calcd: 17.7%). In the second stage, a plateau was observed between 130 and 266 °C. The product began to decompose when the temperature was raised to 266 °C. The purities of the bulk samples of 1 and 2 were confirmed by comparison of the experimental results with the calculated curves obtained from single-crystal structural analyses.

In summary, in this communication we have employed the rarely used multifunctional H4pdtc ligand and the rigid 4,4′-bpy ligand to generate two new 3D porous network structures. The slightly changed coordination modes of the pdtc4− ligand and the coordination environments of the metal centers produced two distinct topological networks, which should provide valuable information for the designed synthesis of some functional solids for potential applications. Most interestingly, the roles of the lattice water molecules in 2 were changed from guests to supramolecular linkers connecting the metal polyhedra forming a 3D supramolecular network structure.

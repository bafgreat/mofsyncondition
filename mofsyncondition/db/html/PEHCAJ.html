Introduction

Nitrogen-rich compounds have been used world-wide in the field of energetic materials in recent years. The high nitrogen content of these compounds results in their great potential to be used as explosives due to the large number of inherent N–N, NN and C–N bonds.1–3 Recently, lots of studies of new high energy density materials (HEDM) continue to focus on the derivatives of furazan,4–6 triazole7–10 and tetrazole.11–14 Furthermore, the nitrogen-rich compounds are green and protect the environment as they mainly release nontoxic N2 upon decomposition.15 In general, tetrazole is more energetic but also more sensitive towards external stimuli, whereas triazoles (1,2,3-triazole and 1,2,4-triazole) are less sensitive.16 In regard to the requirements of the detonation performance and safety, triazoles are the most promising backbones.

At present, a great number of studies have concentrated on 1,2,4-triazoles17–22 and salts,23–25 but only few reports on the derivatives of 1,2,3-triazoles are known. In 1992, the first report on 4-amino-5-nitro-1,2,3-2H-triazole (ANTZ)26 was published, with the nitrogen content being 54%. ANTZ is a high energy candidate and is also an important intermediate in the synthesis of energy materials. Surprisingly, ANTZ (Td, 297 °C) is more thermally stable than 3-amino-5-nitro-1,2,4-triazole (Td, 243 °C). The single-crystal structure of 4-nitramino-5-nitro-1,2,3-2H-triazole27 with a high density (1.919 g cm−3) was obtained in 2003. So, investigation into the nitrogen-rich derivatives of 1,2,3-triazole is a very promising research direction. Here, the goal of this study has therefore been to synthesise nitrogen-rich 4,5-substituted 1,2,3-triazole compounds: 4-carboxamide-5-cyano-2H-1,2,3-triazole (1, nitrogen content: 51%), 4-carboxamide-5-(1H-tetrazol-5-yl)-1H-1,2,3-triazole (2, nitrogen content: 62%), and its sodium complex (3). All of the compounds were comprehensively characterized and the decomposition temperature and kinetics parameters of 2 and 3 were obtained.

Results and discussion

Synthesis

There was a possible synthesis route for the new nitrogen-rich compound 4-carboxamide-5-(1H-tetrazol-5-yl)-1H-1,2,3-triazole (2). The synthesis route is described in Scheme 1. 4,5-Dicyano-2H-1,2,3-triazole28 was prepared from diaminomaleodinitrile using one step reactions reported in the literature. 4,5-Dicyano-2H-triazole could be hydrolysed with 1 M HCl and 1 M acetic acid to obtain 4-carboxamide-5-cyano-2H-1,2,3-triazole (1) in 67% yield. Compound 1 could be recrystallized with hot water. The reaction of compound 1 with sodium azide and zinc chloride in a 1,3-dipolar cycloaddition achieved 4-carboxamide-5-(1H-tetrazol-5-yl)-1H-1,2,3-triazole (2) in 78% yield. The sodium complex of 4-carboxamide-5-(1H-tetrazol-5-yl)-1H-1,2,3-triazole (3) was synthesized by the metathesis reaction of compound 2 and sodium hydroxide. Complex 3 was stable and could be stored in air for extended periods.

Single-crystal X-ray structure analysis

The X-ray crystallographic data of compound 1 hydrate and complex 3 were obtained with a Rigaku AFC-10/Saturn 724+ CCD diffractometer with graphite mono-chromated Mo-Kα radiation. The structures were solved by direct methods using SHELXS97V (ref. 29) and were refined anisotropically on F2 by the full-matrix least-squares technique using the SHELXL97 (ref. 30) programs. The crystallographic data and structure refinements are summarized in Table 1 and can be obtained free of charge on quoting the depository numbers CCDC-1497228 (1) and CCDC-1497227 (3).†

The 4-carboxamide-5-cyano-2H-1,2,3-triazole hydrate (1) crystallized in the monoclinic space group P21/c with four formula units per unit cell and a calculated density of 1.529 g cm−3 at 298 K. As shown in Fig. 1, the bond lengths in the 4,5-substituted 1,2,3-triazole are between the length of the corresponding single and double bonds. The N1–N2 bond (1.320(3) Å) is almost the same length as the N2–N3 bond (1.323(3) Å), and the triazole ring is symmetrical. The amide C1–N4 bond (1.318(4) Å) is shorter than the single bonds, because the amide group is almost planar and is affected by the unshared electron pair of the nitrogen atom. The C4–N5 bond of the cyano (1.139(4) Å) is a corresponding triple bond. An intermolecular hydrogen bond is formed (N5–H4⋯O1) between the amide group and oxygen atom of the neighboring amide (D⋯A: 2.173 Å; D–H⋯A: 167°). A hydrogen bond is also formed between the amide group and a nitrogen atom of a neighboring triazole ring N4–H4⋯N1 (D⋯A: 2.396 Å; D–H⋯A: 162°). Moreover, the 4-carboxamide-5-cyano-2H-1,2,3-triazole crystallizes with one molecule of water to form its monohydrate. The molecule has intermolecular hydrogen bonds: N2–H2⋯O2 (D⋯A: 2.661 Å; D–H⋯A: 169°), O2–H2⋯O1 (D⋯A: 1.962 Å; D–H⋯A: 156°), and O2–H2⋯N5 (D⋯A: 2.125 Å; D–H⋯A: 156°). Furthermore, detailed information on the crystal structure determination has been compiled and is in the ESI (Tables S1 and S2†).

The title nitrogen-rich sodium complex of 4-carboxamide-5-(1H-tetrazol-5-yl)-1H-1,2,3-triazole (3) crystallized in the triclinic space group P with one formula unit per unit cell. The molecular unit is depicted in Fig. 2. The bond lengths of triazole and tetrazole are also between formal single and double bonds. Furthermore, the bond lengths of the triazole ring are different from that of 1·H2O with N2–N3 and N3–N4 bond lengths of 1.310(5) and 1.341(4) Å respectively. It has been found that sodium forms a chelated structure with the triazole ring. The sodium ion displays an octahedral hexa-coordinate complex which is coordinated by the nitrogen atoms N2 and N3 of two triazole rings, the oxygen atom O1 of the amide group and oxygen atoms O2, O2, O3 of the coordinated water molecules. The complex 3 forms a 1D chain-like structure constructed of coordination bonds. The structure is dominated by an extensive hydrogen bonding network involving three nitrogen atoms and four oxygen atoms from the coordinated water molecules. An intermolecular hydrogen bond is formed (N1–H1⋯N8; D⋯A: 2.027 Å, D–H⋯A: 176° and N1–H1⋯N5; D⋯A: 1.964 Å, D–H⋯A: 164°) between the amide group and the nitrogen atom on the neighboring tetrazole (D⋯A: 2.173 Å; D–H⋯A: 167°). Another hydrogen bond is also formed between the nitrogen atom of triazole ring and the oxygen atoms of the neighboring amide group (N4–H4⋯O1; D⋯A: 2.013 Å, D–H⋯A: 160°). Moreover, there are intermolecular hydrogen bonds between the coordinated water molecules and the nitrogen atom of tetrazole (O2–H2⋯N6; D⋯A: 2.085 Å, D–H⋯A: 166° and O3–H3⋯N7; D⋯A: 2.061 Å, D–H⋯A: 158°). Lastly, there is the detailed information of the crystal structure determination h in the ESI (Tables S3 and S4†).

Thermal analyses

The differential scanning calorimetry (DSC) showed the thermal behaviors of 2 and 3 in the temperature range 20–500 °C at a heating rate of 5 °C min−1 (see Fig. 3). The phase transition processes of 2 start at 200 °C with a peak temperature of 223.3 °C, which ends at 241 °C. It is a sharp exothermic process with a peak temperature of 332.6 °C. For 3, it was found in the DSC curve that two exothermic stages appear. Two sharp exothermic processes appear with peak temperatures of 374.1 °C and 410.4 °C respectively. Both of the compounds have the advantages of good thermal stability. Furthermore, 3 has higher thermal stability than 2 in the high temperature conditions.

Theoretical calculations and properties

The density and enthalpy of formation are two important parameters in energetic materials. In this work, the enthalpy of formation was computed using the method of atomization energy and the Gaussian 09 suite of programs. The density also was computed using the Gaussian 09 suite of programs. The geometric optimization of the structure and the frequency analysis carried out using the B3LYP functional with the 6-311++G** basis set. The optimized structure was characterized to be the true local energy minimum on the potential energy surface without imaginary frequencies.

The detonation pressure and detonation velocity of an energetic compound can be calculated by using empirical Kamlet–Jacobs equations34 with the value of enthalpy of formation and density. For further analysis, the HOMO–LUMO orbital was calculated to reveal the potential sensitivity of compound 2 . As shown in Fig. 4, the red parts relate to the positive area and the green parts relate to the negative area. In the HOMO orbital, the electron distributes uniformly in the whole molecule, but in the LUMO orbital, it concentrates on the triazole part and the amide group part, not the tetrazole part. The energy level difference between the HOMO–LUMO orbital is 5.99 eV, which can indicate the sensitivity of the energetic material. Therefore, compound 2 can be considered as a kind of high-energy material with low sensitivity.

For the initial safety testing, the impact and friction sensitivities of 2 were measured by following BAM standard methods. The results are also shown in Table 3. It is not surprising that compound 2 is insensitive towards impact (>40 J) and friction (>360 N).

Experimental section

Material and instruments

All of the chemical reagents and solvents of analytical grade were obtained from commercial sources. Elemental analyses were performed on a Flash EA 1112 fully automatic trace element analyzer. The FT-IR spectra were recorded as KBr pellets on a Bruker Equinox 55. The mass spectra were recorded on an Agilent 500-MS. Differential scanning calorimetry (DSC) was carried out on a model Pyris-1 differential scanning calorimeter in static air. The single-crystal X-ray diffraction analysis was carried out on a Bruker CCD area-detector diffractometer.

4,5-Dicyano-2H-1,2,3-triazole

4,5-Dicyano-2H-1,2,3-triazole was synthesized according to the published method.28

4-Carboxamide-5-cyano-2H-1,2,3-triazole (1)

4,5-Dicyano-2H-1,2,3-triazole (2.38 g, 20 mmol) was refluxed in an aqueous solution of AcOH (1 M 25 mL) and HCl (1 M 25 mL) for 30 min. The solution was cooled to 0 °C, whereby the precipitate formed was filtered off and recrystallized from hot water to afford 1 hydrate colourless needles (1.84 g, 67%). IR (KBr, cm−1): 3545, 3481, 3397, 3305, 3242, 2918, 2850, 2606, 2263, 2151, 1942, 1705, 1612, 1521, 1434, 1366, 1215, 1145, 990, 953, 795, 720, 640. MS (ESI−): m/z = 136.10 ([M]−). EA for C4H3N5O1 (%): calcd: C 35.04, H 2.21; N 51.08; found: C 35.12, H 2.16, N 51.15.

4-Carboxamide-5-(1H-tetrazol-5-yl)-1H-1,2,3-triazole (2)

Compound 1 (1.37 g, 10 mmol) was dissolved in water (50 mL) and sodium azide (0.78 g, 12 mmol) and zinc chloride (1.5 g, 11 mmol) were added. The reaction mixture was stirred and heated under reflux for 4 h. The solution was then cooled to ambient temperature and concentrated hydrochloric acid (10 mL) was added while cooling to 0 °C. The precipitate was removed by filtration under suction and the product was washed with cold water and ethanol to obtain the compound 2 (1.4 g, 78%). IR (KBr, cm−1): 3348, 3223, 3092, 2921, 2858, 2781, 1685, 1587, 1469, 1309, 1217, 1175, 1125, 1048, 992, 858, 714, 666. MS (ESI−): m/z = 179.10 ([M]−). Sodium complex of 4-carboxamide-5-(1H-tetrazol-5-yl)-1H-1,2,3-triazole (3)

Sodium hydroxide (0.08 g, 2 mmol) was added to a suspension of 2 (0.36 g, 2 mmol) and water (10 mL). The mixture was stirred at 50 °C for 1 h, the clear solution was slowly evaporated and single crystals of 3 were obtained. Conclusion

In this work, the synthetic routes, structures and properties of 4-carboxamide-5-(1H-tetrazol-5-yl)-1H-1,2,3-triazole (2) and its sodium complex (3) were reported. A single-crystal structure of complex 3 was obtained for the first time and it showed a 1D chain-like structure, with the sodium ions forming a chelated structure with the triazole. Compound 2 (332.6 °C) and complex 3 (374.1 °C) had the advantage of good thermal stability. The kinetic parameters of the exothermal process of compound 2 and complex 3 were calculated using Kissinger’s and Ozawa-Doyel’s methods. The Arrhenius equations of 2 and 3 were obtained. Compound 2 had a high nitrogen content (62%), and the detonation pressure (21.9 GPa) and detonation velocity (7182 m s−1) were calculated by using Kamlet–Jacobs equations. The impact sensitivity (>40 J) and friction sensitivity (>360 N) of 2 were measured. Based on its detonation properties and stabilities, 4-carboxamide-5-(1H-tetrazol-5-yl)-1H-1,2,3-triazole was an insensitive energetic material.

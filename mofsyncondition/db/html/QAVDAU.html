A novel 3D open framework constructed by [Co15(PMIDA)6(H2O)12] clusters and BTC ligands

Author links open overlay panelZhi-HongLiua
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.inoche.2011.11.002Get rights and content

Abstract

A new cobalt coordination polymer based on N-(Phosphonomethyl)imino-diacetic acid (H4PMIDA) and 1,3,5-benzenetricarboxylate acid (H3BTC) ligands, [Co15(PMIDA)6(BTC)2(H2O)12]·6H2O, has been synthesized under hydrothermal conditions, and characterized by elemental analysis, FT-IR spectroscopy, thermal analysis, and single crystal X-ray diffraction. The structure of the complex features a cubic 3D open framework constructed by [Co15(PMIDA)6(H2O)12] cluster and BTC ligand. The magnetic properties of this compound were also investigated.

The investigation of the assembly of porous metal–organic frameworks (MOFs) has attracted much interest due to their structural diversity and promising applications for ion exchange, gas storage, separation, and catalysis. Many MOFs are constructed from preformed secondary building units, which are typically a few metal atoms grouped into a cluster that is chelated by carboxylic groups in carboxylic acid [[1], [2], [3]]. But very little progress has been made in using large clusters formed by phosphonic acid as building blocks for the hierarchical assembly of MOFs [[4], [5], [6]]. In recent years, the direct use of two types of organic acid ligands, such as a phosphonic acid and a carboxylic acid, has been found to be another effective method for the exploration of MOFs. In 2005, Mao et al. [7] reported the hydrothermal reactions of the lanthanide chlorides with MeN(CH2CO2H)(CH2PO3H2) (or Me2NCH2PO3H2) and sodium oxalate leading to seven new lanthanide oxalate phosphonate hybrids with three types of 3D network structures. In 2006, Mao et al. [8] further synthesized a 3D network complex of [La2(ox)2(H6L)]·2H2O with tunnels running along the a-axis by using the (H2O3PCH2)2NCH2CH2CH2CH2N(CH2PO3H2)2 (H8L) and H2C2O4 as the co-ligands. In 2009, Sevov et al. [9] synthesized a complex of [Co15L6(cis-H3E)2(H2O)18]·12H2O by using the N(CH2COOH)2(CH2PO3H2) (H4L) and C6H6(COOH)6 (H6E) as the co-ligands, which feature as 3D open-framework constructed by Co15L6 clusters and cis-H3E ligands. However, such investigation is still limited, and it still remains a great challenge for the exploration of MOFs with the use of phosphonic acid and a carboxylic acid as the co-ligands. Herein, we report the synthesis and crystal structure of a new cobalt coordination polymer based on N-(Phosphonomethyl)imino-diacetic acid (H4PMIDA) and 1,3,5-benzenetricarboxylate acid (H3BTC) ligands, [Co15(PMIDA)6(BTC)2(H2O)12]·6H2O (1), which has a cubic 3D open framework constructed by [Co15(PMIDA)6(H2O)12] clusters and BTC ligands.

The hydrothermal reaction of Co(CH3COO)2·4H2O with H3BTC and H4PMIDA resulted in crystals with two different morphologies, cubes and hexagonal prisms [10]. The latter crystals were identified by single-crystal X-ray diffraction as Co2[O3PCH2N(CH2COO)2(H2O)5]·H2O, which has been reported [11]. The former complex is a new compound reported here.

X-ray analyses [12] reveal that the compound (1) contains fifteen cobalt(II) ions, six PMIDA4 − ligands, two BTC3 − ligands, and twelve aqua ligands as well as six lattice water molecules per formula unit. There are four crystallographically independent cobalt atoms in each asymmetric unit, which exhibit regular distribution as shown in Fig. 1. The outer Co1 atom is hexahedrally coordinated by one phosphonate oxygen atom (O9), two carboxylic oxygen atoms (O3,O5) from three PMIDA4 − ligands as the monodentate forms, two carboxylic oxygen atoms (O1, O2) from one BTC3 − ligand as the chelate form (Fig. 2), and one coordinated water molecule (O1w). The second outer Co2 atom is hexahedrally coordinated by one nitrogen atom (N1A), three carboxylic oxygen atoms(O4,O4A,O5A) from three PMIDA4 − ligands and two phosphonate oxygen atoms (O7C,O9A) from two PMIDA4 − ligands, in which the N1A, O4A, O5A and O7C come from the same one PMIDA4 − ligand. The central Co3 atom is hexahedrally coordinated by six phosphonate oxygen atoms (O7, O7A, O7B, O7C, O7D, O7E) from six PMIDA4 − ligands as the monodentate forms. The outer Co4 atom is hexahedrally coordinated by three phosphonate oxygen atoms (O8, O8A, O8B) from three PMIDA4 − ligands as the monodentate forms and three coordinated water molecules. The CoO bond lengths are in the range of 2.045(3)–2.215(3) Å, and the CoN bond length is 2.185(3) Å.

As shown in Fig. 2, each PMIDA4 − ligand in the compound coordinates to eight Co atoms, with the phosphonate group adopting a μ5-η2:η2:η1 bridging mode, the two carboxylate groups exhibiting μ2-η2:η0 and μ3-η2:η1 modes respectively, and the N atom only exhibiting μ1 mode.

The interconnection of the fifteen Co(II) ions by six PMIDA4 − bridging ligands resulted in a bulbiform fifteen nuclear cluster unit, [Co15(PMIDA)6(H2O)12]. Each [Co15(PMIDA)6(H2O)12] cluster is connected to six BTC3 − ligands by coordinating deprotonated carboxylic group O atoms to six Co1 atoms as the chelate form, and each BTC3 − ligand is connected to three [Co15(PMIDA)6(H2O)12] clusters by coordinating deprotonated carboxylic group O atoms to Co1 atoms from three different clusters. The alternate connectivity between the [Co15(PMIDA)6(H2O)12] clusters and the BTC3 − ligands through their Co1O bonds as the chelate forms leads to the 3D open-framework with two different kinds of channel tropisms along the a, b and c axes, respectively, in which the lattice water molecules are located (Fig. 3). Taking the Co15 clusters and the BTC3− ligands as 6-connecting nodes and 3-connecting nodes, respectively, the 3D network can be viewed as a 3,6-conneced binodal net. The Schläfli notation is (612 · 83)(63)2.

Moreover, there exist weak inner molecular hydrogen bonds among the coordinated water molecules (O1W and O2W) and adjacent carboxylic group O atoms (O2 and O6), with O⋯O distances in the range of 2.756(5)–3.005(5) Å. There also exist weak hydrogen bonds between the lattice water molecules (O3W) and the coordinated water molecules (O2W), with the O⋯O distances being 2.923(7) Å.

The structure of compound 1 is related to that of [Co15L6(cis-H3E)2(H2O)18]·12H2O in the literature [9], in which they have a similar Co15L6 cluster unit and they all have 3D open-framework, but there are some differences between them in crystal structures. The known [Co15L6(cis-H3E)2(H2O)18]·12H2O compound belongs to the monoclinic space group C2/c, in which each Co15L6 cluster is connected to six cis-H3E ligands using the three deprotonated carboxylic groups at positions 1, 2, and 4 to coordinate via one of the O atoms to peripheral Co atoms (Co3, Co4 and Co6) as the monodentate forms, forming a 3D structure with the larger channels along the b and c axes. While the present compound crystallizes in cubic space group Pa-3, in which each [Co15(PMIDA)6(H2O)12] cluster is connected to six BTC3 − ligands by coordinating deprotonated carboxylic group O atoms to six Co1 atoms as the chelate forms, forming a 3D structure with the same channels along the a, b and c axes. Obviously, the present Co15L6 cluster has higher symmetry than the reported Co15 cluster, and there is triple anti-axis, with four crystallographically independent Co(II) (while with eight crystallographically independent Co(II) in the reported Co15 cluster). In addition, the Co3 atom is the symmetry center of Co15L6 cluster. The structural differences between the two compounds with similar Co15L6 cluster unit can be attributed to the different carboxylic ligands used, namely from the flexible C6H6(COOH)6 to the rigid H3BTC.

TGA curve (Fig. S1) shows that the compound 1 has three step weight loss between 70 and 1000 °C. The first weight loss is 11.10% occurring from 70 to 300 °C, which corresponds to the loss of 18 water molecules and can be compared with calculated value of 10.95%. The following two steps occurring from 300 to 1000 °C correspond to the decomposition of the ligands.

The magnetic susceptibilities (χ) of compound 1 were measured under 1 kOe in the 1.8–300 K range (Fig. 4). In the high temperature range, the two compounds show similar magnetic behaviors. The χT values at 300 K are 42.3 emu mol− 1 K, and this value is in the normal range for octahedral CoII with an unquenched orbital momentum. As the temperature is lowered, the χT values first decrease slowly to round minima of 32.3 emu K mol− 1 at 20 K, then rises rapidly until 1.8 K (χT = 48 emu K mol− 1). The data above 50 K follows the Curie–Weiss law with C = 44.3 emu mol− 1 K and θ = − 13.5 K (see supporting information, Fig S2).This features suggest an overall antiferromagnetic (AFM) coupling between the Co(II) ions within the Co15 cluster, while the behavior below 20 K may be due to the ferrimagnetic-like interaction in the low temperature range. However, because of the rather complicated structure of 1 with many possible exchange interactions between Co(II) ions, any further rationalization or simulation of the magnetic properties of 1 is unavailable.

The isothermal magnetization at 2 K is shown in Fig. 5. The magnetization in the high-field region increases slowly and linearly, and the value of 24.4 Nβ at 35 kOe is far from saturation. These are consistent with the overall AFM interactions of the Co15 cluster, and are similar to the magnetic behavior of the above-mentioned [Co15L6(cis-H3E)2(H2O)18]·12H2O, with also an overall AFM interaction between the Co(II) ions within the Co15 cluster. However, no indications of long-range ordering or slow-relaxation can be found from the field-cooled (FC) and zero-field-cooled (ZFC) magnetization measurements and the thermal ac measurements (see supporting information, Fig S3 and Fig S4).

In summary, we have successfully synthesized a new MOF constructed by [Co15(PMIDA)6(H2O)12] cluster and BTC ligand by adjusting the ligand from the flexible to the rigid one. The magnetic property of 1 was also investigated.

    2017, Polyhedron
    Citation Excerpt :

    N-(phosphonomethyl)iminodiacetic acid (H4pmida), a phosphonic derivative of nitrilotriacetic acid (H3nta), is a multidentate chelating ligand. The presence of two flexible chelating arms containing carboxylic acid groups, a central N-donor atom and a phosphonate group, with additional flexible coordination properties, enables the construction of organic–inorganic hybrid materials (including heterometallic systems) with intriguing architectures and new framework topologies [1–6]. For these reasons, much attention has been focused on the synthesis and structural characterization of multi-dimensional coordination polymers which incorporate the pmida4− ligand.

    The crystal and molecular structures of a new dinuclear oxidovanadium(IV) complex with the N-(phosphonomethyl)iminodiacetate (pmida) ligand and the 4-amino-2-methylquinolinium ([amqH]+) cation of the molecular formula [amqH]4[V2O2(pmida)2]6H2O have been determined. The phosphonate groups in the two pmida4− ligands form two bridges between adjacent V4+ atoms, resulting in the formation of a [V2O2(pmida)2]4− dimer with a V2O4P2 eight-membered ring. This is the first example of a quinoline derivative complex containing the [V2O2(pmida)2]4− ion. The susceptibility curve for the complex exhibits a maximum at approximately 10 K, indicating the presence of antiferromagnetic interactions transmitted in the crystal lattice. Furthermore, the biological properties of the complex in the concentration range 1–100 μM were investigated in relation to its cytoprotective activity against oxidative damage generated exogenously using hydrogen peroxide in the hippocampal neuronal HT22 cell line (MTT tests). The obtained results were subsequently referred to the [amqH][VO(nta)(H2O)]H2O analogue as well as trolox and ascorbic acid, used as known antioxidants. It has been established that the title compound effectively protects HT22 from oxidative damage and is a potentially good candidate for further evaluation of the mechanism of its action.

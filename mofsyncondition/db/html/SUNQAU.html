Metal-directed assembly of organic–inorganic complexes constructed from V-shaped linkages

https://doi.org/10.1016/j.molstruc.2009.03.029Get rights and content

Abstract

Three new complexes based on V-shaped linkages 2,5-bis(4-pyridyl)-1,3,4-thiadiazole (bpt) and 4,4′-oxybis(benzoic acid) (H2oba), [Mn(bpt)(oba)(H2O)3]n·nH2O (1), [Co2(bpt)2(oba)(H2O)7]·(oba) (2), and [Cd2(oba)2(H2O)6]n·2n(bpt)·2nH2O (3) have been synthesized using different transition metal acetates. Non-covalent hydrogen bonding interactions extend complexes 1, 2 and 3 into supramolecular architectures. Complex 1 is a 3D framework, in which two types of helical H-bonded chains appear simultaneously. Complexes 2 and 3 contain 1D open channels encapsulating organic guest molecules – oba and bpt, respectively. Topologically, complex 3 is a α-Po structure. This work indicates that the building blocks oba and bpt can adopt versatile coordination modes depending on the nature of the metal centers. The elemental analysis, IR spectra, XRPD and TG analysis for three complexes, as well as luminescent properties for 3 are discussed.

1. Introduction

The current interest in supramolecular architectures arises not only from their potential applications as luminescent, magnetic, sorption, ion exchange, nonlinear optic and catalytic materials [1], but also due to their intriguing variety of topologies and structural motifs [2]. Generally, besides the strong metal–ligand coordination by covalent bonds, extended high dimensional supramolecular networks could be obtained by the assembly of low dimension coordination polymers via non-covalent intermolecular forces such as hydrogen-bonding and π–π stacking interactions [3]. For example, 2,5-bis(4-pyridyl)-1,3,4-thiadiazole (bpt) has been already employed as a semi-rigid bent bipyridine-like ligand to construct some low dimensional polymeric structures [4]. Furthermore, it has two more potential N-donor atoms than general bipyridine-like ligands and sulphur atom of thiadiazole which could act as hydrogen bond acceptors. Recently, we have reported a series of supramolecular complexes with distinct dimensionality and structures based on bpt ligand [5]. Therefore, 2,5-bis(4-pyridyl)-1,3,4-thiadiazole is an excellent candidate for the construction of supramolecular architectures.

On the other hand, during the formation of supramolecular assembly it is important to establish the possible connections between units. Multicarboxylate ligands are excellent functional building blocks acting not only as hydrogen-bonding acceptors but also as hydrogen-bonding donors, depending upon the degree of deprotonation of carboxylic groups [3](a), [6]. The introduction of an O-containing multicarboxylate auxiliary ligand could affect the self-assembly of polymer leading to structural evolution and allowing fine-tuning of the extended structural motif [7]. At present time the number of publications on bpt and O-containing auxiliary ligand is still limited [4], [5]. A systematic research is required to be developed and a rational design would be a long-range challenge.

In this work, we continue our investigation of above-mentioned problem and choose 4,4′-oxybis(benzoic acid) (H2oba) as a multicarboxylate ligand for the construction of supramolecular architectures with bpt and transition metal ions. The H2oba is a typical example of long V-shaped ligand and possesses a necessary feature for supramolecular architecture formation [8]. Using the ternary metal/bpt/oba system, we have hydrothermally prepared three neutral supramolecular complexes, namely [Mn(bpt)(oba)(H2O)3]n·nH2O (1), [Co2(bpt)2(oba)(H2O)7]·(oba) (2), and [Cd2(oba)2(H2O)6]n·2n(bpt)·2nH2O (3). To the best of our knowledge, complex 1 represents the first example in the {M-oba} system that possesses two types of helical H-bonded chains in its structure. The crystal structures and physico-chemical properties of these complexes, along with systematic investigation on the effect of mixed-ligands and metal ions on the framework structure are represented and discussed.

2. Experimental

2.1. Materials and physical measurements

All reagents were commercially available and used as received without further purification. The infrared spectra on KBr pellets were recorded on a Nicolet 170SX FT-IR spectrophotometer in the range of 4000–400 cm–1. Elemental analyses (C, H and N) were recorded on a Perkin-Elmer model 240C instrument. Luminescence spectra for the solid samples were investigated with a Hitachi F-4500 fluorescence spectrophotometer. Thermal analysis was performed on Netzsch STA 449C microanalyzer under flowing N2 atmosphere at a heating rate of 10 °C min–1. The XRPD pattern was recorded with a Pigaku D/Max 3III diffractometer.

Slow cooling of the reaction mixture to room temperature resulted in yellow crystals. Yield: 61%. Anal. Calc. (%) for C26H24MnN4O9S: C, 50.08; H, 3.88; N, 8.99. Found: C, 50.26; H, 3.64; N, 9.05. 2.3. Crystallographic data collection and refinement

Single-crystal diffraction data for compounds 1, 2 and 3 were collected on a Bruker SMART APEX CCD diffractometer with graphite-monochromated Mo-Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å). The data integration and reduction were processed with SAINT software. The structures were solved by the direct method using SHELXTL and embarked on a full-matrix least-square refinement on F2 with SHELXL-97 program [9], [10]. The structure of 3 contains the disordered thiadiazole fragment of bpt and free water molecules, which adopt two symmetrically associated positions with equal occupancies. All non-hydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically. All hydrogen atoms were placed in calculated positions and refined using a riding model. 3. Results and discussion

3.1. Synthesis

Direct mixing of the starting materials solutions leads to immediate fine precipitate formation, in order to obtain crystals, suitable for X-ray analysis, the hydrothermal method was applied. Three new complexes were synthesized successfully under the similar synthetic conditions (Scheme 1). pH value of the solution seems to play a crucial role for the synthesis of complexes 1–3, because our attempts of the acidity variation have not led to these or any other interesting products formation. The experiments with variation of the synthesis temperature and stoichiometric ratios of used precursors have also been performed, but these factors have the less influence on the formation of the complexes.

3.2. Description of the crystal structures

3.2.1. [Mn(bpt)(oba)(H2O)3]n·nH2O (1)

The elementary unit of compound 1 contains two half occupied Mn atoms, one oba ligand, one bpt ligand, three coordinated water molecules, and one free water (Fig. 1a). Mn1 has {MnO4N2} coordination environment with two oxygen atoms from monodentate oba carboxylates, two oxygen atoms from water molecules, and two axial nitrogen atoms from bpt donors. Mn2 coordinates four water molecules and two nitrogen atoms of bpt. Bond lengths and angles surrounding metal centers in 1 are consistent with a slightly distorted octahedral geometry. The Mn–O bond lengths range from 2.1443(18) to 2.1702(18) Å. The pyridyl nitrogen atoms of bpt coordinate to two Mn(II) ions (see Scheme 2c); while each oba ligand is severely bent at the ether–oxygen site (C–O–C = 121.9°) and adopts a monodentate mode to link only to one Mn(II) ion (see Scheme 3b). All Mn(II) ions are connected by bpt ligands into 1D chains with the side arms of oba (Fig. 1b). The bridging mode of the oba ligand is dissimilar to those reported recently [8], [11].

In chemical and biological systems, multiple hydrogen bonds are known to be of ultimate importance during the control of molecular self-assembly [12]. The zigzag chains of complex 1 recognize each other to construct 2D (4,4) grid parallel to the ac plane via H-bonded interactions between the uncoordinated carboxylic oxygens of the oba ligands and the coordinated water oxygen atoms (O5···O2W 2.690(3) Å) (Fig. 1c). Furthermore, the resultant 2D layers are further extended into a 3D supramolecular network, in which adjacent layers stack in the parallel alignment without positional offset along the ac plane (Fig. S1). The supramolecular network is reinforced by strong hydrogen bonding interactions between the oxygen atoms of water molecules and carboxylate oxygen atoms of oba anions (O···O 2.614(3)–2.822(3) Å).

Complex 1 possesses two types of helical H-bonded chains, which are present alternately around the different metal centers. Interestingly, each type of helical chain shows two distinct chiral helices (one is left-handed and another is right-handed). When viewed along the crystallographic b axis, the hydrogen-bonded chains around Mn1 atoms are composed of coordinated water molecules (O4W) and lattice water molecules (O1W) with a pitch of 14.7074 Å (O···O 2.684(2)–2.726(3) Å) (Fig. 1d). H-bonded helices of the second type are formed by strong hydrogen bonds between the deprotonated carboxylate oxygen atoms (O4 and O5) and the oxygen atoms (O2W and O3W) of coordinated water molecules (O···O 2.636(3)–2.822(3) Å). It can be described as (O3W···O5···O2W···O4···O3W)n chain with the same helical pitch as that of the former helices (Fig. 1e). The four parallel helices exist concurrently to result in a symmetrical 3D supramolecular network. Up to date, such hydrogen-bonded helices with four distinct chiral chains are still quite uncommon within helical coordination polymers [12](d), [13]. Obviously, these strong hydrogen bonds guide the extending direction of the helix [14]. To the best of our knowledge, complex 1 represents the first example of metal/oba complex containing two types of hydrogen-bonded helices.

3.2.2. [Co2(bpt)2(oba)(H2O)7]·(oba) (2)

Replacement of manganese acetate by cobalt acetate in the precursors mixture leads to complex 2 formation. Crystal structure of 2 consists of cationic binuclear units and oba counterions. There are two crystallographically independent Co(II) centers in an asymmetric unit. As illustrated in Fig. 2a, the Co1 center represents a slightly distorted octahedron, defined by two axial nitrogen atoms from two different bpt groups (Co–N 2.151(11)–2.197(11) Å) and four oxygen atoms of four water molecules (Co–O 2.076(8)–2.113(8) Å); Co2 adopts a malformed octahedral coordination environment, composed by three oxygen atoms from one chelating carboxyl of oba and one water molecule (Co–O 2.089(6)–2.252(8) Å) and one bpt nitrogen atom (Co–N 2.177(11) Å) located at an equatorial plane, and two oxygen atoms at the axial positions (Co–O 2.028(7)–2.049(6) Å). The bpt ligands link the Co(II) centers in monodentate and bis(monodentate) bridging modes (Scheme 2b and c). The oba ligands are bent (C–O–C = 123.3(10)–125.8(10)°) less than in the case of 1. The coordination modes of oba ligands in complexes 1 and 2 are different and each of them in complex 2 adopts chelating bidentate or free mode (Scheme 3a and c).

For each unit, the uncoordinated carboxylate O atoms of oba and the bound water molecules are involved in three intermolecular hydrogen bonding interactions (O···O 2.699(11)–2.899(12) Å), linking four adjacent units and thus, resulting in a 2D lamellar supramolecular network along the crystallographic (1 1 0) plane as depicted in (Fig. 2b). Such 2D layers are strengthened by significant aromatic π−π stacking between bpt and oba ligands with the distance of 3.64 Å. In the solid state, these layers are stacked in an antiparallel ABAB repeat fashion into a dense 3D H-bonded framework with irregular channels occupied by free oba anions (O···O 2.676(13)–2.780(12); O···N 2.767(13) Å) (Fig. 2c).

3.2.3. [Cd2(oba)2(H2O)6]n·2n(bpt)·2nH2O (3)

The polymer 3 elementary unit contains one Cd(II) cation, one oba anion, three coordinated water molecules, one free bpt and one free water. As illustrated in Fig. 3a, the Cd(II) center coordinates to five oxygen atoms of two chelating carboxylates of two oba and one water (Cd–O 2.270(8)–2.417(5) Å) in the equatorial plane, and to two axial water molecules (Cd–O 2.309(8)–2.356(8) Å), which can be described as a distorted pentagonal bipyramid geometry. Each bent oba ligand adopts a bis(chelating bidentate) mode (C–O–C = 121.1(8)°) (Scheme 3d), linking two Cd(II) ions to produce a chain (Fig. 3b).

Like in the previous cases plentifulness of H-bonds ensure the higher dimensional framework construction. Therefore, the chains are interconnected into regular rectangular grids by hydrogen bonding between carboxylic groups and jointed water molecules (O3···O6 2.750(8) Å) (Fig. S2). Additionally, the free water molecules behave as both hydrogen-bonding acceptors and donors providing three kinds of hydrogen bonds towards carboxylic oxygen atom O3 and coordinated water spacers O4 and O5 (O···O 2.668(15)–2.931(15) Å). These additional interactions have an important effect on the stability of the whole framework and lead to a pillared-grid structure with large pores, which are found to be filled in with large organic guest bpt molecules (Fig. 3c). Noteworthy, two different molecular porous frameworks occur during the self-assembly of the complexes 2 and 3. Up to now, some porous materials with lots of small guest molecules have been documented, which have been described in three comprehensive reviews by Yaghi [15], Kitagawa [16], and Férey [17]. However, the frameworks encapsulating large guest organic neutral spacers are few [4](c), [18]. Topologically, denoting Cd(II) centers as nodes and hydrogen bonds and oba ions as linkers, the whole structure can be presented as a six-connected cuboid α-Po network (Fig. 3d). Prior and Li have also exhibited two examples of supramolecular networks with α-Po topology [19].

3.2.4. Effect of metal ions nature on the framework structure

The complexes 1–3 structures are completely different due to the changes of metal centers, and the frameworks of the complexes are directly related to the coordination environment of the metal ions. Analysing the structural descriptions above, 1 and 3 represent a 1D polymeric chain while 2 consists of discrete binuclear units. Both of the Mn(II) and Co(II) ions (in 1 and 2) are six-coordinated in distorted octahedral geometries, but show different environments. In complex 1, Mn1 is coordinated to two bpt and two oba ions, and Mn2 is coordinated to two bpt spacers. Alternatively, there are only two ligands around each Co(II) ion in complex 2. This is consistent with the fact that the ionic radius of Mn(II) is larger than that of Co(II). In complex 3, Cd(II) ion adopts a seven-coordinated mode with the distorted pentagonal bipyramid geometry, which may be based on the effect of ionic radius. A rational choice of metal ions is crucial for the formation of novel higher dimensional networks.

3.2.5. Coordination of organic ligands

The connectivities of the complexes strongly depend on the coordination modes of the organic ligand. For multicarboxylate anion/neutral organic ligand system, the synergistic effect of mixed-ligands plays an important role in the formation and structure of the complexes. It is well known that multicarboxylate ligands are good candidates for the construction of complexes with specific structure attributes. In present work, the semi-rigid bpt spacers in three complexes obtained have three distinct linking modes (Scheme 2). Similarly, the oba ligands also adopt four different coordination modes (as shown in Scheme 3) providing various hydrogen-bonding possibilities with the carboxylic oxygens as hydrogen-bonding acceptors. Taking 1 as an example, the oba ligand adopts a monodentate mode as the side arm and the pyridyl nitrogen atoms of bpt coordinate to two Mn(II) ions resulting in the infinite chain. In the case of 2, oba ion exists in mono(chelating bidentate) and free modes, bpt also adopts two monodentate and bidentate linking modes. The oba ligand in 3 with a bis(chelating bidentate) mode is coordinated to metal centers to produce the zigzag chain; bpt is free and fills the channels of the 3D framework. The different coordination modes of oba and bpt ligands are decisive factors during the final covalent structures formation and the enlargement of resultant frameworks through supramolecular interactions. The mixed-ligand strategy seems to be a successful way to assemble programmed coordination frameworks.

3.3. XRPD, thermal analysis, and fluorescent properties

To confirm the phase purity of complexes 1–3, the original powder samples were characterized by X-ray powder diffraction (XRPD) at room temperature. XRPD patterns of the bulk products match well with those simulated from the single-crystal structure data (Fig. S3).

Thermal stability of synthesized complexes was investigated by thermogravimetry (Fig. 4). For 1, the weight loss of 11.69% detected between 87 and 186 °C corresponds to the loss of structural water molecules (calcd. 11.55%). After heating to 276 °C, compound 1 begins to lose the bpt and oba moieties. Finally, the residual weight of 14.04% is in accordance with the percentage (calcd. 11.40%) of MnO. Complex 2 loses water molecules below 177 °C (observed 12.33%, calcd. 10.19%), at higher temperatures consecutive loss of organic ligands happened. Remaining weight of 15.92% at 618 °C is in agreement with the proportional weight (calcd. 12.52%) of CoO. Complex 3 loses water molecules between 70 and 149 °C (observed 6.72%, calcd. 10.57%). Remaining weight of 28.41% (calcd. 28.92%) indicates that the final product is CdO.

The emission spectrum of complex 3 in the solid state was investigated at room temperature (Fig. 5). Excitation at 300 nm of 3 produces one fluorescent emission peak at about 441 nm. The bpt behaves a relatively sharp band at about 385 nm [5], while there is no obvious emission observed for the free oba ligand under the same experimental conditions [8d]. The fluorescent emissions of 3 may therefore be attributed to charge transfer transition between the ligands and cadmium centers (LMCT) [20]. The significant redshift of the band in contrast to that of bpt ligand can be attributed to the metal–ligand coordination interactions.

4. Conclusion

The combination of flexible 4,4′-oxybis(benzoic acid) and 2,5-bis(4-pyridyl)-1,3,4-thiadiazole ligands with transition metal ions (MnII, CoII and CdII) under the same synthetic conditions results in the formation of three different complexes. Multiple helical H-bonded chains and the unique porous networks filled with large guest organic molecules possess excellent structural properties. I was found, that metal ion nature and the synergistic effect of mixed-ligands play an important role in modulating the resulting frameworks. Thus, it could be predicted that more metal complexes containing bipyridine-like ligands and flexible multicarboxylates with fascinating structural properties are to be designed and synthesized.

  • Two novel interpenetrating MOFs constructed from a derivative of phenanthroline and a V-shaped flexible dicarboxylate ligand contains unique chiral structure

    2012, Inorganica Chimica Acta

    Based on 1H-imidazo[4,5-f][1,10]-phenanthroline (IP), 4,4′-oxybis(benzoic acid) (H2oba), and two different metal salts, two novel complexes were hydrothermally constructed, {[Cd2(oba)2(IP)2(H2O)]·H2O}n (1) and {[Mn2(IP)4(oba)2]·(oba)(H2O)2}n (2). Complex 1 exhibits a rare 2D → 2D parallel interpenetration layer , which contains two same-hand chiral sheets with the P1¯ space group. Complex 2 contains 1D left-hand helical chain with the C2 space group. Meanwhile, the dissociate oba and water molecules are connected to be a right-hand helical chain via hydrogen bonds, and form a twofold-interpenetrating structure. In addition, the two compounds also exhibit good thermal stability.

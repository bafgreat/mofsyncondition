Syntheses, structures and luminescence of three copper(I) cyanide coordination polymers incorporating flexible N-donor ligands

Abstract

Three copper(I) cyanide coordination polymers, namely [Cu8(CN)8(btmb)2]n (1), [Cu(CN)(btmb)0.5]n (2) and [Cu2(CN)2(dpa)]n (3) (btmb = 1,4-bis(1,2,4-triazol-1-ylmethyl)benzene, dpa = di(4-pyridyl)amine), were synthesized under hydrothermal conditions and characterized by elemental analyses, IR spectra, PXRD and thermogravimetric analyses. Single-crystal X-ray diffraction analyses reveal that complex 1 is an interesting 2D double-layered network constructing from eight distinct Cu(I) centers, eight μ2-cyanides and two tetradentate cis-btmb ligands. Complex 2 is a ladder-like coordination polymer assembled by CuCN linear chain and bidentate trans-btmb linker. Complex 3 has a 2D wave-like network constructing from CuCN zigzag chain and V-shape dpa spacer. These coordination polymers are thermally stable up to 270–300 °C. They emit blue luminescence originating from ligand-centered emission.

Graphical abstract

Three copper(I) cyanide coordination polymers were constructed based on two flexible N-heterocyclic ligands. [Cu8(CN)8(btmb)2]n (1) is a 2D double-layered network containing eight distinct Cu(I) centers and eight μ2-cyanides. Complex 2 shows a ladder-like chain structure, while complex 3 exhibits a 2D wave-like network. These Cu(I) complexes are stable up to 270–300 °C and emit blue luminescence.

The design, synthesis and crystal engineering of coordination polymers continue to be a quite active research field [1], [2], [3], owing to their intriguing structural motifs and potential applications as functional materials [4], [5], [6]. Much effort has been focused on the rational design and controlled synthesis of novel coordination polymers using various multidentate ligands. Analogous to halide, cyanide as a diatomic pseudohalide ligand is a worthy connector in constructing coordination polymers [7], [8]. As a small anion with little steric hindrance, cyanide is a perfect linear linker with strong coordination ability to transitional metal ions. It is well known that CuCN has a 1D wave-like chain, where Cu(I) features a low 2-coordinated center [9], [10]. Cu(I) is in favor of trigonal or tetrahedral coordination in the presence of halide/pseudohalide and N-heterocyclic ligands [11], [12], [13]. The CuCN chain has a strong tendency to acquire N-heterocyclic ligand to extend the 2-coordinated Cu(I) to 3- or 4-coordinated center. The synthetic strategy of copper(I) cyanide coordination polymer is either combining N-heterocycle to the CuCN chain [14], [15], or using Cu(II) salt as precursor and K3[Fe(CN)6] as cyanide source to afford Cu(I)-cyanide complex under hydrothermal condition [16]. Many N-heterocyclic compounds are good molecular building blocks in constructing coordination polymers with interesting structures and properties. Imidazole, triazole, tetrazole and pyridyl derivatives exhibit diverse coordination modes, which are frequently used to construct various coordination polymers [17], [18], [19]. To date, a number of Cu(I)-cyanide coordination polymers incorporating N-heterocycles have been documented [20], [21], [22], [23]. Cu(I)-cyanide system is still an active research area with special attention paid to their versatile coordination structures and varied luminescence.

In the course of our investigation to Cu(I)-cyanide coordination polymers [24], [25], [26], [27], [28], three novel complexes, [Cu8(CN)8(btmb)2]n (1), [Cu(CN)(btmb)0.5]n (2) and [Cu2(CN)2(dpa)]n (3), were synthesized by using two flexible N-donor ligands, 1,4-bis(1,2,4-triazol-1-ylmethyl)benzene (btmb) and di(4-pyridyl)amine (dpa). CuCN and btmb reacted with/without K3[Fe(CN)6] to afford two obviously different complexes 1 and 2 [29]. However, CuCN and dpa reacted with/without K3[Fe(CN)6] only afforded complex 3. K3[Fe(CN)6] was used as a supplementary cyanide source that can slowly release cyanide upon heat treatment [30], [31]. Infrared spectra exhibit a characteristic cyanide νC ≡ N band at 2133 cm− 1 for 1, 2126 cm− 1 for 2, and two νC ≡ N bands at 2121, 2096 cm− 1 for 3. Complexes 1 and 2 show btmb bands near 1521, 1297, 1141 and 720 cm− 1. Complex 3 displays an amino NH band of dpa at 3315 cm− 1. The CC stretching vibration and CH out-of-plane bending vibration of pyridyl group occur at 1592 and 815 cm− 1, respectively.

X-ray structural analysis [32] reveals that [Cu8(CN)8(btmb)2]n (1) assumes a 2D double-layered network constructing from 1D CuCN chain and tetradentate btmb ligand. The asymmetric unit contains eight crystallographically independent Cu(I) ions, eight cyanides and two btmb ligands. As shown in Fig. 1a, all the Cu(I) centers adopt a trigonal geometry, coordinated by two cyanides and a triazole N-donor. The bond distances and angles around eight Cu(I) centers are slightly different (Table S1, Supporting Information). Sixteen CuC/N(cyanide) bond distances are shorter, varying from 1.861(10) to 1.941(16) Å; while eight CuN(triazole) bond distances are longer, varying from 2.022(8) to 2.117(10) Å.

Complex 1 has eight distinct μ2-cyanides. Each of them links two different Cu(I) centers via both C and N terminals. This connection produces two similar CuCN wave-like chains (Fig. S1, Supporting Information). One CuCN chain is consisting of Cu1, Cu2, Cu7 and Cu8 centers. Another CuCN chain is composed of Cu3, Cu4, Cu5 and Cu6. The CNCuCN bond angles are in the 135.6(5)–142.4(4)° range. Both btmb ligands adopt a tetradentate coordination mode. Two triazole rings present cis-conformation. In one btmb (N2, N4, N6 and N8), two triazole rings show a dihedral angle of 19.6(4)°, which are nearly perpendicular with the central phenyl ring (dihedral angles 81.5(4) and 79.8(4)°). In another btmb (N12, N14, N18 and N20), two triazole rings present a smaller dihedral angle of 15.9(4)° and nearly perpendicular with the central phenyl ring (dihedral angles 82.1(2)°). Each btmb ligand combines four Cu(I) ions in four adjacent CuCN chains through two bidentate triazole groups. Two kinds of CuCN chains are connected by btmb ligands to afford a complicate 2D coordination network (Fig. 1b). Viewed along the b-axis direction, complex 1 exhibits an interesting 2D double-layered network, which is further parallel packed, as shown in Fig. 1c.

[Cu(CN)(btmb)0.5]n (2) displays a 1D ladder-like chain. The asymmetric unit contains an independent Cu(I) ion, a cyanide and a half btmb ligand. As shown in Fig. 2a, Cu1 adopts a trigonal geometry, coordinated by two cyanides and a triazole N-donor. The CuC1, CuN4C and CuN1 bond distances are 1.867(3), 1.904(3) and 2.097(3) Å, respectively. The C1Cu1N4C bond angle is 150.89(12)°. Cyanide acts as a μ2-CN bridge and links Cu(I) ions to form a CuCN linear chain. Obviously different with complex 1, btmb is a bidentate ligand and links two Cu(I) ions via two monodentate triazole groups. The flexible btmb displays a trans-conformation. Two triazole rings are parallel (dihedral angle 0.1(1)°) to each other, and nearly perpendicular with the central phenyl ring (dihedral angle 83.7(1)°). Two identical CuCN linear chains are parallel arranged and further connected by btmb linker to construct a ladder-like chain coordination polymer (Fig. 2b). These ladder-like chains are parallel packed to form a supramolecular system (Fig. S2).

[Cu2(CN)2(dpa)]n (3) exhibits a 2D wave-like network. The asymmetric unit is composed of two independent Cu(I) ions, two cyanides and a dpa ligand. As shown in Fig. 3a, Cu1 and Cu2 both adopt a trigonal geometry. Cu1 is coordinated by two cyanide C-donors and pyridyl N1 atom. Cu2 is coordinated by two cyanide N-donors and pyridyl N3B atom. The bond distances and angles around Cu1 and Cu2 are slightly different. The Cucyanide bond distances vary from 1.887(5) to 1.970(4) Å, while the Cu1N1 and Cu2N3B bond distances are 2.064(4) and 2.044(4) Å, respectively. Two distinct cyanides act as μ2-CN bridge to link Cu1 and Cu2, resulting in the formation of a 1D zigzag chain. Along the CuCN zigzag chain, the C11Cu1C12 and N4ACu2N5 bond angles are 137.2(2) and 132.7(2)°, respectively. The dpa is a V-shape bidentate ligand. Two pyridyl rings twist about 30° around the flexible amino N2 atom. The CuCN zigzag chains are parallel arranged along the a-axis direction and pillared by dpa spacer to generate a 2D wave-like network (Fig. 3b). Two identical 2D networks are parallel packed to afford a supramolecular system (Fig. S3).

Three Cu(I) complexes are stable in air. Their thermogravimetric analyses are performed in the temperature range of 20–800 °C (Fig. S5). Two btmb complexes 1 and 2 show similar thermal stability. Both complexes remain thermally stable up to 270 °C, and then successively decompose to 800 °C without stopping. The dpa complex 3 is stable to 300 °C, and then successively decomposes to 800 °C without stopping. Thermogravimetric analyses demonstrate that anhydrous complexes 1–3 are quite thermally stable. Furthermore, powder X-ray diffraction (PXRD) experiments are carried out to check their phase purity in products. The PXRD patterns of 1–3 are given in Fig. S6. The peak positions of the experimental and simulated PXRD patterns are in good agreement with each other, indicating that the crystal structures are truly representative of the bulk crystal products.

It is well known that Cu(I) with d10 electronic configuration exhibits varied luminescence in various complexes. Organic ligands and coordination modes remarkably affect their emission wavelength and mechanism [34], [35]. The luminescent behaviors of complexes 1–3 are investigated at room temperature in the solid-state. The emission spectra are shown in Fig. 4, while the excitation spectra are described in Fig. S7. Upon excitation at 420 nm, two btmb complexes 1 and 2 exhibit similar blue luminescence with emission maxima at 468 and 467 nm, respectively. In contrast to the emission peak of free btmb at 455 nm, similar emission energies indicate that the most possible mechanism of complexes 1 and 2 is a ligand-centered emission (Fig. S8). Complex 3 shows a strong emission peak at 469 nm under the excitation of 350 nm light, which is similar to the emission peak of free dpa at 465 nm. The luminescence of complex 3 is also assigned to ligand-centered emission.

In conclusion, three copper(I) cyanide coordination polymers have been constructed by tuning different N-heterocyclic ligands and varied synthesis conditions. [Cu8(CN)8(btmb)2]n (1) is an interesting 2D double-layered coordination polymer containing eight independent Cu(I) centers, eight μ2-cyanides and two tetradentate btmb ligands. Complex 2 a 1D ladder-like chain, and complex 3 is a 2D wave-like network. These results show that the flexible 1D CuCN chain is an excellent linear molecular building block in constructing coordination polymers with N-heterocyclic compounds. Three complexes are thermally stable up to 270–300 °C, and emit blue luminescence originating from ligand-centered emission.
